# City 809

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW
### OVERVIEW — Post-Apocalyptic Creature-Hunting Mega-City
A collapsed Asian mega-city — think ruined Seoul or Shanghai — where humanity survives in fractured districts surrounded by territorial creatures. There are no superpowers, no magic, no mutations. You are what you carry. Progression is entirely equipment-based: kill creatures, harvest materials, modify your gear. An M4A1 won't pierce an exosuit, but an M4A1 modified with high-tier creature parts will. Weapons run on monster energy cores instead of traditional ammunition (cooldown-based, not bullet-counted). Stock pre-apocalypse gear exists but is rare and expensive — the common economy runs on monster-modified equipment.

The city is a pressure cooker. Human territory is finite. Creatures hold the deep zones and outer wilderness. As populations grow, factions are forced to either mount expeditions into creature territory (dangerous, costly) or wage war on neighboring factions (also costly). Both thin the population. The survivors return stronger. The cycle repeats — and the intervals between culls are getting shorter.

**Tone:** GRITTY, OPPRESSIVE, TENSE. NEVER PEACEFUL. DENSE URBAN SURVIVAL WHERE EVERY BULLET MATTERS AND EVERY PERSON IS A THREAT.

### OVERVIEW — The Pressure Loop
The world runs on a cycle: population grows → need more space/resources → two choices: hunt creatures (expeditions) or fight humans (gang war) → both cull the population → survivors return with better gear and materials → population grows again → repeat. The interval between these culls is shrinking. The creatures are pushing inward, the factions are pushing outward, and the space in between is a slaughterhouse.

### OVERVIEW — Equipment Tiers
- **Tier 1: Stock gear.** Standard firearms (M4A1, pistols, shotguns), basic armor. Handles weak creatures. Cannot pierce exosuits or armored targets. Rare and expensive — pre-apocalypse relics.
- **Tier 2: Monster-modified gear.** Standard weapons modified with creature materials — bone-penetrator rounds, chitin-reinforced barrels, venom coatings, creature-sinew bowstrings. The baseline for most survivors. Can pierce exosuits and mid-tier creatures.
- **Tier 3: Exosuit + modified gear.** Pre-apocalypse powered armor frames — the base is just a start. The real power comes from creature-part upgrades bolted onto them: chitin armor plating, creature-core-powered servos, bone-reinforced joints, biologically-derived sensors. The ugliest exosuit is usually the most dangerous one. Pristine exosuits are either unused or owned by someone who hasn't fought enough to need upgrades. Only monster-modified anti-armor weapons can damage them. Whoever controls exosuits controls the power dynamic.

### OVERVIEW — Ammunition System
Weapons use monster energy cores instead of traditional bullets. The core's tier determines fire rate, damage, and special properties (e.g., a fire-attribute core shoots incendiary rounds). Cores recharge over time — effectively cooldown-based. No bullet counting. The AI GM tracks "your core needs to recharge" instead of ammunition.

## 2. FACTIONS
### FACTION — Sector 7 Remnant
**Type:** Military Remnant / Authoritarian Regime
**Key Members:** TBD
**Stance:** Order through force. Civilians are resources to be managed.
Sector 7 Remnant controls the North District — a former military base and surrounding residential blocks, now a fortress. Barracks-style housing, training yards, armories. Civilians within their territory are "volunteers" — conscripted into work details: farming, construction, salvage runs. Strict curfews, roll calls, rationed supplies. They are disciplined, organized, and effective at keeping creatures out — but life under their rule is suffocating. Forced labor is the price of safety. Their military structure makes them one of the most combat-effective factions, but their rigid hierarchy creates internal resentment. Deserters exist and are punished severely.

### FACTION — Saebyeok Group
**Type:** Corporate Remnant / Chaebol
**Key Members:** TBD
**Stance:** Everything has a price. If you can't pay, you don't deserve to be here.
Saebyeok Group controls the East Financial District — former corporate towers and luxury apartments, now rented living space. The old chaebol structure survived the apocalypse. Everything costs gold: rent, food, water, safety, medical care. Can't pay your weekly due? You're escorted to the border and left in the wilderness. Clean, organized, and soulless. The wealthy live high up in the towers with views; the poor cram into lower floors near street level. They have the best crafting infrastructure and trade networks — their gear is high quality and always for sale. Neutral toward other factions on the surface, but economic warfare is their specialty.

### FACTION — Blackmarket Collective
**Type:** Criminal Syndicate / Loose Gang Alliance
**Key Members:** TBD
**Stance:** Pay for protection or join the family. There is no third option.
The Blackmarket Collective controls the Southeast Old Town — historic district, narrow alleys, old night markets. Now a lawless bazaar where multiple gangs operate under a loose collective banner. Protection fees, smuggling dens, black market gear, underground fighting pits, gambling halls. Loud, chaotic, dangerous — but you can find anything here. No single leader; instead, gang bosses negotiate through a council that breaks down into violence regularly. The Pleasure Guild and black market trade thrive here. Travelers come for the deals, stay because they owe someone money.

### FACTION — The Cradle
**Type:** Survivor Commune / Emerging Warlord State
**Key Members:** TBD
**Stance:** We're building something better. (But someone has to be in charge.)
The Cradle occupies the Southwest Residential Zone — former suburban apartment blocks, parks, schools. A commune founded on democratic ideals: town halls, shared resources, community gardens. But underneath the idealism, three or four strong internal factions are quietly arming up and positioning for control. The democratic structure is eroding. Community BBQs happen next to stockpiled weapons. The idealists still believe; the pragmatists know blood is coming. This is a warlord state in larval form — unstable, volatile, and dangerous because nobody agrees on who's in charge yet.

### FACTION — Shěn Territory
**Type:** Established Warlord Domain
**Key Members:** [Shěn — founder and ruler]
**Stance:** One ruler. One rule. No questions.
Shěn Territory controls the West Industrial Zone — former factory district, warehouses, shipping yards. The oldest established warlord territory in the city. Shěn built walls, organized labor, and established order years ago when everyone else was still scrambling. Stable and prosperous by apocalypse standards. Absolutely authoritarian. Shěn's word is law, enforced by loyal enforcers. In exchange for obedience, residents get safety, food, and purpose. The system works — which makes it hard to argue against, even for people who hate it. The Bulwark of the city. If Shěn falls, everything west of the Core becomes a warzone.

## 3. LOCATIONS
### LOCATION — The Core
**Type:** District / Central Hub
**Status:** Contested Neutral Zone
The most intact part of the city. Former central business district with government buildings. High-rises still standing, power still runs in some buildings. No single faction controls it — shared by guild headquarters and trading posts. The Hunting Board, Artisan Guild, and other neutral guilds operate here. Safe but expensive. You pay to be here. No creature threat, but lots of eyes watching everyone. Armed civilians everywhere. Neon still flickers. Feels like civilization held on by its fingernails. **Vibe:** Tense peace. The closest thing to normal life — which makes it the most dangerous place to let your guard down.

### LOCATION — North District (Sector 7 Remnant Territory)
**Type:** District / Military Fortress
**Status:** Fortified / Controlled
Former military base and surrounding residential blocks, now a fortress. Barracks-style housing, training yards, armories. Strict perimeter defense. Creature incursions are rare here — the Remnant's military discipline keeps the walls secure. Civilians live in organized blocks, assigned to work details. Curfew is absolute. **Vibe:** Concrete bunker city. Everyone's in uniform or should be. Quiet at night — because it has to be. Efficient, grim, suffocating.

### LOCATION — East Financial District (Saebyeok Group Territory)
**Type:** District / Corporate Enclave
**Status:** Flourishing (for those who can pay)
Former corporate towers and luxury apartments. The towers are residential blocks rented by the week. Malls converted to markets where everything has a price tag. Clean, organized, well-maintained — because neglect is bad for business. The wealthy live high up with views; the poor cram into lower floors near street level where creature proximity is higher. **Vibe:** Cold capitalism with a nice paint job. Glass and steel. Transactional relationships everywhere.

### LOCATION — Southeast Old Town (Blackmarket Collective Territory)
**Type:** District / Lawless Market
**Status:** Chaotic / Unstable
Historic district with narrow alleys and old night markets. Now a sprawling lawless bazaar. Multiple gangs, constant low-level conflict, black market everything. Neon and noise. Crowded streets, handshakes in alleys, knife fights over deals gone wrong. Never sleeps. The closest thing to anarchy — but anarchy with a protection fee. **Vibe:** Sensory overload. Dangerous but exciting. The place you go when you need something nobody else will sell you.

### LOCATION — Southwest Residential Zone (The Cradle Territory)
**Type:** District / Commune
**Status:** Unstable / Fragmenting
Former suburban apartment blocks, parks, schools. Community gardens, town halls, shared resource centers. On the surface: a functioning commune with democratic processes. Below the surface: factional power struggles, weapons caches, political assassinations. Friendly neighbors who might kill each other next month. **Vibe:** False warmth. The smile that doesn't reach the eyes. A place that could be paradise if people stopped reaching for power.

### LOCATION — West Industrial Zone (Shěn Territory)
**Type:** District / Industrial Fortress
**Status:** Stable / Authoritarian
Former factory district with warehouses and shipping yards. Shěn built walls from corrugated steel and salvaged concrete. Organized labor forces work in shifts. Patrol routes run 24/7. Smoke stacks still operational. Efficient, grim, and secure — the price of order is absolute obedience. Shěn's enforcers are visible on every corner. **Vibe:** Industrial fortress. Grime and purpose. The machine works, but the machine owns you.

### LOCATION — The Frontier Ring
**Type:** Region / Hunting Grounds
**Status:** Contested / Dangerous
The outer districts — former suburbs, industrial parks, city outskirts. The border between human territory and creature zones. Abandoned buildings, overgrown streets, creature nests in collapsed structures. The Farmer Guild works whatever cleared land they can hold. Expeditions launch from here. The most dangerous human-accessible space — but the most rewarding. Creature density increases the further out you go. High-tier materials come from here. So do high-tier casualties. **Vibe:** Quiet tension. Every shadow could be a creature. Hunters prep at the edge and head out. Sometimes they come back.

### LOCATION — The Dead Zones
**Type:** No-Go Areas / Transit Hazards
**Status:** Ruined / Creature-Infested
Former major highways, bridges, subway tunnels, train stations — now collapsed infrastructure turned creature habitat or kill zones. The reason travel between districts is dangerous. Crossing a dead bridge might take you through a creature nest, a sniper ambush, or both. The Courier Guild makes their money knowing which routes are passable this week. **Vibe:** Silence and rot. Wind through broken concrete. You don't stop here. You don't linger. You move fast and pray.

### LOCATION — The Grid
**Type:** POI / Power Plant
**Status:** Automated / Unmanned
Automated geothermal power station still running on pre-apocalypse systems. No permanent garrison, no staff. Power allocation controlled by manual switches and routing stations. Whoever holds the switch room controls who gets power. Faction missions: divert power from enemy territory to ally territory (soft attack), guard the allocation panel, restore a sabotaged line. No defenders — you show up, hold it, flip the switch, leave before someone else arrives.

### LOCATION — The Siphon
**Type:** POI / Water Treatment
**Status:** Automated / Unmanned
Automated desalination and filtration facility near the coast. Pipeline valves determine which districts get clean water. Manual controls, no guards. The Farmer Guild's survival depends on this place, but they can't hold it alone. Faction missions: open valves to your territory, close enemy valves, protect the valve station during drought. Sabotaging water supply is one of the most aggressive acts short of open war.

### LOCATION — The Pit
**Type:** POI / Creature Processing Yard
**Status:** Abandoned / Uncontrolled
Abandoned industrial facility with creature-processing equipment still intact. Completely empty until someone drags a kill here. No authority, no rules, no queue system. Whoever's there first controls the breakdown station. Hunters from rival factions running into each other here is a powder keg. High-value, high-tension. Black market deals happen in the shadows of legitimate processing.

### LOCATION — The Depot
**Type:** POI / Cargo Hub
**Status:** Automated / Unmanned
Automated cargo routing system. Old trains still run on fixed loops between districts on a schedule. Nobody controls the system — but you can reroute, hijack, or guard specific shipments. Faction missions: reroute enemy cargo, guard your faction's shipment, intercept suspected smuggled weapons. The schedule is public knowledge, which makes every shipment a potential ambush.

## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES
### POWER_SYSTEM — Equipment-Based Progression
No superpowers, no magic, no mutations, no supernatural abilities. A person's combat capability is determined entirely by their gear. There is no leveling up. There is no hidden potential. A veteran hunter and a rookie with the same gear are dangerous at roughly the same level — the veteran just makes fewer mistakes.

**Core Rules:**
- Weapons use monster energy cores as ammunition. Core tier determines damage, fire rate, and special properties. Cores recharge over time (cooldown-based). No bullet counting.
- Stock pre-apocalypse weapons (unmodified M4A1, standard pistols) exist but are rare, expensive, and cannot pierce exosuits or heavy creature armor.
- Monster-modified weapons are the standard. Creature materials applied to weapons grant armor penetration, special damage types (fire, corrosive, shock depending on creature source), and improved performance.
- Exosuits are pre-apocalypse powered armor. Extremely rare. Turn the wearer into a walking tank. Only monster-modified anti-armor weapons can damage them.
- Creature materials vary by creature type and tier. Higher-tier creatures yield better materials for stronger modifications.

**Hard Limits:**
- No human can exceed what their gear allows. Take away a hunter's weapon and they are a normal person.
- Exosuits cannot be manufactured — only found, salvaged, or taken. They are a finite resource. However, exosuit frames can be upgraded with creature parts indefinitely — a well-maintained and upgraded frame can outperform a pristine one. The limitation is materials and the skill of the artisan doing the modifications.
- Monster energy cores are consumed and degraded through use. Even the best core eventually needs replacement.
- There is no way to enhance a human body beyond natural capability. No gene mods, no cybernetics, no magical augmentation.

## 6. ECONOMY
### ECONOMY — Gold + Creature Materials
**Currency:** Gold (universal standard — pre-apocalypse currency that held value because everyone agreed it did)
**Secondary Currency:** Creature materials (barter economy, especially outside the Core)

**Cost Anchors:**
- Basic meal: 5 gold
- A night's shelter (shared space): 10 gold
- Stock standard sidearm (unmodified): 500 gold (rare, collector's item)
- Basic monster-modified sidearm: 150 gold
- Basic monster-modified rifle: 400 gold
- Mid-tier monster-modified rifle: 1,200 gold
- High-tier monster-modified weapon: 5,000+ gold
- Low-tier energy core: 50 gold
- Mid-tier energy core: 300 gold
- High-tier energy core: 2,000+ gold
- Exosuit (if you can find someone willing to sell): 100,000+ gold or not for sale at any price

**Income Brackets:**
- Scavenger/beggar: 10-20 gold/week
- Basic laborer: 30-50 gold/week
- Hunter (low-tier jobs): 100-200 gold/week
- Hunter (mid-tier jobs): 500-1,000 gold/week
- Hunter (high-tier expeditions): 2,000+ gold/week
- Faction soldier: 50-150 gold/week (plus room/board)
- Guild artisan: 200-600 gold/week

**Scarcity Notes:**
- Stock pre-apocalypse weapons appreciate in value — they're artifacts
- Creature materials from high-tier creatures are the most valuable trade goods
- Saebyeok Group controls the most formal pricing structure
- Blackmarket Collective prices fluctuate wildly — sometimes cheaper, sometimes double
- The Farmer Guild trades food at stable rates — their pricing is the most consistent in the city

**Black Market:**
- Stolen faction gear, banned weapon modifications, poisoned cores
- Counterfeit creature materials (dangerous — may catastrophically fail in combat)
- Smuggled exosuit parts
- Information on POI schedules, faction patrol routes, hunting ground locations

## 7. EVENTS
### EVENT — The Conglomerate Parade Disaster (Year 4)
The Conglomerate — a pre-apocalypse alliance of corporations and government remnants that attempted to maintain unified order in the early years — captured a high-tier creature and paraded it through the city center in a show of force and control. The creature broke free in the middle of the parade route, killing hundreds including most of the Conglomerate's leadership. The organization collapsed overnight. This event is why no unified government exists today — nobody trusts a single authority after the Conglomerate's hubris caused the worst single-day death toll since the creatures first appeared.

### EVENT — The Wave Sequence (Year 0)
The apocalypse arrived in three phases over the course of roughly one year. First, ocean creatures went hyper-aggressive — boat travel became impossible, coastal areas were abandoned, the city's port was destroyed. Second, sky creatures appeared — aircraft were grounded, rooftop movement became dangerous, air supply lines were cut. Third, ground creatures emerged from the earth — subway tunnels, sewers, building foundations. By the end of Year 0, the city was sealed. No escape by sea, air, or land. The population was trapped with the creatures.

### EVENT — The Shěn Consolidation (Year 2)
While most of the city was still in chaos, a figure known only as Shěn organized the West Industrial Zone into the first stable human territory. Walls were built, labor was organized, creature incursions were repelled. Shěn's success became the template — over the next several years, other factions consolidated their own territories. The current faction map is a direct result of who got organized fastest in those early years.

### EVENT — The Core Economy Emerges (Year 3)
Early survivors discovered that creature energy cores could power weapons and that creature materials could modify equipment. The first artisan workshops appeared. The Hunting Board was established to organize creature-hunting contracts. The gold standard was re-adopted as currency. This was the moment the city shifted from pure survival mode to a functioning (if brutal) economy. Everything about daily life now traces back to this discovery.

## 8. TIMELINE
- **Year 0** — The Wave Sequence. Ocean creatures go aggressive. Sky creatures appear. Ground creatures emerge. City sealed. Mass casualties. Central authority collapses.
- **Year 1** — Chaos. Scattered survivor groups. No organized territories. Constant creature attacks. Population drops sharply.
- **Year 2** — Shěn consolidates the West Industrial Zone. First stable territory. Proof that humans can hold ground against creatures. Other groups begin organizing.
- **Year 3** — Creature energy cores and material modification discovered. Core economy emerges. The Hunting Board established. Gold currency re-adopted. First guilds form.
- **Year 4** — The Conglomerate Parade Disaster. The only attempt at unified city-wide governance destroyed by hubris. Fragmented faction system becomes permanent.
- **Years 4-7** — Faction consolidation period. Sector 7 Remnant takes North District. Saebyeok Group establishes corporate control in East Financial District. Blackmarket Collective forms in Southeast Old Town. The Cradle founded in Southwest Residential Zone. Guilds expand and formalize.
- **Year 8** — First recorded Apex-tier creature sighting. A sky jellyfish phenomenon over the eastern outskirts. Multiple Greater and Alpha creatures fled the area. The Cartographer Guild begins systematic territory mapping.
- **Year 9** — Increasing faction border skirmishes. Hunting grounds becoming contested. The pressure loop becomes visible — populations are growing, space is shrinking.
- **Year 10 (Present)** — The current state. Faction wars over territory and hunting grounds. Creature territories are expanding inward. The pressure cycle is accelerating. The interval between culls is getting shorter.

## 10. CULTURE & SOCIETY
### Social Norms and Daily Life
The apocalypse is ongoing. People have adapted, not recovered. Life is defined by routine survival: work, eat, sleep, don't die. The concept of "normal" is whatever keeps you alive tomorrow.

**What's Respected:** Gear quality. A person fully decked in modified gear is an instant "don't mess with this guy" signal. Visual threat assessment is survival — you read someone's equipment before you read their face. Hunters who bring in consistent kills are respected. People who can fix things are valued. Everyone else is expendable.

**What's Feared:** The basic three — hunger, creatures, other humans. Hunger is constant. Creature attacks are random and lethal. Other humans are the most dangerous because they're the most unpredictable. A creature will kill you because it's a creature. A human will kill you because it benefits them.

**What's Shameful:** Nothing. Shame is a luxury. Survival doesn't leave room for guilt. People do what they have to. Stealing, betrayal, abandoning the weak — these aren't moral failures, they're survival strategies. The only thing approaching shame is incompetence. Failing repeatedly at basic survival tasks makes you a liability, and liabilities get left behind.

**What's Celebrated:** Successful hunts. Surviving another day. And above all — **children being born.** New life in a dying city is the most significant event possible. A birth brings the entire community together regardless of faction. Pregnant women are among the most protected people in any territory. A child reaching their first birthday is cause for genuine celebration. This is the one thing that makes people put down their weapons, if only for a day.

**Daily Life Texture:** Most people wake up early, work assigned or chosen tasks through the day, and return to whatever shelter they have before dark. Curfews exist in most territories — not because of law, but because creatures are more active at night. Markets operate during daylight. Guild jobs are posted in the morning. The evening is for maintenance, eating, and whatever comfort you can afford. Entertainment is scarce — the Pleasure Guild provides what little exists. Alcohol is common and cheap. Conversation revolves around creature sightings, hunting stories, faction politics, and who died recently.

**Inter-Faction Culture:** Each territory has its own flavor. Sector 7 Remnant is militaristic and quiet. Saebyeok Group is transactional and sterile. Blackmarket Collective is loud and dangerous. The Cradle is warm but tense. Shěn Territory is orderly and grim. The Core is expensive and watchful. The Frontier is desperate and alert. People who've lived in one territory their whole lives have a different worldview than those who've moved between them.

## 11. NOTABLE RELATIONSHIPS
### RELATIONSHIP — Jin & Jun (The Disaster Twins)
Siblings who would die for each other and also constantly try to get the other one killed through sheer incompetence. Their arguments are legendary — hunters within earshot have been known to relocate just to avoid the headache. Individually functional, together catastrophic. Their bond is genuine and unbreakable, which somehow makes their cooperation worse. They trust each other completely — they just never agree on anything.

### RELATIONSHIP — Dealer Hwang and The Bride
Two of the most information-connected people in the city. Their relationship is unclear — mutual respect, mutual suspicion, or something deeper. They've never been seen meeting publicly, but both have demonstrated knowledge of the other's activities. Some suspect they're working together on a long-term agenda. Others think they're playing each other. Neither will confirm or deny anything.

### RELATIONSHIP — Grandpa Wei and the Artisan Guild
Wei was once a senior Artisan Guild craftsman before being expelled for undisclosed reasons (possibly related to his behavior, possibly related to unauthorized experiments in gear modification). He hates the Guild. The Guild hates him but can't match his skill. They've tried to recruit him back. He refused. They've tried to shut down his operations. He always resurfaces. The current arrangement: grudging tolerance on both sides.

### RELATIONSHIP — The Rat Queen and the Cartographer Guild
The Cartographer Guild has been trying to recruit the Rat Queen for years. She's ignored every approach. Guild scouts who've tried to follow her have lost her in creature territory — or been found later with no memory of what happened. The Guild has classified her as a "do not engage" asset: observe, document, but do not approach. She knows they're watching. She doesn't seem to care.

### RELATIONSHIP — Doc Park and the Medical Guild
Doc Park was expelled from the Medical Guild for unethical human experimentation. The Guild wants him arrested or worse. He operates under Blackmarket Collective protection specifically because the Guild can't reach him there. The irony: Medical Guild specialists quietly refer patients to Doc Park for conditions they can't treat. Both sides pretend this doesn't happen.

### RELATIONSHIP — Shěn and Sector 7 Remnant
The two most militarily capable factions have an uneasy non-aggression pact. Shěn respects military discipline; the Remnant respects Shěn's results. They don't trade, they don't cooperate, and they don't attack each other. The Cold War is stable because neither side wants to find out what happens if it breaks. Both sides maintain detailed intelligence on the other, just in case.

### RELATIONSHIP — Saebyeok Group and the Blackmarket Collective
Economic opposites that secretly depend on each other. Saebyeok Group's formal economy has gaps — certain goods and services they can't officially provide. The Blackmarket Collective fills those gaps. Off the record, Saebyeok executives and Blackmarket bosses have been seen meeting. On the record, they publicly denounce each other as parasites. The economic ecosystem needs both of them.

### RELATIONSHIP — Beard and The Mourner
The Mourner has Beard's original dog tag — the only personal item Beard has ever been recorded caring about. He visits her station after major hunts to confirm the dead and collect tags. She is the only person Beard has been observed speaking to — though witnesses describe it as grunts and gestures, not words. Why he trusts her is unknown. What she knows about him is unknown. Their connection is one of the city's quieter mysteries.

## 12. LEGENDARY ITEMS / ARTIFACTS
### ARTIFACT — Exosuits
Pre-apocalypse powered armor frames — the starting point, not the final form. Each exosuit is unique because every owner modifies it differently with creature parts. A base frame provides enhanced strength and basic protection. Creature-part upgrades transform it: chitin plating over joints, Emperor-tier bone struts reinforcing the spine, creature-core arrays powering servo systems, biologically-derived sensor suites grafted to the helmet. The most powerful exosuits look like nightmares — asymmetrical, ugly, clearly modified in the field by whoever was desperate enough to weld creature parts onto military hardware. Pristine exosuits belong to collectors or cowards. Factions have been known to go to war over a single functional frame. The known frames in City 809 are counted on one hand. Exosuits cannot be manufactured — only found, salvaged, or taken. Each frame is a depreciating asset; parts break, cores degrade, and replacement requires either salvage or creature materials.

### ARTIFACT — The Conglomerate Data Core
A pre-apocalypse data storage device recovered from the Conglomerate's ruined headquarters. Allegedly contains research on creature biology, energy core optimization, and possibly information about the creatures' origin. Has been bought, stolen, and fought over multiple times. Nobody has successfully accessed the data — it's encrypted with pre-apocalypse security. Currently missing. Multiple factions and guilds have standing bounties for its recovery.

## 13. AI/GM USAGE GUIDE
### GUIDE — Core GM Principles
This world runs on **reality, not morality.** There is no right way to win. The GM does not reward cleverness over violence, or vice versa. The GM rewards **results.** A player in an exosuit who solves problems with force is playing correctly. A player with a rusty modified pistol who solves problems with traps and deception is also playing correctly. Method is determined by capability. The world is indifferent to how you survive.

**No power of friendship.** No moral victories. No narrative convenience. If a player makes a bad decision, consequences follow. If they make a good decision with the resources they have, they succeed. The world does not bend to make the story work.

### GUIDE — Tone Calibration
Gritty, oppressive, tense. Never peaceful. Even in safe zones, the atmosphere is watchful. People don't relax — they pause. Humor exists but is dark, tired, or absurdist (see: Jin & Jun). Beauty exists but is always temporary — a sunset over ruined skyline, a moment of quiet between firefights. The apocalypse is ongoing. People have adapted, not recovered. There is no "before things get better" because things don't get better. They just keep going.

### GUIDE — Running Combat
Combat is dangerous and consequential. Wounds matter. A creature wound can be biologically active — venom, spores, acid residue. Humans die when shot, even with modified gear. Exosuits are terrifying but not invincible — coordinated attacks with armor-piercing modified weapons can bring one down. Use the creature generation system to create varied encounters. Not every fight should be winnable. Some encounters should teach the player to retreat.

### GUIDE — Using the Recurring NPCs
Drop recurring NPCs into campaigns organically. Grandpa Wei appears when players need gear mods. Dealer Hwang shows up when players are desperate. The Disaster Twins appear as comic relief or accidental complications. The Rat Queen appears in creature territory as an eerie encounter. The Mourner appears after violence. Beard appears on high-tier hunts. Doc Park appears when conventional medicine fails. The Bride appears when political intrigue is happening. Two-Teeth Song appears when players need route intel or comic relief. These NPCs make the world feel alive and connected.

### GUIDE — Equipment Resonance (Rumour)
This is a narrative tool, not a confirmed mechanic. Occasionally, a piece of gear a player has used for a long time may perform beyond its specifications during moments of extreme emotional intensity. This is never confirmed as supernatural — it could be muscle memory, psychological state, equipment wear patterns, or pure coincidence. Use it sparingly as a dramatic moment. Never explain it. Let the player wonder.

### GUIDE — Faction Relations
Factions are in constant low-level conflict. The GM should present faction tension as ambient background — border skirmishes, trade disputes, POI conflicts. Players working for one faction should feel the friction with others. Faction missions should always have political consequences. Protecting the Grid? Someone else's power got cut. Delivering cargo? Someone else's shipment was on that train. There are no clean victories in this city.

### GUIDE — Breakout Events
Use breakout events as campaign-level threats. A breakout should feel like a natural disaster — sudden, overwhelming, and indifferent. Creatures spilling into human territory in waves. Normal operations halt. Factions temporarily cooperate (or don't). After a breakout, territory maps shift. Factions that held their ground gain territory. Factions that retreated lose it. Players caught in a breakout have one job: survive.

### GUIDE — Economy Management
Price things consistently using the gold standard. Players should always feel the squeeze — just enough money to maintain gear, never enough to feel secure. Big scores (high-tier hunts, successful faction missions) should feel significant. Financial desperation is a plot driver. Players who can't pay rent in Saebyeok territory get kicked out. Players who can't afford medical care go to Doc Park. Money problems create story.

## GUILDS (Neutral Organizations)

### GUILD — The Hunting Board
**Type:** Monster Hunting Job Board
**Stance:** Neutral. Anyone can post or take jobs. Commission on every contract.
Posts creature-hunting contracts ranging from low-tier pest clearing to high-tier expedition bids. Administers fee collection, verifies kills (through Cartographer Guild field reports), and resolves disputes. Operates primarily from the Core but has posting boards in every district. The closest thing to a universal institution — even faction soldiers use it for side work.

### GUILD — Salvage Guild
**Type:** Pre-Apocalypse Tech Recovery
**Stance:** Neutral. Sells recovered tech to highest bidder or contracts recovery ops.
Specializes in expeditions into dead zones and collapsed structures to recover pre-apocalypse technology, stock weapons, and functional components. Their knowledge of the old city's infrastructure is second to none. Often hired to assess POIs like the Grid and Siphon for repair work.

### GUILD — Artisan Guild
**Type:** Gear Modification and Crafting
**Stance:** Neutral. Sells services to anyone who can pay. Sets crafting standards.
Controls the standards for monster-modified gear. A weapon certified by the Artisan Guild holds its value; uncertified modifications are suspect. Best crafters in the city. Operate out of well-equipped workshops, mostly in the Core. Higher-tier modifications require rarer creature materials and more skilled artisans — creating a natural progression ceiling.

### GUILD — Courier Guild
**Type:** Safe Passage and Logistics Intel
**Stance:** Neutral. Sells route information and escort services.
Knows which dead zones are passable this week, which bridges have creature nests, which tunnels are clear. Their intel is literally life and death for inter-district travel. Also handles physical package delivery for those willing to pay. The Depot is their primary operating hub. Their route maps are among the most valuable trade goods in the city.

### GUILD — Medical Guild
**Type:** Creature Wound Treatment and Core Medicine
**Stance:** Neutral. Treats anyone who can pay.
Regular doctors can't handle creature wounds — the damage patterns, venom effects, and core contamination require specialized knowledge. The Medical Guild trains specialists in creature-related medicine. They also study creature biology to develop antivenoms and treatments. Frequently hired by hunters dealing with unusual wounds.

### GUILD — Cartographer Guild
**Type:** Territory Mapping and Creature Tracking
**Stance:** Neutral. Sells maps, migration data, and expedition intel.
Maps creature territories, tracks migration patterns, and predicts breakout events. Their data is essential for expedition planning and faction defense strategy. They sell general maps publicly and premium intelligence to private clients. Their field scouts are among the most skilled (and paranoid) survivalists in the city.

### GUILD — Pleasure Guild
**Type:** Companionship, Entertainment, and Information Networking
**Stance:** Neutral on the surface. Politically connected to everyone.
Comfort, companionship, and entertainment in a bleak world. Also one of the most politically connected organizations — powerful people let their guard down in private. The Pleasure Guild hears everything. They trade in secrets as much as services. Intelligence brokerage is their quiet secondary business. Every faction has members who frequent their establishments.

### GUILD — Farmer Guild
**Type:** Food Production
**Stance:** Neutral. Trades food at stable rates. Quietly the most powerful guild.
Controls arable land in the Frontier Ring — rooftop gardens, hydroponics in cleared buildings, reclaimed parkland. The only reliable food source in the city. Every faction depends on them. If the Farmer Guild stopped delivering, the entire city starves in weeks. They know this. They don't flaunt it, but they don't forget it either. Their pricing is the most stable in the city because they can't afford to price-gouge — and they don't need to. Demand is eternal.

## CREATURE GENERATION SYSTEM
Creatures in this world are not named mythological species. They are generated procedurally based on the following parameters:

**Threat Tiers:**
- **Common** — Everyday creatures found in wilderness and frontier zones. Threat to unprepared individuals. White core. Source of basic materials. Most numerous. The bread and butter of hunting.
- **Greater** — Larger, more dangerous. Threat to small groups. Requires planning and decent gear. Yellow core. Source of mid-grade materials. Fewer natural predators.
- **Alpha** — Exceptional individual within a species — pack leader, territorial dominator. Can coordinate lesser creatures of its type. Red core. Not a separate species, but the rare standout. Any creature species can produce an Alpha. Threat to full hunting parties.
- **Emperor/Empress** — Rules a zone. Guaranteed to exist in established creature territories. Apex of its local ecosystem. Black core. The reason certain areas are designated no-go zones. Threat to entire expedition teams — may require exosuit support. If an Emperor/Empress is displaced or killed, the local creature hierarchy collapses into chaos until a new one emerges.
- **Apex** — Phenomenon-level. Walking natural disaster. Prismatic core. Not just dangerous — an environmental event. Other creatures flee its path. Example: a sky jellyfish that floats invulnerable to most damage unless struck on a specific point on top of its head, while raining spores that kill everything below. Can theoretically be killed, but the cost in lives and gear would be catastrophic. The correct response to an Apex creature is evacuation. Encountering one should be a campaign-defining event. Extremely rare — possibly one active in the entire region at any given time.

**Key Rule:** Any species can have an Alpha variant. Any established territory has an Emperor/Empress. Apex creatures are unique, rare, and terrifying. Within a tier, threats are roughly equivalent — but the possibility of an Alpha/Emperor/Empress variant always exists, keeping hunters paranoid.

**Size Classes:**
- Vermin (rat-sized, swarm threat)
- Hound (dog-sized, pack hunters)
- Brute (bear-sized, solitary or small groups)
- Behemoth (vehicle-sized, territorial)
- Leviathan (building-sized, district-level phenomenon, Apex-tier only)

**Threat Attributes:**
- Armor type (chitin, hide, scales, bone plating, soft-bodied with regeneration)
- Movement type (ground, climbing, burrowing, flying, aquatic, multi-terrain)
- Attack pattern (ambush, charge, swarm, ranged spit/projectile, grab-and-drag, area denial)
- Special property (venom, fire, acid, electric, sonic, adhesive web, regenerative, spore/pollen, hypersonic lure)
- Territory type (underground, rooftop, waterway, building interior, open ground, forested ruin, sky, coastal)
- Activity cycle (nocturnal, diurnal, seasonal, triggered by disturbances, territorial defense only)
- Intelligence indicator (instinct-only, pack coordination, tool use, territorial strategy, unknown)

**Breakout Events:**
Creatures normally stay within their territory. Breakout events occur when:
- Their ecosystem is disturbed (construction, explosions, salvage ops)
- Seasonal migration cycles trigger
- A higher-tier creature (Alpha/Emperor) displaces lower-tier ones
- An Apex creature moves through, scattering everything in its path
- Human activity over-hunts an area, collapsing the local food chain
- An Emperor/Empress is killed, causing territorial collapse and mass creature displacement
- Unknown environmental factors (the GM's wildcard)

During a breakout, creatures spill into human territory in unpredictable numbers and types. This is the primary environmental disaster driver in the world. A breakout can reshape faction territory overnight.
