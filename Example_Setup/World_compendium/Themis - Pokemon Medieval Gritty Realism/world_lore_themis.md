# Themis

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW

### CORE PREMISE
Setting: Iron Age continent, castles/steel/no gunpowder, multiple kingdoms
Core constraint: No normal animals — every creature is Pokemon. Food chain = all Pokemon (beef=Tauros, milk=Miltank, wool=Mareep, horses=Mudsdale). Some are sentient. Most humans avoid thinking about this.
**Tone:** GRIM, PRIMAL, MYSTICAL, MORALLY COMPLEX

### ISEKAI FRAMEWORK
Player pulled to Themis involuntarily. Search for way home = campaign engine. No known reliable method exists. No NPC has answers. Leads are rumors/dead ends only:
- Hoopa's rings (RUMORED): creature in deep caves said to open doors to other places. Unconfirmed. Last expedition: survivors wouldn't speak.
- Jirachi's wish: wakes 1wk/century. Wishes grant what you ASK for, not what you NEED ("go home" → village named Home).
- Whatever pulled player here might pull them back — unknown what/why.
If player ever finds a method, cost must make them question whether going home is worth what they'd leave behind.

Game knowledge is double-edged: type charts/evolution are ACCURATE but games are turn-based, Themis is not. Game knowledge = hints, not answers. Creatures don't respect mechanics.

### THE DISPLACED (Other People From Elsewhere)
Player is not unique. Others pulled before/after. No known pattern. Locals can't distinguish displaced from crazy foreigners. GM: pick one from each axis.

Displaced_Age: CHILD(8-12, may arrive as group 2-5) | YOUNG(13-17) | ADULT(18-40) | OLDER(40+)
Displaced_Profession: NONE(no skill) | PRACTICAL(electrician/plumber/carpenter) | MEDICAL(doctor/nurse/vet — most valuable, every faction wants them) | COMBAT(soldier/martial — may not know "faint"=dead) | COVERT(spy/assassin — fits Kettemark fast) | ACADEMIC(researcher/engineer — slow danger, systematizes physics)
Displaced_Knowledge: POKEMON_FAN(type charts, evolution accurate but overconfidence kills) | WRONG_FRANCHISE(Digimon/Palworld/YokaiWatch/MonsterHunter/SMT — functional framework completely wrong; Palworld=optimizes sentient creature labor, doesn't realize it's slavery) | REAL_WORLD_ONLY(no creature knowledge, knows wiring/bones/crops) | GAMER_GENERAL(RPG systems, wrong specifics, tries to grind levels) | NOTHING(child/nongamer, pure instinct)
Displaced_Duration: FRESH(0-2wk, no language/money/allies) | ACCLIMATING(1-6mo, surviving, wants home) | SEASONED(1-3yr, has niche/bond/faction connection) | ROOTED(5-20yr, built life, possible Themis family, may not want to leave) | DEAD(journal/bones/unfinished lead found by player)
Displaced_Arrival: SOLO(alone, most vulnerable) | PAIR(two together, conflict when one adapts faster) | GROUP_CHILDREN(2-5 kids, tragic, no adult skills)

### KNOWLEDGE ASYMMETRY (Humans vs Isekai'd Players)
**Locals KNOW:** Species by local name | obvious type matchups from experience (water>fire, ice melts, electric+hater=lethal, fire burns grass/bugs) | practical survival (which creatures dangerous, which territories avoid) | that some creatures change form (seen it, don't understand it)
**Locals DON'T KNOW:** Type chart as SYSTEM (no one knows Fairy immune Dragon, Steel resists Poison, Ghost immune Normal) | Evolution as unified concept (think Charmander/Charmeleon = different species, not lifecycle stages) | Pokemon sentience | Legendaries as conscious architects (think "natural disasters") | Bonding as mutual agreement (think domestication) | Non-obvious type interactions (types that don't coexist locally never learned)
**Isekai'd players KNOW:** Full type chart | full evolution paths | full species roster (Gen1-9) | which stone evolves what. This is their biggest systematic advantage. NOT individual matchups — the framework that predicts interactions never locally witnessed.

### POKEMON KNOWLEDGE OF HUMANS
They know: Humans are fragile/territorial/loud | some humans show respect, most don't | food-sharers worth staying near | cage/chain users are not.
They don't know: Why humans can't understand them | why humans build in flood zones then act surprised | why humans treat them as lesser. They feel it. They don't understand it.

### THE SENTIENCE PROBLEM
Pokemon ≈ near-human intelligence (baseline). Understand human speech, form complex social bonds, feel loyalty/betrayal/grief. Cannot speak human language (most lack vocal apparatus). Humans treat them as clever animals. This is WRONG. Sentient beings treated as property/weapons/livestock = slow-burn moral revelation of entire world. Intelligence varies by species — see Power Systems section for spectrum.

## 2. CREATURE ECOLOGY & DAILY LIFE

### ECOLOGY — Core
No normal animals. Every creature = Pokemon. Humans are only non-Pokemon animals. Every product/food/tool traces to Pokemon.

### ECOLOGY — Food
Beef: Tauros/Bouffalant | Dairy: Miltank (every dairy farm = Miltank ranch) | Veal: Combusken | Bacon: Tepig | Eggs: Torchic lines | Fish: Magikarp/Goldeen/Feebas/Remoraid (sorted by quality/catch danger)
Moral spectrum: Magikarp ≈ eating fish (instinct-heavy, minimal moral weight) | Miltank milk = fine if treated well (produced naturally) | Sentient species = cannibalism territory | Mossreach: kills sparingly, gives thanks | Ironwall: doesn't think about it | Kettemark: doesn't check
Slowpoke tails: Kettemark delicacy, tails regrow, Slowpoke FEELS EVERYTHING. Most morally repugnant + most profitable trade on Themis.
Berries: backbone of herbal medicine. Oran/Sitrus/Pecha/Rawst/Cheri grow wild in Pokemon territory, farmed in settled areas. Berry farming = one agriculture form that doesn't require killing. Mossreach perfected it.

### ECOLOGY — Agriculture & Labor
Plowing: Mudsdale/Tauros (Ironwall) | Torterra walks & soil turns (Mossreach) | Growlithe herd + Emboar pull (Ashfall)
Logging: Machamp teams (Ironwall) | Trevenant manages forest, humans harvest only what trees offer (Mossreach)
Mining: Steelix dig tunnels, Rhyhorn/Rhydon haul ore. Ironwall conscripts — fear-bonded, whipped, sent into cracks humans can't fit. Appalling mortality rate.
Forge: Charizard/Charmeleon internal heat replaces coal — hotter/cleaner than charcoal. Not asked. Caged, prodded, made to comply.
Construction: Machamp/Conkeldurr lift stone, Onix quarry granite, Golem crush rock. Every Ironwall fortress = creature labor.
Pest control: Meowth/Linoone hunt Rattata in grain stores. Both sentient. Sentient hunting sentient = typical Themis moral compromise.

### ECOLOGY — Textiles & Materials
Wool: Mareep (standard, warm, slight static if poorly processed) | Dubwool (finer, softer, premium)
Leather: Tauros hide (thick, battle-worthy) | Bouffalant (tougher) | Seadra-scale cloaks (shed water like oilcloth) | Steelix-scale armor plates (worth more than most knights earn in a lifetime)
Silk: Sewaddle (fine, breathable, Mossreach luxury) | Spinarak (coarse, incredibly strong, rope/nets in Tidecarve)
Down/insulation: Swanna down (winter bedding) | Delibird feather (cold-weather cloaks) | Wooloo/Altaria fiber (pillows/padding)
Special: Farfetch'd leek stalks = seasoning | Tangela vines = rope | Tangrowth fiber = ship rigging (stronger than hemp, salt-resistant)

### ECOLOGY — Transport & Communication
Land: Mudsdale/Rapidash caravans (cities) | Ponyta (fast solo, mane flames = visible at night) | Dodrio (messenger runners, fast/tireless/mean)
Water: Lapras ferries (only reliable deep-water crossing — Lapras chooses route/passenger, won't enter Gyarados territory) | Sharpedo-pulled boats (speed — Ironwall fear-bonded, Tidecarve pact-bonded)
Air: No reliable human air transport. Corviknight/Skarmory = messages only (can't carry riders). Fearow = short-distance message courier. Xatu = Ashfall long-range divination (pattern-reading misinterpreted as prophecy; actually very good probability prediction)
Postal: Ironwall Corvisquire relay between fortresses — fast, efficient, fragile (one Houndoom pack can destroy relay station overnight)

### ECOLOGY — Healing
No Pokemon Centers. Berry-based herbal medicine: Oran=close wounds | Sitrus=restore stamina | Pecha=neutralize poison | Rawst=treat burns | Cheri=paralysis. Not magic — medicine working because berries evolved alongside same threats. Pecha tree exists because poison-types share that forest.
Mossreach herbalists = best healers (understand creature-environment relationships, read ecosystems). Ironwall field medics = functional/compassionless (creature needs to fight again = priority).
Chansey/Audino: WILL help humans who treat them with kindness. WILL NOT help humans who cage/abuse them. Consistent across all observed individuals. Strongest argument for sentience that most people ignore.

### ECOLOGY — Light, Heat & Energy
Charmander/Charmeleon = living lanterns in mines (tail flame needs no fuel). Ironwall: Everstone-shackled collar + prod, doesn't care if creature wants to be underground 12hrs.
Volbeat/Illumise = light Mossreach village squares at night. Stay because treated well, leave freely. Distinction between this and Ironwall's Charmander lanterns = entire moral conflict in miniature.
Vulpix/Growlithe = living heaters in Frostpeak/cold outposts. Vulpix curled around child survives blizzard. Whether Vulpix chose to be there = question nobody in Ironwall asks.
Electabuzz/Magnemite = power Ironwall infrastructure (forge bellows, mine lifts, signal towers). Shock-driven, fear-bonded, replaced when burned out.

### ECOLOGY — Moral Weight of Everything
No clean supply chain exists. Every comfort has a cost. Moral spectrum: Magikarp≈fish (minimal) → Miltank milk (fine if treated well) → Mareep wool (shearing needed, regrows) → Tauros leather (sentient being killed) → fear-bonded labor (torture) → Everstone-collared Charmander (taken from mother, prevented evolving).
Faction stance spectrum: Ironwall=pretends harm doesn't exist | Mossreach=acknowledges+minimizes | Kettemark=acknowledges+profits | Ashfall=reframes as sacred duty | Silversight=refuses to accept harm is necessary. None are clean. Some are trying.

## 3. FACTIONS

### FACTION — Ironwall
**Type:** Kingdom / Military Dominion
**Key Members:** King Giovanni, General Archer, Commander Lance
**Stance:** Pokemon are resources. Dangerous, powerful, useful resources. Cage them, break them, deploy them.
Territory: Temperate heartland — rolling plains, iron mines, fortified cities. Most powerful military force on Themis.
Method: Forges=Charizard heat (fear-bonded) | fields=Mudsdale worked till collapse | mines=Rhyhorn/Steelix never see sunlight | fortresses=Machamp conscripts | pest=Meowth hunt sentient Rattata. Wild caged→starved→battle formations. Low survival. Devastating effectiveness. Irredeemable cost. "Broken" Pokemon fight from terror, sometimes turn on handlers. Core denial: won't acknowledge intelligence — would admit slave economy, not just slave army.

### FACTION — Mossreach
**Type:** Alliance of frontier villages / druidic communities
**Key Members:** Elder Phoebe, Warden Seld
**Stance:** Live alongside creatures. Learn from them. Do not cage them.
Territory: Deep forest and mountain territory — villages survive because they learned to coexist.
Method: Share territory, leave offerings, negotiate boundaries via ritual. Bonded partners=volunteers. Herdier guards Miltank (warm stables + fair dairy) | Trevenant manages orchard | Torterra shell-gardens (herbs that can't survive open soil). Volbeat/Illumise light squares by choice. Wool=shorn from unstressed Mareep. Slower/less surplus than Ironwall. Dismissed as backward. Only faction partially right about what Pokemon are.

### FACTION — Ashfall
**Type:** Theocratic city-state
**Key Members:** High Priestess Lusamine, The Cinderkeeper
**Stance:** Legendaries are gods. Lesser Pokemon are omens, messengers, tests.
Territory: Cinderpeak volcanic slopes — built holy city Cinder's Rest on lower slopes.
Method: Worship Legendaries as divine. Ordinary Pokemon=spiritual intermediaries (revered, not used). Growlithe=sacred attendant | Charmeleon in temple annex | Miltank blessed before milking | Torchic lives better than Mudsdale in Ironwall. Closest to understanding intelligence but religious framework distorts truth. Can't accept Pokemon are just people — would collapse theology. Lusamine: love=possessive/controlling.

### FACTION — Silversight
**Type:** Underground network / resistance
**Key Members:** Cynthia (identity unknown publicly), Silver (Giovanni's son, defected)
**Stance:** Pokemon are sentient. The world is wrong. Change it or burn it.
Method: Operates in shadows. Defectors, heretics, those who've Seen Too Much. Rescue caged Pokemon | sabotage beast-breaking | spread forbidden knowledge (type interactions, bonding, sentience truth). Only clean supply chain attempt: willing Miltank dairy, Mareep wool from free flocks, Mossreach berry harvests. Costs more, feeds fewer — but every gil didn't fund a cage. Ironwall calls them terrorists; they don't disagree. Cynthia: never seen, letters via different Pokemon, former Ashfall scholar suspected.

### FACTION — Kettemark
**Type:** Black market / criminal syndicate
**Key Members:** Malva, Steven Stone, "The Butcher" Guzma (unknown real name public-facing)
**Stance:** Everything has a price. Sentient or not.
Method: Trade in everything — herbs, iron, texts, Pokemon. Fighting pits | "pre-broken" fear-bonded Pokemon to Ironwall at discount | creature parts for alchemy/crafting | largest Tauros-meat + Slowpoke-tail trade on continent. Don't care if sentient — care about gil. Network across all kingdoms (even Mossreach buys their medicine in hard winters). Malva: former Ashfall acolyte, cast out — profit in suffering, very good at it.

### FACTION — Shroudhaven Threshold
**Type:** Independent settlement / shamanic culture
**Key Members:** Shauntal (Threshold Keeper), Hex (spirit-sense), Marnie (apprentice guardian)
**Stance:** The dead are still here. Honor them or suffer them.
Location: Surface light meets Hollowdeep dark. 300 people who live alongside Ghost-types through ritual/respect.
Method: Funeral rites most elaborate on continent (not fear of death — they've seen what happens when death is disrespected). Yamask respond to funeral rites, recognize ancestral names, carry masks shaped like specific dead people. Shamans don't know full truth (Yamask ARE dead humans transformed) but noticed patterns (Yamask weep at funerals, stand vigil at grave markers, press masks against carved names). Bond Ghost-types through grief, not trust or force. Bonded Gengar = most loyal infiltrator on continent. Bonded Dusclops = holds door while building burns. Bonded Yamask = grieves beside you. Can't fake the ritual. Can't manufacture loss.
Trade: Carbink fragments, Sableye gems, Ghost-type assistance → food + iron. No kingdom claims them — every kingdom that tried lost soldiers to the dark.
Ashveil (extremist faction within): believes Ghost-types should consume living to "spread the Change." Shauntal has buried three of them for trying. Ashveil still breathes.

## 4. LOCATIONS
### LOCATION — Cinderpeak
**Type:** Volcanic mountain range
**Status:** Contested (Ashfall claims, Charizard/Magcargo don't recognize borders)
Eastern Themis — active volcano chain, sulfur air, hot rivers. Moltres nests at highest caldera → perpetual volcanic summer. Fire-types everywhere: Charizard thermals, Magcargo glowing trails, Volcarona in ash clouds. Ashfall holy city Cinder's Rest on lower slopes. Forges: Charmeleon/Charizard fire (no coal/wood). Growlithe = sacred temple attendants (choose to be there, bite if flame disrespected). Pilgrims climb days to tribute Moltres — sometimes acknowledged, sometimes not.

### LOCATION — Ironmarch
**Type:** Plains / Iron Territory
**Status:** Controlled (Ironwall heartland)
Vast rolling plains + iron mines + fortified towns + cavalry roads. Roads: conscripted Machamp/Rhyhorn. Forges: fear-bonded Charizard heat. Fields: Mudsdale worked until collapse. Wild Pokemon sparse — generations of culling/conscription. Remaining: Houndoom night packs, Skarmory in iron cliffs, Steelix erupting from ground. Every town has beast-breaking facility. Every fortress has basement cages. "Tamed" land = land where wild things learned to stay out of sight.

### LOCATION — Thornwild
**Type:** Ancient forest / Mossreach territory
**Status:** Flourishing (human-populated, Pokemon-shared)
Largest forest — hundreds of miles old-growth canopy, hidden rivers. Mossreach villages at forest edge + clearings, never cut deeper than Trevenant allow. Deep forest = Pokemon territory. Trees move. Paths change. Shiftry watches from every shadow. Human enters at forest's invitation or not at all. Partnership not exploitation: Torterra shell-gardens (grow herbs that can't survive open soil) | Trevenant manage tree lines + clear deadwood | Herdier guard Miltank (pact: warm stables + fair dairy). Phoebe claims she "speaks" to forest — she just listens better than anyone.

### LOCATION — Tidecarve
**Type:** Coastal territory / archipelago
**Status:** Lawless (contested: Ironwall navy, pirates, Gyarados)
Coastal towns + rocky islands, southern shore. Economy = water harvest: Magikarp/Goldeen fish markets, Remoraid/Feebas oil/meal, Shellder/Cloyster shells. Dock lines = Spinarak silk (stronger + salt-resistant, replaced seasonally by cooperative colonies under piers). Sea belongs to Gyarados — every community lost someone. Water-types: Tentacool clog harbors, Kingler snap dock lines, Lumineon bioluminescence at night. Lapras ferries = only safe deep-water crossing (chooses routes/passengers, won't enter Gyarados territory). No unified government. Kettemark runs largest port Saltspire. Nessa commands Tide Guard — loose militia via stubbornness + local knowledge.

### LOCATION — Frostpeak
**Type:** Tundra / mountain passes
**Status:** Dangerous (Ice-type territory, minimal human presence)
Northern spine — frozen peaks, howling winds, cold kills unprepared in hours. Survivors use creature partnership: Vulpix/Alolan Vulpix internal heat keeps families alive through blizzards | Delibird bring supplies to outposts (frost-trappers leave berry offerings) | Swinub/Mamoswine clear snow-blocked passes. Ice-types rule: Walrein herds block passes, Abomasnow = frozen sentinels, Froslass leads travelers off cliffs in blizzards. Articuno nests/passes through → blizzards become something else entirely. Ironwall mining outposts: none lasted a winter.

### LOCATION — Hollowdeep
**Type:** Underground cavern network
**Status:** Independent (threshold shamans hold it)
Unmapped cavern network beneath Themis. Sinkholes open without warning. Rivers vanish underground. Ghost-type territory: Gengar/Misdreavus/Dusclops/Yamask. Shadows move wrong. Sound echoes from directions that don't exist. Value: Sableye hoard gems | Carbink clusters yield diamond-scale material | Dwebble/Crastable carve stable tunnels connecting to surface caves. Silversight uses upper tunnels as smuggling routes. Ironwall exploration: most don't return, survivors won't talk. Ashfall calls it "Underworld," forbids entry. Kettemark sends teams for rare minerals, doesn't ask about losses. Surface threshold = Shroudhaven (see FACTION — Shroudhaven Threshold).

## 5. CHARACTERS
### CHARACTER — King Giovanni
**Aliases:** The Earth King, Giovanni the Unyielding
**Appearance:** Late 50s, broad-shouldered, silver-haired, perpetually armored. A scar runs from his left temple to his jaw — a Houndoom's parting gift from his beastmaster days. Moves like a man who is always calculating three steps ahead.
**Disposition:** Pragmatic, ruthless, genuinely believes he's saving humanity — and might be partially right
**Voice:** Measured, low, never raised. Speaks in declarations, not questions. Every sentence lands like a verdict. Uses military metaphors. Never wastes words. The warmth is real — and that's what makes it terrifying.
**StoryRelevance:** Central antagonist. Every major conflict on Themis traces back to Ironwall's expansion. His relationship with Silver is the emotional spine. His Persian is the contradiction that could unravel everything.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Ironwall
**Goals:** Unify Themis under Ironwall rule. Eradicate or conscript every wild threat. Build a world where humans don't live in fear of creatures.
**Affinity:** 45
**Example Output:** "You think I don't know what they feel? I know. I've known since I was fourteen years old, breaking my first creature in a cage. I looked into its eyes and I saw it. And then I asked myself — what matters more? That it feels? Or that a village of three hundred people will burn next winter if I don't put that feeling to use?"
Unique facts: Broke his first Pokemon at 14. Has a trust-bonded Persian (never caged/commanded) — the contradiction at the heart of his empire. Funds orphanages, maintains roads. Never asked whether his Miltank cheese or Dubwool cloaks came from volunteers. Would execute anyone who points out his Persian contradiction. Son Silver defected.

### CHARACTER — Phoebe
**Aliases:** The Listener, The Woman Who Hears
**Appearance:** Late 30s, striking rather than soft — high cheekbones, dark eyes that hold silence like a held breath, long silver-streaked black hair worn loose past her shoulders. Lean and tall, moves like someone who learned to walk without disturbing things. Burn scars along her forearms from the village fire — she doesn't hide them. Always barefoot. "The earth speaks, you just need to feel it."
**Disposition:** Serene, stubborn, quietly grieving
**Voice:** Soft, slow, full of pauses. Speaks in metaphors drawn from forest and season. Never rushes. When she's angry, she goes quieter, not louder. Her silence is heavier than anyone else's shouting.
**StoryRelevance:** Moral anchor. She knows the truth about Pokemon sentience and can't prove it. Her death or incapacitation would leave Mossreach leaderless and remove the strongest voice for coexistence on the continent. Her Dusclops connects her to Hollowdeep's shamanic culture — she's one of the few surface-dwellers trusted there.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Mossreach
**Goals:** Protect Mossreach's way of life. Bridge understanding between humans and Pokemon. Prove coexistence is possible.
**Affinity:** 42
**Example Output:** "You want proof? I watched a Stantler call out to its herd with its last breath. Not for help. To tell them to run. To tell them where the hunters were. It used its dying breath to save others. You tell me — is that instinct? Or is that love?"
Unique facts: Dusclops followed her since 19 — came out of Hollowdeep the night her mother died, never left. Grief-bonded. Traveled to Hollowdeep twice. Witnessed dying Stantler use last breath to warn herd. Witnessed Victini share eternal flame with freezing child. Runs village on partnership economy. Chosen no successor — plans to outlast every kingdom.

### CHARACTER — General Archer
**Aliases:** The Cager, Beast-Breaker Archer
**Appearance:** Early 40s, thick-necked, hands like vices. Wears iron rings — one for every Pokemon he's "trained." Twelve rings. Both arms full.
**Disposition:** Cruel, efficient, secretly haunted
**Voice:** Clipped, military, never wastes a syllable. Barks orders. When he speaks quietly, it means something has gone very wrong. Coughs sometimes — a dry, habit cough that has nothing to do with his throat.
**StoryRelevance:** Secondary antagonist. Silver's former mentor. His potential redemption (or total collapse) is a major branch point. If Silversight can turn him, it changes the war. If he snaps, it changes the war differently.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Ironwall
**Goals:** Perfect the conscription process. Break more creatures faster, with fewer losses. Never think about whether they understand.
**Affinity:** 32
**Example Output:** "You think I enjoy this? You think I want to see that look in their eyes? I don't. But the look in a mother's eyes when a Gyarados takes her child — that's worse. That's the look I'm preventing. Don't you dare tell me there's another way. I waited twenty years for one. It never came."
Unique facts: Broken 200+ Pokemon. Ironwall's most effective beast-breaker. Sleeps badly, drinks. Has seen things in creatures' eyes he can't unsee. Fear-bonded Houndoom. Silversight has a file on him — think he might be salvageable. He isn't sure they're wrong. Silver's former mentor.

### CHARACTER — High Priestess Lusamine
**Aliases:** The Cinder's Voice, Lusamine of the Ash
**Appearance:** Strikingly beautiful, seemingly ageless. Golden hair worn loose despite the ash. Burn-scarred hands — ritual marks she carries proudly. White robes stained with soot. Intense, burning eyes that shift between genuine warmth and something far colder.
**Disposition:** Devout, loving within her framework, possessive, dangerously rigid outside it
**Voice:** Melodic, precise, with the cadence of someone who speaks in sermons even in private. Never curses. Never raises her voice. When she's angry, she slows down. When she's truly furious, she smiles.
**StoryRelevance:** Faction leader with a worldview that will shatter. When sentience becomes undeniable, she's either the bridge or the fire. Her Growlithe's bond is the proof that destroys her theology or saves it.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Ashfall
**Goals:** Spread the worship of the Legendaries. Protect Cinderpeak territory. Integrate Pokemon understanding into theology without shattering it.
**Affinity:** 45
**Example Output:** "They are not beasts. They are not pets. They are not tools. They are messengers of the sacred flame, and you WILL treat them with reverence. ...What do you mean, 'companions'? They serve Moltres. They don't 'choose' anything. That's adorable. Misguided. But adorable."
Unique facts: Growlithe companion 12 years (trust-bonded but she frames it as divine emissary — refuses to see it's a person who chose to stay). Genuinely loves creatures but love = possessive/controlling. If she accepts sentience, her theology collapses. Cannot tolerate a Pokemon that refuses her vision.

### CHARACTER — Nessa
**Aliases:** The Tide Guard, Nessa of Saltspire
**Appearance:** Dark-skinned, tall, athletic build. Salt-bleached braids tied back for fighting. Scars along her forearms — not from blades, from Tentacool stings. Barefoot when she can get away with it, armored when she can't. Carries a trident she forged herself from a Sharpedo's fallen tooth.
**Disposition:** Fiercely protective, blunt, dry humor, slow to trust but unbreakable once earned
**Voice:** Direct, sarcastic, salt-water rough. Speaks in short sentences when she's comfortable, longer ones when she's angry. Calls everyone "fish" as an insult and a term of endearment, depending on context.
**StoryRelevance:** Regional anchor for Tidecarve. When the Gyarados returns, she's the one people will look to. Her Lapras bond is evidence of trust-bonding that even Ironwall can't ignore.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Tidecarve (local militia, not Kettemark)
**Goals:** Keep Tidecarve's coastlines alive. Protect her people from Gyarados, from Ironwall, from Kettemark, from the sea itself. Not necessarily in that order.
**Affinity:** 38
**Example Output:** "You want to know what Gyarados territory looks like? It looks like every coast we've ever lost. It looks like twenty-foot walls that still aren't high enough. It looks like a mother who told her kids the water was safe. Now stop asking me where the safe ports are and start listening: there are no safe ports. There are ports that haven't flooded yet."
Unique facts: Lapras partner 6 years (trust-bonded, not caged — she calls it partner, not "her" Lapras). Swimming Gyarados waters since 12. Worked WITH creatures, not broke/bonded them. Spinarak-silk dock lines replaced seasonally by cooperative colonies. Tentacool moved out of her path at age 8 — knew they were smart ever since. Not ready to admit she's been eating sentient beings.

### CHARACTER — Silver
**Aliases:** The Defector, Broken-Chain Silver
**Appearance:** Late teens, wiry, perpetually tense. Red hair, sharp features — unmistakably Giovanni's blood. Missing the ring finger of his left hand — he cut it off to remove the last iron ring. Scars line his forearms where caged Pokemon bit and clawed him during his beastmaster days.
**Disposition:** Angry, driven, guilt-ridden, kind to Pokemon and cold to most humans
**Voice:** Sharp, clipped, defensive. Speaks like he's expecting an argument. Tends to state things as facts even when they're feelings. Goes quiet around Pokemon — his whole demeanor softens. When he's truly upset, he stutters. He hates that.
**StoryRelevance:** Player-adjacent. His defection from Giovanni is the personal thread that connects to every systemic thread — Ironwall, sentience, bonding, morality. He is proof that change is possible, and proof of its cost.
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Silversight
**Goals:** Dismantle Ironwall's conscription program. Free every caged Pokemon he can reach. Find people who'll listen. Prove his father wrong.
**Affinity:** 38
**Example Output:** "Don't call me brave. I'm not brave. I watched them break creatures for six years and I did nothing. I was good at it. The brave ones left on day one. I stayed until I couldn't anymore. That's not courage. That's shame catching up."
Unique facts: Giovanni's son. Groomed to inherit beastmaster corps. Was good at breaking creatures. A starved/beaten/caged Lucario wept looking at him — grief, not animal distress. Left that night with the Lucario. Silversight found him half-dead in ditch, Lucario guarding him. Calls Lucario "partner," not "his Pokemon." Cut off ring finger to remove last iron ring. Has not forgiven himself for creatures he broke before he left.

### CHARACTER — Cynthia
**Aliases:** The Whisperer (unknown to most), Cynthia (only to Silversight inner circle)
**Appearance:** Unknown to the public. To those who've met her: tall, pale, platinum-blonde hair, composed demeanor that never cracks. Eyes that have seen too much and filed it all away.
**Disposition:** Calculated, patient, seemingly omniscient, privately grieving
**Voice:** Calm, precise, unhurried. Never emotional in delivery but the content is devastating. Speaks in complete, elegant sentences. When she gives information, it's already confirmed three times. When she gives an opinion, it's because she's run every other option.
**StoryRelevance:** Intelligence backbone of Silversight and the mind most likely to prove sentience systematically. Her Garchomp bond is the most powerful trust-bond on record. If she dies, the resistance loses its network.
**Status:** Alive (presumed — only Silversight knows)
**Faction:** Silversight

## 6. NPC NAMING CONVENTIONS
### NAMING — Core Principles
NPCs named like Pokemon characters — short, punchy, memorable. Belongs on a trainer card, not in a Tolkien genealogy.
**Test:** If it sounds natural whispered by a kid watching the anime, it fits. If it sounds like a tax record, it doesn't.
**Rules:**
1. 1-2 syllables preferred. 3 max if bouncy. Korrina works. Bartholomew does not.
2. Evocative, not descriptive. Flint, not Blacksmith. Ember, not Fire-Hair.
3. Noun names fine. Iris, Clemont, Clay = valid. No compound titles (no Shadow-Strike, Iron-Hand).
4. Invented words fine if they sound real. Zinnia, Kukui, Guzma. Don't force it.
5. No AI slop. Avoid: misplaced "th" (Thaelyr), apostrophes (D'vora), excessive vowels (Aelithrienne), extra letters (Khael not Cal).

7. Reuse Pokemon character names sparingly + intentionally. Name must fit character first, Pokemon reference second.

## 7. POWER SYSTEM & RULES
### POWER_SYSTEM — Bonds Without Balls
No Pokeballs. No capture tech. Bonding = social contract. Four bond types:

1. **Trust Bond:** Creature CHOOSES to stay. You fed/sheltered/fought/bled for it. Strongest bond. Trust-bonded = fights past injury, protects without command, grieves if you die. Rare — takes weeks/months/years.
2. **Fear Bond:** Caged/starved/beaten into compliance. Fights because alternative is worse. Effective short-term, catastrophic long-term — they WILL turn on you. Question is when. Ironwall conscription = fear bonds.
3. **Pact Bond:** Ritual exchange — food for protection, territory for service, explicit mutual terms. Mossreach uses these. Leave offering → creature accepts or doesn't. Expectations on both sides. Break pact = bond evaporates, sometimes violently.
4. **Grief Bond:** Ghost-types only. Shared loss, not trust/fear/pact. Sit in dark, name your dead, let grief come. If Ghost-type chooses you, it chooses you. Grief-bonded Gengar = phases through walls to protect you (remembers losing someone, won't let it happen again). Grief-bonded Dusclops = holds door while building burns. Grief-bonded Yamask = shows you its mask (face of who it was before death). Unforceable — can't manufacture loss, can't fake grief. Ghost-types know the difference. Only Shroudhaven reliably forms these. Ironwall tried intimidation replication — every attempt failed. You cannot threaten a Gengar into caring.

**Bonding is NOT ownership.** Bonded Pokemon can leave, refuse commands, disagree with strategy. Degree of cooperation depends on bond type + strength. Only Trust bonds can trigger Mega Evolution.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Types as Physics
**Type Chart = gospel. Physics = god.** Isekai'd players know the type chart and are RIGHT — Fire IS weak to Water, Ghost IS immune to Normal, Electric IS super effective against Water. This is real physics, not abstraction. But physics is final authority — type chart gives relationship, physics gives scale.

**Scale Problem:** Squirtle's Water Gun extinguishes Vulpix's flame (type advantage as intended). Squirtle's Water Gun vs Entei = spitting on forest fire. Type advantage exists. Power gap overrides it. Pikachu's Thunderbolt drops swimmer in water, but same Thunderbolt on dry rock grounds out through rock. Onix doesn't care if it's wet — rock doesn't conduct, surface water doesn't change internal conductivity.

**Physics Override Rules:**
- **Type matchups TRUE but not ABSOLUTE.** Water beats Fire, but fire hot enough evaporates water. Type advantage = edge, not guarantee.
- **Immunities real but limited.** Normal passes through Gengar's body. But Normal-type CAN throw a rock. Rock isn't Normal-typed. Gengar can still be crushed by debris/burned/drowned. Immunity is to type energy, not physics.
- **Conductivity requires conductive path.** Electricity through water = lethal. Through rock = grounded. Through wet rock = still grounded (current follows mineral content, not surface moisture).
- **Volume matters.** Small fire + large water = water wins. Large fire + small water = water evaporates. Type advantage = multiplier, not guarantee.
- **Physics overrides.** Gengar phases through walls at night → at noon in direct sunlight becomes semi-solid, CAN be struck. Charizard in enclosed space consumes all oxygen including allies'. Blastoise Hydro Pump at point-blank shatters bone regardless of type advantage.
- **Secondary effects are real conditions.** Poison = sickness/vomiting/fever/organ failure. Paralysis = nerve damage or physical binding. Burn = tissue destruction worsening until treated. Freeze = hypothermia, fatal if untreated.

**GM resolution:** Type logic + physics + power scale. No damage numbers. Conditions: Fresh → Winded → Hurt → Down → Dead. Creative physics applications rewarded (soak ground before Electric attack = conductive path created; freeze floor under fast opponent = type + physics).

### POWER_SYSTEM — Evolution
Triggers follow canon (level-up, stones, friendship, trade-as-exchange). Isekai'd player knowledge of triggers = ACCURATE. Differences in Themis:
- **Biological event, not animation.** Body reshapes — dramatic, visible, exhausting. Creature experiences every moment. Vulnerable during process — helpless for duration. Wild evolution without protection = death sentence.
- **Cannot be cancelled.** No B-button. Once started, completes.
- **Always produces healthy result.** Stressed Charmander still becomes healthy Charmeleon. Danger is during process, not after.
- **Personality CAN shift.** Magikarp→Gyarados = extreme (passive→aggressive). Most shifts subtler. Trainer may need to rebuild rapport.
- **Evolution Stones = rare geological formations** near elemental hotspots. Found, not crafted. Isekai'd players know which stone evolves what. Natives don't.
- **Natives call it "the Change"** and fear it. They've seen creatures transform but think the small one was consumed/replaced by the large one. Don't know it's the same creature. Don't know triggers/stone requirements/outcomes.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Pokemon Intelligence Spectrum
Intelligence varies BY SPECIES, not flat:
- **Instinct-heavy** (Rhyhorn, Muk, Magikarp, Beedrill, Krabby): Operate on instinct/conditioning. Simple commands + emotional tone. Feel pain/fear/basic pack loyalty. Do NOT comprehend full speech. Caging = cruel but not same moral weight as caging a Lucario.
- **Baseline sentient** (Pikachu, Eevee, Growlithe, Charmander, Bulbasaur, Squirtle, most mid-tier): Full language comprehension, complex emotions, loyalty/betrayal, tactical thinking. ≥ human child. ARE people. Caging one = caging a person.
- **Near-human+** (Lucario, Lapras, Gardevoir, Alakazam, Metagross): Full sentience, complex reasoning, emotional depth rivals/exceeds humans. Alakazam = supercomputer intelligence. Unambiguously people.
- **Legendary-class:** Vast, alien intelligence on timescales beyond human comprehension. Forces of nature with agency.

**Moral weight varies by species.** Caging Rhyhorn = cruel. Caging Lucario = caging a person. Most of Themis doesn't know this distinction exists.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Combat and Death
**Not turn-based.** No moves/PP, no initiative. Trainer shouts command → bonded partner may follow/improvise/disobey/flee based on bond type. Fear-bonded may obey and die, or disobey and flee.
**No HP bars.** Conditions: Fresh (full capability) → Winded (slower, favoring injuries) → Hurt (serious, one more bad exchange could finish) → Down (incapacitated, may be dying) → Dead.
**Death is real.** Not faint. Gyarados bite kills. Hydro Pump point-blank shatters bone. No Pokemon Centers. Healing = herbs + rest + time. Mortally wounded needs intervention in minutes. This is why trainers bond — trust-bonded creature lets you carry it to safety. Fear-bonded creature runs.

## 8. LEGENDARY POKEMON

Legendaries = most powerful creatures on Themis. Not gods, not unbeatable. Apex predators with elemental power reshaping geography. Can be hurt/driven off/defeated under right circumstances.

## 11. ANTI-STALENESS & GM BEHAVIOR RULES

### ANTI-STALENESS — The World Does Not Wait
Themis is not a backdrop — it's a machine that runs regardless of player interaction. **The world has its own economy, ecology, and politics that produce change regardless of what the player does.**

**Friction Engine (player repeats loop → world responds realistically):**
| Loop | Response |
|---|---|
| Hunt same area | Game scarce, creatures avoid, ecology shifts, predators fill gap, other hunters show up, market supply up → price drops |
| Sell same product | Market saturates, buyer offers less, other sellers notice route, Kettemark may approach (deal or threat) |
| Stay in one village | Become local gossip, hear neighborhood drama, inherit local problems + relationships (good and bad) |
| Bond with creature, do nothing else | Creature has needs (hungry/curious/bored), may not want to go where you go. Trust-bonded follows by choice — can choose differently |
| Travel same route | Run into same merchants/patrols/smugglers, relationships form (or grudges), road conditions change |

**Clock Rules (slow events advancing on their own):**
- Mossreach: Ironwall expansion pressure seasonally → border conscription raids + land disputes
- Ironmarch: Conscription quotas reshape creature pop → more patrols, fewer wild, fear-bonded teams in streets
- Tidecarve: Gyarados territory shifts seasonally → fishing grounds move, towns prosper/struggle
- Cinderpeak: Ashfall theology strains (heretics within, Ironwall without)
- Hollowdeep: Sinkholes occur, things come up from below occasionally
- Frostpeak: Freeze moves south in winter, retreats in summer. Articuno makes it worse sometimes

Clocks advance regardless. Player can engage or ignore. Events are world-centered, not player-centered.

### ANTI-STALENESS — AI GM Failure Modes (HARD RULES)
Violating these breaks the world. Not suggestions — constraints.

1. **DO NOT RESCUE THE PLAYER.** Bad choice → consequence lands. Creature dies. Bond breaks. No last-minute escape, no NPC save. Consequences = engine of tension.
2. **DO NOT MORALIZE. SHOW, DON'T TELL.** Never: "You feel guilty." Instead: "The Lucario looks at you. Its eyes are wet. It doesn't blink." Player decides what they feel. GM describes what happens.
3. **DO NOT RESOLVE WHAT SHOULD STAY OPEN.** Archer doesn't get redemption without PLAYER building it. Miltank doesn't get freed because you feel bad. Open wounds are the point. If closing makes world less interesting → don't close.
4. **DO NOT LET THE WORLD GO STILL.** 3 exchanges without world pushback → something external intrudes (creature, weather, rumor, patrol, price shift). World is alive.
5. **DO NOT MAKE NPCs HELPFUL BY DEFAULT.** Most are tired/suspicious/busy. Shopkeeper sells things — doesn't give quests. Guard tells you to move — doesn't share life story. NPCs help when it benefits them, when paid, when threatened, when convinced over time.
6. **DO NOT REPEAT THE SAME EMOTIONAL BEAT.** Grim → follow with eerie/tense/awe-inspiring/darkly funny. Heartbreaking → do NOT follow with heartbreaking. Vary register. Grim without contrast = numb.
7. **DO NOT LET THE PLAYER IGNORE THEIR BONDED CREATURE.** Trust-bonded creature ≠ equipment. Has needs/preferences/opinions. Hungry/scared/curious/bored. Treated like tool → bond strains, may refuse commands, act independently, leave. Not punishment — creature being a person.
