# Tenebria

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW

### OVERVIEW — Core Premise
In the year 1994, the sun did not rise. It did not explode or fade; it simply vanished, leaving a dead sky of pitch-black static. For forty years, the world of Tenebria has existed in perpetual night, illuminated only by the cold neon of surviving industrial cities and the rare, rusted light of the Blood Moon. Humanity lives huddled under artificial light. The darkness is not merely empty; it is a hostile medium, occupied by anomalies that operate under strict, anomalous rules. 

Urban civilization survives via coal-fired grids, chemical lanterns, and treated candles. Traditional agriculture is impossible; food is grown in massive, underground hydroponic vaults fueled by UV lamps or harvested from damp, subterranean fungus farms. Firearms are standard issue, loaded with magnesium, phosphor, and salt-shot. Outside the city walls lies the Dead Sough, an endless wasteland of freezing dark where lost anomalies and country-deleting horrors drift.

**Tone:** GRIM, URBAN_REALISM, ANOMALOUS_HORROR, UTILITY_PUNK, DESPERATE
**Core Theme:** Survival at the cost of light. The tension between public utility, private greed, and the terrifying conditional rules of a world where darkness is active biology.
**Current Year:** 2034 (Forty years after the Great Eclipse)

## 2. FACTIONS

### FACTION — Halden Utilities Board
**Type:** Municipal Corporation / Bureaucracy
**Key Members:** Superintendent Charles Mercer, Chief Engineer Arthur Vance, Director Evelyn Gray
**Stance:** Pro-establishment, monopolistic, authoritarian.
The Halden Utilities Board (HUB) controls the coal-fired turbines, the nuclear steam vaults, and the high-voltage grid lines that feed the core wards. Light is life, and the HUB treats it as a currency. They ration electricity, cut power to non-paying sectors, and use the Threat Grid—a system of high-intensity carbon-arc searchlights—to repel anomalies. They view any off-grid light generation or Relic-use as a threat to municipal order and corporate control. They maintain a private police force, the Grid Wardens, who audit energy usage and confiscate bootleg batteries.

### FACTION — The Chandlers' Union
**Type:** Trade Guild / Artisan Cartel
**Key Members:** Elder Clara Oswald, Master Chandler David Finch, Alchemist Joseph Thorne
**Stance:** Neutral, protective of trade secrets, essential to survival.
The Chandlers' Union controls the manufacture of specialized candles and oil blends. Realizing that electrical currents attract certain high-frequency anomalies, the Union harvests tallow from Rank I anomalies and blends it with salt and chemical stabilizers to create "Warded Wax." These candles produce a dim, flickering glow that creates a localized ward against cognitive and sensory anomalies. They are the primary protectors of the lower wards and slums. The Union is highly insular, guarding their secret recipes with lethal force.

### FACTION — The Night Watch
**Type:** Militarized Constabulary
**Key Members:** Captain Thomas Thorne, Lieutenant Sarah Miller, Warden-Inspector Gregory Holt
**Stance:** Pro-establishment, protective, heavily armed.
The Night Watch is the municipal defense force responsible for patrolling the unlit borders, the slums, and the breach zones. Armed with heavy-caliber firearms, magnesium flares, and searchlight-mounted vehicles, they are the thin line between the city and the horrors of the Dead Sough. They are overworked, underpaid, and highly suspicious of anyone carrying unauthorized light sources or unregistered Relics. They operate under a strict code of conduct but are frequently forced to make morally gray decisions to contain outbreaks.

### FACTION — The Heliocentric Society
**Type:** Underground Movement / Outlaw Scientists
**Key Members:** Dr. Irene Adler, Marcus Cole, Researcher Chen Wei
**Stance:** Hostile to HUB, revolutionary.
An illegal network of engineers and physicists operating out of the abandoned subway lines. They believe the HUB is intentionally maintaining the sunless status quo to keep humanity enslaved to the grid. The Society conducts dangerous experiments with fusion cores and anomalous light sources in an attempt to spark an artificial sun. Their actions often cause catastrophic blackouts and attract high-rank anomalies to the city borders, making them a target for both the HUB and the Night Watch.

### FACTION — The Ash-Sifters' Guild
**Type:** Labor Union / Scavenger Syndicate
**Key Members:** Guildmaster Liam "Sooty" Briggs, Chief Dredger Maeve O'Connor
**Stance:** Neutral, opportunistic, blue-collar.
The Ash-Sifters are the laborers who venture into the borders of the Dead Sough and the slag heaps of the coal plants to harvest fuel, scrap, and anomaly residues. They wear thick, soot-stained hazard suits and respirator masks to survive the ash-clogged air. The Guild controls the supply of industrial raw materials and operates the "Sift-Lines"—massive steam-crawler routes that haul scrap back to the city. They have a tense relationship with the HUB, which constantly tries to tax their hauls.

### FACTION — Fellowship of the Filament
**Type:** Religious Sect / Light Cult
**Key Members:** High Priest Isaiah Lumos, Sister Miriam
**Stance:** Hostile to the HUB (doctrinal), proselytizing.
A religious group that worships high-intensity light as a divine savior and views the darkness as a literal, spiritual plague. They cover their skin in glowing phosphorescent paint, wear reflective clothing, and carry lanterns at all times. The Fellowship believes that the "Bright" will return if they perform continuous light-bearing ceremonies. They frequently clash with the HUB because they steal electricity from grid lines to power their massive "Filament Altars"—structures covered in thousands of burning lightbulbs.

### FACTION — The Quietude
**Type:** Ascetic Order / Shadow Cult
**Key Members:** The Silent One (real identity unknown), Brother Kenneth
**Stance:** Hostile to all light-bearing factions, isolationist.
A faction of ascetics who live in absolute silence in the dark zones. They believe that sound is the primary trigger for higher-tier anomalies and that light is an unnatural violation of the world's current state. The Quietude communicates via a complex sign language and is highly hostile to loud machinery and bright lights. They conduct sabotage missions to cut power cables, disable generators, and smash streetlights in an effort to "restore the natural dark."

### FACTION — The Solar Archivists
**Type:** Historical Preservation Society
**Key Members:** Curator Beatrice Vance, Archivist Daniel Kim
**Stance:** Neutral, intellectual, secretive.
A group of scholars dedicated to cataloging and translating pre-1994 literature, technology, and science. They operate secretly inside retrofitted libraries and deep vaults, attempting to keep the memories of the sun, plant biology, and blue skies alive for a generation that has never seen them. They are targeted by the HUB, which views pre-Eclipse history as a source of civil unrest, and are frequently hunted for their old-world books and schematics.

## 3. LOCATIONS

### LOCATION — The High Grid
**Type:** Administrative and Residential Core
**Status:** Flourishing (Highly Guarded)
The administrative heart of New Halden. Illuminated 24/7 by massive, humming overhead neon rigs, copper cables, and carbon searchlights. The wealthy and the HUB directors live here, insulated from the terrors of the dark by thick grid-lines, high-voltage gates, and triple-redundant coal generators. The air here constantly smells of ozone, coal smoke, and chemical vapor. The streets are swept clean of soot daily.

### LOCATION — The Sump (Outskirt Wards)
**Type:** Urban Slums and Industrial Wards
**Status:** Decaying and Contested
The sprawling residential and factory districts where the HUB's power is erratic and rationed. Streetlights are rare, and residents rely on oil lamps, chemical glow-tubes, and Warded Wax from the Chandlers' Union. The Sump is divided by "Gloom Zones"—pockets of pitch-black alleys where Rank I anomalies manifest nightly. The walls are stained black with coal soot, and the streets are crowded with factories, public kitchens, and cheap candle-stalls.

### LOCATION — The Dead Sough
**Type:** Post-Industrial Wilderness
**Status:** Ruined and Lethal
The frozen, unlit wasteland beyond the city's searchlight perimeter. It consists of abandoned towns, frozen forests, and rusting factories. It is completely dark, save for the rare Blood Moon, which turns the sky the color of dried liver. The Sough is home to nomadic scavengers, feral outcasts, and Sovereign anomalies that can erase entire landscapes. The ground is covered in a layer of black ash and frozen sleet.

### LOCATION — Pump Station 4 (The Iron Shallows)
**Type:** Industrial Infrastructure / Waterway Hub
**Status:** Operational (High Threat)
A massive, half-flooded industrial district where steam-powered pumps run constantly to prevent the city's coal reserves from flooding. It is dark, damp, and infested with water-borne anomalies. The air is thick with steam and rust-scent. Workers navigate the district on elevated iron catwalks suspended over dark, boiling water. A single pump failure could plunge the industrial sector into a catastrophic blackout.

### LOCATION — The Grey Dump (The Ash Flats)
**Type:** Waste Deposit / Scavenger Zone
**Status:** Contested
A grey, desolate plain just outside the city walls where decades of coal plant waste have been dumped. The ground is soft, warm soot, and the air is thick with black ash that obscures searchlights. It is controlled by the Ash-Sifters' Guild, who scavenge the dumps for unburnt coal and anomalous residues. It is a highly dangerous area due to shifting ash dunes and Rank I "Soot-Feeders."

### LOCATION — Ward 9 (The Silent Sector)
**Type:** Quarantined Ruins
**Status:** Quarantined / Abandoned
An abandoned residential sector that was quarantined after a Rank III anomaly outbreak in 2024. The grid was cut, and the gates were welded shut. It is now an unlit, overgrown concrete jungle where no one is allowed to speak or make sound. The Night Watch patrols the perimeter walls, under orders to shoot anyone attempting to enter or leave. 

### LOCATION — The River Gate (The Black Docks)
**Type:** Trade Port / Smuggler Outpost
**Status:** Contested
A dry dock area built along a frozen, oil-slicked river. Smugglers and Ash-Sifters use specialized steam-barges equipped with massive front-mounted searchlights and iron hulls to navigate the frozen waterways into the Dead Sough. The HUB maintains a toll station here, but the local dockworkers are loyal to the Ash-Sifters' Guild and run a massive gray-market shipping network.

### LOCATION — The Filament Cathedral
**Type:** Temple / Light Sanctuary
**Status:** Flourishing (Sect Controlled)
The grand temple of the Fellowship of the Filament, located in a retrofitted gothic cathedral in the lower wards. It is covered in thousands of burning lightbulbs, glowing neon tubes, and chemical lanterns, making it the brightest place in the Sump. The heat from the lights makes the cathedral warm and dry, but the hum of the electricity is deafening. The Fellowship guards the cathedral with armed zealots.

### LOCATION — The Sub-Transit Labyrinth
**Type:** Underground Tunnel Network
**Status:** Abandoned (High Threat)
The vast network of abandoned underground subways, maintenance tunnels, and sewer lines beneath New Halden. It is pitch black, used by smugglers, the Heliocentric Society, and the Quietude. The tunnels are heavily infested with Rank II shadows and are avoided by the Night Watch, who only enter during active containment breaches.

### LOCATION — The Scrap Hills (The Rusted Reach)
**Type:** Junkyard / Scrap Field
**Status:** Scrap Yard
A massive junkyard of old-world cars, trains, and machinery located at the edge of the city. Scrap metal is harvested here under the protective beams of Watch towers. It is a maze of rusting iron and sharp edges, where scavengers dodge both Watch patrols and Rank II "Magnetic Snappers" that hide in the rusted piles.

### LOCATION — The Blood Moon Observatory
**Type:** Research Outpost
**Status:** Operational (HUB Controlled)
A high-altitude research outpost built atop a crumbling skyscraper in the High Grid, used by the HUB to track the Blood Moon's cycle and predict anomaly surges. It contains the city's largest optical telescopes and anomalous energy sensors. The rooftop is heavily guarded by Grid Wardens and automated searchlights.

## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES

### POWER_SYSTEM — Luminescence Dynamics & Anomaly Relics
The world of Tenebria is governed by the interaction between Light, Darkness, and the rules of the Anomalies that dwell within them.

#### 1. Light Dynamics
Different light sources have distinct physical and anomalous properties:
*   **Grid Electricity:** High-intensity and covers large areas, but its low-frequency electromagnetic hum attracts "Voltage Feeders" (Rank III anomalies that drain current and cause explosions).
*   **Warded Wax (Candlelight):** Low-intensity and easily extinguished, but the salt-and-tallow chemistry creates an occult barrier that repels cognitive hazards.
*   **Magnesium/Chemical Light:** Extremely bright and quiet, but short-lived. Used primarily by the Night Watch for tactical illumination.

#### 2. The Anomaly Classification System (Rank × Vector)
Anomalies are classified along two independent axes: Impact Scope (Rank) and Threat Vector (Vector).

##### Axis 1: Rank (Impact Scope)
*   **Rank I (Incident):** Affects 1–3 individuals. Household-level threat. Easily managed with warded wax or basic protocol.
*   **Rank II (Sector):** Affects a street, building, or block. Requires localized Night Watch patrol response.
*   **Rank III (District):** Affects a ward or neighborhood. Requires heavy military containment and Threat Grid activation.
*   **Rank IV (Municipal):** Affects the entire city of New Halden. Requires full grid mobilization and district evacuations.
*   **Rank V (Sovereign):** World-altering, regional phenomena. Uncontainable, representing a fundamental shift in natural laws.

##### Axis 2: Vector (Nature of Threat)
*   **Vector P (Physical):** Tangible, material entities that can be fought or contained with weaponry.
*   **Vector C (Cognitive):** Attacks sensory perception and the mind; countered by mental discipline or warded wax.
*   **Vector E (Environmental):** Reshapes physical laws, weather, or terrain; a condition to endure rather than fight.
*   **Vector M (Memetic):** Spreads via speech, info, or observation; triggered simply by knowing or witnessing.

#### 3. Relic Drops and Attunement
When an anomaly is destroyed (an exceptionally rare and dangerous feat), it occasionally leaves behind a physical, anomalous object known as a **Relic** or **Remnant** (e.g., a soot-encrusted bell, a shard of dark glass, a calcified biological valve). 
*   **Attunement:** A human can attune to a Relic through prolonged physical contact and mental alignment. Attunement is highly dangerous, risking madness or physical mutation, but successful attunement turns the user into a supernatural powerhouse.
*   **Powers:** The attuned individual gains anomalous abilities directly related to the defeated anomaly (e.g., the ability to walk through shadows, suppress localized sound, bend light around their body, or sense the heartbeat of any living thing within a mile). A single Rank III Relic can turn an ordinary human into a city-level tactical asset.

#### 4. Rules of the Dark (Example Anomalies)
Anomalies do not hunt by sight or sound alone; they are bound by conditional laws. For canon anomaly entries, see Section 11: Unique Anomalies.

## 6. ECONOMY

### ECONOMY — Watts & Tallow
In Tenebria, traditional currencies have collapsed. The economy is built on utility and survival:
*   **The Watt:** The standard currency of the High Grid. Measured in battery charge or grid-link credits. A single Watt credit can buy enough grid light for a small apartment for one hour.
*   **Tallow & Wax:** The currency of the Outskirt Wards. Raw animal fat, paraffin, and salt are traded for food and medicine. Refined "Warded Wax" candles are highly valuable and function as a stable bartering tool.
*   **Magnesium & Phosphor:** Rare, military-grade resources used to manufacture specialized flares and phosphor rounds for the Night Watch. They are heavily rationed and traded only on the black market.

## 7. EVENTS

### EVENT — The Great Eclipse (1994)
The day the sun failed to rise. Within forty-eight hours, the global grid collapsed, crops failed, and the first anomalies crawled out of the shadows. It is the boundary line of human history; everything before is "The Bright," and everything after is "The Dark."

### EVENT — The Blackout of Year Ten (2004)
A catastrophic failure of the HUB's primary coal plants that plunged the entire city into darkness for three days. Over ten thousand people died as Rank II and Rank III anomalies flooded the core wards. It led to the militarization of the Night Watch and the strict rationing of electricity.

### EVENT — The Treaty of the Filament (2018)
A formal peace agreement signed between the HUB and the Fellowship of the Filament after a series of violent grid-theft riots. The HUB agreed to lease a portion of the lower ward grids to the Fellowship in exchange for their assistance in maintaining public order and repelling Rank III anomalies using the Filament Cathedral's searchlights.

### EVENT — The Great Rusting (2029)
A week-long surge of water-borne anomalies in Pump Station 4 that corroded half of the city's steam-pipe network. The resulting steam leaks scalded hundreds of residents and caused a massive coal shortage, forcing the HUB to cut power to the Outskirt Wards for a month.

## 8. DAILY LIFE & CULTURE

### CULTURE — Food, Drink, & Socializing
*   **Diet:** The main source of calories is "Muck-Mash," a dense, earthy paste made from yeast and cultivated vault-mushrooms. Hydroponic farms in the lower High Grid produce "Hydro-Greens" (tough, bitter kale and cabbage), which are heavily rationed. Scavengers and miners eat "Tallow-Pemmicans"—hard bars of dried anomaly protein bound together with warded tallow.
*   **Beverages:** Water is recycled and heavily chlorinated, known colloquially as "Grid-Wash." TA-level citizens drink "Sump-Stout," a rough, fermented potato-and-yeast mash brewed in the slums. Chicory and dried vault-mold are steeped to make a bitter "Black-Tea," which is drunk hot to fend off the deep chill of the unlit wards.
*   **Socializing:** People gather in "Glow-Parlors," low-ceilinged basement taverns lit by Warded Wax candles or low-amperage orange bulbs. Socializing is tense; people rarely sit with their back to a dark doorway, and loud laughter is discouraged to avoid attracting Rank II anomalies. Walking home alone after the Third Shift pulse is considered a death sentence.

### CULTURE — Fashion & Dress
*   **Overview:** Clothing is designed for warmth, utility, and protection. Layers of heavy wool and oilskins are standard. People wear high rubber boots due to constant soot-sleet and damp industrial runoff. Yellow-tinted goggles are worn to improve contrast in low-light environments.
*   **Faction Markers:**
    *   **Grid Wardens (HUB):** Wear clean, rubberized grey greatcoats with polished brass buttons and orange high-visibility stripes on the cuffs. They carry heavy battery-powered inspectors' lanterns.
    *   **Chandlers' Union:** Wear thick canvas aprons stained with yellow paraffin and salt, smelling heavily of sulfur and animal fat. They wear leather bandoliers designed to hold warded candles.
    *   **Night Watch:** Wear dark blue wool greatcoats, steel gorgets, and high-collared leather liners to protect their necks. They carry military-grade magnesium flares on their chests.
    *   **Ash-Sifters' Guild:** Wear bulky, soot-encrusted leather hazard suits sealed with pitch, complete with heavy dual-filter respirators and brass-rimmed goggles.
    *   **Fellowship of the Filament:** Wear reflective, silver-threaded ponchos and apply glowing phosphorescent paint to their sleeves and collars. They carry hand-cranked copper lanterns.
    *   **The Quietude:** Wear simple, undyed hemp robes with no metallic buttons or zippers to eliminate noise. They cover their lower faces with black cloth masks.
    *   **Solar Archivists:** Wear heavy, dark tweed coats with reinforced elbow patches and carry oilskin satchels containing charcoal pencils and ledger books.

### CULTURE — Superstitions, Slang, & Sayings
*   **Superstitions:**
    *   *The Last Drop:* Never blow out a candle completely; pinch the wick with wet fingers, or the rising smoke will draw a shadow anomaly to your bed.
    *   *The Red Glance:* If you spot a red light in the wasteland, blink three times and look away; looking twice invites the Blood Horizon.
    *   *Mirror-Soot:* Painting a thick black soot stripe across the center of a mirror prevents anomalies from using your reflection as a doorway.
    *   *Ozone-Spit:* Spitting when you cross beneath a humming transformer line wards off Static-Claws.
    *   *Salt-Sowing:* Keeping a pinch of rock salt in your boots prevents Rank I pests from nesting in your clothing.
*   **Slang:**
    *   *Burner:* A reckless person who wastes battery charge or candle wax.
    *   *Gloom-Blind:* Suffering from sensory hallucinations caused by long-term light deprivation.
    *   *Sinker:* A suicide or desperate exile who walks into the unlit zone without a light source.
    *   *Watt-poor:* Destitute; unable to pay the HUB's monthly electricity taxes.
    *   *Slick-crawler:* Smugglers who navigate the frozen, oil-slicked rivers on armored steam-barges.
*   **Sayings:**
    *   *"Keep the wick short and the salt dry."* (A standard parting phrase meaning stay alert and prepared).
    *   *"The HUB takes your Watts; the Dark takes the rest."* (A common complaint about the inevitability of loss).
    *   *"A quiet mouth keeps the throat whole."* (A warning to remain silent when anomalies are near).
    *   *"Only the dead look at the moon."* (A warning against looking at the sky during a Blood Moon).
    *   *"Cold stove, cold bones."* (A reminder that running out of coal is fatal).

### CULTURE — Timekeeping
*   **System:** Without the sun, conventional day and night do not exist. The HUB maintains the municipal cycle by pulsing the grid voltage three times every 24 hours to signal shift changes:
    *   **First Shift (Work):** The grid voltage is boosted, and steam whistles sound across the wards.
    *   **Second Shift (Maintenance):** Power is throttled, and streetlights are dimmed to conserve coal.
    *   **Third Shift (Gloom/Lockdown):** Power is cut to the Sump and residential wards, forcing residents to rely on warded candles. Street activity is strictly prohibited.
*   **Devices:** Citizens use mechanical wind-up pocket watches (cherished pre-Eclipse relics) or simple sand-glasses filled with fine coal-ash calibrated to the 8-hour shift cycles.

### CULTURE — Entertainment & Leisure
*   **Shadow-Plays:** Puppet theatres operate in the lower wards, using single-filament bulbs behind stretched wax sheets to project paper silhouettes. They perform traditional plays depicting old-world stories of sunlit oceans and green forests.
*   **Glow-Bones:** A popular dice game played in taverns, using dice carved from Rank I anomaly bone painted with glowing green phosphorus.
*   **Recitations:** Gathering in vaults to listen to elderly citizens describe the taste of fresh fruit, the color of a blue sky, or the warmth of summer.
*   **Sump-Brawls:** Blindfolded bare-knuckle boxing matches held in unlit Sump basements, where fighters and spectators must remain silent, relying entirely on hearing the opponent's breathing.

## 9. GEOGRAPHY — BEYOND NEW HALDEN

### SETTLEMENT — Oakhaven Coal-Sough
*   **Population:** ~1,200 (Estimated)
*   **Survival Method:** Built directly inside a massive, open-pit coal mine. The settlement is warmed by underground coal-seams that have been burning for decades, venting thick, toxic smoke that the residents filter using steam-powered fans. They survive by exporting raw anthracite coal.
*   **Relationship to New Halden:** A heavily guarded vassal colony. The HUB maintains a permanent garrison of Grid Wardens to enforce coal quotas, and the Night Watch patrols the rim. The miners are highly rebellious, frequently sabotaging the steam-crawlers that haul the coal to the city.

### SETTLEMENT — The Salt-Spire Outpost
*   **Population:** ~350 (Estimated)
*   **Survival Method:** Built at the edge of a vast, crystalline salt flat. Residents mine anomalous rock salt deposits that are essential for making defensive salt-shot ammunition and blending warded wax. They grow dry-resistant root crops in geothermal greenhouses.
*   **Relationship to New Halden:** Transactional trade partner. The outpost is independent but relies entirely on New Halden for replacement machine parts, refined oil, and medical supplies.

### SETTLEMENT — Station Bravo
*   **Population:** ~800 (Estimated)
*   **Survival Method:** A deep, multi-level military bunker complex built before the Eclipse. They utilize a deep geothermal tap to power massive, automated hydroponic chambers that grow high-yield wheat and antibiotic molds.
*   **Relationship to New Halden:** Strained and isolationist. Station Bravo is governed by a militaristic junta composed of descendants of the old-world army. They trade surplus grain to New Halden but refuse to share their automated technology or allow HUB inspectors inside.

### SETTLEMENT — Black-Water Pier
*   **Population:** ~500 (Estimated)
*   **Survival Method:** A stilt-village built over the steaming, unfrozen waters of a geothermal lake deep in the Dead Sough. Residents harvest blind cave-fish, oily water-beetles, and black kelp from the warm waters.
*   **Relationship to New Halden:** A notorious smuggler haven. The HUB has no official presence here, and the village is run by the Ash-Sifters' Guild and slick-crawlers who use it as a staging post for expeditions deeper into the wasteland.

### GEOGRAPHY — External Contact
*   **Status:** All radio and telegraph contact with other major pre-Eclipse metropolitan areas (such as London, New York, or Tokyo) was lost during the winter of 1994 and has never been restored. While Sough-scavengers occasionally find rusted vehicles from old military expeditions, there is no proof that any other major cities survived. To the residents of New Halden, the city is the last light on a dead planet.

## 10. ANOMALY EQUIPMENT & COUNTERMEASURES

### TECH — Detection Instruments & Warning Signs
*   **Sift-Vials:** Small glass shakers containing high-purity iron filings or fine lead powder. Civilians dust their doorsteps or window frames with them; the powder drifts or aligns into geometric patterns if an anomaly with a magnetic or static field is nearby.
*   **The Tick-Box:** Portable, brass-cased vacuum-tube electroscopes carried by the Night Watch. They produce a rhythmic, clicking static hum that spikes in pitch and frequency when anomalous distortion waves pass through the sector.
*   **Soot-Plumes:** Specialized matchsticks infused with sulfur and copper salts. When lit, the flame burns green; if it bends sharply in stagnant air or turns a deep blue, it indicates a nearby thermodynamic or cognitive anomaly.
*   **Canary Cages:** Small, wire-and-glass jars holding blind vault-crickets or pale cave-beetles. These insects are highly sensitive to anomalous static; they stop chirping and self-mutilate or turn grey when an anomaly is within 20 meters.
*   **Somatic Warning Signs:** Standard indicators include an instant metallic taste in the mouth (indicating Rank III and above), hair standing on end (Static-Claw), sudden local freezing of moisture (cold-burning anomalies), or a phantom pressure in the ears.

### TECH — Defensive & Protective Gear
*   **Warded Wax:** Paraffin wax harvested from Rank I Soot-Feeders, refined and blended with salt and zinc-magnesium oxides. When burned, the chemical vapor acts as a cognitive dampener, creating a localized frequency shield that repels Rank II and lower cognitive hazards. Warded candles must be shielded from drafts to prevent accidental extinguishing.
*   **Salt-Shot & Salt-Brooms:** Cartridges packed with coarse rock salt and lead pellets. Civilians use salt-brooms to sweep ash-weeper soot from their thresholds and maintain protective salt-barriers around their beds.
*   **Soot-Suits:** Bulky, heavy canvas and rubber hazard suits lined with lead-foil to block anomalous static charge and prevent cognitive possession. They feature dual-cartridge respirators to filter ash-sleet and toxic soot.
*   **Silent-Soles & Felt Collars:** Press-felt pads glued to boot soles to muffle walking noise. Night Watch patrol coats feature thick felt-lined collars that absorb the vibration of the user's heartbeat, reducing their detection radius against Heart-Takers.
*   **Gloom-Goggles:** Goggles fitted with polished amber lenses that filter out low-frequency visual static, making details in the dark easier to distinguish.

### TECH — Containment Systems
*   **The Threat Grid:** Massive carbon-arc searchlights mounted on HUB-controlled platforms that create a boundary wall of light that minor anomalies cannot cross. They require steam generators and draw massive coal rations.
*   **Grounded Spikes:** Copper-clad steel spikes driven 3 meters into the ground around perimeter walls, connected to high-voltage capacitor banks. They discharge localized static build-up, preventing anomalies like Static-Claws from manifesting on the walls.
*   **Mobile Containment Crawlers:** Armored steam-crawlers operated by the Night Watch. They feature front-mounted searchlights, chemical brine-throwers to dissolve Rank I physical anomalies, and lead-shielded cages to transport recovered Relics.

### TECH — Weaponry & Special Ammunition
*   **Breach-Lanterns:** Double-barrel shotguns fitted with an under-barrel mechanical launcher that fires magnesium flares. Used to blind and stun anomalies before firing.
*   **Pneumatic Sling-Guns:** Silent, air-powered rifles that fire heavy lead bearings or salt-tipped darts. Used when operating in sectors where sound-sensitive anomalies are present.
*   **Magnesium Slugs:** Heavy hollow-point rounds packed with compressed magnesium powder. On impact, the slug ignites, burning at 2,200°C (4,000°F) to incinerate anomalous tissue.
*   **Phosphor Tracers:** Bullets coated in slow-burning green phosphorus. They leave a glowing trail in the dark, marking targets and providing temporary illumination for squads.
*   **UV Rounds:** Shotgun shells containing glass beads filled with liquid phosphorus that releases intense ultraviolet light upon crushing, burning light-sensitive underground entities.

### TECH — Relic-Derived Gear
*   **Filament Battery:** A battery cell containing an attuned Rank II shard that slowly recharges itself by absorbing thermal energy from the ambient air, making the surrounding area freezing cold.
*   **Sough-Lantern:** A lantern that uses a Rank III Relic core to project a cold, dark-purple beam. It doesn't illuminate for human eyes but reveals hidden anomalies and repels Rank I and Rank II shadows.
*   **Static-Gauntlet:** A wrist-mounted gauntlet made from a Rank III Relic (from a Copper-Drainer) that can absorb a single high-voltage strike or anomalous static blast, burning out the gauntlet but saving the wearer.

### TECH — Counter-Anomaly Protocols
*   **Civilian Emergency Protocols:**
    *   **Rank I Encounter (Incident):** Smother physical pests with salt; if followed by cognitive shadows, keep moving forward and do not look back.
    *   **Rank II Encounter (Sector):** Stand completely still, hold breath, and do not speak or respond to voices.
    *   **Rank III Encounter (District):** Retreat immediately; notify the nearest Night Watch outpost via telegraph.
    *   **Rank IV & V Encounter (Municipal/Sovereign):** Seek underground shelter; seal all doors; extinguish all non-warded lights.
*   **Night Watch Patrol Protocols:**
    *   **Rank I (Incident):** Dispatch a single patrol warden with a salt-broom or pneumatic sling-gun.

## 12. ANOMALY ENGINE — GENERATOR RULES

### ENGINE — Threat Rank Framework
*   **Rank I (Incident):** Affects 1-3 individuals. Household-level threat. Can be managed with Warded Wax and basic protocol. E.g., a shadow that follows you home, a face in the soot.
*   **Rank II (Sector):** Affects a street, building, or block. Requires Night Watch patrol response. E.g., a blind humanoid hunting through a district.
*   **Rank III (District):** Affects a ward or neighborhood. Requires heavy military containment and Threat Grid activation. E.g., an entity that shatters all electrical components.
*   **Rank IV (Municipal):** Affects the entire city of New Halden. Requires city-wide protocol and full mobilization. E.g., a weather phenomenon inverting heat.
*   **Rank V (Sovereign):** Affects the entire known world. Uncontainable, representing a fundamental alteration of natural laws.
*   **Escalation Criteria:** Anomalies of Rank I and Rank II that go uncontained in high-static Gloom Zones for more than 72 hours will escalate one Rank level (Rank I → Rank II, Rank II → Rank III). Rank III and above do not escalate further but grow more aggressive over time if not contained.

### ENGINE — Trigger Taxonomy
Anomalies do not search or hunt by sight; they are bound to trigger conditions classified under these categories:
*   **Auditory (AUD):** Triggered by sound volume, specific words, heartbeats, or metallic metal-on-metal vibrations.
*   **Visual (VIS):** Triggered by direct observation (looking at the Blood Moon, looking back at shadows), using high-intensity arc-lights, or mirror reflections.
*   **Cognitive/Emotional (COG):** Triggered by panic, intense grief, thinking of a specific name, or showing fear.
*   **Kinetic/Action (KIN):** Triggered by running, striking matches, splashing water, drawing electrical current, or carrying iron.
*   **Somatic/Presence (SOM):** Triggered by body heat, skin exposure, static charge, or exhaled breath.
*   **Chronological (CHR):** Triggered by shift changes, midnight hours, or Blood Moon intervals.

### ENGINE — Survival-Axiom Framework
For every trigger category, there is a corresponding, absolute survival rule that must be followed to avoid activation or escape lethality:
*   *Auditory Trigger* -> **Axiom of Silence:** Muffle all sounds, hold breath, suppress heartbeat, do not speak.
*   *Visual Trigger* -> **Axiom of Aversion:** Bow your head, close eyes, wear soot-goggles, cover mirror reflections.
*   *Cognitive Trigger* -> **Axiom of Apathy:** Maintain absolute mental detachment, suppress fear, ignore panic.
*   *Kinetic Trigger* -> **Axiom of Stasis:** Freeze all physical motion, extinguish open flames, drop iron tools.
*   *Somatic Trigger* -> **Axiom of Insulation:** Wear lead-foil warded suits, ground static charge, seal exposed skin.
*   *Chronological Trigger* -> **Axiom of Shelter:** Retreat to reinforced vaults, lock down grid terminals, wait out the cycle.

### ENGINE — Behavior Archetypes
*   **Pursuer:** Relentlessly tracks and closes the distance to the target once the trigger occurs.
*   **Trap-Layer:** Mimics harmless, everyday items (stoves, doors, chairs) and waits for physical contact.
*   **Area-Denial:** Emits toxic soot, static fields, or extreme cold in a fixed zone to make it lethal.
*   **Mimicry:** Replicates voices of loved ones or lights of patrol searchlights to lure prey into unlit zones.
*   **Memetic:** Spreads through written text or spoken concepts; simply knowing its conditional rule triggers it.
*   **Environmental:** Mutates physical terrain, causes walls to close in, or turns local atmosphere to soot.

### ENGINE — Naming Conventions
*   **Common Name:** Descriptive title given by Sump residents or Ash-Sifters, based on its appearance or lethal effect (e.g., "The Magnetic Snapper").
*   **HUB Classification Code:** The administrative alphanumeric code designated by the Halden Utilities Board. Format: `[Rank]-[Sector Code]-[Trigger Category].[Sequence Number]`.
    *   Useful Sector Codes: `HGD` (High Grid), `SMP` (The Sump), `DSH` (Dead Sough), `PST` (Pump Stations), `W09` (Ward 9).
    *   Trigger Categories: `AUD`, `VIS`, `COG`, `KIN`, `SOM`, `CHR`.
    *   *Example:* The Heart-Taker is officially cataloged as `III-SMP-AUD.06`.

## 13. GOVERNMENT, LAW & CIVIL STRUCTURE

### LAW — Administrative Bodies & Courts
*   **Courts:** Run by HUB-appointed Magistrates. The legal system is purely transactional; contract disputes and property claims are settled based on who can pay the highest Watt tariff to the court registry.
*   **Marriages:** Registered as "Domestic Utility Contracts." Partners must prove their combined income to receive a shared grid-allowance bonus. Unregistered partnerships are denied power hookups.
*   **Property:** The HUB owns all land, buildings, and infrastructure in New Halden. Citizens lease "Light Rights" to live in specific rooms or wards.

### LAW — HUB Governance & Public Order
*   The HUB governs through utility control. They regulate water, coal rations, hydroponic food distribution, and sanitation. The **Grid Wardens** act as tax inspectors and municipal police, conducting surprise raids to check for unregistered batteries or illegal wiring. They control public information through the *Daily Filament*, the city's state-approved newsletter.

### LAW — Common Crimes & Punishments
*   **Grid-Theft:** Splicing into HUB cables to steal electricity. Punishment: Public shunning, permanent cut of grid-link to the offender's household, and three years of forced labor in the coal mines.
*   **Light-Hoarding:** Possession of unregistered batteries, generators, or Relics. Punishment: Confiscation of all assets and exile to the Dead Sough.
*   **Vocal Breach:** Making loud noises or speaking in Ward 9 or during active curfew events. Punishment: Immediate execution by the Night Watch to prevent anomaly attraction.
*   **Slick-Running:** Smuggling coal, salt, or warded wax without paying HUB tariffs. Punishment: Stripping of Watt privileges and transfer to the hazardous maintenance teams at Pump Station 4.
*   **Heliocentric Association:** Possession of blueprints for artificial suns or outlaw fusion reactors. Punishment: Public execution by hanging from a High Grid transformer pole.

### LAW — Civil Rights & Liberties
*   **Rights:** Civil rights do not exist. Under the **Emergency Survival Decree of 1995**, all civil rights are suspended in the interest of municipal preservation. Residents have no right to free speech, assembly, or privacy. The only recognized legal right is the **Light Guarantee**—citizens who pay their monthly Watt tax are legally entitled to 4 hours of low-intensity electrical grid connection per day.

## 14. CLIMATE & WEATHER

### CLIMATE — Atmospheric Conditions
*   **Temperature:** Temperatures are cold, ranging from -15°C (5°F) in the Sump to -40°C (-40°F) in the Dead Sough. Without solar heating, the earth's crust is slowly freezing.
*   **Precipitation:** There is no rain. The sky drops a constant, fine sleet mixed with black soot, known as **Soot-Sleet**. It coats everything in a greasy, dark ice that is highly corrosive to iron pipes.

### CLIMATE — The Blood Moon Cycle
*   **Frequency:** The Blood Moon occurs once every 45 days.
*   **Duration:** The sky clears of static, and the rust-red moon remains visible for exactly 12 hours.
*   **Effects:** Anomaly activity spikes by 300%. Warded Wax becomes less effective. The Night Watch initiates a city-wide curfew, shutting down all non-essential grid lines to avoid attracting Rank III and Rank IV anomalies.

### CLIMATE — Anomalous Weather Patterns
*   **Static Storms:** High-voltage clouds that roll over the city, discharging blue lightning that can trigger dormant anomalies and overload the High Grid.
*   **The Grey Fog:** A thick, ash-heavy fog that rises from the Sump, reducing visibility to zero and dampening sound. Lower-rank anomalies (Rank I and II) crawl inside it.

### CLIMATE — Weather-Anomaly Interactions
*   Extreme cold slows down Rank I physical anomalies, but Static Storms supercharge electrical and cognitive anomalies (like Voltage Feeders and Static-Claws), making them highly aggressive.

## 15. PRE-ECLIPSE HISTORY (BRIEF)

### HISTORY — The Pre-Eclipse Era
Before the Eclipse, the world was in the final decade of the 20th century. The global economy was growing, cities were lit by electricity and sunlight, and agriculture fed billions. The seasons were regular, oceans were unfrozen, and the concept of an "anomaly" belonged only to science fiction. Technology was transitioning into the digital age, with early personal computers and dial-up internet.

### HISTORY — The Aftermath (1994-1995)
When the sun failed to rise on October 12, 1994, global panic erupted within hours. Governments declared martial law, believing it was a nuclear strike or solar eclipse. Within a week, the global temperature plummeted, crops froze in the fields, and the first anomalies emerged from the new, permanent shadows. Within a month, the global transportation and food supply chains collapsed, leading to mass starvation. By the end of the first year, 90% of the human population had died from cold, starvation, or anomaly attacks.

### HISTORY — Memory vs. Recorded Text
Today, the generation born after 1994 has never seen a blue sky, green grass, or a warm sun. They know of these things only from old textbooks preserved by the Solar Archivists or the stories told by the elderly. To the youth of New Halden, the "Bright" is a mythological fantasy, and the idea of a sky that is blue and warm is as alien as the anomalies that hunt them in the dark.
