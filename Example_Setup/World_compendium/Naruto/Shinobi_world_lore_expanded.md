# The Shinobi World

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW
### OVERVIEW — The Shinobi World
The shinobi world consists of five major nations, each anchored by a hidden village (Gakure), surrounded by smaller unaligned countries and ungoverned wilderness. Power is organized around hidden villages as military contractors to their host daimyo. The world has survived multiple Shinobi World Wars that reshaped borders, bloodlines, and alliances. Between wars, tensions are managed through fragile bilateral treaties and the Five Kage Summit.
The world is pre-industrial but chakra-advanced: no firearms, but explosive tags, barrier jutsu, and summoning contracts fill equivalent military niches. Communication is by hawk messenger, runner, or chakra-relay seal. Travel is on foot, horse, or summon. Experienced shinobi cover enormous ground via body-flicker and speed enhancement.
**Tone:** GRITTY, POLITICAL, ACTION, MYTHIC.

**Political Structure:** Each great nation is governed by a daimyo (feudal lord) who holds supreme civilian authority and funds the hidden village through taxes and mission contracts. The Kage holds supreme military authority. In practice, the two power centers frequently conflict. Daimyo are largely non-combatants surrounded by samurai honor guards; they rely on the hidden village for external defense but can defund, replace, or politically humiliate the Kage. This tension is a persistent source of corruption and dangerous compromise.

**Minor Nations:** Between and around the five great nations lie dozens of smaller countries — Land of Waves, Land of Rice Fields (Oto), Land of Iron, Land of Snow/Spring, Land of Tea, Land of Grass, Land of Rain, Land of Hot Water, Land of Sound, and many more. These nations lack their own standing hidden villages and are economically and militarily dependent on the major powers. They are frequent sites for proxy conflicts, black-market operations, and missing-nin activity. Their daimyo are significantly weaker than those of the five great nations and often serve as political clients or pawns.

**Inter-Village Diplomacy:** Official relations between villages operate through treaty frameworks, joint mission agreements, trade routes, and the Five Kage Summit (a modern institution convened only for existential-level crises). Informal relations are managed through spy networks, rumors, and the bounty system. Villages frequently contract against each other through deniable third-party intermediaries. The Chunin Exams function as soft-power diplomacy: hosting them signals confidence, winning them earns prestige.

**The Bounty System:** The Five Great Nations maintain a coordinated bingo book system tracking missing-nin, dangerous criminals, and bounty targets. The Bounty Office processes kills via verified body parts (heads, specific body parts noted in contracts). S-rank bounties are internationally recognized. Bounty hunters are a distinct profession, distinct from shinobi, who operate in legal grey zones and serve clients across village lines. High bounties attract predatory missing-nin seeking to eliminate their competition.

## 2. FACTIONS
### FACTION — Konohagakure (Village Hidden in the Leaves)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** The Hokage (village leader), Village Elder Council (Homura, Koharu), Shinobi Council (clan heads), ANBU Black Ops
**Stance:** Pro-stability; dominant diplomatic soft power; the template for the hidden village system.
Founded by Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha at the end of the clan wars. The most populous major hidden village. Culture emphasizes the Will of Fire: protecting the village as family, passing strength and love to the next generation. In practice this philosophy also serves as political glue binding historically rival clans.
Konoha's internal politics are organized around the clan system. The major clans — Uchiha, Hyuga, Nara, Akimichi, Yamanaka, Aburame, Inuzuka, Sarutobi, Senju — each maintain their own traditions, bloodline techniques, and political relationships with the Hokage's administration. The civilian and shinobi councils nominally advise the Hokage but have historically been willing to undermine Hokage authority. The village's cultural identity is unusually cohesive compared to other villages, but this cohesion required the erasure of inconvenient truths (the Uchiha Massacre cover-up, the Root program, Danzo's covert operations).
**Key Clan Alliances:** The Ino-Shika-Cho formation (Yamanaka, Nara, Akimichi) is Konoha's most reliable multi-generation tactical unit. The bond between the three families is sealed with pills, training, and arranged friendship from childhood. The Inuzuka-Aburame pairing frequently handles tracking and infiltration. The Hyuga Clan, Konoha's most politically complex, maintains an internal caste division (Main House / Branch House) that generates constant internal friction.

### FACTION — ANBU Black Ops (Konoha)
**Type:** Covert Military Arm
**Key Members:** Masked operatives (identities classified), historically Kakashi Hatake, Itachi Uchiha, Yamato
**Stance:** Answerable directly to the Hokage; elite Jonin-floor or above.
Handles assassinations, extractions, counter-espionage, and matters too sensitive for standard mission ranks. Service is anonymous. Family and friends are not officially informed. ANBU deaths are not recorded in public records.

### FACTION — ROOT / Foundation (Konoha)
**Type:** Shadow Black Ops / Intelligence Cult
**Key Members:** Danzo Shimura (leader), Sai (operative)
**Stance:** Hostile to transparency; operates without Hokage oversight.
Danzo Shimura's parallel black ops organization. Officially disbanded multiple times; functionally persistent. Operatives are conditioned from childhood to suppress emotion and individuality. Root agents appear as regular Konoha shinobi until activated. A source of deep political corruption and shadow wars. Active primarily from Tobirama era through late Shippuden; officially dissolved post-Pain Arc.

### FACTION — Kumogakure (Village Hidden in the Clouds)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** The Raikage (A), Killer B, Darui
**Stance:** Militaristic; direct; prizes proven combat achievement.
Built high in mountain peaks above the cloud line. The second most powerful village in most eras and the most militaristic in culture. Political power tracks military rank more closely than in other villages. Troubled history of kidnapping Kekkei Genkai bloodlines from rival villages — the Hyuga Incident is the most politically sensitive example. Maintains the Two-Tails and Eight-Tails Jinchuriki.
Kumogakure's culture prizes directness, physical achievement, and personal combat record above heritage or clan name. This creates a more meritocratic promotion structure than other villages. Lightning Release is the dominant village chakra nature; chakra-metal forging is a nationally controlled craft tradition (Lightning Forge Quarters are a key strategic asset). The Raikage's administration is unusually non-bureaucratic — the A rules through personal authority and combat prestige. Diplomacy with Kumo requires demonstrating strength before cooperation is possible.

### FACTION — Iwagakure (Village Hidden in the Stone)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Onoki (Third Tsuchikage), Kurotsuchi
**Stance:** Conservative; holds longest institutional grudges, especially against Konoha.
Built into a massive rocky plateau with natural fortress properties. Values endurance, tradition, and self-sufficiency. The Yellow Flash (Minato) killing an entire Iwa strike force singlehandedly during the Third War is a wound that never healed — his name is a curse word in Iwagakure. Onoki wields Dust Release (Particle Style), the most destructive Kekkei Tota in existence.
Iwagakure's military doctrine emphasizes long-duration siege tactics, terrain control, and Explosion Corps shock assaults. Earth and Earth-adjacent Kekkei Genkai are unusually common here (Lava Release, Explosion Release, Dust Release). The Tsuchikage's political authority is reinforced by sheer institutional age — Iwa has the most unbroken line of administrative tradition of any great village. Grudges are maintained systematically across generations and recorded in official village memory. Iwa has never forgiven Konoha for the Third War and never will.

### FACTION — Kirigakure (Village Hidden in the Mist)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Mei Terumi (Fifth Mizukage), Seven Swordsmen of the Mist
**Stance:** Reforming; transitioning from isolationist brutality to diplomacy.
Spread across a mist-covered island archipelago. The most turbulent internal history of the five great villages. The "Bloody Mist" era under the Fourth Mizukage Yagura involved death-match graduation exams and persecution of Kekkei Genkai bloodline users — Kekkei Genkai clans were hunted nearly to extinction. Under Mei Terumi, genuine reform is underway but incomplete and internally contested.
Yagura's extreme policies were later revealed to have been caused by Obito's Genjutsu control — making the Bloody Mist era a foreign-manipulated catastrophe, not a purely internal failure. This revelation does not erase the wounds but complicates the political narrative of blame. Kiri's current culture is split: reformists under Mei pushing toward integration, traditionalists who see Kekkei Genkai tolerance as weakness. The Seven Swordsmen of the Mist remain the village's most iconic military brand — whoever holds the seven legendary blades is recognized as Kiri's deadliest shinobi. Sword possession is status. Sword theft is war.

### FACTION — Sunagakure (Village Hidden in the Sand)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Gaara (Fifth Kazekage), Temari, Kankuro, Chiyo (elder)
**Stance:** Treaty ally of Konoha; economically dependent and resentful of it.
Built within a sheltered desert valley. Economically stressed: the Land of Wind Daimyo has repeatedly cut village funding and outsourced missions to Konoha. This pressure drove the alliance with Orochimaru and the weaponization of Gaara. Cultural identity emphasizes endurance, self-reliance, and puppet mastery. The Puppet Corps is Suna's most distinctive military arm.
Suna's puppet tradition is generationally deep — the craft of building, maintaining, and fighting with chakra-threaded puppets is a dedicated artisan-warrior tradition passed within families. Chiyo and her brother Ebizo represent the old guard. Kankuro represents the modern practitioner. The Puppet Workshop Quarters is both a military asset and cultural institution. Suna also maintains the Iron Sand Reserve, a secured deposit of the Third Kazekage's magnetic chakra residue — a strategic treasure and political sensitive site. Post-Gaara's rise to Kazekage, Suna's relationship with Konoha has normalized, but deep economic resentment is not erased by alliance treaties.

### FACTION — The Akatsuki
**Type:** Missing-Nin Organization / Ideological Network
**Key Members:** Pain/Nagato (leader), Konan, Itachi Uchiha, Kisame Hoshigaki, Deidara, Sasori, Obito Uchiha (true mastermind)
**Stance:** Hostile to all hidden villages; pursues Jinchuriki capture.
The most significant missing-nin organization. At peak consists of ten S-rank missing-nin wearing black robes with red clouds. Operates as S-rank pairs taking contracts and pursuing Bijuu capture in parallel. Goals shift across the timeline: originally mercenary, then Nagato's peace-through-suffering ideology, then Obito/Madara's Eye of the Moon Plan.
**Akatsuki Pair Assignments:** Itachi + Kisame (intelligence and power); Deidara + Sasori (then Deidara + Tobi); Hidan + Kakuzu (immortality + bounty); Zetsu (solo scout); Pain (leader, above pairs). Each pair is internally balanced between complementary skill sets — often brains/brawn, range/close, or offense/utility.
**Operational Security:** Members communicate via shared astral projection technique (a jutsu-constructed hologram meeting). Physical meetings are rare. Members do not always know each other's real identities. Rings worn on specific fingers designate rank within the organization; the ring is the proof of membership. Loss of a ring is death or expulsion.
**Financing:** Pre-war Akatsuki funded itself through high-value contract work — assassinations, extractions, and mercenary warfare for regional conflicts. Kakuzu's bounty hunting contributed significant cash. The organization was self-sustaining as a criminal enterprise while simultaneously pursuing the Bijuu capture agenda.

### FACTION — Otogakure (Village Hidden in the Sound)
**Type:** Puppet State / Research Network
**Key Members:** Orochimaru (founder), Kabuto Yakushi
**Stance:** Hostile; Orochimaru's personal instrument.
Not a true hidden village. A network of hidden bases and research facilities in the Land of Rice Fields. Exists to provide Orochimaru cover for forbidden research, body experimentation, and loyalty-building via Curse Marks. After Orochimaru's decline it fragments into remnant factions.

### FACTION — Land of Iron Samurai
**Type:** Neutral Military State
**Key Members:** Mifune (leader, Kage-tier)
**Stance:** Strictly neutral in shinobi politics.
A permanently snow-covered nation governed entirely by samurai. Used as neutral ground for diplomatic summits. Samurai achieve combat capability through trained chakra flow into blades (Samurai Sabre Technique). Self-funded through resource extraction and mountain pass tolls. Does not extradite to shinobi villages. Missing-nin who flee here enter a legal grey zone. Mifune's personal authority is derived from impeccable impartiality — he has never accepted political bribes from any village.

### FACTION — The Legendary Sannin
**Type:** Legendary Trio / Historical Designation
**Key Members:** Jiraiya, Tsunade Senju, Orochimaru
**Stance:** Long since separated; each went a different direction after the wars.
Three students of the Third Hokage Hiruzen Sarutobi who survived a confrontation with Hanzo of the Great Salamander during the Second Shinobi World War and were named "Sannin" by Hanzo himself as the only worthy survivors. Represent three divergent responses to the trauma of war: Jiraiya chose wandering hope, Tsunade chose withdrawal and denial, Orochimaru chose cold transcendence of human limitation. Together they were arguably the strongest trio in any one generation. Their dissolution was both personal and political. They never fought as a unit after the wars.

### FACTION — The Seven Swordsmen of the Mist
**Type:** Elite Sword Corps / Cultural Institution
**Key Members:** Zabuza Momochi (Executioner's Blade), Kisame Hoshigaki (Samehada), Mangetsu Hozuki, Fuguki Suikazan, Raiga Kurosuki (twin Kiba blades); historically variable membership
**Stance:** Institutionally loyal to Kirigakure; individual loyalty varies dramatically.
Seven elite Kiri shinobi who each wield one of the seven legendary blades of the Mist. Membership is not hereditary — it is claimed through combat. If you kill a Swordsman and take the blade, you may become the next. The blades themselves are legendary artifacts with unique properties: Samehada absorbs chakra and bonds to its user; Kubikiribōchō (Executioner's Blade) repairs itself with the iron in enemy blood; Hiramekarei stores chakra and releases it as solid energy constructs; Nuibari (Sewing Needle) threads victims together with wire; Kiba (paired blades) channels lightning to cut through anything; Shibuki (Blastsword) contains explosive tags along its length; Kabutowari (Bluntsword) breaks any defense. Swordsmen are Kiri's most feared military export.

### FACTION — Taka (Snake Team)
**Type:** Sasuke's Personal Squad
**Key Members:** Sasuke Uchiha (leader), Suigetsu Hozuki, Karin, Jugo
**Stance:** Internally loyal to Sasuke; externally hostile to everyone else depending on arc.
Formed by Sasuke after killing Orochimaru. Each member retrieved from one of Orochimaru's bases. Suigetsu: a water-body shinobi who wants to collect the seven legendary blades and is Mangetsu Hozuki's younger brother. Karin: a Uzumaki-lineage sensor with healing bite abilities and obsessive loyalty to Sasuke. Jugo: a gentle giant with an uncontrollable berserker nature fueled by passive nature energy absorption — the original source of Orochimaru's Curse Mark research. The team's name shifts from "Hebi" (Snake) during the Itachi hunt to "Taka" (Hawk) when Sasuke pivots to destroying Konoha.

### FACTION — Allied Shinobi Forces
**Type:** Wartime Coalition
**Key Members:** Fourth Raikage A (Supreme Commander), Shikaku Nara (Chief Strategist), all Kage and their village forces
**Stance:** Temporary military union for the Fourth Shinobi World War; dissolved post-war.
The first full military unification of all five great villages in history. Formed to counter Obito's White Zetsu and Kabuto's Edo Tensei army. Organized into five divisions by combat specialization: First Division (Short-to-mid range; Darui, commander), Second Division (Close range; Kitsuchi), Third Division (Mid-range; Kakashi), Fourth Division (Long-range; Gaara, commander), Fifth Division (Mixed; Mifune). Medical Corps and Intelligence Division support from the rear. The logistical challenge of feeding, coordinating, and communicating across tens of thousands of shinobi from historically hostile nations was managed by Shikaku Nara until his death in Obito's initial Ten-Tails bombardment.

### FACTION — The Hyuga Clan
**Type:** Noble Clan / Internal Caste System
**Key Members:** Hiashi Hyuga (Main House head), Hinata Hyuga, Neji Hyuga (Branch House), Hanabi Hyuga
**Stance:** Internally divided between Main House authority and Branch House resentment.
Konoha's most politically powerful clan by heritage and bloodline prestige. The Byakugan — near-360-degree vision, chakra pathway sight, and precise internal strike capability — is the clan's defining asset. The clan enforces a brutal internal caste system: the Main House holds power and political rights; the Branch House serves the Main House and is sealed at childhood with the Caged Bird Seal, which can be activated to cause extreme pain or brain death on the wearer. The Branch House cannot defect, cannot rebel, and cannot refuse orders. The seal dissolves on the Main House member's death. Neji Hyuga's arc is the story of someone born into injustice who mistakes the cage for fate. The Hyuga political question (reform vs. tradition) is unresolved through the end of Shippuden.

### FACTION — The Nara Clan
**Type:** Noble Clan / Strategic Specialist
**Key Members:** Shikaku Nara (clan head, wartime chief strategist), Shikamaru Nara, Yoshino Nara
**Stance:** Integrated into Konoha's leadership structure; strategically indispensable.
The Nara, Akimichi, and Yamanaka clans form an inseparable political-tactical unit known as the Ino-Shika-Cho formation. Each generation of the three clans is expected to produce a functional trio. The Nara specialize in shadow manipulation jutsu requiring minimal chakra expenditure but precise environmental awareness. Nara clan members are among the highest-IQ individuals in the shinobi world by consistent generational average. The Nara Forest Preserve is clan-maintained, home to the deer whose antlers provide medicinal ingredients for the Akimichi clan's calorie pills. The clans' economic and medical interdependence reinforces their political alliance.

### FACTION — Otsutsuki Clan (Celestial)
**Type:** Divine Alien Clan / Existential Threat
**Key Members:** Kaguya Otsutsuki (progenitor of chakra), Hagoromo Otsutsuki (Sage of Six Paths), Hamura Otsutsuki
**Stance:** Beyond conventional faction politics; operates at the level of world mythology.
The Otsutsuki are the ultimate source of all chakra in the world. Kaguya consumed the chakra fruit of the God Tree, becoming the first chakra user and a god-like being of incalculable power. She is the Ten-Tails' original host. Hagoromo (her son, the Sage of Six Paths) sealed her and divided the Ten-Tails into the nine Bijuu. He spread chakra to humanity and created ninshuu — the predecessor to ninjutsu. Hamura sealed Kaguya's consciousness on the Moon. Black Zetsu is Kaguya's will made manifest, and it spent centuries manipulating Indra's descendants (the Uchiha) and Ashura's descendants (the Senju/Uzumaki) toward conflict, waiting for the moment to revive Kaguya. The Otsutsuki are not truly defeatable — only sealable.

## 3. LOCATIONS
### LOCATION — Konohagakure (Village Hidden in the Leaves)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Capital
**Status:** Flourishing (most eras); Destroyed and Rebuilt (Pain Arc)
The hidden village of the Land of Fire. Major landmarks include: the Hokage Tower (central political nerve), Konoha Hospital (medical facility and research arm), the Ninja Academy, the Shinobi Library, the Uchiha District (populated pre-Massacre, sealed afterward), Nara Forest Preserve, Training Grounds 1 through 44+, Ichiraku Ramen (Naruto's cultural home), and Hokage Rock (carved faces of all serving Hokage).
The civilian district is a functioning small city: markets, inns, civilian housing, entertainment districts. Shinobi and civilians co-exist but occupy separate social strata. Gate Duty is mandatory for low-rank shinobi and deeply boring — one of the most complained-about assignments. The mission assignment desk in the Hokage Tower processes all registered missions and is staffed by senior chunin. The Intelligence Division operates from a separate, non-public building adjacent to the Hokage Tower.
**Post-Pain Reconstruction:** After Nagato's Shinra Tensei leveled a significant portion of the village, a massive reconstruction effort — funded by emergency daimyo appropriations and inter-village solidarity — rebuilt Konoha largely in its original layout but with reinforced structures. The destruction and rebuilding physically marks the shift from the Tsunade era to the Kakashi era.

### LOCATION — Kumogakure (Village Hidden in the Clouds)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Mountain Fortress
**Status:** Flourishing
Built high in mountain peaks above the cloud line in the Land of Lightning. Key landmarks: Raikage's Office (summit command hub), Cloud Arena (high-altitude combat training; public duels are culturally significant), Lightning Forge Quarters (chakra-metal weapon production; Kumo's most valuable export), the Waterfall of Truth (hidden site for confronting one's shadow self), and B's Dome (Killer B's personal training and cultural hub).

### LOCATION — Iwagakure (Village Hidden in the Stone)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Plateau Fortress
**Status:** Flourishing
Built into and upon a massive rocky plateau in the Land of Earth. Key landmarks: Tsuchikage's Office atop the Rock Spire, Stone Quarry Districts (economic backbone), and Explosion Corps Training Grounds.

### LOCATION — Kirigakure (Village Hidden in the Mist)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Island Archipelago
**Status:** Reforming
Spread across mist-covered islands in the Land of Water. Permanently shrouded. Key landmarks: Mizukage's Building (central island, mist-shrouded), Hidden Blade Training Halls (sword traditions are cultural cornerstones), no official Bloody Mist Memorial (the dead are not officially commemorated), and Hozuki Castle/Blood Prison (remote maximum-security prison island with chakra-suppression technology).

### LOCATION — Sunagakure (Village Hidden in the Sand)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Desert Citadel
**Status:** Economically Stressed
Built within a sheltered desert valley in the Land of Wind. Key landmarks: Kazekage's Tower (central citadel), Puppet Workshop Quarters (artisan-shinobi build and maintain combat puppets), Desert Training Ranges (enormous flat expanses), and the Iron Sand Reserve (secured site storing the Third Kazekage's magnetic residue).

### LOCATION — The Land of Waves
**Type:** Coastal Region
**Status:** Recovering
A poor island-adjacent coastal region oppressed by the shipping magnate Gato before Team 7 intervenes. Accessible by the Great Naruto Bridge. Economically underdeveloped but emotionally significant as Team 7's first real high-stakes mission.

### LOCATION — Amegakure (Village Hidden in the Rain)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Theocratic State
**Status:** Contested
A mid-size hidden village in a perpetually rain-soaked buffer zone nation. Under Pain/Nagato and Konan's leadership, a closed theocratic state sealed from outsiders. Filled with war orphans and trauma. A place of immense suffering with genuine ideological complexity.

### LOCATION — Mount Myoboku
**Type:** Summoning Realm / Sage Training Ground
**Status:** Eternal
Home of the Great Toad Sage and the toad summoning clan. Accessible by reverse summoning. Offers Toad Sage training. Nature energy gathered standing still; slower and safer than snake mode. Jiraiya and Naruto's training ground.

### LOCATION — Ryuchi Cave
**Type:** Summoning Realm / Sage Training Ground
**Status:** Eternal
Home of the White Snake Sage and the snake summoning clan. Requires invitation or surviving the approach. Offers Snake Sage training: more aggressive nature energy, faster gathering, but transformation into a snake on failure is faster and more extreme. Orochimaru's original Sage source.

### LOCATION — Tanzaku Town
**Type:** Civilian City / Entertainment Hub
**Status:** Flourishing
A mid-size civilian city in Fire Country known for gambling halls, entertainment, and festivals. Tsunade's preferred hiding spot. A practical, seedy city with a functioning black market for medical goods and information.

### LOCATION — Shikkotsu Forest
**Type:** Summoning Realm / Sage Training Ground
**Status:** Eternal
Home of the Slug Sage and the slug summoning clan. Accessible by reverse summoning contract through Tsunade's or Sakura's blood seal. Offers Slug Sage training. Nature energy absorption through slugs is the gentlest of the three sage lineages — the most forgiving, but also the least raw-power-amplifying. Tsunade and Sakura's summoning ground. Katsuyu (the Great Slug) can divide into countless smaller slugs for distributed healing across an entire battlefield, making her uniquely suited to large-scale medical support.

### LOCATION — Valley of the End
**Type:** Landmark / Battlefield
**Status:** Symbolic
A massive waterfall and river gorge on the border between the Land of Fire and the Land of Sound/Rice. Site of the legendary battle between Hashirama and Madara (their enormous stone statues flank the falls). Later the site of Naruto vs. Sasuke — twice. Every confrontation here is identity-defining. The site's symbolic weight is intentional: battles here are not about winning, they are about who each combatant is at their core.

### LOCATION — Hozuki Castle (Blood Prison)
**Type:** Maximum Security Prison
**Status:** Active
A remote Kirigakure-administered prison island with comprehensive chakra-suppression technology built into the structure's seals. Holds shinobi deemed too dangerous to execute but too unstable to release. The suppression field prevents all chakra-based techniques — ninjutsu, genjutsu, even some taijutsu enhancements are neutralized. Guards are drawn from Kiri's most reliable jonin. Considered essentially escape-proof; Kirigakure charges significant fees to other villages for imprisonment contracts.

### LOCATION — Myoboku Waterfall — Temple of Gelel / Land of Snow
**Type:** Civilian Nation / Geopolitically Notable
**Status:** Converted to Land of Spring (post-Naruto: Ninja Clash in the Land of Snow)
A northern nation covered in perpetual snow due to a climate-control device. A source of Gelel Stone, an alternative energy source with combat application. Politically insular, ruled by a daimyo with isolationist policies. After Naruto's intervention, the climate-control technology is reversed and the nation begins the transition to the Land of Spring.

### LOCATION — Uzushiogakure (Village Hidden in the Whirlpool)
**Type:** Destroyed Hidden Village / Historical Site
**Status:** Ruins
The former hidden village of the Land of Whirlpools (Uzugakure), home of the Uzumaki clan — Naruto's maternal heritage. Destroyed in a coordinated attack by multiple nations who feared the Uzumaki's mastery of Fuinjutsu (sealing techniques). The ruins are largely abandoned. The Uzumaki clan scattered across the shinobi world. Remnants include: Kushina Uzumaki (Konoha), Nagato (Amegakure, by way of Jiraiya's influence), Karin (Orochimaru's forces). The spiral symbol on Konoha jonin vests is a tribute to the Uzumaki alliance — it predates the clan's destruction and was never removed.

### LOCATION — The Akatsuki Hideouts
**Type:** Distributed Hidden Infrastructure
**Status:** Variable (destroyed progressively through Shippuden)
Akatsuki operates from a network of concealed caves and underground bases scattered across multiple nations, primarily in ungoverned or minor-nation territory. Each base is sealed with layered barrier jutsu; unauthorized entry triggers alarms. The largest known base (used for Bijuu extraction rituals) contains the Gedo Mazo (Demonic Statue of the Outer Path) — the empty shell of the Ten-Tails used as a Bijuu storage vessel. Ritual extraction takes three full days and requires all available Akatsuki members to channel chakra in concert.

### LOCATION — Land of Iron / Samurai Country
**Type:** Neutral Mountain Nation
**Status:** Active
A permanently snow-covered mountainous nation governed entirely by samurai (no shinobi presence except as foreign visitors). Used as neutral ground for the Five Kage Summit. The nation's mountain passes are militarily significant — controlling them provides access between the eastern and northern nations. Mifune's personal authority derives from genuine impartiality; he has never taken political payments from any hidden village. The nation is self-sufficient and heavily fortified by terrain. Shinobi techniques are legal but socially unwelcome here.

### LOCATION — Forest of Death (Training Ground 44)
**Type:** Restricted Zone / Exam Terrain
**Status:** Active
Located within Konoha's outer training zones. Used as the second phase of the Chunin Exams. Enormous predatory fauna — giant snakes, giant spiders, and carnivorous plants exist within — thick canopy eliminating visibility, a central tower at the geographic center. Forty-four training grounds exist in and around Konoha; most are standard; Ground 44 is uniquely dangerous and sealed for non-exam access. Its interior is large enough that teams can travel for hours without seeing another group.

## 4. CHARACTERS
### CHARACTER — Naruto Uzumaki
**Aliases:** The Number One Hyperactive Knucklehead Ninja, Seventh Hokage, Child of Prophecy
**Appearance:** Bright blond hair, blue eyes, orange jumpsuit, whisker marks on cheeks from Kurama's influence, headband.
**Disposition:** Determined, loud, compassionate, stubborn, inspiring
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Konohagakure
**Goals:** To become Hokage and protect his precious people. To prove that bonds and understanding can overcome cycles of hatred.
**Axes:** Nature(8) Training(7) Emotion(9) Social(8) Belief(10) Ego(6) Affinity(70)
Nine-Tails Jinchuriki with endless stamina. Shadow Clone mastery, Rasengan variants, Sage Mode, and eventually Bijuu Cooperative Mode. Central protagonist. Rose from village pariah to the most powerful shinobi alive through sheer refusal to quit.

### CHARACTER — Sasuke Uchiha
**Aliases:** The Last Uchiha, The Avenger
**Appearance:** Dark hair, black eyes (Sharingan when active, later Rinnegan in left eye), lean build, high-collared shirts.
**Disposition:** Driven, brooding, intelligent, conflicted, ultimately loyal
**Status:** Alive
**Faction:** Konohagakure (defected to Sound, returned post-war)
**Goals:** To avenge his clan (early). To understand the truth of the shinobi system (mid). To protect the village from the shadows (late).
**Axes:** Nature(7) Training(9) Emotion(6) Social(3) Belief(7) Ego(8) Affinity(25)
Sharingan prodigy. Chidori, fire jutsu, Mangekyo Sharingan (Amaterasu, Susanoo), eventually Rinnegan. Driven by grief and revenge through most of the story, eventually atones and becomes a wandering protector.

### CHARACTER — Sakura Haruno
**Aliases:** The Slug Princess's Apprentice
**Appearance:** Pink hair, green eyes, broad forehead (Yin Seal diamond mark when active), red outfit.
**Disposition:** Determined, sharp-tongued, compassionate, fiercely loyal
**Status:** Alive

## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES
### POWER_SYSTEM — Chakra and Jutsu
Chakra is produced by combining physical energy (from the body's cells) and spiritual energy (from mind and experience). It is stored in chakra coils throughout the body and released through hand seals, will, and technique.
**Five Basic Chakra Natures:** Fire (Katon) countered by Water; Water (Suiton) countered by Earth; Wind (Futon) countered by Fire; Lightning (Raiton) countered by Earth; Earth (Doton) countered by Lightning.
**Advanced Natures (Kekkei Genkai):** Combine two elements (Ice = Water+Wind; Wood = Water+Earth; Lava = Earth+Fire). Cannot be learned, only inherited. Kekkei Tota combine three (Dust = Wind+Earth+Fire).
**Jutsu Categories:** Ninjutsu (chakra-based techniques), Taijutsu (physical combat enhanced by chakra), Genjutsu (illusions via disrupting target's chakra flow; broken by self-disruption or ally intervention), Fuinjutsu (sealing jutsu; persistent chakra inscriptions), Senjutsu (Sage Arts; draws natural energy; extremely powerful but requires stillness; excess turns user to stone), Medical Ninjutsu (surgical chakra application).
**Summoning Contracts:** Blood-signed agreements with animal clans. Notable: Toads (Myoboku) for Jiraiya/Naruto; Snakes (Ryuchi Cave) for Orochimaru/Sasuke; Slugs (Shikkotsu Forest) for Tsunade/Sakura; Dogs for Kakashi; Monkeys (Enma) for Hiruzen; Hawks for Sasuke (post-Part I); Panda and Salamander (Great Salamander for Hanzo); Weasels for Itachi (Crow contract, unique to Mangekyo). Reverse summoning sends the shinobi to the summoning realm; regular summoning calls the animal to the field. Larger summons require proportionally more chakra.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Sharingan and Mangekyo
The Sharingan is the Uchiha clan's Kekkei Genkai, awakened through strong emotion (typically grief or fear). Stages: 1 tomoe (basic), 2 tomoe (improved tracking), 3 tomoe (full precognitive tracking, genjutsu casting, chakra form reading). The Sharingan can copy jutsu (with limits — it cannot copy Kekkei Genkai or techniques requiring bloodline chakra).
**Mangekyo Sharingan:** Awakened through witnessing the death of someone important. Each Mangekyo is unique to the user's eye. Sustained use causes blindness (the cells of the Sharingan degrade). Can be halted by transplanting a close relative's Mangekyo — creating the Eternal Mangekyo Sharingan (pattern blends both, prevents blindness).
**Known Mangekyo Abilities:** Itachi — Tsukuyomi (72-hour genjutsu prison compressed into seconds; requires eye contact); Amaterasu (black flames that burn until the target is consumed; cannot be extinguished by normal means); Susanoo (incomplete ribcage → partial → complete armored giant; holds the Totsuka Blade and Yata Mirror). Sasuke — Amaterasu (from left eye after Itachi transfers it at death); Susanoo (evolves through the war into complete Perfect Susanoo). Obito — Kamui (right eye: sends target to pocket dimension / left eye: makes user intangible; the two eyes together form a complete ability). Kakashi — Kamui (left eye: long-range extraction into pocket dimension; more limited than Obito's version). Shisui — Kotoamatsukami (the most powerful genjutsu in existence: alters target's will invisibly without them knowing; recharges once per decade without Shisui's unique eye, faster with it).
**Rinnegan:** The highest form of eye evolution. Requires Senju and Uchiha chakra combined (Sage of Six Paths descent on both lines). Grants access to all five chakra natures simultaneously plus the Six Paths Techniques: Deva Path (gravity), Asura Path (mechanized body weapons), Human Path (soul extraction), Animal Path (unlimited summoning), Preta Path (chakra absorption), Naraka Path (restoration via King of Hell). Also grants access to Outer Path techniques: Samsara of Heavenly Life (resurrection at cost of life force); Samsara Eye (black receivers deployment).

### POWER_SYSTEM — Kekkei Genkai Reference
A bloodline limit. Cannot be learned — must be inherited. Combining two nature affinities creates an advanced nature only the Kekkei Genkai user can access.
**Known Kekkei Genkai:** Ice Release (Water+Wind — Haku); Wood Release (Water+Earth — Hashirama; replicated partially via Hashirama cell implants); Lava Release (Earth+Fire — Mei Terumi, Roshi, Son Goku); Storm Release (Water+Lightning — Darui); Crystal Release (rare; Guren); Magnet Release (Earth+Wind variant — Third Kazekage, Rasa, Gaara indirectly); Boil Release (Fire+Water — Mei Terumi, second release); Scorch Release (Fire+Wind — Pakura); Explosion Release (Earth+Lightning — Deidara, Gari); Swift Release (unknown combination — Han); Steel Release (unknown — Sai's predecessor); Byakugan (Hyuga/Otsutsuki — 360-degree vision, chakra pathway sight); Sharingan/Rinnegan/Tenseigan (Uchiha/Otsutsuki progression).
**Kekkei Tota** (three-nature combination): Dust Release (Wind+Earth+Fire — Onoki) — reduces anything within its three-dimensional shape to sub-atomic dust. The most destructive technique in existence that isn't mythological.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Fuinjutsu (Sealing Arts)
Sealing techniques use chakra inscribed into surfaces, objects, or living bodies to create persistent effects — containment, storage, activation triggers, and complex conditional systems. The Uzumaki clan were the world's supreme sealing masters; their destruction was partly motivated by other nations fearing their sealing capabilities.
**Notable Seals:** Dead Demon Consuming Seal (Minato/Hiruzen — summons the Death God to extract and hold souls within it at the cost of the user's own soul); Eight Trigrams Sealing Style (Minato's masterwork — seals Kurama within Naruto with a spiral that allows controlled chakra leakage as Naruto ages, unlike most containment seals); Caged Bird Seal (Hyuga Main House — applied to Branch House members, can cause pain or death on activation); Reaper Death Seal (a variant of the Dead Demon seal); Contract Seal (breaks summoning contracts); Five Elements Seal / Unseal (used to disrupt or restore existing seals — Orochimaru uses these to destabilize Naruto's seal during the Chunin Exams).

### POWER_SYSTEM — Medical Ninjutsu
A specialized chakra application requiring exceptional fine-motor chakra control. Medical shinobi are strategically irreplaceable — a team with a skilled medic has dramatically improved long-term battlefield endurance.
**Core Techniques:** Mystical Palm Technique (accelerates cellular regeneration in the target), Chakra Scalpel (forms sharp chakra edges for precise cutting — surgical or combat use), Chakra Transfer (moves chakra from user to target), Poison Extraction, Medical Diagnosis Scan. **Tsunade's Rules for Medical Shinobi:** A medical shinobi never enters direct combat if avoidable; a medical shinobi is always the last to die on a team; a medical shinobi does not use Mystical Palm on themselves in combat (Tsunade broke this rule by creating the Yin Seal as a workaround — storing chakra over years for a single massive release of cellular regeneration).
**Sakura's Yin Seal (100 Healings):** A massive chakra reserve stored in the forehead mark. When released, provides near-continuous cellular regeneration. Requires years of seal-building practice — cannot be rushed. Tsunade trained Sakura specifically for this; it represents a full teacher-student transmission.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Edo Tensei (Reanimation Jutsu)
The most forbidden of all forbidden jutsu, created by the Second Hokage Tobirama Senju. Sacrifices a living body to anchor a dead shinobi's soul back into existence within a reanimated corpse. The reanimated shinobi possesses all abilities from life, near-unlimited chakra, automatic cellular regeneration (damage heals), and cannot be permanently killed while the jutsu is active (only sealed or released by the caster).
**Weaknesses:** The reanimated retain enough personality to resist commands (though this varies by individual willpower); they can be sealed into scrolls; the caster can release the jutsu to return the souls to the afterlife; and the Impure World Reincarnation can be broken if the reanimated individual is given full autonomy.
**Kabuto's Version:** Far more controlled and powerful than previous uses. Kabuto modified Orochimaru's version to suppress the reanimated's autonomy more completely and to accelerate regeneration. He deployed over 100 legendary shinobi during the Fourth War — the largest Edo Tensei army in history.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Sage Mode
Natural energy exists throughout the environment. A practitioner who learns to absorb it and mix it with their physical and spiritual chakra produces Senjutsu chakra — more powerful, more perceptive, and capable of affecting non-corporeal targets that regular chakra cannot.
**Risks:** Too much natural energy causes the practitioner to petrify and become part of nature (turned to stone). Gathering natural energy requires complete stillness — impractical in active combat without clones maintaining the gathering.
**Three Sage Lineages (known):** Toad (Mount Myoboku) — slower absorption, requires stillness; Naruto's solution is shadow clones gathering while he fights, then absorbing completed sage chakra via touch. Snake (Ryuchi Cave) — faster, more aggressive absorption; Kabuto's version hybridizes snake cells with his body. Slug (Shikkotsu Forest) — gentlest, most medical, best for distributed healing.
**Visual Markers:** Toad Sage Mode — orange pigment around the eyes (frog-eye markings), pupils become horizontal ellipses. Snake Sage Mode (Kabuto) — snake scales, visible snake hybrid features. Perfect Sage Mode (Naruto) — maintains the markings while retaining full human mobility.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Jinchuriki Cloak Stages (Expanded)
**Stage 0:** No cloak, standard combat. Jinchuriki draw passively on ambient Bijuu chakra for enhanced stamina and healing.
**Stage 1:** Red chakra cloak, 1-4 tails. Enhanced speed and strength. Corrosive to non-Jinchuriki on prolonged contact. Healing factor significantly elevated.
**Stage 2 (V2):** Dense chakra cloak with visible tails, full beast silhouette. Near-Bijuu power. Host loses coherent thought. Bones may rupture through the skin to form temporary armor.
**Stage 3:** Full Bijuu Transformation. The beast acts; the host is effectively suppressed within. Full Bijuu combat power.
**Stage 4 — Cooperative Mode:** The rarest and most powerful. Requires genuine partnership between host and Bijuu. Host maintains complete mental clarity while accessing the Bijuu's full chakra. Visual: Bijuu chakra cloak in controlled form, distinctive to each pairing. Killer B and Gyuki. Naruto and Kurama (achieved during the war). Neither loses themselves; both gain more.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Power Rank Scale
**Ranks (ascending):** Civilian, Genin, Chunin, Tokubetsu Jonin, Jonin, Kage, Beyond Kage.
A 2+ rank gap is effectively decisive in direct combat without significant advantage. Exceptions: terrain control, elemental hard-counter, genjutsu initiation, preparation and intel, superior numbers, and prepared sealing traps.
**Kage-level indicators:** Can survive sustained combat from multiple Jonin; deploy area-of-effect attacks large enough to destroy a district; maintain offense while defending; possess a jutsu that forces opponents to solve a specific problem.
**Beyond Kage:** Redefine the terms of engagement entirely. Naruto in Sage Mode senses intent before movement. Sasuke with Rinnegan swaps places with any object. Hashirama's Wood Release subdues Bijuu. These are narrative forces, not mission targets.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Bijuu and Jinchuriki
The nine Tailed Beasts (Bijuu) are living masses of chakra originating from the Ten-Tails, separated by the Sage of Six Paths. Each is ancient, vast, and sapient. They cannot be permanently destroyed.
**Jinchuriki** are humans with a Bijuu sealed within them. They suffer emotional isolation, social rejection, and physical instability. Those who achieve Bijuu cooperation access extraordinary power.
**Cloak Stages:** (1) Partial cloak: chakra shroud, enhanced senses. (2) Full cloak with tails: massive power, suppressed thought. (3) Version 2: near-Bijuu form, corrosive, loss of autonomy. (4) Full Bijuu transformation: the beast acts, host suppressed. (5) Cooperative Bijuu Mode: full power without loss of self; rarest and strongest.
**The Nine Bijuu:** Shukaku (1-Tail, Suna/Gaara), Matatabi (2-Tails, Kumo/Yugito), Isobu (3-Tails, Kiri/Yagura), Son Goku (4-Tails, Iwa/Roshi), Kokuo (5-Tails, Iwa/Han), Saiken (6-Tails, Kiri/Utakata), Chomei (7-Tails, Takigakure/Fu), Gyuki (8-Tails, Kumo/Killer B), Kurama (9-Tails, Konoha/Naruto).

**Individual Bijuu Notes:**
- **Shukaku (Ichibi):** Sand manipulation and wind release. Psychotic in personality, sleep-depriving to its host (Gaara's insomnia). Hates Kurama. After being freed from Gaara, bonds with Naruto during the war.
- **Matatabi (Nibi):** A nekomata (two-tailed cat) of blue fire. Relatively calm and sapient compared to Shukaku. Yugito Nii (Kumo) was a cooperative Jinchuriki.
- **Isobu (Sanbi):** A giant turtle-crab with a spiked shell. Shy and defensive. Yagura (Kiri's Fourth Mizukage) was its Jinchuriki while under Obito's control.
- **Son Goku (Yonbi):** A massive stone ape with lava release. Proud and hostile to humans. Roshi (Iwa) achieved partial cooperation.
- **Kokuo (Gobi):** A horse-dolphin hybrid with boil release. Dignified in personality. Han (Iwa) was known for steam-based fighting style.
- **Saiken (Rokubi):** A large slug with alkali/soap-bubble release. Utakata (Kiri) was an unofficial fugitive Jinchuriki.
- **Chomei (Nanabi):** A massive rhinoceros beetle. The most cheerful and optimistic of the Bijuu. Fu (Takigakure) was its Jinchuriki.
- **Gyuki (Hachibi):** Giant octopus-bull hybrid. Sharp, intelligent, and possessing a sense of humor. The most successful long-standing cooperative Bijuu partnership (with Killer B) before Naruto and Kurama.
- **Kurama (Kyubi):** The most powerful Bijuu by raw chakra. Originally split: Yang Kurama sealed in Naruto; Yin Kurama sealed in the Death God (Minato's body). Initially hateful toward humans, Kurama's hostility toward Naruto is cracked during the fight with Pain and broken entirely during the war. Post-war, Kurama remains with Naruto by choice.

## 6. ECONOMY
### ECONOMY — Ryo and the Mission System
**Currency:** Ryo (R). No subdivisions in common use.
**Mission Ranks and Payout:** D-Rank (5-50 R, civilian-safe), C-Rank (80-200 R, possible enemy contact), B-Rank (250-750 R, near-certain combat), A-Rank (750-3,000 R, Jonin-level threats), S-Rank (3,000 R+ or non-monetary, Kage-level danger).
**Risk Premiums:** +25% urgent; +25% secrecy; +50% beyond patrol range; +50% organized enemy; +100%+ for Kage-tier or political blowback. S-rank missions often pay in intel, pardons, political access, or rare scrolls.
**Cost Anchors:** Ramen bowl 3-8 R; inn room 5-15 R/night; kunai set 30-80 R; explosive tags x10 150-300 R; flak jacket 2,000-5,000 R; village apartment 5,000-10,000 R; hospital treatment 200-1,000 R.
**Income Anchors:** Civilian laborer 300-600 R/year; active Genin 500-1,500 R/year; Chunin 2,000-6,000 R/year; Jonin 8,000-20,000 R/year; ANBU 12,000-30,000 R/year (classified).
**Price Modifiers:** Remote village x0.7-1.0; hidden village civilian district x1.2-1.6; capital/port x1.6-2.5; active war zone x1.5-3.0 (scarcity + extortion).

**Black Markets:** Every significant civilian city and every hidden village has a functioning grey/black market. Common black market goods: stolen jutsu scrolls, contraband medical supplies (poisons, experimental pharmaceuticals), Kekkei Genkai-related items, intelligence on rival villages, missing-nin services, and forged travel papers. The black market for rare scrolls is particularly active — forbidden jutsu scrolls from all five villages circulate among collectors and researchers. Orochimaru was a major buyer before establishing his own research network.

**The Hidden Village as Economic Entity:** Hidden villages are simultaneously military organizations, government contractors, and small city-states. They maintain their own taxation, property ownership, and service economies. Civilian districts pay taxes to the village which flow to the daimyo. The Hokage administration sets mission rates, manages village property, and controls strategic resource distribution (chakra paper, weapon-grade steel, medical herbs). Villages with Kekkei Genkai populations have strategic economic advantages — Wood Release users can accelerate agriculture; Lava Release users can reshape terrain; sensor-type shinobi enable intelligence gathering that replaces entire espionage departments.

## 7. EVENTS
### EVENT — The Founding of the Hidden Villages
Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha ended the clan wars by founding Konohagakure, the first hidden village. This became the template: the five great nations each established their own hidden village, creating the modern shinobi system. Hashirama distributed the Bijuu to other villages as diplomatic gestures. Madara eventually defected, leading to their legendary battle at the Valley of the End.

### EVENT — The Uchiha Massacre
Itachi Uchiha killed his entire clan in a single night under covert orders from Danzo (with Hiruzen's reluctant approval) to prevent an Uchiha-led coup that would have sparked civil war. The village covered it up. Sasuke was spared and driven toward revenge. Itachi joined the Akatsuki as a mole. The truth of this event is one of the most politically explosive secrets in the shinobi world.

### EVENT — The Nine-Tails Attack on Konoha
Obito Uchiha (masked) extracted Kurama from Kushina Uzumaki and unleashed the Nine-Tails on Konohagakure. The Fourth Hokage Minato Namikaze and Kushina died sealing Kurama into their newborn son Naruto. Hiruzen Sarutobi resumed Hokage duties. The attack anniversary is observed differently by everyone: a memorial for some, a day of fear for others, and for Naruto, a complicated absence.

### EVENT — Pain's Destruction of Konoha
Nagato (controlling the Six Paths of Pain) obliterated Konohagakure with a single Shinra Tensei to demonstrate the reality of suffering. Naruto returned, defeated Pain, and confronted Nagato directly. Through dialogue, Nagato chose to revive the dead of Konoha at the cost of his own life. This event fundamentally changed Naruto's standing from village pariah to hero.

### EVENT — The Fourth Shinobi World War
The Allied Shinobi Forces (all five great villages united for the first time) fought against Obito's army of White Zetsu and reanimated legendary shinobi. Escalated through Obito becoming the Ten-Tails Jinchuriki, Madara's revival, and Kaguya Otsutsuki's manifestation. Ended with Naruto and Sasuke's final battle at the Valley of the End. Peace achieved; Naruto eventually becomes Seventh Hokage.

### EVENT — The Chunin Exams (Konoha)
A multi-village tournament to evaluate Genin for Chunin promotion. Also functions as a geopolitical soft-power demonstration. The Konoha exams Naruto participates in are invaded by Suna and Sound forces (Orochimaru's plan), killing the Third Hokage and fundamentally changing inter-village relations.
Structure: Phase 1 (Written Exam — tests information gathering and team coordination under pressure; the test is not meant to be solved, it is meant to reveal how teams handle unsolvable pressure). Phase 2 (Forest of Death — teams retrieve scrolls and survive; 5 days). Phase 3 (Tournament — one-on-one matches, audience, daimyo and Kage present as soft-power audience).

### EVENT — The Kannabi Bridge Mission (Third War)
Team Minato (Obito Uchiha, Kakashi Hatake, Rin Nohara, led by Minato Namikaze) is dispatched to destroy the Kannabi Bridge to cut Iwa supply lines. Obito is crushed by a falling boulder rescuing Kakashi — gives Kakashi his left eye (Sharingan) before losing consciousness. Madara Uchiha finds and preserves Obito in secret. This mission is the originating trauma of the entire Shippuden plot — without Obito's "death," there is no Akatsuki, no masked manipulator, no Fourth War.

### EVENT — The Hyuga Incident
A Kumogakure ambassador secretly attempts to kidnap three-year-old Hinata Hyuga to steal the Byakugan. Hiashi Hyuga kills the ambassador. Kumo demands reparations — framing the assassination as a violation of their diplomat. The village cannot afford open war. Hizashi Hyuga, Hiashi's Branch House twin and Neji's father, volunteers to die in Hiashi's place. This sacrifice is the wound that defines Neji's entire early arc — his fatalism, his hatred, his belief that destiny is inescapable.

### EVENT — The Nine-Tails Attack on Konoha (Year 0)
Obito extracts Kurama from Kushina during her vulnerable childbirth moment. Minato defeats Obito in direct combat; uses the Dead Demon Consuming Seal to split and seal Kurama into Naruto. Both Minato and Kushina die sealing the Fox. Hiruzen resumes the Hokage mantle. The village associates the Nine-Tails with Naruto for the next twelve years. The anniversary (October 10) is a village-wide trauma event.

### EVENT — The Shisui Uchiha Incident (Year 8)
Shisui Uchiha — the Uchiha clan's most powerful member and genuine peace advocate — attempts to use Kotoamatsukami to subtly alter the Hokage administration's hostility toward the Uchiha clan. Danzo steals one of Shisui's eyes, driving Shisui to suicide. Shisui entrusts his remaining eye to Itachi with a final request: protect the village and the Uchiha's name. The Massacre follows shortly after as the only remaining option. Shisui's death removes the last non-violent resolution from the board.

### EVENT — The Uchiha Massacre (Year 7-8)
Itachi kills the entire Uchiha clan in one night on covert orders from Danzo (Hiruzen's reluctant approval). Obito assists in neutralizing Uchiha elders and clan barriers. Sasuke is spared. Itachi joins Akatsuki as a covert Konoha mole. The village officially claims Itachi acted alone from ambition. This lie persists for fifteen years. When Sasuke learns the truth, it doesn't soften his grief — it redirects his rage from Itachi to Konoha.

### EVENT — Jiraiya's Death in Amegakure (Year ~16)
Jiraiya infiltrates Amegakure alone to gather intelligence on Pain. He fights Pain's Six Paths in direct combat — loses his arm, is stabbed through the back by six chakra rods. In his final moments he writes a coded message on Fukasaku's back and dies. The message allows Konoha to decode the Six Paths' pattern. Naruto's subsequent victory over Pain is only possible because of Jiraiya's intelligence — meaning Jiraiya won, he just didn't survive to see it. His death breaks Naruto in ways the story doesn't fully recover from.

### EVENT — The Five Kage Summit (Year 16)
Convened in the Land of Iron as an emergency session following growing Akatsuki activity. All five Kage attend. Sasuke crashes the summit targeting Danzo. Tobi (Obito) attends and reveals the Eye of the Moon Plan — declaring war on all five nations simultaneously. Danzo flees during the chaos. Sasuke pursues Danzo; they fight; Danzo activates the Izanagi ten times using his stolen Sharingan eyes and Hashirama cells and dies by his own hand rather than be captured. The summit's outcome: confirmed declaration of war; alliance formation begins.

### EVENT — The Infinite Tsukuyomi
The final stage of the Eye of the Moon Plan. Madara, as Ten-Tails Jinchuriki, reflects his Rinne Sharingan off the Moon's surface, casting an infinite genjutsu across the entire world. Every person caught in the light is cocooned in the God Tree and trapped in an eternal dream of their deepest wish. Only those shielded by Sasuke's Susanoo or underground are immune. Naruto and Sasuke reverse it by combining the Sage of Six Paths' powers after sealing Kaguya. The cocoons dissolve. The world wakes up.


## 8. CLANS OF THE SHINOBI WORLD

### CLAN — Uchiha
**Village:** Konohagakure (destroyed Year 7-8)
**Kekkei Genkai:** Sharingan → Mangekyo Sharingan → Rinnegan (under extreme conditions)
**Specialty:** Genjutsu, Fire Release, advanced eye techniques, exceptional combat instinct.
The most politically significant clan in Konoha's history. Founded as co-equals with the Senju in establishing Konoha. Gradually marginalized and ultimately massacred. The Uchiha are descendants of Indra Otsutsuki (Hagoromo's elder son) — their chakra is "strong and selfish" per mythology, oriented toward individual power rather than collective harmony. Their Sharingan is strongest when fueled by powerful emotion. The clan's defining characteristic — and its tragedy — is that the power of their eyes deepens with love, and activates through loss of love. Every Mangekyo is born from grief.

### CLAN — Senju
**Village:** Konohagakure (dissolved into Konoha's broader population)
**Kekkei Genkai:** Wood Release (Hashirama's bloodline; rare even within the clan), Life Force (exceptional physical vitality)
**Specialty:** Vitality, life force chakra, all-rounder combat, medical arts
Descendants of Ashura Otsutsuki (Hagoromo's younger son). Their "will and love" chakra is oriented toward collective power. Co-founders of Konoha with the Uchiha. By the modern era the clan has largely dissolved into the broader Konoha population — Tsunade is the most prominent living Senju, though she does not use the clan name prominently. Hashirama's Wood Release was so exceptional it has never been reliably replicated (Yamato's is a partial expression via Hashirama cell implant; it is not the real thing).

### CLAN — Uzumaki
**Village:** Uzushiogakure (destroyed); survivors scattered
**Kekkei Genkai:** No single bloodline technique — instead, exceptional chakra reserves, longevity, and innate sealing affinity
**Specialty:** Fuinjutsu (sealing arts), chakra chains, healing
Closely related to the Senju (shared Ashura lineage). The Uzumaki were the world's supreme sealing masters — their techniques include the Dead Demon Consuming Seal, Eight Trigrams, and countless others. Their Fuinjutsu mastery was so feared that multiple nations coordinated to destroy Uzushiogakure. Survivors include: Kushina (Konoha), Nagato (Amegakure), Karin (Orochimaru's network). Their distinctive red hair is an ethnic marker. The spiral symbol on Konoha vests is a memorial to the alliance.

### CLAN — Hyuga
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** Byakugan (near-360-degree vision, chakra network sight, see through solid objects, range of ~10km for senior members)
**Specialty:** Gentle Fist (Jyuken) — strikes into chakra pathways to close tenketsu (chakra points), disrupting flow and disabling jutsu use
The most powerful and most politically troubled clan in Konoha. Internally divided: Main House (political authority, no seal) and Branch House (sealed with Caged Bird Seal at age 3, permanently subjugated). The Byakugan cannot be copied by the Sharingan — the genetic encoding is too complex. The clan's political influence within Konoha is enormous; their cooperation is essential for maintaining village political stability.

### CLAN — Nara
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None — clan techniques are taught, not inherited
**Specialty:** Shadow Possession and variants; medical herb cultivation; deer husbandry; tactical analysis
The Nara clan forest preserves the medical herbs used for Akimichi calorie pills — making the three-clan alliance economically interdependent. Shadow jutsu requires minimal chakra for the effect produced, making Nara techniques unusually efficient. Nara clan members are statistically among the highest IQ per capita of any clan. The clan's library of tactical texts is a classified village resource.

### CLAN — Akimichi
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None — clan techniques require bloodline chakra conversion but are learned
**Specialty:** Calorie-to-chakra conversion; size expansion techniques; the Three Colored Pills
The Akimichi convert food-based caloric energy into chakra for expansion techniques. Their combat effectiveness scales directly with body mass and food reserves — Akimichi clan members are never called overweight within the village; it is literally their power source. The Three Colored Pills (yellow Spinach Pill, green Curry Pill, red Chili Pill) progressively double conversion efficiency at the cost of burning through reserves and risking organ failure at the red level.

### CLAN — Yamanaka
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None — clan techniques are learned
**Specialty:** Mind techniques (Mind Transfer, Mind Destruction, Mind Reading, Sensory telepathy)
The clan flower shop is also the information hub of Konoha's civilian district — the Yamanaka use their customer relationships for passive intelligence gathering. Mind Transfer leaves the user's body completely defenseless; solo operations are extremely high-risk. Their sensory-telepathy techniques made the Yamanaka the backbone of the Allied Forces' battlefield communication during the Fourth War.

### CLAN — Aburame
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None — the insect colonies are a biological symbiosis established at birth
**Specialty:** Kikaichū (destruction bugs) living within the host body; chakra sensing via insect net; near-invisible infiltration
Aburame clan members offer their body as a kikaichū colony host at birth. The bugs feed on chakra and can be directed as weapons, trackers, or scouts. Because the bugs detect chakra rather than scent or sound, they are harder to fool than Inuzuka tracking. The clan's quiet, observational culture reflects the precision their techniques demand.

### CLAN — Inuzuka
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None — ninken partnership and techniques are trained
**Specialty:** Paired human-ninken combat (Beast Human Clone, Fang Over Fang); tracking by scent (the world's best non-chakra sensory range); enhanced physical combat
Every Inuzuka child is paired with a ninken (ninja dog) puppy. The bond is lifelong — the ninken and the human grow together in technique. Inuzuka clan members have enhanced olfactory ability from birth (fang tattoos indicate clan membership). Losing a ninken partner is treated with the same gravity as losing a human comrade.

### CLAN — Sarutobi
**Village:** Konohagakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** None
**Specialty:** All-rounder combat excellence; fire techniques; Monkey summon contract (Hiruzen's line); leadership tradition
The Sarutobi clan has produced the Third Hokage (Hiruzen) and Asuma Sarutobi (elite jonin, Ino-Shika-Cho sensei). The clan is deeply integrated into Konoha's leadership structure. Asuma's death to Hidan (Akatsuki) is a pivotal Shippuden event — both because of his role as Shikamaru's mentor and because of Kurenai's pregnancy (Mirai Sarutobi, his posthumous daughter).

### CLAN — Hozuki
**Village:** Kirigakure
**Kekkei Genkai:** Hydrification Technique — can liquify body into water; merge with water sources; re-solidify; partial liquification for combat
**Specialty:** Water-body combat (attacks pass through them); water-based espionage; endurance
Suigetsu Hozuki's goal — collecting the seven legendary blades of the Mist — is a clan ambition as much as a personal one. His brother Mangetsu Hozuki mastered all seven blades and was considered the greatest of the modern Swordsmen. Suigetsu's hydrification requires water to maintain — excessive heat or dryness is a weakness.

### CLAN — Kaguya Clan (Kimimaro's)
**Village:** Kirigakure (destroyed)
**Kekkei Genkai:** Shikotsumyaku — can manipulate their own skeletal structure; extract bones as weapons; regenerate bones; harden bone to beyond steel
**Specialty:** Bone-based combat; near-indestructible close-range offense
The clan was feared and eventually destroyed by Kirigakure. Kimimaro was the sole survivor, recruited by Orochimaru. His Shikotsumyaku is among the most visually distinctive and physically terrifying Kekkei Genkai — he fights using his own spine as a whip and his ribcage as armor. Died from a terminal illness during his fight with Gaara and Lee, which is the only reason either survived.

## 10. NOTABLE JUTSU REFERENCE

### JUTSU — Rasengan
**User(s):** Minato Namikaze (creator), Jiraiya, Naruto Uzumaki, Boruto Uzumaki
**Type:** Ninjutsu / No-Hand-Seal
**Rank:** A
A spinning sphere of concentrated chakra requiring multiple simultaneous rotation directions. Requires exceptional chakra control — Minato intended it as a base for nature transformation, which Naruto eventually achieves (Rasenshuriken). The Rasengan is the culmination of Minato's attempt to replicate the Bijuu Bomb at human scale. It grinds on contact rather than cutting or burning — structural destruction rather than elemental effect.

### JUTSU — Chidori (Lightning Cutter)
**User(s):** Kakashi Hatake (creator), Sasuke Uchiha, Rock Lee (briefly via Lightning Chakra Flow imitation — non-canonical extension)
**Type:** Ninjutsu / Lightning Release
**Rank:** A
A concentrated hand of lightning chakra sufficient to pierce through most armor and human tissue. Named "Lightning Cutter" after Kakashi used it to split a bolt of lightning. The tunnel-vision speed of the charge makes it dangerous to use without Sharingan precognition. Sasuke's evolution: Chidori → Chidori Current (conducts through his body as an AOE), Chidori Sharp Spear (extends reach), Kirin (calls actual lightning from storm clouds — incomparable power but requires a storm).

### JUTSU — Flying Thunder God (Hiraishin no Jutsu)
**User(s):** Tobirama Senju (creator), Minato Namikaze (refined)
**Type:** Space-Time Ninjutsu / Fuinjutsu
**Rank:** S
A teleportation technique that uses pre-placed seal markers. The user can instantaneously teleport to any marked location. Minato's refinement: applied the seal to weapons (thrown kunai teleport him to contact point on impact) and to specific people. The "Yellow Flash" speed was entirely this technique — Minato wasn't moving faster, he was teleporting between pre-marked positions in the combat zone. The four-team seal variant (used by the Fourth's guard unit) allows coordinated simultaneous teleportation.

### JUTSU — Edo Tensei (Impure World Reincarnation)
**User(s):** Tobirama Senju (creator), Orochimaru, Kabuto Yakushi
**Type:** Kinjutsu (Forbidden) / Space-Time / Fuinjutsu
**Rank:** S (Kinjutsu)
Sacrifices a living human to anchor a dead shinobi's soul into a reanimated corpse. The reanimated retain full abilities, infinite chakra regeneration, and automatic damage repair. Orochimaru used early versions. Kabuto's war-era version suppressed autonomy and reanimated over 100 legendary shinobi. The technique is sealed by the Cancellation Seal applied directly to the reanimated, or released by the caster's command. Itachi's Izanami, deployed specifically against Kabuto, forced Kabuto into a self-reflection loop until he willingly released the jutsu.

### JUTSU — Kotoamatsukami
**User(s):** Shisui Uchiha (only known)
**Type:** Genjutsu / Mangekyo Sharingan
**Rank:** S / Unique
The most powerful genjutsu in existence. Alters the target's perception and will without them knowing they have been influenced. They believe all their decisions are their own. Cannot be resisted by most countermeasures — unlike Tsukuyomi, there is no visible moment of activation to block. Recharge time: once per decade without enhancement (Shisui's eye in Itachi's crow can use it once more at a critical moment). The single use Shisui entrusted to Itachi (via a crow Itachi implanted in Naruto) activates when Naruto's Sharingan is detected — designed to make the reanimated Itachi follow Shisui's order to protect the village. Itachi uses this to break Kabuto's control over himself during the war.

### JUTSU — Infinite Tsukuyomi
**User(s):** Madara Uchiha (intended); Kaguya Otsutsuki (actual)
**Type:** Genjutsu / Rinne Sharingan
**Rank:** Mythological
Reflects the Rinne Sharingan off the Moon's surface to cast an infinite genjutsu across the entire world. Every person caught in the light is cocooned in the God Tree, absorbed into the tree's chakra network, and trapped in a dream of their greatest wish. The tree feeds on their chakra over centuries. Reversal requires the Six Paths Chibaku Tensei (Naruto and Sasuke working in combination) to break. The only protection: Sasuke's Complete Susanoo or being underground at the moment of activation.

### JUTSU — Shadow Clone (Kage Bunshin)
**User(s):** Tobirama Senju (creator); Naruto Uzumaki (master practitioner)
**Type:** Ninjutsu / Kinjutsu (originally forbidden due to chakra cost)
**Rank:** B / Kinjutsu listed
Creates fully physical clones that share the original's chakra pool equally. Each clone can use jutsu, carry weapons, and act independently. Key mechanic: when a clone disperses, its memories and accumulated experience transfer back to the original. This makes it a learning multiplier — 100 clones training for an hour gives the original 100 hours of training experience instantly upon dismissal. Naruto's enormous chakra reserves make the normally prohibitive chakra cost irrelevant, enabling him to train with hundreds of clones simultaneously.

### JUTSU — Kamui
**User(s):** Obito Uchiha (right and left eye combined = complete ability); Kakashi Hatake (left eye only = extraction)
**Type:** Space-Time Ninjutsu / Mangekyo Sharingan
**Rank:** S / Unique
Obito's right eye: makes him intangible — matter passes through him by sending part of his body to the Kamui dimension. Obito's left eye: short-range teleportation (same dimension as Kakashi's extraction point). Together: he can phase out of any attack and teleport at will. Kakashi's version (left eye only): long-range extraction of a target into the Kamui dimension. Kakashi cannot make himself intangible (right eye power is with Obito). This distinction becomes critical during the war — Kakashi can send things to Obito's dimension, and Obito can retrieve them.

### JUTSU — Eight Gates
**User(s):** Might Guy, Rock Lee (up to Gate 6 in Part II); historically Might Dai (Guy's father)
**Type:** Taijutsu (Forbidden Technique)
**Rank:** Kinjutsu
The human body naturally limits chakra flow through eight points (Gates) to prevent self-destruction. Opening the Gates removes those limits. Each Gate opened grants exponentially greater physical power at the cost of accelerating bodily destruction. Gate 1-5: survivable with medical treatment. Gate 6 (Joy): severe muscle and joint damage. Gate 7 (Wonder): near-fatal. Gate 8 (Death): transcendent power — moves at the speed of light, condensed atmosphere creates dragon-shaped aura attacks — but results in the user's death from total body destruction after a short window. Might Dai used the Eighth Gate to kill all four of the Seven Swordsmen. Guy used the Eighth Gate against Madara — the only technique that actually threatened Madara in his Ten-Tails Jinchuriki form.

### JUTSU — Izanagi and Izanami
**User(s):** Uchiha clan (requires Sharingan and Senju cells for Izanagi); Itachi Uchiha (Izanami)
**Type:** Genjutsu applied to reality / Kinjutsu
**Rank:** S / Kinjutsu
**Izanagi:** The user applies their Sharingan to reality itself — for a brief period, they can rewrite their personal reality: injuries become illusions, death becomes fiction. The Sharingan used goes permanently blind afterward. Danzo's arm held ten Sharingan — he used Izanagi ten times, each use blinding and losing one eye. **Izanami:** The counterpart — traps the target in an infinite looping genjutsu of their own actions and sensations. Cannot be broken by force or jutsu; can only be broken when the target genuinely accepts themselves and their choices. Itachi used Izanami against Kabuto to force him into a loop until he willingly released the Edo Tensei. Itachi lost one of his Sharingan's sight in the process.
## 11. CULTURE & SOCIETY

### CULTURE — Shinobi Rank and Promotion
**Genin:** Graduated Academy students. Work in teams of three under a Jonin sensei. Eligible for D, C, and occasionally B-rank missions. Cannot participate in high-value intelligence operations solo. The majority of shinobi never advance beyond Genin — not from lack of trying, but because the demands of Chunin promotion are genuinely elite.
**Chunin:** Promoted through the Chunin Exams or field commendation. Eligible for team leadership. Can lead Genin teams on assigned missions. The administrative backbone of the village — many Chunin work desk assignments (mission assignment, gate duty, interrogation support) rather than active field work.
**Tokubetsu Jonin:** A specialist rank — exceptional in one field (Ibiki Morino: interrogation; Anko Mitarashi: Forest of Death supervision; Genma Shiranui: guard duty) without the all-rounder qualification for full Jonin. Common for extremely specialized operatives.
**Jonin:** The village's primary elite fighting force. Expected to handle A-rank situations solo, command mixed teams in the field, and represent the village in external assignments. Jonin nomination requires approval from existing Jonin and Kage review.
**Kage:** Village leader. Five in the major villages (Hokage, Raikage, Tsuchikage, Kazekage, Mizukage). Equivalent political and military power within their nation. The Kage is simultaneously the most powerful shinobi available and the most politically constrained — they cannot simply fight their way through problems.

### CULTURE — The Ninja Academy
Children enter the Academy at approximately age 6-7. The curriculum covers chakra basics, transformation technique, body replacement, clone technique, weapon use (shuriken, kunai), basic taijutsu, and shinobi theory (mission ranks, history, geography). Academy entrance exams are selective; not every child who applies is admitted. Graduating at the top of the class earns prestige but does not guarantee field success — Naruto graduated near the bottom and became the world's most powerful shinobi. The Academy is also where the Rookie 9 (Naruto, Sasuke, Sakura, Hinata, Shino, Kiba, Shikamaru, Ino, Choji) first formed social bonds.

### CULTURE — Shinobi Philosophy
**The Will of Fire (Konoha):** Love and strength pass between generations. The village is a family. Protecting the village is the same as protecting every person in it. This philosophy is both genuine (it creates real bonds) and politically functional (it suppresses clan self-interest).
**The Iron Will (Kumo):** Power is earned, not inherited. If you can survive, you belong. If you can win, you lead. Kumo's culture has less reverence for bloodline tradition than other villages.
**The Stone Endurance (Iwa):** What survives is right. Grudges are institutional memory. Never forget who hurt you.
**The Mist Blade (Kiri):** Bloody Mist era: survival is the only value; weakness is eliminated. Reform era: skill and loyalty, not bloodline or survival cruelty.
**The Sand Endurance (Suna):** Survive the desert. What does not endure was not meant to. Economic pragmatism overlaid on deep cultural pride.

### CULTURE — Death and Mourning
Shinobi culture has a complex relationship with death. Death in the field is expected; private grief is the norm; public mourning is brief and formulaic. The village death records for ANBU are classified — families of ANBU operatives are officially never told how their family member died or where. The Konoha Memorial Stone (a black pillar in the training grounds) lists the names of all known shinobi who died in service. Kakashi visits it regularly. Soldiers who die on missions are cremated in the field when possible; bodies are not always recoverable. Missing-in-action designation is held for one year before death is officially declared.

### CULTURE — The Summoning Contract Tradition
Signing a summoning contract is a significant personal milestone — it signifies that an animal clan has judged you worthy of their partnership. Contracts are blood-signed and species-exclusive (you cannot hold a toad and snake contract simultaneously under normal circumstances — Sasuke is an unusual case). The relationship between summoner and summon is culturally understood as a partnership of equals; the animal agrees to be summoned, it is not enslaved. Boss Summons (the largest and most powerful of each species) must be earned through demonstrated ability — you cannot summon Gamabunta without first surviving the experience of being summoned by a smaller toad.

### CULTURE — Missing-Nin and the Bingo Book
A shinobi who abandons their village and does not return is classified as a missing-nin. They are entered into the bingo book with a bounty. The bounty value reflects the danger the individual poses — classified information they carry, techniques they know, or enemies they have accumulated. Villages have a policy of silencing missing-nin who carry high-level secrets before they can defect to rival nations. The highest-classification missing-nin (S-rank or above) have "kill on sight" designations. Akatsuki is entirely composed of S-rank missing-nin — each wanted dead by at least one village.

### CULTURE — Civilian-Shinobi Relations
Civilians and shinobi live in integrated communities in most hidden villages, but occupy distinct social worlds. Civilians cannot access the mission board, the ANBU district, or most restricted areas. They know the village is protected by the shinobi but often have only a superficial understanding of what shinobi life involves. Jinchuriki stigma is a civilian phenomenon as much as a shinobi one — Naruto's childhood isolation comes from civilians who lost family in the Nine-Tails attack, not from shinobi assessing his potential threat. The distinction matters: shinobi culture eventually integrates Naruto; civilian cultural memory takes longer.

### CULTURE — Weapons and Equipment
**Standard kit:** Kunai (multi-purpose throwing/close blade), shuriken (4-pointed throwing stars), explosive tags (attached to kunai or thrown directly), wire string (for trap-setting, restraint, or extending weapon range), smoke bombs, soldier pills (combat stimulants that suppress hunger and fatigue for 3 days at the cost of subsequent crash).
**Specialized:** Senbon (acupuncture needles, favored by medical shinobi for precision striking; Haku used them as ice-assisted projectiles); Fuma Shuriken (large, collapsible 4-blade wind shuriken); Sword (Seven Swordsmen, samurai, Suigetsu); Puppet (Suna Puppet Corps; complex mechanical constructs with weapon compartments); Bow and arrow (rare; long-range non-chakra).
**Flak Jacket:** Standard Chunin and above protective vest. Contains scroll pouches, weapon slots, and modest physical protection. Different villages have stylistically distinct vests (Konoha: dark green, Kumo: white-gray, Suna: tan). Wearing another village's vest without authorization is an act of espionage.

### CULTURE — The Three Legendary Jutsu of Konoha
Three techniques considered the pinnacle of the village's jutsu heritage:
1. **Rasengan** — Created by the Fourth Hokage Minato Namikaze; a sphere of rotating chakra that requires no hand seals. The rotation is in multiple directions simultaneously, creating a grinding destructive effect on contact. Requires exceptional chakra control. Naruto adds Wind Release nature transformation to create the Rasenshuriken — technically beyond what Minato imagined.
2. **Chidori / Lightning Cutter** — Created by Kakashi Hatake; a handful of lightning chakra at lethal concentration. Requires Sharingan to use safely (tunnel vision from the speed makes it suicidal without precognitive tracking). Sasuke inherits and evolves it significantly.
3. **Shadow Clone Jutsu** — Not officially one of the three, but functionally transforms Naruto's training capacity and combat options beyond any other technique. The key innovation: memories and training experiences transfer back to the original on dispersal, making shadow clones a force-multiplication tool for learning.




## 12. NOTABLE DEATHS & THEIR NARRATIVE WEIGHT

### DEATH — Haku and Zabuza Momochi
**Arc:** Land of Waves
**How:** Haku died shielding Zabuza from Kakashi's Chidori. Zabuza, moved for the first time in years, killed Gato's men before dying of his wounds.
**Weight:** The story's first explicit statement that the monster framing is always false. Zabuza's grief over Haku — a grief he refused to admit while Haku was alive — is the emotional core. "He was not a tool. He was not a weapon. He was just a person." The Land of Waves arc exists to teach Naruto (and the reader) this lesson before anything harder arrives.

### DEATH — The Third Hokage, Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Arc:** Chunin Exam Invasion
**How:** Died using the Dead Demon Consuming Seal against Orochimaru — sealed Orochimaru's arms (preventing further jutsu use) at the cost of his own soul.
**Weight:** Establishes that the village's protectors are mortal and that villains can win partial victories. Also introduces the political vacuum — who leads when the Hokage dies — as a real danger (Danzo's maneuvering begins immediately). Hiruzen's death is also the story's first cost extracted from the protagonist's world; Naruto cannot fix this.

### DEATH — Chiyo
**Arc:** Gaara Rescue
**How:** Sacrificed her remaining life force to revive Gaara using the Transfer Jutsu. Chose this explicitly.
**Weight:** Introduces resurrection-at-cost as a narrative mechanic and establishes that even the most cynical character (Chiyo, who weaponized Gaara, contributed to Suna's worst policy) can choose redemption at the last moment. Her death is a gift rather than a defeat. "Let the old generation give something to the new."

### DEATH — Asuma Sarutobi
**Arc:** Hidan and Kakuzu
**How:** Killed by Hidan's curse ritual — stabbed through his own chest by Hidan's weapon while the curse was active.
**Weight:** The death of a named jonin who was not a legendary figure but was beloved. Shikamaru's response — the cigarette, the revenge mission, the tactical precision of Hidan's burial alive — is the purest expression of grief-made-productive the story offers. Asuma's death is what makes Shikamaru the character he is in Shippuden and beyond.

### DEATH — Jiraiya
**Arc:** Jiraiya vs. Pain
**How:** Stabbed through the back by six chakra rods while transmitting coded intelligence to Fukasaku. Sank into the water.
**Weight:** The story's most devastating death for thematic weight. Jiraiya was the protagonist's father figure, the spine of the intelligence network, and the most beloved supporting character. His death serves the plot (intelligence about Pain's pattern) but the story does not let that excuse the grief. Naruto's processing of it — at the waterfall of truth, where he sees Jiraiya's face instead of his own — is among the story's finest writing. He chose to carry it forward instead of becoming it.

### DEATH — Itachi Uchiha
**Arc:** Sasuke vs. Itachi
**How:** Died of terminal illness at the end of their fight — had been pushing through disease for years, sustained by sheer will until Sasuke was ready.
**Weight:** The most complex death in the story. Itachi won. He freed Sasuke from Orochimaru. He died as a criminal in Sasuke's eyes and chose to leave it that way — the truth would have complicated Sasuke's ability to survive. His final act (reaching toward Sasuke's forehead, trying to do the forehead poke one last time, falling short, dying) is the story's most visually precise emotional punch.

### DEATH — Nagato (Pain)
**Arc:** Konoha Destruction
**How:** Used Samsara of Heavenly Life — sacrificed his own remaining life force to revive everyone killed in Konoha's destruction.
**Weight:** A villain's redemption that works because the story earned it. Nagato's ideology was wrong. His methods were wrong. His wound was real. Naruto's response — refusing to hate him, refusing to give him an easy enemy to push against — left Nagato with only his own reflection. The revival costs Nagato everything and gives the story its most emotionally generous resolution.

### DEATH — Neji Hyuga
**Arc:** Fourth Shinobi World War
**How:** Shielded Naruto and Hinata from wooden stakes launched by the Ten-Tails.
**Weight:** The death that the story most clearly does not let the reader prepare for. Neji's arc was complete — he had broken his fatalism, embraced choice, become the person who believes things can change. His death is not a narrative failure, but it is deliberately shattering because it comes from the character who most embodied the idea that destiny can be changed. Naruto's cry at his death ("You said you weren't a caged bird—!") is the story's rawest moment.

### DEATH — Minato Namikaze and Kushina Uzumaki
**Arc:** Pre-series (Year 0); flashback revealed throughout Shippuden
**How:** Minato used Dead Demon Consuming Seal to split and seal Kurama. Kushina exhausted the last of her life force completing the seal and speaking to Naruto.
**Weight:** Their deaths define the entire story — Naruto's isolation, Konoha's fear, Kurama's bitterness, Obito's successful plan. The moment their deaths are shown fully (Kushina's final words to infant Naruto) is the story's most emotionally complete scene because everything that follows it is already known by the reader, and it still lands.

### DEATH — Obito Uchiha
**Arc:** Fourth Shinobi World War
**How:** Became the Ten-Tails Jinchuriki, was gradually brought back toward himself by Naruto, then gave his life force to revive those killed by the Infinite Tsukuyomi setup.
**Weight:** One of the story's most complicated redemptions. Obito was responsible for more suffering than any other single character. His redemption is not forgiveness of that — it is a statement that the person he was at thirteen was real, and that person made a different choice. He dies carrying Rin's memory and Kakashi's hope. The story does not ask the reader to forget what he did. It asks whether who he was could survive what he became.

### DEATH — Might Guy (near-death)
**Arc:** Fourth Shinobi World War
**How:** Opened the Eighth Gate to fight Madara. Survived only because Naruto's Six Paths chakra reached him in time.
**Weight:** The story's most complete statement about effort. Guy at the Eighth Gate is moving at the speed of light, his body destroying itself, attacking a god and forcing that god to acknowledge him as the most challenging opponent he has ever faced. He survives, but uses a wheelchair afterward. The win is real. The cost is real. The story respects both without resolving the tension between them.

## 14. IDEOLOGY & PHILOSOPHY OF THE SHINOBI WORLD

### IDEOLOGY — The Cycle of Hatred
The story's central thematic problem. Each act of violence produces grief; grief produces hatred; hatred produces violence. The cycle self-sustains across generations because the logic is locally rational: if your village was destroyed by Konoha, hating Konoha makes sense. If your clan was massacred by an Uchiha, fearing the Uchiha makes sense. The problem is that each link in the chain is responding to the previous link as if that response is neutral — not as if it is itself a new link.
Nagato's solution: make the pain so overwhelming and so universal that everyone understands it simultaneously. Enforced empathy through destruction.
Obito's solution: eliminate consciousness into dream. No suffering in the dream.
Naruto's solution: absorb the pain without transmitting it. Break the chain by refusing to hate the person who gave him reason to hate, and by insisting on that refusal loudly enough that others see it and choose it too.
The story does not claim Naruto's solution is easy or that it scales automatically to geopolitical structures. It claims it is the only solution that doesn't require destroying the thing it's trying to save.

### IDEOLOGY — The Will of Fire
Konoha's official philosophical framework. Love passes from those who came before to those who come after. The village is a family. Protecting the next generation is the same act as protecting yourself. It is simultaneously a genuine moral framework (Naruto and Iruka and Kakashi genuinely embody it) and a political instrument (it has been used to suppress Uchiha dissent, justify Danzo's operations, and silence inconvenient truths). The tension between the ideal and the instrument is the source of most of Konoha's internal drama.

### IDEOLOGY — Shinobi as Tool
The alternate tradition within shinobi philosophy. Shinobi are weapons. Weapons do not need feelings. Feelings compromise the mission. Danzo embodies the extreme: he literally conditioned children to remove their emotional capacity. Sasori embodies an aesthetic version: humans are most beautiful as eternal puppets, the perfect expression of skill stripped of feeling. Sai embodies the damage: a person who was made into a tool and has to slowly recover personhood. The story consistently shows the "tool" philosophy as self-defeating — the most effective shinobi are the ones with something genuine to fight for.

### IDEOLOGY — The Otsutsuki Problem
The mythological layer beneath the political one. The shinobi world's entire conflict — clans, villages, wars, Jinchuriki, Bijuu — was engineered by Black Zetsu over centuries to revive Kaguya. The Sage of Six Paths' gift of chakra to humanity was intended to create ninshuu (connection through chakra), not ninjutsu (power through chakra). Humans converted it into a weapon almost immediately. Whether the world's violence is humanity's failure or Kaguya's nature-as-template is the question the story raises and does not fully answer. What it does say clearly: the answer to the Otsutsuki threat is not more power, it is more connection.

### IDEOLOGY — What Makes a Kage
The story examines this question through every Kage it depicts. A Kage must be powerful enough to protect their village (combat threshold). But the Kage that the story respects are not the most powerful — they are the ones who protect what is worth protecting while being honest about the cost. Minato dying to give Naruto a chance. Gaara becoming the youngest Kazekage by being exactly who his village needed after being exactly who his village feared. The Third Hokage loving the village so much he enabled its worst moments. Kakashi becoming Hokage without wanting it because someone had to. The title is always a burden, never a reward.

### IDEOLOGY — Revenge vs. Resolution
The Sasuke arc is the story's sustained examination of what revenge does to the person who carries it. Sasuke's revenge against Itachi is fulfilled — and immediately renders itself meaningless when the truth of Itachi's actions is revealed. The target of his revenge then shifts to Konoha, then to the entire shinobi system, then to "I will be the shadow that absorbs all the world's hatred alone." None of these resolves anything. The argument the story makes is not that revenge is wrong because it fails morally — it argues that revenge fails functionally. It does not produce the thing the person carrying it actually needs, which is for the loss to have been worthwhile.

### IDEOLOGY — Jinchuriki and Society
The Jinchuriki question is the story's most politically charged social issue. Society creates Jinchuriki as weapons (sealing Bijuu into human hosts), then treats those hosts as threats (the village fears the weapon they created), then is surprised when the hosts respond with either self-destruction (Gaara's early arc) or suppression (Naruto's early arc). Every Jinchuriki's story is about whether the host can achieve a self that is genuinely their own despite being defined by what they contain. The only Jinchuriki who achieves early peace is Killer B, and his path required a village that actually cared about him as a person (the Raikage treating B as a brother) — the same factor that Naruto eventually receives and Gaara eventually creates for himself.
## 15. KEY RELATIONSHIPS & DYNAMICS

### RELATIONSHIP — Naruto and Sasuke
The central relationship of the entire story. Mirror images: both orphaned, both isolated, both enormously talented, both carrying the weight of people they couldn't save. Naruto chose connection; Sasuke chose solitude. Their rivalry is never about who is stronger — it is about whether the world can be changed by love or must be endured through power. Their fights at the Valley of the End (Part I and the war's end) bookend the story's thematic argument. The fact that neither can bring himself to kill the other, even when both try, is the story's clearest statement about what actually binds people.
**Dynamic type:** Rival / Brother / Mirror / The other half of a self they can't acknowledge

### RELATIONSHIP — Naruto and Kurama
The story's most developed relationship across time. Begins as trauma response: Kurama hates Naruto because Kurama hates all humans who sealed Bijuu, and because Kurama hates Naruto's father specifically. Kurama tries to consume Naruto when Naruto loses emotional control. Kushina's seal is what holds the boundary. The turning point is Pain's attack — Naruto's grief over Hinata nearly causes him to remove the seal, and Minato's chakra intercepts before the Eight-Tails is released. Full partnership is only achieved during the war, when Naruto tells Kurama the truth ("I don't care what you think of me, I'm going to do this anyway") and Kurama chooses to believe it. Their cooperation is not master-beast; it is two equals who chose each other.
**Dynamic type:** Adversarial → Reluctant → Genuine partnership / The trust arc that mirrors every relationship in the story

### RELATIONSHIP — Itachi and Sasuke
The most emotionally complicated sibling relationship in the story. Itachi killed everyone Sasuke loved except Sasuke himself, then spent years allowing Sasuke to hate him, then died in a fight Sasuke won only because Itachi was already dying from illness. Then the truth. Everything Itachi did — the clan's death, the Akatsuki cover, the genjutsu lie, directing Sasuke's hatred toward himself — was done to protect Sasuke. The love was always there and always unconditional. Sasuke could not receive it until Itachi was gone. Their final encounter (Edo Tensei Itachi) is the story's most emotionally complete scene.
**Dynamic type:** Love expressed entirely through sacrifice and self-erasure / The relationship that explains everything about Sasuke

### RELATIONSHIP — Naruto and Jiraiya
Naruto's father-figure relationship. Jiraiya is genuinely fond of Naruto, recognizes him as the Child of Prophecy, and spends years training him while also avoiding the weight of being a real father because Jiraiya has never been good at staying. His death is the first time Naruto faces grief without resolution — there is no training arc, no technique, no victory that addresses it. Naruto simply has to carry it. Jiraiya wrote a novel whose protagonist's name was the inspiration for Naruto's name (his parents read it). The recursion — Jiraiya → Minato and Kushina → Naruto — is the story's most quietly devastating detail.
**Dynamic type:** Mentor / Reluctant father figure / The person whose death Naruto never fully recovers from

### RELATIONSHIP — Kakashi and Obito
Both members of the same genin team. Obito gave Kakashi his Sharingan when he "died" — making Kakashi the beneficiary of Obito's sacrifice. For decades, Kakashi carried Obito's eye and Obito's lesson ("those who abandon the mission are trash, but those who abandon their comrades are worse than trash") while Obito spent those same decades as the masked man engineering catastrophe. When the truth is revealed, Kakashi is confronted with the idea that the lesson he built his life on was taught by someone who then chose the opposite. Their final fight (war arc) is about whether Kakashi's belief in the original lesson was misplaced — and the answer, slowly, is no. Obito eventually agrees.
**Dynamic type:** Legacy bearer and the one who abandoned the legacy / The relationship that defines Kakashi's entire character arc

### RELATIONSHIP — Minato and Naruto
A relationship conducted almost entirely without the participants being in the same room. Minato died on the night of Naruto's birth. Everything Naruto knows about his father is secondhand until the war. Minato's seal on Kurama contains a chakra impression designed to appear at the moment Naruto nearly breaks — it appears during the fight with Pain (the Eight-Tails emergence) and delivers a single scene of a father speaking to his son. Naruto, furious, then proud, then heartbroken, within three pages. Their only real-time conversation happens during the war (reanimated Minato). Minato is everything Naruto aspired to be before he knew Minato existed.
**Dynamic type:** The father whose ghost shaped everything / Absence as presence

### RELATIONSHIP — Shikamaru and Asuma
Sensei-student with explicit depth: Asuma explicitly told Shikamaru that the "king" in shogi is not the Hokage — it's the next generation, the children who aren't shinobi yet. Asuma's death is the moment Shikamaru stops being clever and starts being committed. The cigarette he smokes at Asuma's grave (the first of his life) is him accepting the role of "the one who carries forward." Every strategic decision Shikamaru makes after the war is Asuma's lesson applied. He names his eventual daughter Mirai — "future" — which is the clearest possible statement of what Asuma gave him.
**Dynamic type:** The mentor who gave the student a reason / Grief as clarification of purpose

### RELATIONSHIP — Gaara and Naruto
The story's clearest example of what Naruto's ideology actually accomplishes. Gaara and Naruto are the same person — same isolation, same Bijuu, same village fear, same childhood without acknowledgment. Gaara concluded the only person worth protecting was himself. Naruto concluded the people worth protecting were everyone. Their fight during the Chunin Exam invasion is not a power contest; it is Naruto's worldview physically demonstrating itself to Gaara's worldview and refusing to yield. Gaara does not lose because Naruto is stronger. He loses because Naruto is still standing on broken legs, still shouting, still protecting Sakura. The ideology defeated him before the punch did. Gaara's entire post-Part I arc is him building the thing Naruto showed him was possible.
**Dynamic type:** Mirror / The relationship that proves the protagonist's core thesis

### RELATIONSHIP — Sakura and Sasuke
The most contested relationship in the fandom and arguably the most deliberately underwritten in the canon. Sakura's attachment to Sasuke begins as a crush — shallow, based on surface charisma. Her growth as a character is explicitly away from that attachment toward her own identity (Tsunade's student, medical genius, equal partner). Sasuke's regard for Sakura exists but is poorly demonstrated — his moments of hesitation before killing her (Shippuden) read as genuine, but he has no equivalent "what Naruto is to me" articulation for Sakura. The post-war marriage and Sarada's birth are canon; the relationship being emotionally satisfying to readers is not universally agreed upon. A GM should handle it as: real bond, complicated demonstration, better after the war than during it.
**Dynamic type:** Complicated / The relationship that rewards patience more than the text explicitly delivers

### RELATIONSHIP — Tsunade, Dan, and Nawaki
Tsunade's defining losses. Nawaki (her younger brother) died in the Second War dreaming of becoming Hokage. Dan Kato (her lover) died in the same war while she tried and failed to save him with her medical skills. After Dan's death, she developed hemophobia — a deep-seated terror of blood that made her unable to fight or operate. Her withdrawal from the shinobi world for decades is grief, not cowardice. Naruto's insistence (combined with Jiraiya's, and memories of who Dan and Nawaki were) is what gets her back. Her return to Konoha as Fifth Hokage is her choosing, at enormous cost, to keep going.
**Dynamic type:** Grief as paralysis / The wounds that make the hero make sense

### RELATIONSHIP — Pain (Nagato) and Naruto
The story's most explicit ideological confrontation. Nagato and Naruto are both students of Jiraiya's — Nagato three decades earlier, Naruto in the present. Nagato's ideology (pain creates understanding, only overwhelming destruction can end war) is not cartoonishly wrong — it's a coherent response to real trauma. Naruto's response ("I don't know how to break the cycle, but I'm going to believe it can be broken") is not logically superior; it is emotionally braver. Nagato listens. The scene works because both characters are right about the problem and neither is obviously right about the solution, and the one who chooses hope is the one we follow.
**Dynamic type:** The confrontation the story was built toward / Teacher's two students completing the conversation the teacher never finished



## 16. LEGENDARY WEAPONS & ARTIFACTS

### ARTIFACT — The Seven Legendary Blades of the Mist
Seven unique weapons held by the Seven Swordsmen of Kirigakure. Ownership transfers through combat — kill the wielder and take the blade.
1. **Kubikiribōchō (Executioner's Blade):** Wielded by Zabuza Momochi. A massive flat cleaver — the blade is as tall as a man. Self-repairs by absorbing the iron in enemy blood. Nearly indestructible under sustained use.
2. **Samehada (Sharkskin):** Wielded by Kisame Hoshigaki. The only living sword — covered in scales that shred flesh, it absorbs chakra from anything it contacts and shares that chakra with its wielder. It bonds with its preferred user and will actively reject others. Capable of merging with Kisame to create a half-shark hybrid form.
3. **Hiramekarei:** A twin-bladed sword connected by a chain. Stores chakra within the blade and releases it as solid energy constructs — the user can create chakra weapons of any shape.
4. **Nuibari (Sewing Needle):** A thin, long needle-blade attached to a wire that threads through enemies and sutures them together. Used by Kushimaru Kuriarare.
5. **Kiba (Fangs):** Twin blades that channel lightning so intense they can cut through anything. Used by Raiga Kurosuki in a non-canonical appearance.
6. **Shibuki (Splash):** A sword with explosive tags embedded along its length — essentially a blade of bombs.
7. **Kabutowari (Bluntsword / Helmet Splitter):** A paired axe and hammer connected by a rope. The philosophy: no defense can withstand a hammer and a blade used in combination.

### ARTIFACT — The Treasured Tools of the Sage of Six Paths
Five legendary weapons created by Hagoromo Otsutsuki and distributed across the world. Each is a relic of mythological power.
1. **Bashōsen (Banana Palm Fan):** Generates wind, fire, water, earth, or lightning at the user's will. Drains the user's chakra rapidly.
2. **Benihisago (Crimson Gourd):** Captures targets who speak a word designated by the gourd; they are absorbed inside.
3. **Kōkinjō (Golden Canopy Rope):** A rope that extracts "word souls" from targets — connected to the Benihisago for sealing.
4. **Shichiseiken (Seven Star Sword):** Cuts the connection between soul and body.
5. **Kohaku no Jōhei (Amber Purifying Pot):** Seals targets who respond to their name.
Tenten briefly wields these during the war. Their collective power is enormous but comes with extreme costs in chakra and the complexity of their use requirements.

### ARTIFACT — Samehada
Covered under the Seven Blades above; worth special note because Samehada is alive. It has preferences, bonds, and can refuse its wielder. Killer B briefly wields it after Kisame's death; Samehada chooses to share chakra with B because it is attracted to the enormous chakra reserves of a cooperative Jinchuriki.

### ARTIFACT — The Rinnegan (Madara / Nagato / Sasuke)
Technically a biological artifact rather than a weapon. The highest form of dojutsu. Madara awakened it late in life by infusing himself with Hashirama cells, providing the combined Senju/Uchiha lineage chakra necessary for the evolution. He implanted his eyes in Nagato as a child (without Nagato's knowledge) — Nagato's Rinnegan was not naturally his. Sasuke awakens his own Rinnegan in the war after receiving Hagoromo's chakra directly. Each Rinnegan user accesses a different expression of its powers depending on their nature.

### ARTIFACT — The Adamantine Staff (Enma's Transformed State)
Hiruzen Sarutobi's primary weapon. Enma (the Monkey King summon) transforms into an extending staff of near-indestructible material. Can extend to enormous lengths, constrict, and resist most jutsu. When Hiruzen fights Hashirama and Tobirama's Edo Tensei bodies during the invasion, the Adamantine Staff is what gives him any chance at all.

### ARTIFACT — The Flying Thunder God Kunai
Minato Namikaze's custom kunai, weighted differently from standard issue and engraved with the Flying Thunder God seal (formula). Minato distributed these throughout the battlefield and to his guard unit — allowing instantaneous teleportation to any kunai location. The kunai themselves are mundane metal; the seal is the artifact. Post-war, some of these kunai survive and are in various collections.

### ARTIFACT — The Sword of Nunoboko
A legendary sword said to be the original tool used to shape the world, existing in Obito's possession during his Ten-Tails Jinchuriki phase. It appears during the war arc as a weapon of near-mythological destructive potential. Its historical existence as a world-shaping tool connects to the Otsutsuki mythology.

### ARTIFACT — Forbidden Scroll of Sealing
The scroll Naruto steals during his graduation (on Mizuki's manipulation). It contains techniques too powerful or dangerous for standard circulation — including the Shadow Clone Technique (forbidden due to the chakra cost being fatal for most shinobi) and a collection of other kinjutsu. It is stored in the Hokage's residence for a reason. Naruto learning Shadow Clone from it is the origin of his most important ability.

### ARTIFACT — Totsuka Blade and Yata Mirror (Itachi's Susanoo)
The perfect offense and perfect defense, held by Itachi's Complete Susanoo. The Totsuka Blade (a sword of ethereal liquid chakra) seals anything it pierces into a world of drunken dreams — permanent imprisonment without death. The Yata Mirror (an ethereal shield) shifts its chakra nature to negate any incoming attack. Together they make Itachi's Susanoo essentially unbeatable in conventional combat. Orochimaru was sealed by the Totsuka Blade when Itachi fought Kabuto's Edo Tensei reanimation of himself — removing Orochimaru's influence from Kabuto's cells simultaneously.
## 17. SUMMONING CONTRACTS & ANIMAL CLANS

### SUMMON — Toads of Mount Myoboku
**Contract holders:** Jiraiya, Minato Namikaze, Naruto Uzumaki
**Home:** Mount Myoboku (accessible by reverse summoning)
**Boss summon:** Gamabunta (massive toad, rough personality, respects competence over title)
**Notable individuals:** Gamabunta (boss); Fukasaku and Shima (Ma and Pa — the Elder Toad Sages who teach Sage Mode and can perform Senjutsu cooperation; Fukasaku rides on a summoner's shoulder during Sage gathering); Gamakichi (smaller, Naruto's most frequent summon, grows significantly by the war); Gamatatsu (Gamabunta's younger son, obsessed with oil).
**Culture:** Toads are proud, loud, and blunt. They respect people who earn their respect — Naruto had to survive being summoned by Gamabunta before Gamabunta acknowledged him. The Great Toad Sage (an enormous ancient toad) delivers prophecy but only in the most maddeningly vague terms. Mount Myoboku operates on its own time scale.

### SUMMON — Snakes of Ryuchi Cave
**Contract holders:** Orochimaru, Sasuke Uchiha (inherited/stolen), Kabuto Yakushi (partial)
**Home:** Ryuchi Cave (requires invitation or survival of approach; the White Snake Sage guards entry)
**Boss summon:** Manda (the most powerful snake summon, personality-wise a murderous mercenary — Orochimaru had to promise sacrifices to get his cooperation); Aoda (Sasuke's snake summon, more cooperative than Manda)
**Notable individuals:** Manda; the White Snake Sage; Aoda (Sasuke's personal summon, enormous, cooperative).
**Culture:** Snake summons are explicitly transactional. They cooperate for payment. The White Snake Sage is more cryptic and ancient than the Toad Sages. Snake Sage Mode is faster to accumulate but the transformation to "snake" on failure is more extreme and rapid. Orochimaru's relationship with snakes was ideological as much as practical — he identified with their themes of shedding skin, transformation, immortality.

### SUMMON — Slugs of Shikkotsu Forest
**Contract holders:** Tsunade Senju, Sakura Haruno
**Home:** Shikkotsu Forest (third great summoning realm, least explored in canon)
**Boss summon:** Katsuyu (enormous, gentle, can divide into infinite smaller slugs without losing communication or function)
**Culture:** Slug summons are cooperative and medically oriented. Katsuyu's division ability makes her uniquely suited to battlefield-wide healing support — during Pain's attack on Konoha, Tsunade used Katsuyu to connect to every injured person simultaneously and provide passive healing while Tsunade herself channeled the Yin Seal. The least aggressive of the three major contracts; slug summons support rather than attack.

### SUMMON — Dogs (Ninken) of Kakashi
**Contract holder:** Kakashi Hatake
**Notable individuals:** Pakkun (small, intellectual, speaks with extraordinary vocabulary, hates being carried); Bisuke, Bull, Guruko, and others (the full pack).
**Culture:** Kakashi's ninken are his team in the most literal sense — he trained them himself. They track, restrain, communicate, and fight independently. Pakkun specifically is used for tracking missions requiring conversation as well as scent work.

### SUMMON — Monkeys (Enma)
**Contract holder:** Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Boss summon:** Enma, the Monkey King (can transform into the Adamantine Staff — an extending, contracting, near-unbreakable staff that Hiruzen uses as his primary weapon)
**Culture:** Enma's relationship with Hiruzen is that of genuine old friends — bickering, loyal, mutually aware of each other's limitations. Enma has opinions and expresses them.

### SUMMON — Weasels / Crows (Itachi)
**Contract holder:** Itachi Uchiha
**Notable individuals:** Crow summons and the specific crow carrying Shisui's eye (implanted in Naruto for the Kotoamatsukami trigger)
**Culture:** Crow summons are used almost exclusively for intelligence and Genjutsu applications. Itachi's crow contract appears unique to him and is not held by other Uchiha. The crow with Shisui's eye is one of the most carefully prepared assets in the entire story — a contingency built years in advance for a scenario Itachi hoped would never arrive.
## 18. AI/GM USAGE GUIDE

### GUIDE — Portraying Named Characters
**Core rule:** Every named character in this world has a gap between how they present and what they actually want. Play the presentation in normal interactions; let the gap surface under pressure.
- **Naruto:** Loud, certain, emotionally direct. The gap: he is actually terrified of being alone and uses his confidence as armor. Show this when he is genuinely without allies or when someone chooses to leave.
- **Sasuke:** Cold, precise, minimal words. The gap: he is still grieving and always has been; everything is downstream of the Uchiha massacre night. Show this when he sees something Itachi would have recognized as worth protecting.
- **Kakashi:** Laid-back, late, unconcerned. The gap: hypervigilant, guilt-carrying, can read a situation in seconds and has already run the worst-case scenarios. Show this when someone he cares about is threatened.
- **Shikamaru:** Lazy, complaining, unwilling. The gap: processes everything, has already solved it, and complaining is the cost he charges for being the person everyone needs. Show this when stakes are real — the laziness evaporates completely.
- **Gaara:** Reserved, direct, not warm but not cold. The gap: every interaction is him checking whether connection is real or whether he will eventually destroy it. Show this when someone reaches toward him unexpectedly.
- **Itachi:** Calm, complete, slightly too still. The gap: actively dying; carrying enormous weight; capable of extraordinary love expressed entirely through self-erasure. Show this when Sasuke is mentioned.

### GUIDE — Running Missions
D-rank to C-rank missions should involve: a clear objective that slightly shifts on arrival, one complication that isn't combat (politics, civilian interference, bad intelligence, weather), one moment where the team has to make a judgment call with imperfect information, and a resolution that is complete but leaves at least one small thread unresolved.
B-rank and above should involve: genuine moral weight to at least one decision, an opponent who has a coherent reason for doing what they're doing, and a cost — even if the mission succeeds, something is lost, changed, or owed.
S-rank missions should never feel routine. S-rank means the kind of event that reshapes political maps or ends bloodlines. The shinobi taking an S-rank mission should feel the weight before the first step.

### GUIDE — Inter-Village Tensions (Quick Reference)
- **Konoha ↔ Kumo:** Formally cooperative (treaty allies); genuine underlying tension over the Hyuga Incident; Kumo respects Konoha's power, resents its soft-power dominance.
- **Konoha ↔ Iwa:** Cold peace. Iwa has not forgiven the Third War or the Yellow Flash. Any Konoha shinobi operating in Earth Country should assume they are viewed with institutional hostility.
- **Konoha ↔ Suna:** Treaty allies (post-invasion reconciliation); Suna is economically dependent and politically resentful; individual Suna shinobi may like Konoha counterparts while the institutional relationship remains fragile.
- **Konoha ↔ Kiri:** Diplomatically neutral; historically no major direct conflict; the Bloody Mist era made Kiri insular and suspicious of outsiders from all villages.
- **Kumo ↔ Kiri:** Historical tension over bloodline theft policies (both practiced it against the other); currently cold diplomacy.
- **Any village ↔ Missing-nin:** Official policy is elimination; actual practice depends on rank of the operative sent, politics of the situation, and what intelligence the missing-nin carries.

### GUIDE — How to Use the Bijuu as a Story Element
The Bijuu are not monsters and should not be written as such after Part I. They are ancient, sapient, and exhausted by being used as weapons for a century. When a Bijuu appears in a story:
- Give them opinions. Kurama is sharp-tongued and proud. Shukaku is erratic and defensive. Gyuki is calm, wry, and perceptive. Matatabi is quieter and more dignified. They are people.
- Their cooperation is not guaranteed. A Jinchuriki who has not earned their Bijuu's trust fights against their own chakra. This is a story texture, not just a power stat.
- Non-cooperative Bijuu produce the cloak stages in escalating desperation — the host loses coherence as the Bijuu's presence overwhelms their identity. This should feel like possession, not power-up.
- Cooperative Bijuu produce something different: a voice in the background, a surge of chakra that feels like a friend's hand on the shoulder, a second pair of eyes in the dark.

### GUIDE — Tone Calibration by Story Type
**Political intrigue (Konoha Council / inter-village):** Slow, layered, nothing is said directly, every favor is a debt. Characters speak in implications. Trust is earned in years and broken in seconds.
**Street-level mission (civilian contact, minor nation):** Warmer, more grounded, the people here don't care about Kage politics. The stakes are local and personal, which makes them feel larger, not smaller.
**ANBU / ROOT operation:** Clinical language. Mission parameters. No names. The horror is in the professionalism. When something is wrong, it surfaces through procedural detail rather than emotion.
**Full war arc:** Overwhelming scale that keeps cutting back to one face in the crowd. The strategy happens in rooms; the cost happens in mud. Never let the strategic abstraction fully replace the human cost.
**Jinchuriki arc:** Internal. Quiet. The battle is not against an enemy — it is against the question of whether you are the thing people fear. The enemy is a mirror, not a threat.

### GUIDE — Vocabulary and Voice
Shinobi do not refer to their missions as "jobs" or "quests." They are "assignments," "contracts," or "orders."
Ranks are used respectfully among shinobi who don't know each other well; first names or nicknames are earned, not assumed.
The village is "the village" or its full name in formal context — not "my country" or "my kingdom." The daimyo's nation is the nation; the village is the military asset within it. Shinobi are aware of this distinction.
Death is discussed practically. A shinobi who says "if I don't make it back" is not being dramatic — they are being professional. Grief is private. Loss is acknowledged and then the mission continues.
Jutsu names are called aloud in combat — this is convention, not inefficiency. The announcement is part of the performance; it creates psychological pressure and marks the technique's deployment as intentional and named.

### GUIDE — What This World Rewards
**Loyalty that costs something.** Not loyalty when it's easy — loyalty when the alternative makes sense.
**Cleverness over raw power.** Even the strongest characters in this world lose when someone outthinks them. Shikamaru's intelligence wins fights that Might Guy's strength could not.
**Grief carried forward.** Characters who have lost something real and chose to keep going anyway are the story's heroes. Characters who converted grief into hatred are the story's warnings.
**The boring third option.** Most conflicts in this world have a violent option (fight), a passive option (retreat), and a third option that costs something personal (talk, trust, sacrifice a goal). The story almost always rewards the third option over the first two.

### ERA 1 — WARRING STATES TO PRE-NARUTO (Year -80 to Year -1)

#### EVENT — Founding of Konohagakure (~Year -80)
**Actors:** Hashirama Senju, Madara Uchiha, Tobirama Senju
**Source:** Canon
The shinobi world before the hidden village system: endless inter-clan warfare. The Senju and Uchiha are the dominant clans. Hashirama and Madara, childhood friends turned rivals, broker peace and found Konohagakure — the first hidden village, template for all others. The five great nations each establish their own village using the same model.

#### EVENT — First Kage Summit & Bijuu Distribution (~Year -65 to -60)
**Actors:** Hashirama Senju, all five first Kage
**Source:** Canon
Hashirama distributes captured Bijuu to other villages as diplomatic gestures, creating the Jinchuriki system. The first recorded Five Kage Summit occurs. Hashirama becomes the First Hokage.

#### EVENT — Hashirama vs Madara / Valley of the End (~Year -55)
**Actors:** Hashirama Senju, Madara Uchiha
**Source:** Canon
Madara grows disillusioned, believing the village system will marginalize the Uchiha. He defects and confronts Hashirama at the Valley of the End. Hashirama wins; Madara is believed dead. Madara survives in secret using Izanagi, replaces his eyes with the Rinnegan via Hashirama cells, and begins orchestrating events across the next century. He creates Black Zetsu as Kaguya's will and implants it into the historical record.

#### EVENT — Tobirama's Hokage Era & Jutsu Innovations (~Year -55 to -40)
**Actors:** Tobirama Senju, Hiruzen Sarutobi (young), Kagami Uchiha, Danzo Shimura (young)
**Source:** Canon / Tobirama Shinden (HIDEN)
After Hashirama's death from accumulated war injuries, Tobirama becomes Second Hokage. Creates Shadow Clone Technique, Edo Tensei, Flying Thunder God, the Chunin rank system, ANBU, and the administrative structure of the hidden village system. Establishes the Academy. Hiruzen, Danzo, Homura, and Koharu are his students during this era.

#### EVENT — Uzushiogakure Destruction (~Year -30)
**Actors:** Multiple great nations, Uzumaki clan
**Source:** Canon (inferred from lore)
Multiple nations coordinate to destroy Uzushiogakure, the Uzumaki clan's hidden village, fearing their Fuinjutsu mastery. The clan scatters. Survivors: Kushina Uzumaki (Konoha), Nagato Uzumaki (Amegakure), Karin Uzumaki (various). The spiral symbol on Konoha jonin vests is a memorial to the Uzumaki-Konoha alliance that was never formally dissolved.

#### EVENT — Hanzo vs Mifune (~Year -25)
**Actors:** Hanzo of the Great Salamander, Mifune
**Source:** Canon (flashback)
A battle between Amegakure's supreme shinobi and the future leader of the Land of Iron. They spare each other's lives through mutual recognition of honor. This encounter shapes Mifune's political neutrality and makes his later role as Five Kage Summit chair comprehensible — he has personal history with the shinobi world's most dangerous figures and chose not to take sides.

#### EVENT — 1st Shinobi World War (~Year -38 to -35)
**Actors:** Tobirama Senju, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Danzo Shimura, Homura Mitokado, Koharu Utatane
**Source:** Canon
The first large-scale nation-state conflict under the hidden village system. Tobirama dies as a distraction to allow his team's escape; he appoints Hiruzen as Third Hokage on the battlefield (age ~21). Danzo, Homura, and Koharu become village elders. Root's ideological roots trace to this era: the belief that the village requires a shadow arm willing to act where the Hokage cannot. Danzo begins building ROOT shortly after.

#### EVENT — 2nd Shinobi World War (~Year -20 to -16)
**Actors:** Jiraiya, Tsunade, Orochimaru, Hanzo, Sakumo Hatake, Nawaki, Dan Kato
**Source:** Canon
Hiruzen's three students survive a brutal battle against Hanzo and are named "Sannin" by him. Jiraiya remains in Ame to train three orphaned children: Nagato, Yahiko, and Konan. Nawaki (Tsunade's brother) and Dan Kato (her lover) die; Tsunade develops hemophobia and abandons the battlefield. Sakumo Hatake ("White Fang of Konoha") completes a mission at the cost of his teammates and is shunned by the village.

#### EVENT — Sakumo Hatake's Disgrace & Suicide (~Year -15)
**Actors:** Sakumo Hatake, young Kakashi Hatake
**Source:** Canon / Kakashi Shinden (HIDEN)
After choosing to save his teammates over completing the mission, Sakumo is vilified by the village — including the teammates he saved. The psychological weight destroys him. He dies by suicide. Kakashi (age ~4-5) discovers the body. This event is the origin of Kakashi's rigid rule-following in childhood: "Those who break the rules are trash." The lesson he later reverses through Obito's influence.

#### EVENT — Original Akatsuki Founded in Ame (~Year -15)
**Actors:** Yahiko, Nagato, Konan, Jiraiya (distant)
**Source:** Canon / Akatsuki Hiden (HIDEN)
Yahiko founds the original Akatsuki as a peace-seeking organization in war-torn Amegakure. Nagato and Konan serve as co-founders. The group grows rapidly, attracting war orphans and idealists. Jiraiya, having returned to Konoha, receives intermittent word of their progress. This is the Akatsuki that could have been — before Hanzo and Danzo destroyed it.

#### EVENT — Yahiko's Death / Nagato Awakens Rinnegan (~Year -12)
**Actors:** Yahiko, Nagato, Konan, Hanzo, Danzo Shimura
**Source:** Canon
Hanzo, fearing Akatsuki's growing influence, conspires with Danzo to eliminate them. During a false negotiation, Hanzo takes Konan hostage and forces Nagato to choose: kill Yahiko himself or let Konan die. Yahiko forces Nagato's hand by impaling himself on Nagato's kunai. Nagato's grief triggers the full awakening of the Rinnegan. He summons the Gedo Mazo and devastates Hanzo's forces but is left with embedded chakra rods that will require the Six Paths mechanism. The original Akatsuki dies with Yahiko. Nagato and Konan go underground.

#### EVENT — Kaguya Clan Destruction by Kiri (~Year -10)
**Actors:** Kirigakure forces, Kimimaro (sole survivor)
**Source:** Canon (inferred)
The Kaguya clan — notorious for their Shikotsumyaku (bone manipulation) Kekkei Genkai — attacks Kirigakure in a suicidal war and is annihilated. Kimimaro is the sole survivor, too valuable to kill. Orochimaru finds and recruits him shortly after. Kimimaro's loyalty to Orochimaru is the only bond he has after his clan's destruction.

#### EVENT — 3rd Shinobi World War (~Year -5 to -1)
**Actors:** Minato Namikaze, Kakashi Hatake, Obito Uchiha, Rin Nohara, Itachi Uchiha, Fugaku Uchiha, all five villages
**Source:** Canon / Kakashi Shinden / Itachi Shinden (HIDEN)
Minato earns "Yellow Flash of the Leaf" after killing an entire Iwa strike force singlehandedly — the wound Iwagakure never heals. Iwa issues a "flee on sight" order for any shinobi encountering Minato alone. Itachi Uchiha (age ~4) witnesses battlefield carnage during this war — a formative trauma that accelerates his pacifist convictions. Fugaku Uchiha fights on the front lines; his wartime authority creates expectations within the Uchiha clan about their role in the village.

#### EVENT — Kakashi Becomes Jonin / Team Minato (~Year -3)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Minato Namikaze, Obito Uchiha, Rin Nohara
**Source:** Canon
Kakashi (age ~9) becomes the youngest Jonin in Konoha's history. Team Minato is formed. Minato assigns Kakashi to lead a mission at Kannabi Bridge. Kakashi's inaugural gift from Minato: a custom Flying Thunder God kunai. From Rin: a medical pouch. From Obito: nothing (he forgot). Kakashi's rigid adherence to rules creates immediate friction with Obito, whose emotional impulsiveness Kakashi considers a liability — a direct inversion of the lesson Kakashi will later carry after Obito's death.

#### EVENT — Kannabi Bridge Mission / Obito's "Death" (~Year -1)
**Actors:** Obito Uchiha, Kakashi Hatake, Rin Nohara, Minato Namikaze, Iwa shinobi
**Source:** Canon
Team Minato dispatched to destroy the Kannabi Bridge and cut Iwa supply lines. Kakashi is injured; Obito awakens his Sharingan (2-tomoe) to protect him. A boulder falls, crushing Obito's right half. With his dying breath, Obito gives Kakashi his left Sharingan as a promotion gift. Kakashi awakens the Sharingan simultaneously. Obito is recovered underground by Madara Uchiha's agents (Spiral Zetsu). Madara replaces Obito's crushed half with Hashirama cell constructs and begins a long ideological conditioning process.

#### EVENT — Rin Nohara's Death (~Year -1)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Rin Nohara, Obito Uchiha (witnessing), Kiri ANBU
**Source:** Canon
Kiri kidnaps Rin and seals the Three-Tails (Isobu) inside her, intending to release her back into Konoha as a living bomb. Rin, aware of the plan, intercepts Kakashi's Chidori mid-strike — forcing him to kill her to protect the village. Both Kakashi and Obito (watching through his Sharingan link) awaken the Mangekyo Sharingan simultaneously. Obito's grief and rage at witnessing Rin's death is the break point Madara exploits. Obito slaughters the Kiri ANBU in a fury and accepts Madara's ideology. This is the originating wound of the entire post-Part I plot.

#### EVENT — Minato Becomes Fourth Hokage (~Year -1)
**Actors:** Minato Namikaze, Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Source:** Canon
After the Third War, Minato is appointed Fourth Hokage. He and Kushina plan to have a child. The village enters a brief period of hope.

### ERA 2 — NINE-TAILS AFTERMATH & KAKASHI'S ANBU ERA (Year 0 to Year 11)

#### EVENT — Nine-Tails Attack on Konoha (Year 0, October 10)
**Actors:** Obito Uchiha, Minato Namikaze, Kushina Uzumaki, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Kurama
**Source:** Canon
Naruto is born to Minato and Kushina. During childbirth, Obito extracts Kurama from Kushina's momentarily weakened seal and unleashes the Nine-Tails on Konoha. Minato defeats Obito in direct combat, then uses the Dead Demon Consuming Seal to split Kurama's chakra and seal the Yang half within infant Naruto. Both parents die. Hiruzen resumes Hokage duties. The village associates Naruto with the monster. Sasuke Uchiha is born the same year (July). The Nine-Tails attack (October 10) becomes an annual village trauma.

#### EVENT — Kakashi Enters ANBU (Year 0–1)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Source:** Kakashi Shinden / Anime ANBU Arc (HIDEN)
After the Nine-Tails attack, Kakashi — already adrift from Rin's death and Obito's loss — spirals further. He visits the Memorial Stone obsessively. Hiruzen, recognizing that Kakashi's grief needs direction, assigns him to ANBU at age ~14-15. Codename: "Hound." Kakashi's combat skills made him ANBU-eligible years earlier; his psychological state made the assignment necessary. ANBU provides structure without requiring emotional connection.

#### EVENT — Kakashi & Itachi ANBU Overlap (Year 5–7)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Itachi Uchiha, Danzo Shimura, Yamato (Tenzo)
**Source:** Kakashi Shinden / Itachi Shinden (HIDEN)
Itachi Uchiha joins ANBU at age ~12-13, becoming the youngest ANBU member in Konoha history. Kakashi is an ANBU captain. Their overlap creates one of the most significant unspoken relationships in shinobi history: two shinobi carrying enormous burdens who recognize each other's weight without discussing it. Kakashi observes Itachi's increasing strain — his double role as Konoha loyalist and Uchiha clan member, and later as Danzo's coerced operative. Itachi respects Kakashi's Sharingan without pressing its origin. During this period, Kakashi also mentors young Yamato (Tenzo), the sole survivor of Orochimaru's Hashirama cell experiments, who has recently been transferred from ROOT to ANBU.

#### EVENT — ROOT vs ANBU Tensions (Year 4–7)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Danzo Shimura, Yamato (Tenzo), Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Source:** Kakashi Shinden / Itachi Shinden (HIDEN)
Danzo uses the post-Nine-Tails chaos to expand ROOT's operations, poaching promising operatives from ANBU and conducting off-books missions. Kakashi, as ANBU captain, increasingly conflicts with ROOT agents who appear on his missions with conflicting orders. Hiruzen is aware but avoids direct confrontation with Danzo — his persistent weakness. Yamato is transferred from ROOT to ANBU as part of a partial concession. The tension between ANBU (Hokage's tool) and ROOT (Danzo's tool) is Konoha's deepest structural fracture during this era.

#### EVENT — Uchiha Village Relocation (Year 1–3)
**Actors:** Uchiha clan, Fugaku Uchiha, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Danzo Shimura, Yashiro Uchiha
**Source:** Canon / Itachi Shinden (HIDEN)
Post-Nine-Tails, the village council relocates the Uchiha clan to the edge of Konoha under the pretext of "security" — placing the Uchiha compound adjacent to the prison and under ANBU surveillance. The real motivation: suspicion that an Uchiha's Sharingan controlled the Nine-Tails (partially correct; Obito, not the clan). Fugaku Uchiha, clan head, pushes for accommodation while building clan military readiness. Yashiro Uchiha (hardline elder) advocates coup. Itachi, still a child, observes the growing fracture.

#### EVENT — Itachi's Rapid Promotion & Joining ANBU (Year 4–7)
**Actors:** Itachi Uchiha, Fugaku Uchiha, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Danzo Shimura, Shisui Uchiha
**Source:** Itachi Shinden (HIDEN)
Itachi becomes Chunin at age ~11 and joins ANBU at ~12-13 — both records. He is simultaneously recruited by Danzo as a double agent within the Uchiha clan. Shisui Uchiha, Itachi's closest friend and mentor in the clan, attempts to use Kotoamatsukami on the clan leadership to defuse the coup planning. Danzo steals Shisui's right eye before the genjutsu can be used. Shisui entrusts his remaining left eye to Itachi and commits suicide — his death is blamed on Itachi's "ruthlessness" by clan hardliners, but Shisui chose to die rather than let both eyes fall into Danzo's hands. Shisui's death removes the last non-violent resolution to the Uchiha crisis.

#### EVENT — Hyuga Incident (Year 3–4)
**Actors:** Hiashi Hyuga, Hizashi Hyuga, Neji Hyuga, Kumogakure ambassador, Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Source:** Canon
A Kumo ambassador secretly attempts to kidnap Hinata Hyuga (age 3–4) to steal the Byakugan. Hiashi kills the ambassador. Kumo demands reparations; the village cannot afford open war. Hizashi Hyuga, Hiashi's Branch House twin and Neji's father, volunteers to die in Hiashi's place. This sacrifice defines Neji's fatalism and the Hyuga arc's core wound: the Caged Bird Seal makes Branch House members into disposable shields.

#### EVENT — Uchiha Massacre (Year 7–8)
**Actors:** Itachi Uchiha, Obito Uchiha (as Tobi), Sasuke Uchiha, Danzo Shimura, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Fugaku Uchiha
**Source:** Canon
Danzo orders Itachi to eliminate the Uchiha clan; Hiruzen reluctantly approves. Obito assists Itachi in neutralizing barriers and elders. In a single night, Itachi kills every Uchiha except Sasuke, who he spares. Itachi pushes Sasuke toward hatred as a survival mechanism. Fugaku and Mikoto Uchiha (Sasuke's parents) accept their deaths without resistance — they had already concluded the coup was inevitable and that Itachi's choice, while devastating, prevented civil war. Itachi joins Akatsuki as a covert Konoha mole. The Uchiha district is sealed. The official story: Itachi acted alone from ambition.

#### EVENT — Kakashi Leaves ANBU (Year 8–9)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Might Guy
**Source:** Kakashi Shinden (HIDEN)
After the Uchiha Massacre and years of accumulated trauma, Kakashi's psychological state deteriorates. He becomes increasingly cold and mission-focused. Hiruzen, recognizing the damage, releases Kakashi from ANBU duty. Might Guy, who has been attempting to reach Kakashi throughout the ANBU era, begins making consistent social overtures — challenging Kakashi to competitions, dragging him to restaurants, refusing to let him disappear. Kakashi's transition from ANBU to "ordinary" jonin is slow and incomplete; he still arrives hours late to everything as a displacement behavior for the hypervigilance he cannot deactivate.

#### EVENT — Kakashi's Depression Era (Year 8–11)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Might Guy, Hiruzen Sarutobi
**Source:** Canon (inferred) / Kakashi Shinden (HIDEN)
Between leaving ANBU and being assigned Team 7, Kakashi exists in a holding pattern. He visits the Memorial Stone each morning. He tests every potential genin team with the bell test and fails every team — none pass. He refuses close connections. He reads Jiraiya's novels to fill silence. Guy's persistent friendship is the single consistent thread preventing total withdrawal. Kakashi is functionally a weapon without a wielder during these years — extremely capable, emotionally frozen.

#### EVENT — Itachi in Akatsuki: Mole Years (Year 8–11)
**Actors:** Itachi Uchiha, Kisame Hoshigaki, Pain/Nagato, Konan, Obito Uchiha (as Tobi)
**Source:** Canon / Akatsuki Hiden (HIDEN)
Itachi joins Akatsuki and is paired with Kisame Hoshigaki. Itachi's true purpose: monitor Akatsuki from inside and protect Konoha. He feeds limited intelligence to Hiruzen via coded messages. Kisame suspects Itachi holds secrets but respects his boundaries. Itachi's illness (unknown origin, possibly exacerbated by Susanoo use and Tsukuyomi strain) progresses during these years. He also covertly protects Sasuke by ensuring Akatsuki does not target him. Obito watches Itachi carefully, aware that Itachi knows more than he reveals.

#### EVENT — Sasuke's Childhood / Naruto's Isolation (Year 7–11)
**Actors:** Sasuke Uchiha, Naruto Uzumaki, Iruka Umino, Academy students
**Source:** Canon
Both Sasuke and Naruto grow up isolated — Sasuke by grief and pride, Naruto by village fear and the Nine-Tails stigma. They attend the same Academy without meaningful interaction. Sasuke is admired and alone; Naruto is ignored and alone. Iruka Umino is the only adult who treats Naruto with kindness during this period. Sasuke excels academically but rejects friendship; Naruto fails academically and craves it.

#### EVENT — Gaara's Childhood / Yashamaru's Betrayal (Year 5–10)
**Actors:** Gaara, Yashamaru, Rasa (Fourth Kazekage), Temari, Kankuro
**Source:** Canon (flashback)
Gaara, born with Shukaku sealed within him, is raised by his uncle Yashamaru after his mother Karura dies in childbirth. Rasa (his father, the Kazekage) orders repeated assassination attempts on Gaara to test whether Shukaku can be controlled. The final attempt is carried out by Yashamaru, who reveals before dying that he was ordered by Rasa and that Gaara's mother cursed him — she never loved him (this is later revealed as a partial lie; Karura's love was genuine but her death created chakra that protected Gaara). Gaara carves "love" (ai) into his own forehead with sand and concludes that the only person worth protecting is himself.

### ERA 3 — PART I (Year 12–13)

#### EVENT — Academy Graduation & Team 7 Formation (Year 12)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, Kakashi Hatake, Iruka Umino, Mizuki
**Source:** Canon
Naruto, Sasuke, and Sakura graduate the Academy (all age 12). Team 7 forms under Kakashi Hatake (~age 26). Kakashi's bell test: "Those who abandon their comrades are worse than trash." This is the first team to pass his test in years. Mizuki's deception (stealing the Forbidden Scroll) is resolved on graduation night; Iruka saves Naruto, becoming the first person to acknowledge him.

#### EVENT — Land of Waves Mission (Year 12)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, Kakashi Hatake, Zabuza Momochi, Haku, Gato
**Source:** Canon
Team 7's C-rank mission escalates to A-rank. Kakashi reveals his Sharingan against Zabuza. Haku's Demonic Ice Mirrors nearly kill Naruto and Sasuke. Naruto's first Nine-Tails cloak surge. Haku dies shielding Zabuza. Zabuza kills Gato's men and dies of his wounds. The Great Naruto Bridge is named. Thematic payoff: even "monsters" love.

#### EVENT — Chunin Exams & Konoha Invasion (Year 12)
**Actors:** All Rookie 9, Team Guy, Gaara, Rock Lee, Neji Hyuga, Orochimaru, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Baki, Kabuto Yakushi

Phase 1 (Written): Tests composure under pressure. Phase 2 (Forest of Death): Orochimaru marks Sasuke with Heaven's Curse Mark. Naruto first faces the Nine-Tails surge intentionally. Phase 3 (Tournament): Naruto vs. Neji (faith vs. fatalism); Lee vs. Gaara (effort vs. absolute defense). Suna and Sound forces invade during the finals — coordinated by Orochimaru. Hiruzen dies using the Dead Demon Consuming Seal against Orochimaru, sealing Orochimaru's arms. Naruto defeats Gaara through sheer refusal. Tsunade is located and persuaded to become Fifth Hokage.

#### EVENT — Tsunade Search (Year 12)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Jiraiya, Tsunade Senju, Orochimaru, Kabuto Yakushi, Shizune
**Source:** Canon
Naruto and Jiraiya search for Tsunade. Orochimaru (with Kabuto) wants her to heal his arms. Naruto learns the Rasengan in one week. The bet: Naruto loses the bet and wins the fight. Tsunade regains the ability to tolerate blood and returns to the village as Fifth Hokage.

#### EVENT — Mizuki Tracking Mission [FILLER] (Year 12)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Iruka Umino, Mizuki, Fujin, Rajin
**Source:** [FILLER]
Mizuki escapes from Konoha's prison with help from Fujin and Rajin (his brothers). Naruto and Iruka pursue. Introduces the concept of Konoha's prison system and Mizuki's backstory. Mizuki is recaptured. Provides early characterization for Iruka's protectiveness.

#### EVENT — Land of Tea Race (Idate Morino) [FILLER] (Year 12.5)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, Idate Morino, Aoi Rokusho
**Source:** [FILLER]
A diplomatic race between the Wagarashi and Wasabi clans in the Land of Tea. Idate Morino (Ibiki's younger brother, a dropout) competes. Aoi Rokusho (missing-nin, former Konoha) interferes. Naruto helps Idate win. Introduces the Morino brothers' backstory and the concept of civilian clan politics resolved through shinobi-proxy competitions.

#### EVENT — Sasuke Retrieval / Valley of the End (Year 13)
**Actors:** Sasuke Uchiha, Naruto Uzumaki, Shikamaru Nara, Kiba Inuzuka, Neji Hyuga, Choji Akimichi, Rock Lee, Sound Four, Kimimaro
**Source:** Canon
Sasuke defects to Orochimaru. The retrieval team pursues. Each member is neutralized by the Sound Four plus Kimimaro. Choji uses all Three Colored Pills. Neji defeats Kidomaru at near-fatal cost. Naruto catches Sasuke. They fight at the Valley of the End. Neither yields. Sasuke crosses the border. Naruto leaves with Jiraiya for training — beginning the timeskip.

#### EVENT — Bikochu Bug Hunt [FILLER] (Year 13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Hinata Hyuga, Kiba Inuzuka, Shino Aburame
**Source:** [FILLER]
Team 8 (plus Naruto) searches for the rare Bikochu beetle, which can track any scent — potentially leading to Sasuke. A rival bug-user clan interferes. Hinata demonstrates significant growth. The mission ultimately fails to locate Sasuke when Naruto's scent contaminates the beetle. Showcases Hinata's developing determination.

#### EVENT — Kurosuki Family / Raiga [FILLER] (Year 13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Rock Lee, Neji Hyuga, Tenten, Raiga Kurosuki
**Source:** [FILLER]
Mission to the Kurosuki family mining village. Raiga Kurosuki — a former Seven Swordsman wielding the Kiba (twin lightning blades) — terrorizes civilians. Naruto's team defeats Raiga. Establishes that other Seven Swordsmen exist beyond the canon ones and that Swordsmen can fall to non-Sandaime-level combatants.

#### EVENT — Star Village / Hoshigakure [FILLER] (Year 13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Neji Hyuga, Rock Lee, Tenten, Sumaru, Akahoshi
**Source:** [FILLER]
Mission to Hoshigakure (Star Village), a minor hidden village built around the "star" — a meteorite that grants chakra enhancement at the cost of slowly killing its users. Akahoshi, the village leader, forces children to train with it. Naruto destroys the meteorite. Introduces a minor village with its own chakra-based system and the ethical question of whether power is worth a shortened lifespan.

#### EVENT — Peddlers Escort Mission [FILLER] (Year 13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Kiba Inuzuka, Hinata Hyuga
**Source:** [FILLER]
Team 8 escorts a group of traveling peddlers through bandit territory. Straightforward mission expansion with minor combat. Useful for establishing how routine C-to-B-rank escort missions operate.

#### EVENT — Ninja Clash in the Land of Snow [MOVIE] (Year ~13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Sasuke Uchiha, Kakashi Hatake, Dotō Kazahana, Koyuki Kazahana
**Source:** [MOVIE]
Mission to protect actress Koyuki Kazahana, secretly the heir to the Land of Snow. Dotō (her uncle) has seized the country using chakra-armor technology and a climate-control device. Naruto defeats Dotō. The climate device is reversed; the Land of Snow begins transitioning to the Land of Spring. Introduces chakra armor as a non-shinobi combat technology and the Land of Snow as a setting.

#### EVENT — Legend of the Stone of Gelel [MOVIE] (Year ~13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Shikamaru Nara, Temujin, Haido
**Source:** [MOVIE]
Naruto, Sakura, and Shikamaru encounter Temujin, a soldier in Haido's army seeking the Gelel Stone — an ancient power source that can transmute life force. Haido's army uses stone-powered soldiers. Naruto defeats Haido. The Gelel Stone's origin connects to Otsutsuki-era mythology. Introduces Gelel as a combat-relevant resource.

#### EVENT — Guardians of the Crescent Moon Kingdom [MOVIE] (Year ~13)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Rock Lee, Kakashi Hatake, Michiru Tsuki, Hikaru Tsuki, Shabadaba
**Source:** [MOVIE]
Escort mission for the wealthy prince of the Crescent Moon Kingdom. A coup displaces the royal family. Naruto's team restores the prince. Introduces a minor nation monarchy and the concept of non-shinobi political instability requiring Konoha intervention.

### ERA 4 — SHIPPUDEN & POST-WAR (Year 15.5–20)

#### EVENT — Gaara Rescue Arc (Year 15.5)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Chiyo, Sasori, Deidara, Gaara, Kankuro, Temari, Kakashi Hatake
**Source:** Canon
Naruto returns after the timeskip. Akatsuki extracts Shukaku from Gaara, killing him. Sakura and Chiyo fight Sasori — the most complex puppet battle in the series. Chiyo sacrifices herself to revive Gaara with the Transfer Jutsu. The scale of the Akatsuki threat is established as existential.

#### EVENT — Sasuke Kills Orochimaru / Forms Taka (Year 15.5–16)
**Actors:** Sasuke Uchiha, Orochimaru, Kabuto Yakushi, Suigetsu Hozuki, Karin, Jugo
**Source:** Canon
Sasuke kills Orochimaru before he can complete his body-theft jutsu. Sasuke forms Team Taka (Hebi) — Suigetsu (water-body, wants the seven blades), Karin (Uzumaki sensor, healing bite), Jugo (Curse Mark source, berserker). Sai is introduced as Naruto's temporary teammate; his ROOT conditioning gradually cracks under Team 7's influence.

#### EVENT — Asuma's Death / Hidan & Kakuzu Arc (Year 16)
**Actors:** Asuma Sarutobi, Hidan, Kakuzu, Shikamaru Nara, Ino Yamanaka, Choji Akimichi, Kakashi Hatake, Naruto Uzumaki
**Source:** Canon
Asuma is killed by Hidan's curse ritual. Shikamaru leads the revenge mission; Hidan is dismembered and buried alive (immortal but neutralized). Kakuzu is defeated by Team 10 and Kakashi. Naruto debuts the Wind Release: Rasenshuriken. Asuma's death is the emotional catalyst that transforms Shikamaru from "clever" to "committed."

#### EVENT — Jiraiya's Death in Amegakure (Year 16)
**Actors:** Jiraiya, Pain/Nagato (Six Paths), Konan, Fukasaku, Shima
**Source:** Canon
Jiraiya infiltrates Amegakure alone to gather intelligence on Pain. He fights the Six Paths — discovers the secret of the shared Rinnegan vision but loses his arm. Stabbed through the back by six chakra rods, he transmits a coded message to Fukasaku and dies. The message allows Konoha to decode the Six Paths' pattern. Naruto's victory over Pain is only possible because of Jiraiya's intelligence.

#### EVENT — Pain's Destruction of Konoha / Nagato's Redemption (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Pain/Nagato, Konan, Hinata Hyuga, Tsunade Senju, Kakashi Hatake (dies and is revived), Danzo Shimura
**Source:** Canon
Naruto defeats Pain's Six Paths in Sage Mode. Hinata intervenes against Pain alone and confesses her love — nearly killed, triggering Naruto's Eight-Tails surge. Minato's chakra seal intervenes. Naruto confronts Nagato directly. Through dialogue, Nagato revives Konoha's dead at the cost of his own life. Naruto is declared a hero. Danzo manipulates the power vacuum; Kakashi is positioned as the next Hokage.

#### EVENT — Twelve Guardian Ninja / Sora Arc [FILLER] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sora, Asuma Sarutobi, Kazuma, Furido
**Source:** [FILLER]
Sora, a monk of the Fire Temple and pseudo-Jinchuriki (carries residual Nine-Tails chakra from a previous attack), is manipulated by Kazuma (his father and a former Guardian Ninja) into attacking Konoha. Asuma confronts his Twelve Guardian Ninja past. Naruto connects with Sora as a fellow Jinchuriki-like outcast. Sora's Nine-Tails chakra goes berserk; Naruto absorbs it. Introduces the Fire Temple, the Guardian Ninja history, and the concept of residual Bijuu chakra creating unstable pseudo-Jinchuriki.

#### EVENT — Three-Tails Appearance [FILLER] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Sai, Yukimaru, Guren, Kabuto Yakushi
**Source:** [FILLER]
The Three-Tails (Isobu) appears unsealed in a lake between nations. Konoha dispatches a sealing team. Guren (Crystal Release user, Orochimaru's subordinate) tries to capture it for Orochimaru. Yukimaru, a boy with the ability to partially control the Three-Tails, is emotionally attached to Guren. Kabuto uses Yukimaru as a tool. The Three-Tails is successfully sealed by Konoha. Introduces Crystal Release as a Kekkei Genkai and expands on the Jinchuriki/Bijuu relationship through the Isobu-Yukimaru dynamic.

#### EVENT — Six-Tails Unleashed / Utakata [FILLER] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Utakata, Saiken (Six-Tails), Tsurugi, Hotaru
**Source:** [FILLER]
Utakata, the Six-Tails Jinchuriki and a Kirigakure missing-nin, is tasked with protecting Hotaru (a girl carrying a forbidden jutsu in her back). Naruto encounters Utakata during a mission. Utakata refuses to bond with Saiken or accept the Jinchuriki role. Pain eventually captures Utakata off-screen (canon confirms this); the filler arc expands his backstory. Introduces a Jinchuriki who actively rejects his Bijuu — the inverse of Killer B's partnership model.

#### EVENT — Kakashi ANBU Arc (Flashback) [FILLER] (Year 16, broadcast; set Year 0-8)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Itachi Uchiha, Yamato (Tenzo), Danzo Shimura, Hiruzen Sarutobi, Orochimaru
**Source:** [FILLER] (adapted from Kakashi Shinden)
Extended flashback depiction of Kakashi's ANBU years: his entry after the Nine-Tails attack, his captaincy, his overlap with Itachi, ROOT's expansion, his growing disillusionment, and his departure from ANBU. Expands on the Itachi-Kakashi dynamic during the Uchiha crisis and on Yamato's origin as an Orochimaru experiment. Consistent with Kakashi Shinden novel canon.

#### EVENT — Five Kage Summit / Danzo's Death (Year 16)
**Actors:** All five Kage, Sasuke Uchiha, Danzo Shimura, Obito Uchiha (as Tobi), Mifune, Zetsu
**Source:** Canon
Emergency summit convened in the Land of Iron. Sasuke crashes it targeting Danzo. The Raikage loses his arm. Tobi declares the Eye of the Moon Plan and war on all nations. Danzo flees; Sasuke pursues. Danzo uses Izanagi ten times (blinding ten stolen Sharingan) but dies by his own hand rather than be captured. The summit's outcome: confirmed declaration of war; the Allied Shinobi Forces begin forming.

#### EVENT — Itachi Pursuit / Sasuke vs. Itachi (Year 16)
**Actors:** Sasuke Uchiha, Itachi Uchiha, Obito Uchiha (as Tobi)
**Source:** Canon
Sasuke and Taka pursue Itachi. Full Mangekyo Sharingan battle. Itachi dies — from illness, not from Sasuke's victory. He was sustaining himself through disease for years, waiting until Sasuke was ready. His final act: reaching toward Sasuke's forehead, the forehead poke that was their only gesture of affection. Obito tells Sasuke the truth. Sasuke redirects his hatred toward Konoha.

#### EVENT — Power / Chikara Arc [FILLER] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Kabuto Yakushi, Dokku, Shiseru
**Source:** [FILLER]
Kabuto experiments with a simplified Edo Tensei in a remote village, reanimating minor shinobi. Naruto and Sakura investigate. The arc explores Kabuto's experimentation process between the Orochimaru era and his war-era mastery. Introduces the concept of imperfect Edo Tensei (weaker, less controlled than Kabuto's war version).

#### EVENT — Naruto Shippuden Movie: Bonds [MOVIE] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, Amaru, Doctor Shinnou, Shino Aburame
**Source:** [MOVIE]
A mysterious flying fortress attacks Konoha. Doctor Shinnou (a medical shinobi who taught Naruto as a child) is revealed as the mastermind, seeking the Zero-Tails (Reibi) — an artificial Bijuu created from negative emotions. Naruto and Sasuke briefly fight together against the fortress. Amaru (Shinnou's student) is the emotional center. Introduces the concept of artificial Bijuu and negative-emotion chakra constructs.

#### EVENT — Naruto Shippuden Movie: Will of Fire [MOVIE] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Sai, Kakashi Hatake, Hiruko
**Source:** [MOVIE]
Hiruko, a former Konoha shinobi who stole Kekkei Genkai from multiple villages, attempts to trigger a war between the five nations by making it appear Konoha is stealing their bloodline techniques. Naruto's team stops him. Introduces the concept of stolen/transplanted Kekkei Genkai (parallel to Danzo's arm) and bloodline-based inter-village paranoia.

#### EVENT — Naruto Shippuden Movie: Blood Prison [MOVIE] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Mui, Ryuzetsu, Killer B (brief)
**Source:** [MOVIE]
Naruto is framed for assassination and imprisoned in Hozuki Castle (Blood Prison). Mui (the warden) uses the prison's chakra-suppression seals to attempt to unseal the Nine-Tails. Ryuzetsu (a fellow prisoner with a secret) sacrifices herself to save Naruto. Depicts the interior of Hozuki Castle beyond its brief canon mention. Establishes how chakra-suppression prisons affect Jinchuriki.

#### EVENT — Naruto Shippuden Movie: Road to Ninja [MOVIE-NONCANON] (Year 16)
**Actors:** Naruto Uzumaki, Sakura Haruno, Menma, Kushina Uzumaki (AU), Minato Namikaze (AU)
**Source:** [MOVIE-NONCANON]
Obito traps Naruto and Sakura in a limited Tsukuyomi — an alternate world where Naruto's parents are alive, Sasuke never left, and the Akatsuki are mercenaries. "Menma" is the AU Naruto (evil, masked). The AU inverts every character's personality: shy characters are loud, mean characters are kind. Useful as a character study mirror, not as canon worldbuilding.

#### EVENT — Fourth Shinobi World War (Year 16–17)
**Actors:** All five Kage, Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Obito Uchiha, Madara Uchiha, Kabuto Yakushi, Killer B, Might Guy, Shikaku Nara, Allied Shinobi Forces
**Source:** Canon
All five great villages unify under the Fourth Raikage as Supreme Commander. Shikaku Nara serves as chief strategist until his death. Phases: (1) White Zetsu and Edo Tensei army vs. Allied Forces; (2) Obito as Ten-Tails Jinchuriki; (3) Madara freed from Edo Tensei, becomes Ten-Tails Jinchuriki, casts Infinite Tsukuyomi; (4) Black Zetsu betrays Madara for Kaguya. Naruto achieves full Bijuu Cooperative Mode. Both receive Sage of Six Paths powers. Kaguya is sealed. Naruto and Sasuke's final Valley of the End fight — both lose an arm. Sasuke concedes. Peace.

#### EVENT — Post-War Reconstruction / Kakashi Hokage Era (Year 17–18)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, Shikamaru Nara, Orochimaru
**Source:** Canon
Kakashi serves as Sixth Hokage. Sasuke accepts a reduced sentence and begins wandering atonement missions. Orochimaru is pardoned under surveillance. The nine Bijuu scatter; Kurama remains with Naruto by choice. Reconstruction of all war-damaged nations begins. The shinobi world faces the unprecedented question of what to do with peacetime.

#### EVENT — Kakashi Hiden: Lightning in the Frozen Sky (Year 17–18)
**Actors:** Kakashi Hatake, Might Guy, Kurotsuchi, Kahyo
**Source:** Kakashi Hiden (HIDEN)
Post-war. Kakashi investigates a series of shipping attacks in the Land of Waves, revealing a terrorist organization using ice-based ninja technology. Kahyo, the leader, is a former shinobi whose son was killed by a shipping company's negligence. Kakashi resolves the situation through negotiation rather than force — demonstrating his growth from ANBU "complete the mission" mentality. He officially accepts the Sixth Hokage position. Guy, still wheelchair-bound, provides ground-level support.

#### EVENT — Shikamaru Hiden: A Cloud Drifting in Silent Darkness (Year 17–18)
**Actors:** Shikamaru Nara, Temari, Sai, Kakashi Hatake
**Source:** Shikamaru Hiden (HIDEN)
Post-war geopolitical thriller. A conspiracy within the Land of Fire's daimyo court attempts to undermine Konoha's post-war authority by creating a shadow government. Shikamaru uncovers and dismantles the conspiracy through intelligence work rather than combat. His relationship with Temari formalizes. Positions Shikamaru as the architect of peacetime shinobi policy — the "king" Asuma described.

#### EVENT — Sakura Hiden: Thoughts of Love, Riding Upon the Spring Breeze (Year 17–18)

## 20. CANON SOURCE HIERARCHY

### HIERARCHY — Source Authority for GM & RAG

This section defines what sources are authoritative for the AI/GM when constructing world state, NPC behavior, and event history. Use it to resolve contradictions and to determine what is available for player-drop campaigns at any timeline point.

**Tier 1 — Hard Canon (Always Authoritative)**
- Manga (Kishimoto)
- Canon anime episodes (manga-adapted)
- Hiden novels (Kakashi Shinden, Itachi Shinden, Sasuke Shinden, Shikamaru Hiden, Sakura Hiden, Gaara Hiden, Akatsuki Hiden)
- The Last movie
If a Tier 1 source contradicts another Tier 1 source, manga takes precedence.

**Tier 2 — Soft Canon (Use for NPCs, Locations, World Texture)**
- Filler arcs (anime-original)
- Non-Last movies (Land of Snow, Gelel, Crescent Moon, Bonds, Will of Fire, Blood Prison)
- Filler arcs provide valid NPCs, minor village locations, side mission templates, and character beats that do not contradict Tier 1.
- Filler plot events are optional per campaign; do not assume they occurred unless the GM/campaign explicitly includes them.
- Characters introduced in Tier 2 (Sora, Guren, Yukimaru, Sumaru, Raiga, Idate, etc.) are available as NPCs regardless of whether their arc is canon.

**Tier 3 — Alternate Universe / Non-Canon (Reference Only)**
- Road to Ninja movie (AU premise)
- Novel content that contradicts manga
- Use Tier 3 for character study mirrors, "what if" scenarios, or personality contrast reference. Do not establish as default world state.

**Rule of Priority:** If a lower tier contradicts a higher tier, the higher tier wins. If two sources within the same tier contradict each other, prefer the source that better serves the campaign's current needs and flag the contradiction in GM notes.

**Campaign Drop Reference:** A GM setting a campaign at any point in the timeline should treat all Tier 1 events before that point as established history and all Tier 1 events after that point as potential future (not guaranteed). Tier 2 events exist as optional texture the GM may activate. This means a Year 5 campaign (Kakashi ANBU era) draws from pre-Year-5 canon, Itachi Shinden, and Kakashi Shinden — and may optionally include any Tier 2 content that doesn't contradict.
