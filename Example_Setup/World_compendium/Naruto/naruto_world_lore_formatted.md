# The Shinobi World

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW
### OVERVIEW — The Shinobi World
The shinobi world consists of five major nations, each anchored by a hidden village (Gakure), surrounded by smaller unaligned countries and ungoverned wilderness. Power is organized around hidden villages as military contractors to their host daimyo. The world has survived multiple Shinobi World Wars that reshaped borders, bloodlines, and alliances. Between wars, tensions are managed through fragile bilateral treaties and the Five Kage Summit.
The world is pre-industrial but chakra-advanced: no firearms, but explosive tags, barrier jutsu, and summoning contracts fill equivalent military niches. Communication is by hawk messenger, runner, or chakra-relay seal. Travel is on foot, horse, or summon. Experienced shinobi cover enormous ground via body-flicker and speed enhancement.
**Tone:** GRITTY, POLITICAL, ACTION, MYTHIC.

## 2. FACTIONS
### FACTION — Konohagakure (Village Hidden in the Leaves)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** The Hokage (village leader), Village Elder Council (Homura, Koharu), Shinobi Council (clan heads), ANBU Black Ops
**Stance:** Pro-stability; dominant diplomatic soft power; the template for the hidden village system.
Founded by Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha at the end of the clan wars. The most populous major hidden village. Culture emphasizes the Will of Fire: protecting the village as family, passing strength and love to the next generation. In practice this philosophy also serves as political glue binding historically rival clans.

### FACTION — ANBU Black Ops (Konoha)
**Type:** Covert Military Arm
**Key Members:** Masked operatives (identities classified), historically Kakashi Hatake, Itachi Uchiha, Yamato
**Stance:** Answerable directly to the Hokage; elite Jonin-floor or above.
Handles assassinations, extractions, counter-espionage, and matters too sensitive for standard mission ranks. Service is anonymous. Family and friends are not officially informed. ANBU deaths are not recorded in public records.

### FACTION — ROOT / Foundation (Konoha)
**Type:** Shadow Black Ops / Intelligence Cult
**Key Members:** Danzo Shimura (leader), Sai (operative)
**Stance:** Hostile to transparency; operates without Hokage oversight.
Danzo Shimura's parallel black ops organization. Officially disbanded multiple times; functionally persistent. Operatives are conditioned from childhood to suppress emotion and individuality. Root agents appear as regular Konoha shinobi until activated. A source of deep political corruption and shadow wars. Active primarily from Tobirama era through late Shippuden; officially dissolved post-Pain Arc.

### FACTION — Kumogakure (Village Hidden in the Clouds)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** The Raikage (A), Killer B, Darui
**Stance:** Militaristic; direct; prizes proven combat achievement.
Built high in mountain peaks above the cloud line. The second most powerful village in most eras and the most militaristic in culture. Political power tracks military rank more closely than in other villages. Troubled history of kidnapping Kekkei Genkai bloodlines from rival villages. Maintains the Two-Tails and Eight-Tails Jinchuriki.

### FACTION — Iwagakure (Village Hidden in the Stone)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Onoki (Third Tsuchikage), Kurotsuchi
**Stance:** Conservative; holds longest institutional grudges, especially against Konoha.
Built into a massive rocky plateau with natural fortress properties. Values endurance, tradition, and self-sufficiency. The Yellow Flash (Minato) killing an entire Iwa strike force singlehandedly during the Third War is a wound that never healed. Onoki wields Dust Release (Particle Style), the most destructive Kekkei Tota in existence.

### FACTION — Kirigakure (Village Hidden in the Mist)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Mei Terumi (Fifth Mizukage), Seven Swordsmen of the Mist
**Stance:** Reforming; transitioning from isolationist brutality to diplomacy.
Spread across a mist-covered island archipelago. The most turbulent internal history of the five great villages. The "Bloody Mist" era under the Fourth Mizukage Yagura involved death-match graduation exams and persecution of Kekkei Genkai bloodline users. Under Mei Terumi, genuine reform is underway but incomplete and internally contested.

### FACTION — Sunagakure (Village Hidden in the Sand)
**Type:** Hidden Military Village
**Key Members:** Gaara (Fifth Kazekage), Temari, Kankuro, Chiyo (elder)
**Stance:** Treaty ally of Konoha; economically dependent and resentful of it.
Built within a sheltered desert valley. Economically stressed: the Land of Wind Daimyo has repeatedly cut village funding and outsourced missions to Konoha. This pressure drove the alliance with Orochimaru and the weaponization of Gaara. Cultural identity emphasizes endurance, self-reliance, and puppet mastery. The Puppet Corps is Suna's most distinctive military arm.

### FACTION — The Akatsuki
**Type:** Missing-Nin Organization / Ideological Network
**Key Members:** Pain/Nagato (leader), Konan, Itachi Uchiha, Kisame Hoshigaki, Deidara, Sasori, Obito Uchiha (true mastermind)
**Stance:** Hostile to all hidden villages; pursues Jinchuriki capture.
The most significant missing-nin organization. At peak consists of ten S-rank missing-nin wearing black robes with red clouds. Operates as S-rank pairs taking contracts and pursuing Bijuu capture in parallel. Goals shift across the timeline: originally mercenary, then Nagato's peace-through-suffering ideology, then Obito/Madara's Eye of the Moon Plan.

### FACTION — Otogakure (Village Hidden in the Sound)
**Type:** Puppet State / Research Network
**Key Members:** Orochimaru (founder), Kabuto Yakushi
**Stance:** Hostile; Orochimaru's personal instrument.
Not a true hidden village. A network of hidden bases and research facilities in the Land of Rice Fields. Exists to provide Orochimaru cover for forbidden research, body experimentation, and loyalty-building via Curse Marks. After Orochimaru's decline it fragments into remnant factions.

### FACTION — Land of Iron Samurai
**Type:** Neutral Military State
**Key Members:** Mifune (leader, Kage-tier)
**Stance:** Strictly neutral in shinobi politics.
A permanently snow-covered nation governed entirely by samurai. Used as neutral ground for diplomatic summits. Samurai achieve combat capability through trained chakra flow into blades. Self-funded through resource extraction and mountain pass tolls. Does not extradite to shinobi villages. Missing-nin who flee here enter a legal grey zone.

## 3. LOCATIONS
### LOCATION — Konohagakure (Village Hidden in the Leaves)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Capital
**Status:** Flourishing (most eras); Destroyed and Rebuilt (Pain Arc)
The hidden village of the Land of Fire. Major landmarks include: the Hokage Tower (central political nerve), Konoha Hospital (medical facility and research arm), the Ninja Academy, the Shinobi Library, the Uchiha District (populated pre-Massacre, sealed afterward), Nara Forest Preserve, Training Grounds 1 through 44+, Ichiraku Ramen (Naruto's cultural home), and Hokage Rock (carved faces of all serving Hokage).

### LOCATION — Kumogakure (Village Hidden in the Clouds)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Mountain Fortress
**Status:** Flourishing
Built high in mountain peaks above the cloud line in the Land of Lightning. Key landmarks: Raikage's Office (summit command hub), Cloud Arena (high-altitude combat training; public duels are culturally significant), Lightning Forge Quarters (chakra-metal weapon production; Kumo's most valuable export), the Waterfall of Truth (hidden site for confronting one's shadow self), and B's Dome (Killer B's personal training and cultural hub).

### LOCATION — Iwagakure (Village Hidden in the Stone)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Plateau Fortress
**Status:** Flourishing
Built into and upon a massive rocky plateau in the Land of Earth. Key landmarks: Tsuchikage's Office atop the Rock Spire, Stone Quarry Districts (economic backbone), and Explosion Corps Training Grounds.

### LOCATION — Kirigakure (Village Hidden in the Mist)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Island Archipelago
**Status:** Reforming
Spread across mist-covered islands in the Land of Water. Permanently shrouded. Key landmarks: Mizukage's Building (central island, mist-shrouded), Hidden Blade Training Halls (sword traditions are cultural cornerstones), no official Bloody Mist Memorial (the dead are not officially commemorated), and Hozuki Castle/Blood Prison (remote maximum-security prison island with chakra-suppression technology).

### LOCATION — Sunagakure (Village Hidden in the Sand)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Desert Citadel
**Status:** Economically Stressed
Built within a sheltered desert valley in the Land of Wind. Key landmarks: Kazekage's Tower (central citadel), Puppet Workshop Quarters (artisan-shinobi build and maintain combat puppets), Desert Training Ranges (enormous flat expanses), and the Iron Sand Reserve (secured site storing the Third Kazekage's magnetic residue).

### LOCATION — The Valley of the End
**Type:** Landmark / Battlefield
**Status:** Symbolic
A massive waterfall and river gorge on the border between the Land of Fire and the Land of Sound/Rice. Site of the legendary battle between Hashirama and Madara (their statues flank the falls). Later the site of Naruto vs. Sasuke. Every confrontation here is identity-defining.

### LOCATION — The Forest of Death (Training Area 44)
**Type:** Restricted Zone / Exam Terrain
**Status:** Active
Located in Konoha. Used as the second phase of the Chunin Exams. Contains enormous predatory fauna (giant snakes, insects), thick canopy blocking visibility, and a central tower. Entry without authorization is heavily restricted.

### LOCATION — The Land of Waves
**Type:** Coastal Region
**Status:** Recovering
A poor island-adjacent coastal region oppressed by the shipping magnate Gato before Team 7 intervenes. Accessible by the Great Naruto Bridge. Economically underdeveloped but emotionally significant as Team 7's first real high-stakes mission.

### LOCATION — Amegakure (Village Hidden in the Rain)
**Type:** Hidden Village / Theocratic State
**Status:** Contested
A mid-size hidden village in a perpetually rain-soaked buffer zone nation. Under Pain/Nagato and Konan's leadership, a closed theocratic state sealed from outsiders. Filled with war orphans and trauma. A place of immense suffering with genuine ideological complexity.

### LOCATION — Mount Myoboku
**Type:** Summoning Realm / Sage Training Ground
**Status:** Eternal
Home of the Great Toad Sage and the toad summoning clan. Accessible by reverse summoning. Offers Toad Sage training. Nature energy gathered standing still; slower and safer than snake mode. Jiraiya and Naruto's training ground.

### LOCATION — Ryuchi Cave
**Type:** Summoning Realm / Sage Training Ground
**Status:** Eternal
Home of the White Snake Sage and the snake summoning clan. Requires invitation or surviving the approach. Offers Snake Sage training: more aggressive nature energy, faster gathering, but transformation into a snake on failure is faster and more extreme. Orochimaru's original Sage source.

### LOCATION — Tanzaku Town
**Type:** Civilian City / Entertainment Hub
**Status:** Flourishing
A mid-size civilian city in Fire Country known for gambling halls, entertainment, and festivals. Tsunade's preferred hiding spot. A practical, seedy city with a functioning black market for medical goods and information.

## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES
### POWER_SYSTEM — Chakra and Jutsu
Chakra is produced by combining physical energy (from the body's cells) and spiritual energy (from mind and experience). It is stored in chakra coils throughout the body and released through hand seals, will, and technique.
**Five Basic Chakra Natures:** Fire (Katon) countered by Water; Water (Suiton) countered by Earth; Wind (Futon) countered by Fire; Lightning (Raiton) countered by Earth; Earth (Doton) countered by Lightning.
**Advanced Natures (Kekkei Genkai):** Combine two elements (Ice = Water+Wind; Wood = Water+Earth; Lava = Earth+Fire). Cannot be learned, only inherited. Kekkei Tota combine three (Dust = Wind+Earth+Fire).
**Jutsu Categories:** Ninjutsu (chakra-based techniques), Taijutsu (physical combat enhanced by chakra), Genjutsu (illusions via disrupting target's chakra flow; broken by self-disruption or ally intervention), Fuinjutsu (sealing jutsu; persistent chakra inscriptions), Senjutsu (Sage Arts; draws natural energy; extremely powerful but requires stillness; excess turns user to stone), Medical Ninjutsu (surgical chakra application).
**Summoning Contracts:** Blood-signed agreements with animal clans. Notable: Toads (Myoboku) for Jiraiya/Naruto; Snakes (Ryuchi Cave) for Orochimaru/Sasuke; Slugs (Shikkotsu Forest) for Tsunade/Sakura; Dogs for Kakashi.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Power Rank Scale
**Ranks (ascending):** Civilian, Genin, Chunin, Tokubetsu Jonin, Jonin, Kage, Beyond Kage.
A 2+ rank gap is effectively decisive in direct combat without significant advantage. Exceptions: terrain control, elemental hard-counter, genjutsu initiation, preparation and intel, superior numbers, and prepared sealing traps.
**Kage-level indicators:** Can survive sustained combat from multiple Jonin; deploy area-of-effect attacks large enough to destroy a district; maintain offense while defending; possess a jutsu that forces opponents to solve a specific problem.
**Beyond Kage:** Redefine the terms of engagement entirely. Naruto in Sage Mode senses intent before movement. Sasuke with Rinnegan swaps places with any object. Hashirama's Wood Release subdues Bijuu. These are narrative forces, not mission targets.

### POWER_SYSTEM — Bijuu and Jinchuriki
The nine Tailed Beasts (Bijuu) are living masses of chakra originating from the Ten-Tails, separated by the Sage of Six Paths. Each is ancient, vast, and sapient. They cannot be permanently destroyed.
**Jinchuriki** are humans with a Bijuu sealed within them. They suffer emotional isolation, social rejection, and physical instability. Those who achieve Bijuu cooperation access extraordinary power.
**Cloak Stages:** (1) Partial cloak: chakra shroud, enhanced senses. (2) Full cloak with tails: massive power, suppressed thought. (3) Version 2: near-Bijuu form, corrosive, loss of autonomy. (4) Full Bijuu transformation: the beast acts, host suppressed. (5) Cooperative Bijuu Mode: full power without loss of self; rarest and strongest.
**The Nine Bijuu:** Shukaku (1-Tail, Suna/Gaara), Matatabi (2-Tails, Kumo/Yugito), Isobu (3-Tails, Kiri/Yagura), Son Goku (4-Tails, Iwa/Roshi), Kokuo (5-Tails, Iwa/Han), Saiken (6-Tails, Kiri/Utakata), Chomei (7-Tails, Takigakure/Fu), Gyuki (8-Tails, Kumo/Killer B), Kurama (9-Tails, Konoha/Naruto).

## 6. ECONOMY
### ECONOMY — Ryo and the Mission System
**Currency:** Ryo (R). No subdivisions in common use.
**Mission Ranks and Payout:** D-Rank (5-50 R, civilian-safe), C-Rank (80-200 R, possible enemy contact), B-Rank (250-750 R, near-certain combat), A-Rank (750-3,000 R, Jonin-level threats), S-Rank (3,000 R+ or non-monetary, Kage-level danger).
**Risk Premiums:** +25% urgent; +25% secrecy; +50% beyond patrol range; +50% organized enemy; +100%+ for Kage-tier or political blowback. S-rank missions often pay in intel, pardons, political access, or rare scrolls.
**Cost Anchors:** Ramen bowl 3-8 R; inn room 5-15 R/night; kunai set 30-80 R; explosive tags x10 150-300 R; flak jacket 2,000-5,000 R; village apartment 5,000-10,000 R; hospital treatment 200-1,000 R.
**Income Anchors:** Civilian laborer 300-600 R/year; active Genin 500-1,500 R/year; Chunin 2,000-6,000 R/year; Jonin 8,000-20,000 R/year; ANBU 12,000-30,000 R/year (classified).

## 7. EVENTS
### EVENT — The Founding of the Hidden Villages
Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha ended the clan wars by founding Konohagakure, the first hidden village. This became the template: the five great nations each established their own hidden village, creating the modern shinobi system. Hashirama distributed the Bijuu to other villages as diplomatic gestures. Madara eventually defected, leading to their legendary battle at the Valley of the End.

### EVENT — The Uchiha Massacre
Itachi Uchiha killed his entire clan in a single night under covert orders from Danzo (with Hiruzen's reluctant approval) to prevent an Uchiha-led coup that would have sparked civil war. The village covered it up. Sasuke was spared and driven toward revenge. Itachi joined the Akatsuki as a mole. The truth of this event is one of the most politically explosive secrets in the shinobi world.

### EVENT — The Nine-Tails Attack on Konoha
Obito Uchiha (masked) extracted Kurama from Kushina Uzumaki and unleashed the Nine-Tails on Konohagakure. The Fourth Hokage Minato Namikaze and Kushina died sealing Kurama into their newborn son Naruto. Hiruzen Sarutobi resumed Hokage duties. The attack anniversary is observed differently by everyone: a memorial for some, a day of fear for others, and for Naruto, a complicated absence.

### EVENT — Pain's Destruction of Konoha
Nagato (controlling the Six Paths of Pain) obliterated Konohagakure with a single Shinra Tensei to demonstrate the reality of suffering. Naruto returned, defeated Pain, and confronted Nagato directly. Through dialogue, Nagato chose to revive the dead of Konoha at the cost of his own life. This event fundamentally changed Naruto's standing from village pariah to hero.

### EVENT — The Fourth Shinobi World War
The Allied Shinobi Forces (all five great villages united for the first time) fought against Obito's army of White Zetsu and reanimated legendary shinobi. Escalated through Obito becoming the Ten-Tails Jinchuriki, Madara's revival, and Kaguya Otsutsuki's manifestation. Ended with Naruto and Sasuke's final battle at the Valley of the End. Peace achieved; Naruto eventually becomes Seventh Hokage.

### EVENT — The Chunin Exams (Konoha)
A multi-village tournament to evaluate Genin for Chunin promotion. Also functions as a geopolitical soft-power demonstration. The Konoha exams Naruto participates in are invaded by Suna and Sound forces (Orochimaru's plan), killing the Third Hokage and fundamentally changing inter-village relations.

## 8. TIMELINE REFERENCE

### NOTATION — Anchor & Year System
Year 0 = Naruto Uzumaki's birth (October 10). All character ages and event dates are calculated from this anchor. Ages listed are approximate and may vary by a few months depending on birth month vs. event month. "Year -X" = X years before Naruto's birth. "Year X" = X years after.

### PRE-SERIES TIMELINE

#### Warring States / Founding (~Year -80)
The shinobi world before the hidden village system: endless inter-clan warfare. The Senju and Uchiha are the dominant clans, their conflict the axis of every proxy war. Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha, childhood friends turned rivals, broker a peace between their clans and found Konohagakure — the first hidden village, template for all others. The five great nations each establish their own village using the same model. Hashirama distributes captured Bijuu to other villages as diplomatic gestures, creating the Jinchuriki system. Madara eventually grows disillusioned and defects, leading to their legendary confrontation at the Valley of the End. Hashirama wins; Madara is believed dead but survives in secret, orchestrating events across the next century.

#### 1st Shinobi World War (~Year -38 to -35)
The first large-scale nation-state conflict under the hidden village system. Tobirama Senju (Second Hokage) dies during the war as a distraction to allow his team's escape; he appoints Hiruzen Sarutobi as Third Hokage on the battlefield (age ~21). Danzo Shimura, Homura Mitokado, and Koharu Utatane — Hiruzen's contemporaries and fellow students of Tobirama — become village elders. Root's ideological roots trace to this era: the belief that the village requires a shadow arm willing to act where the Hokage cannot.

#### 2nd Shinobi World War (~Year -20 to -16)
The war in which the three Legendary Sannin earn their name. Jiraiya, Tsunade, and Orochimaru — Hiruzen's students — survive a brutal battle against Hanzo of the Great Salamander (Amegakure's supreme shinobi) and are the only ones Hanzo deems worthy of the title "Sannin." Jiraiya remains in Amegakure and trains three orphaned children: Nagato, Yahiko, and Konan. Nawaki (Tsunade's younger brother) and Dan Kato (her lover) both die in the war; Tsunade develops hemophobia (deep fear of blood) and abandons the battlefield. Sakumo Hatake (Kakashi's father, "White Fang of Konoha") completes a mission at the cost of his teammates and is shunned — he dies by suicide; Kakashi is a young child witnessing this.

#### 3rd Shinobi World War (~Year -5 to -1)
The war that defines the generation of Kakashi, Obito, Rin, Minato, and young Itachi. Minato Namikaze earns the title "Yellow Flash of the Leaf" after the Flying Thunder God technique allows him to kill an entire Iwa strike force singlehandedly — the wound Iwagakure never heals. Obito Uchiha "dies" at the Kannabi Bridge (age ~13, Year -1) when a falling boulder crushes half his body. Before losing consciousness, he gives Kakashi his Sharingan. Obito is found and preserved by Madara Uchiha, who replaces his destroyed body parts with Hashirama cells and begins shaping him ideologically. Itachi Uchiha (age ~4) witnesses battlefield carnage during this war — a formative trauma that accelerates his pacifist convictions. The war concludes. Minato becomes the Fourth Hokage.

#### Year 0 — Nine-Tails Attack on Konoha (October 10)
Naruto Uzumaki is born to Minato Namikaze and Kushina Uzumaki. During childbirth, Obito (still masked, identity unknown) extracts Kurama the Nine-Tails from Kushina during her momentarily weakened seal, takes control of Kurama, and unleashes it on Konoha. Minato defeats Obito in direct combat, then uses the Dead Demon Consuming Seal to split Kurama's chakra and seal the Yang half within infant Naruto, dying in the process. Kushina uses the final moments of her life to complete the seal. Both parents die on Naruto's birth night. Hiruzen Sarutobi resumes Hokage duties. Sasuke Uchiha is born the same year (July — a few months older than Naruto).

#### Years 0–7 Post-Attack: Uchiha Marginalization
Naruto grows up orphaned and feared, the village associating him with the monster that killed their people. The Uchiha clan, already viewed with suspicion, are politically sidelined. They are moved to the edge of the village and excluded from central governance. Resentment builds toward a coup. Danzo operates in the background, accelerating the distrust between the clan and the Hokage's administration. Shisui Uchiha, the clan's most powerful member and a genuine peace advocate, attempts to use his Genjutsu (Kotoamatsukami) to ease tensions — Danzo steals his eye and drives him to suicide. Shisui's death removes the last realistic alternative.

#### Years 3–4 — Hyuga Incident
A Kumogakure ambassador secretly attempts to kidnap Hinata Hyuga (age 3–4), daughter of the Hyuga clan head Hiashi, to steal the Byakugan. Hiashi kills the ambassador. Kumo demands reparations under a kidnapping-as-diplomatic-incident pretext; the village cannot afford open war. Hizashi Hyuga, Hiashi's twin brother and Neji's father, volunteers to die in Hiashi's place as the "guilty party." This sacrifice haunts Neji for years and drives his fatalism about destiny. The core wound of the Hyuga arc.

#### Years 7–8 — Uchiha Massacre
Sasuke is 7–8. Naruto is 7–8. Itachi is 13. Danzo orders Itachi to eliminate the Uchiha clan before the coup can be launched; Hiruzen reluctantly approves as the only alternative to civil war. Obito assists Itachi in neutralizing barriers and clan elders. In a single night, Itachi kills every Uchiha except Sasuke, who he spares and deliberately pushes toward hatred and revenge as a survival mechanism — and because he cannot bear to kill him. Itachi joins Akatsuki under his secret Konoha mole arrangement. The Uchiha district is sealed. The village officially frames the event as Itachi acting alone out of ambition. This lie shapes the next fifteen years of Sasuke's life.

### PART I (Year 12–13)

**Year 12:** Naruto, Sasuke, and Sakura graduate the Academy (all age 12). Team 7 forms under Kakashi Hatake (~age 26). First major mission: the Land of Waves arc (C-rank that becomes A-rank), introducing Zabuza and Haku; the team's first contact with death and genuine stakes. Shortly after: the Chunin Exams. Orochimaru marks Sasuke with a Curse Mark during the exam's Forest of Death phase. Suna and Sound forces invade Konoha during the final exam. Orochimaru kills the Third Hokage Hiruzen (age ~69) with the Dead Demon Consuming Seal. Naruto defeats Gaara. Tsunade is persuaded out of self-imposed exile and becomes the Fifth Hokage.

**Year 13:** Itachi and Kisame return to Konoha, triggering Sasuke's obsession with his brother's power. Sasuke defects to Orochimaru, drawn by the promise of power to achieve his revenge. Naruto and Sasuke's fight at the Valley of the End ends in a draw; Sasuke crosses the border. Naruto, unable to bring Sasuke back alone, leaves Konoha with Jiraiya to train — beginning the two-and-a-half-year timeskip.

---

### TIME SKIP (Year 13–15.5)

Naruto trains with Jiraiya across two and a half years: deepening Sage Mode groundwork, mastering advanced Rasengan variants, and hardening emotionally. Sasuke trains under Orochimaru; Kabuto Yakushi serves as Orochimaru's lieutenant. Sakura trains directly under Tsunade, achieving mastery of medical ninjutsu and the Yin Seal. Gaara becomes the Fifth Kazekage — the youngest in history. Akatsuki continues systematic Jinchuriki capture across the nations. The global political situation deteriorates quietly.

---

### PART II / SHIPPUDEN (Year 15.5–17)

**Year 15.5–16:** Naruto returns to Konoha (age ~15–16). The Gaara Rescue arc: Akatsuki extracts Shukaku from Gaara, killing him; Chiyo sacrifices herself to revive him with a Transfer Jutsu. Sasuke kills Orochimaru before Orochimaru can complete his body-theft. Sasuke forms Taka (with Suigetsu, Karin, Jugo) and pursues Itachi. Sai is introduced as Naruto's temporary teammate; a ROOT operative, his humanity is gradually restored.

**Year 16 — Dense with critical events:**
- Asuma Sarutobi is killed by Hidan (Akatsuki); Shikamaru leads the revenge mission and kills Hidan.
- Naruto develops the Wind Release: Rasenshuriken, achieving a nature transformation beyond his rank.
- Jiraiya infiltrates Amegakure on a solo intelligence mission. He fights Pain and transmits critical intel before dying — age ~54. His death is the emotional turning point of Shippuden.
- Naruto enters Sage Mode training at Mount Myoboku.
- Pain destroys Konoha in a single Shinra Tensei. Naruto defeats Pain's Six Paths, then confronts Nagato directly. Through dialogue, Nagato revives Konoha's dead at the cost of his own life. Naruto is declared a hero.
- Danzo briefly maneuvers himself into the Hokage seat during the chaos; it does not last.
- The Five Kage Summit is convened. Sasuke crashes it, targeting Danzo. Tobi (Obito) declares war on all five nations.
- Kakashi is named Sixth Hokage.

**Year 16–17 — Fourth Shinobi World War:**
The Allied Shinobi Forces (all five great villages unified under the Fourth Raikage as Supreme Commander, with Shikamaru's father Shikaku as chief strategist) mobilize against Obito's White Zetsu army and Kabuto's Edo Tensei army of reanimated legends. The war escalates through several phases: Obito becomes the Ten-Tails Jinchuriki; the reanimated Madara Uchiha is freed from Kabuto's control and overwhelms the battlefield; Madara becomes the Ten-Tails Jinchuriki himself; Kaguya Otsutsuki is manifested through Black Zetsu's manipulation. Naruto, through battle, finally befriends Kurama and accesses full Bijuu Cooperative Mode. Naruto and Sasuke each receive the powers of the Sage of Six Paths (Hagoromo's chakra). Kaguya is sealed. Sasuke then reveals his intent to start a "revolution" — Naruto and Sasuke fight their final battle at the Valley of the End. Both lose an arm (age 16–17). Neither yields. Sasuke concedes, surrenders, and accepts imprisonment; later begins a life of atonement and wandering.

---

### POST-WAR (Year 17+)

**Year 17+:** Kakashi serves as Sixth Hokage. Sasuke accepts a reduced sentence and begins wandering missions atonement-style. Orochimaru is pardoned under surveillance — too useful to imprison, too dangerous to ignore. The nine Bijuu are freed from their collective sealing and scatter — Kurama remains with Naruto by choice. Reconstruction of Konoha and the other war-damaged nations begins.

**~Year 19–20:** Naruto marries Hinata Hyuga. Sasuke marries Sakura Haruno. Boruto Uzumaki is born (Naruto and Hinata's son). Sarada Uchiha is born (Sasuke and Sakura's daughter, approximately same year as Boruto).

**~Year 27–28 (Boruto Era):** Naruto Uzumaki becomes the Seventh Hokage. Boruto and Sarada are roughly 10–12. The political landscape has stabilized into a tenuous peace, complicated by emerging threats from outside the shinobi system's historical scope.
