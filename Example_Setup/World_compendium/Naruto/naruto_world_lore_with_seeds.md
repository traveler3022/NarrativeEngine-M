## GM RULES [WL-GM-RULES]

### Power Rank Logic [WL-GM-POWER-LOGIC]
A 2+ rank gap dominates in direct combat unless ambush, terrain, numbers, preparation, elemental counters, or specific technique matchups are explicitly in play. Chūnin vs. Genin is decisive; Jōnin vs. Chūnin is overwhelming unless conditions tilt it. Kage-level and above operate on a different axis — collateral damage, area denial, and strategic deterrence matter as much as individual combat.

Ranks represent **sustained peak performance**, not a single lucky strike. A Genin can land a hit on a Jōnin via trap or surprise; they cannot **win** the engagement.

### Mission Rank & Payout [WL-GM-MISSION-RANKS]
| Mission Rank | Threat Level | Typical Payout |

## THE WORLD [WL-WORLD]

### Overview [WL-WORLD-OVERVIEW]
The shinobi world consists of **five major nations** each anchored by a **hidden village (Gakure)**, smaller countries without hidden villages, and ungoverned territory. Power is organized around the hidden villages as military contractors to their host daimyō. The world has experienced multiple Shinobi World Wars — each reshaping borders, bloodlines, and alliances. Between wars, tensions are managed through the **Five Kage Summit** (formalized late in canon) and bilateral treaties, most of which are fragile.

The world is **pre-industrial but chakra-advanced**: no firearms, but explosive tags, barrier jutsu, and summoning contracts fill equivalent military niches. Communication is by hawk messenger, runner, or chakra-relay seal. Travel is on foot, horse, or summon — experienced shinobi can cover enormous ground quickly via body-flicker and speed enhancement.

### The Five Great Nations [WL-WORLD-FIVE-NATIONS]
| Nation | Hidden Village | Kage Title | Primary Terrain | Dominant Exports |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Land of Fire | Konohagakure (Konoha) | Hokage | Forest, plains | Shinobi services, civilian goods |
| Land of Lightning | Kumogakure (Kumo) | Raikage | Mountains, storm coasts | Chakra-metal, military contracts |
| Land of Earth | Iwagakure (Iwa) | Tsuchikage | Plateau, canyon, desert | Earth-release labor, stone goods |
| Land of Water | Kirigakure (Kiri) | Mizukage | Mist islands, coast | Naval trade, Kekkei Genkai services |
| Land of Wind | Sunagakure (Suna) | Kazekage | Desert, arid plains | Puppet-tech, desert trade routes |

### Minor Nations [WL-WORLD-MINOR-NATIONS]
Dozens of smaller countries exist between the Great Five. Most lack hidden villages and hire shinobi from the nearest major village. Notable examples: **Land of Waves** (coastal, oppressed; bridge economy), **Land of Spring/Snow** (isolated; film industry front, then genuine governance), **Land of Iron** (samurai-only; officially neutral in shinobi politics), **Land of Grass**, **Land of Rain** (chronic war buffer zone), **Land of Sound** (Orochimaru's puppet state), **Land of Hot Water**, **Land of Birds**, and many others. These nations are perpetual mission environments — politically unstable, underprotected, and full of civilian entanglement.

### Ungoverned Territory [WL-WORLD-UNGOVERNED]
Large stretches of terrain exist outside any nation's effective control: deep forests, mountain wastes, swamp networks, island chains. These zones are where missing-nin establish bases, where forbidden experiments occur, and where ancient ruins and sealed dangers wait. No nation patrols them reliably. They are lawless by default and dangerous at all rank tiers depending on what has moved in.

## KONOHA — VILLAGE OF THE HIDDEN LEAF [WL-KONOHA]

### Overview [WL-KONOHA-OVERVIEW]
**Konohagakure no Satō** (Village Hidden in the Leaves) is the hidden village of the Land of Fire and the most politically central village of the era. Founded by Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha at the end of the clan wars, it became the template for the hidden village system itself. It is the most populous major hidden village and commands enormous diplomatic soft power through its reputation, its Hokage lineage, and its consistent role as mediating party in inter-village disputes.

Konoha's culture emphasizes the **Will of Fire** — a philosophy of protecting the village as family, passing strength and love to the next generation. In practice this philosophy also serves as political glue binding otherwise diverse and historically rival clans.

**Government:** The Hokage is head of the military and administrative arm. The **Village Elder Council** (including Homura Mitokado and Koharu Utatane in most eras) advises the Hokage and wields significant structural power. The **Shinobi Council** (clan heads) has informal but powerful sway over policy. The civilian side is governed by the **Land of Fire Daimyō** from the Capital, but in practice Konoha's Hokage handles most internal security and treaty matters.

**ANBU Black Ops** [WL-KONOHA-ANBU]: Konoha's covert arm. Masked, stripped of identity records, answerable directly to the Hokage (or, corruptly, to Root under Danzō). Handles assassinations, extractions, counter-espionage, and matters too sensitive for standard mission ranks. ANBU members are elite Jōnin-floor or above. Service is anonymous — family and friends are not officially informed.

**ROOT / Foundation** [WL-KONOHA-ROOT]: Danzō Shimura's parallel black ops organization. Officially disbanded multiple times; functionally persistent. Operatives are conditioned from childhood to suppress emotion and individuality. They operate without Hokage oversight. Root agents are hard to identify — they often appear as regular Konoha shinobi until activated. Root is a source of deep political corruption and shadow wars within the village. [ERA-SPECIFIC: Active primarily from Tobirama era through late Shippuden; officially dissolved post-Pain Arc]

### Konoha Districts & Landmarks [WL-KONOHA-LOCATIONS]
- **Hokage's Tower / Administration Building:** Central political nerve. Mission assignment office on lower floors. Hokage's office at apex. Council chambers adjacent.
- **Konoha Hospital:** Primary medical facility. Also research arm for medical ninjutsu. Significant political weight — the chief medical officer has access to classified casualty and health data.
- **The Academy (Ninja Academy):** Where civilian children train to become Genin. 4–6 year standard program; prodigies accelerate. Iruka Umino's era is the most well-documented.
- **Konoha Shinobi Library:** Contains non-classified jutsu scrolls, mission records, historical texts. Restricted sections exist. ANBU records are not kept here.
- **The Uchiha District:** [ERA-SPECIFIC: Populated pre-Massacre; abandoned post-Massacre] Large walled compound in eastern Konoha. Post-Massacre it is sealed and treated as a political wound.
- **Nara Forest Preserve:** Deer herd; medicinal herbs; also conceals the Nara clan's most sensitive scroll caches.
- **Training Grounds (Areas 1–44+):** Designated sparring and mission-prep areas. Area 44 = "Forest of Death" — exam terrain, genuinely lethal fauna and flora.
- **Ichiraku Ramen:** Civilian noodle stall; significant as Naruto Uzumaki's cultural home base. Teuchi and Ayame are trusted civilian allies.
- **The Monument / Hokage Rock:** Carved faces of all serving Hokage. New face added upon assumption of title. Cultural symbol of village continuity.

### Konoha Clans [WL-KONOHA-CLANS]
Each major clan controls a compound, has clan-specific jutsu (some kekkei genkai), internal politics, and a historical relationship with the village that is sometimes cooperative and sometimes adversarial.

**Nara Clan [WL-CLAN-NARA]:** Shadow manipulation (Shadow Possession, Shadow Stitching, Shadow Imitation). High intelligence tradition — the Nara are the village's strategic thinkers and analysts. Tend toward low-energy presentation hiding exceptional competence. Allied historically with Akimichi and Yamanaka (the "Ino-Shika-Chō" formation). Maintain a medical herb forest and an encyclopedic jutsu-theory library.

**Akimichi Clan [WL-CLAN-AKIMICHI]:** Caloric expansion — convert food energy to chakra for physical enlargement and multi-size techniques. Large builds; warm personalities; fierce in sustained combat. Part of the Ino-Shika-Chō alliance. High loyalty to clan and village. Clan pills (Spinach Pill, Curry Pill, Chilli Pill) are emergency combat drugs that burn life force for a massive power surge — last resort only.

**Yamanaka Clan [WL-CLAN-YAMANAKA]:** Mind-body techniques — Mind Transfer, Mind Destruction, telepathy variants. Run a flower shop as civilian cover and income. The clan is Konoha's primary intelligence analysis and interrogation resource. Deeply embedded in the chakra-communication network during wartime.

**Hyūga Clan [WL-CLAN-HYUGA]:** Byakugan (360° near-infinite range vision, chakra pathway sight) + Gentle Fist style (strikes chakra points directly, damaging internal organs and disrupting flow). The largest clan in Konoha by membership. Internally divided: **Main Branch** (holds the Byakugan and clan leadership) vs. **Branch House** (sealed with the Caged Bird Seal — triggers death-by-curse-mark if activated, can destroy the Byakugan upon death). This system is a source of constant internal tension and moral horror. The clan's military value is enormous; its internal politics are a campaign in themselves.

**Uchiha Clan [WL-CLAN-UCHIHA]** [ERA-SPECIFIC: Active in full pre-Massacre; effectively extinct post-Massacre except for Sasuke and later Itachi's death]: Fire Release affinity; Sharingan (tracks movement, copies jutsu, induces genjutsu); Mangekyō Sharingan (unique per user; immensely powerful; blinds with overuse); Eternal Mangekyō (transplant from kin, restores sight and power). The Uchiha were Konoha's co-founders and historically its police force. Relations with village leadership were never fully warm. The massacre and its cover-up are a festering secret.

**Inuzuka Clan [WL-CLAN-INUZUKA]:** Combat partnership with ninken (ninja dogs raised together with their partner from birth). Bestial fighting style; enhanced senses (smell especially); pack tactics. The clan bond between shinobi and dog is the center of identity — attacking one's ninken is a serious provocation.

**Aburame Clan [WL-CLAN-ABURAME]:** Insect-based jutsu — at birth, a chakra insect colony is introduced to the child's body and uses it as a hive. In return the clan can direct the insects as weapons, sensors, and chakra-draining tools. Clan members are deliberately understated, quiet, and analytical. Their insect colonies are irreplaceable — loss of the hive is catastrophic.

**Senju Clan [WL-CLAN-SENJU]** [ERA-SPECIFIC: Prominent in founding era; effectively sparse by Naruto's time]: Wood Release (only Hashirama and Yamato/Tenzō via Hashirama cell transplant). Enormous chakra reserves; life-giving jutsu; village founders. By Naruto's era the clan has no significant active presence; its legacy is the Hokage system itself.

**Sarutobi Clan [WL-CLAN-SARUTOBI]:** Hiruzen's clan; fire and staff techniques; strong loyalty tradition. Multiple shinobi per generation at high rank. Asuma Sarutobi is the most prominent member in Naruto's era. The clan is deeply integrated into village structure.

## KUMOGAKURE — VILLAGE OF THE HIDDEN CLOUD [WL-KUMO]

### Overview [WL-KUMO-OVERVIEW]
**Kumogakure no Satō** (Village Hidden in the Clouds) is the hidden village of the Land of Lightning, built high in mountain peaks above the cloud line. It is the second most powerful village in most eras and the most militaristic in culture. Kumo prizes strength, directness, and proven combat achievement. Political power tracks military rank more closely than in other villages.

Kumo has a troubled historical relationship with other nations, particularly regarding the kidnapping of Kekkei Genkai bloodlines from other villages to bolster their own (most notably the attempted kidnapping of Hinata Hyūga). This is an open secret that shapes diplomatic relations.

**Jinchūriki Program:** Kumo has historically maintained the Two-Tails (Matatabi) and Eight-Tails (Gyūki) within its ranks. Killer B is the Eight-Tails Jinchūriki and the most famous — and rarest — case of a Jinchūriki with a fully cooperative relationship with their bijū.

**Government:** The Raikage leads with a direct, command-and-control style. The office has less council friction than Konoha — the Raikage's word carries more immediate military authority. A is the most well-documented Raikage in the main Naruto era.

### Kumo Landmarks [WL-KUMO-LOCATIONS]
- **Raikage's Office:** Summit of the central peak. Military command hub.
- **Cloud Arena:** High-altitude combat training and ranked sparring arena. Public duels are culturally significant.
- **Lightning Forge Quarters:** Where chakra-metal tools and weapons are produced. Kumo's most valuable export.
- **The Waterfall of Truth:** A hidden site where shinobi confront their inner selves (shadow self). Used as a training ordeal for advanced practitioners. [ERA-SPECIFIC: Used by Naruto Shippuden era]
- **B's Dome / Training Ground Annex:** Killer B's personal training area; also an informal cultural hub — music, rap, bōjutsu practice.

### Notable Kumo Techniques & Traditions [WL-KUMO-TECHNIQUES]
- **Lightning Release Armor:** Raikage A's signature — full-body lightning chakra coating that enhances speed and strength to superhuman levels at the cost of high chakra consumption. Destroys a limb that loses contact.
- **Black Lightning:** Darui's rare variant. Different frequency than standard lightning chakra; bypasses some standard resistances.
- **Lariat:** Cooperative high-speed clothesline technique between A and Killer B; decapitation-level force.
- **Seven Swords tradition (historically):** While Kiri holds the Seven Swordsmen, Kumo has its own sword-tradition; several Kumo shinobi in pre-canon were famous dual-wielders.
- **Cloud Sealing / Chakra Absorption:** Samehada (held by Kisame, formerly Kiri) can absorb chakra; Kumo has developed some counter-sealing traditions as a result of this threat.

## IWAGAKURE — VILLAGE OF THE HIDDEN STONE [WL-IWA]

### Overview [WL-IWA-OVERVIEW]
**Iwagakure no Satō** (Village Hidden in the Stone) is the hidden village of the Land of Earth, built into and upon a massive rocky plateau with natural fortress properties. Iwa values endurance, tradition, and self-sufficiency. The clan system is strong; bloodline elders hold significant power. Iwa has the longest institutional memory of inter-village grievances, particularly toward Konoha — the Yellow Flash (Minato) killed an entire Iwa strike force singlehandedly during the Third Shinobi World War, a wound that never fully healed.

**Government:** The Tsuchikage leads, with significant council weight from elder advisors. Ōnoki (Third Tsuchikage) is the most prominent figure in the main Naruto era — ancient, stubborn, enormous power in a tiny body (Particle Release). Kurotsuchi is the most visible next-generation candidate.

**Dust Release (Jinton) [WL-IWA-JINTON]:** The most destructive Kekkei Tōta (three-element combination) in the series. Disintegrates matter at the molecular level within a geometric containment jutsu. Ōnoki is its primary known user. It requires Wind + Earth + Fire nature simultaneously and is extraordinarily rare.

### Iwa Landmarks [WL-IWA-LOCATIONS]
- **Tsuchikage's Office / Rock Spire:** Atop the plateau. Visually imposing; naturally defensible.
- **Stone Quarry Districts:** Economic backbone; also source of construction chakra-workers for hire.
- **Explosion Corps Training Grounds:** Iwagakure has a dedicated unit specializing in explosive clay and detonation techniques.

## KIRIGAKURE — VILLAGE OF THE HIDDEN MIST [WL-KIRI]

### Overview [WL-KIRI-OVERVIEW]
**Kirigakure no Satō** (Village Hidden in the Mist) is the hidden village of the Land of Water, spread across a mist-covered island archipelago. Kiri has the most turbulent internal history of the five great villages — the era known as the **"Bloody Mist"** [ERA-SPECIFIC: primarily under the Fourth Mizukage Yagura, who was under Obito's control] involved systematic extermination of Academy graduates in battle-to-the-death graduation exams, and a campaign of persecution against Kekkei Genkai bloodline users. The trauma of this era shapes every Kiri NPC who lived through it.

Post-Yagura, under **Mei Terumī (Fifth Mizukage)**, Kiri undertakes genuine political and cultural reform — Kekkei Genkai users are welcomed, the graduation ritual is abolished, and diplomatic outreach replaces isolation. This reform is incomplete and contested internally.

**Government:** The Mizukage's office has more independent military authority than most — island geography and the mist itself make Kiri a natural fortress that can operate in relative isolation. Council influence is present but the Mizukage has historically ruled by force of personality.

### Seven Swordsmen of the Mist [WL-KIRI-SEVEN-SWORDS]
Kiri's elite named squad. The title "Seven Swordsmen" refers to the group, not always seven simultaneous members. The swords are legendary weapons with unique properties, passed between worthy successors. Members are S-rank or Jōnin-floor minimum.

| **Kubikiribōchō** (Decapitating Carving Knife) | Regenerates from absorbed blood iron |
| **Kabutowari** (Helmet Splitter) | Hammer + axe; breaks any defense |
| **Nuibari** (Sewing Needle) | Long wire needle; sews targets together |
| **Shibuki** (Splash) | Blade with explosive tag reservoir |
| **Kiba** (Fangs) | Twin blades; lightning-channeling pair |

[ERA-SPECIFIC: Samehada is with Kisame Hoshigaki / Akatsuki during most of Naruto's era. Kubikiribōchō is recovered by Suigetsu Hōzuki in Shippuden.]

### Kiri Landmarks [WL-KIRI-LOCATIONS]
- **Mizukage's Building:** Central island, shrouded in permanent mist. Politically fortified.
- **Hidden Blade Training Halls:** Sword traditions are cultural cornerstones; shinobi without blade training are unusual.
- **The Bloody Mist Memorial (informal):** No official monument — the dead of that era are not officially commemorated, a political wound.
- **Hōzuki Castle (Blood Prison):** Remote maximum-security prison island; operated semi-independently; uses a chakra-suppression barrier technology. [ERA-SPECIFIC: Relevant in Naruto Shippuden era]

## SUNAGAKURE — VILLAGE OF THE HIDDEN SAND [WL-SUNA]

### Overview [WL-SUNA-OVERVIEW]
**Sunagakure no Satō** (Village Hidden in the Sand) is the hidden village of the Land of Wind, built within a sheltered desert valley. Suna is economically stressed — the Land of Wind Daimyō has repeatedly cut village funding and outsourced missions to Konoha, forcing Suna into an uncomfortable dependency. This financial pressure drives political decisions including the alliance with Orochimaru and the use of Gaara as a weapon.

Suna's cultural identity emphasizes endurance, self-reliance, and puppet mastery as a martial art. The **Puppet Corps** is Suna's most distinctive military arm — shinobi who control mechanical puppets via chakra threads, allowing ranged combat and elaborate trap-warfare without personal risk. Mastery of puppetry is a prestige tradition; grandmasters like Chiyo are cultural heroes.

**Government:** The Kazekage commands with strong daimyō influence over resource allocation — more than any other village, Suna's leadership must negotiate with a daimyō who actively undermines them. The Fourth Kazekage (Rasa) made the catastrophic decision to use Gaara as a failed weapon. The Fifth Kazekage is Gaara himself — the youngest Kage in history at time of appointment.

### Suna Landmarks [WL-SUNA-LOCATIONS]
- **Kazekage's Tower:** Central desert citadel. Conference room for external diplomacy.
- **Puppet Workshop Quarters:** Where artisan-shinobi build and maintain combat puppets. Trade secret ecosystem.
- **Desert Training Ranges:** Enormous flat desert expanses used for large-scale jutsu testing; the environment itself is a training tool.
- **The Iron Sand Reserve:** Secured site where the Third Kazekage's Iron Sand (magnetic Kekkei Genkai residue) is stored as a military asset.

### Suna Techniques [WL-SUNA-TECHNIQUES]
- **Puppet Jutsu (Kugutsu no Jutsu):** Chakra threads control mechanical puppets. Puppets can contain weapons, poisons, barriers, and other mechanisms. A master can operate many simultaneously. Counter: sever the threads, interfere with hand signs, close the distance faster than the puppet can react.
- **Sand Manipulation (Gaara specific):** Gaara's absolute defense and sand attacks are initially fueled by Shukaku's chakra acting reflexively; post-Shukaku removal he develops genuine sand control through his own chakra.
- **Poison Techniques:** Chiyo and other Suna medic-shinobi specialize in potent poisons delivered via puppet mechanisms.

## LAND OF IRON [WL-LAND-OF-IRON]

### Overview [WL-LAND-OF-IRON-OVERVIEW]
The Land of Iron is a permanently snow-covered nation governed entirely by samurai. It maintains strict neutrality in shinobi political conflicts and has historically been used as neutral ground for diplomatic summits (the Five Kage Summit was held here). Samurai here achieve combat capability through trained chakra flow into blades — not ninjutsu, not genjutsu, but pure kenjutsu tradition. Top samurai reach Jōnin-equivalent capability; their leader, **Mifune**, is Kage-tier.

The Land of Iron does not take missions from shinobi villages. It is self-funded through resource extraction and tolls on traders crossing its mountain passes. It operates its own internal law and will not extradite to shinobi villages. Missing-nin who flee here enter a legal grey zone.

## MISSING-NIN & THE BINGO BOOK [WL-MISSING-NIN]

### Overview [WL-MISSING-NIN-OVERVIEW]
A **missing-nin (nukenin)** is a shinobi who has deserted their village. Desertion is treated as treason in all major villages. Missing-nin are listed in the **Bingo Book** — classified hunter-nin reference books distributed to authorized shinobi containing information on the missing-nin's techniques, danger level, and bounty. The Bingo Book is not public; civilians do not have access.

**Hunter-nin** are specialized Konoha (and Kiri especially) operatives tasked with tracking and killing missing-nin — and destroying the body to prevent technique recovery by enemies. This is why Kiri hunter-nin traditionally burn or dissolve corpses on-site.

**Danger Classification:**
- Low-priority: Genin/Chūnin; small bounty; recover or kill.
- Mid-priority: Jōnin-level; significant bounty; hunter-nin squads deployed.
- High-priority: Named individual; Kage-level bounty; village-authorized S-rank mission.
- "Flee on sight": Instruction printed in the Bingo Book for opponents too dangerous to engage — run and report, do not engage.

### The Akatsuki [WL-MISSING-NIN-AKATSUKI]
The Akatsuki is the most significant missing-nin organization in the main Naruto era. At peak it consists of ten S-rank missing-nin, each wearing a black robe with red clouds and an Akatsuki ring (ten rings total, each unique). Their goal shifts across the timeline — originally a mercenary organization, then Nagato/Pain's ideological peace-through-suffering movement, then Obito/Madara's Eye of the Moon Plan.

**Akatsuki operational pattern:** S-rank pairs. Each pair takes contracts and pursues the Jinchūriki capture mission in parallel. Members operate across all five nations simultaneously. The organization is a network, not a hierarchy in traditional terms — members have individual agendas that roughly align.

## JINCHŪRIKI & THE TAILED BEASTS [WL-JINCHURIKI]

### Overview [WL-JINCHURIKI-OVERVIEW]
**Tailed Beasts (Bijū)** are living masses of chakra originating from the Ten-Tails (Juubi). The Sage of Six Paths distributed the Ten-Tails' chakra into nine individual entities. They are ancient, vast, and sapient — though their ability to communicate with their Jinchūriki varies. Each has a number of tails corresponding to their number (One-Tails = Shukaku; Nine-Tails = Kurama). They cannot be permanently destroyed in the conventional sense — their chakra disperses and eventually reconcentrates.

**Jinchūriki** are humans who have had a Bijū sealed within them. The sealing is a political act — it creates a living weapon and a deterrent. Jinchūriki who cannot control their Bijū are dangerous to their own villages. Those who achieve cooperation with their Bijū access extraordinary power. Those who fail the psychological challenge of hosting a Bijū often suffer:
- Emotional isolation (the Bijū's malice bleeds through)
- Social rejection (villagers fear them)
- Physical instability (Bijū chakra corrodes the seal and the body in cloak mode)

**Bijū Cloak Stages:**
1. **Partial cloak:** Chakra shroud, enhanced senses, healing. Bijū influence present but manageable.
2. **Full cloak (tails manifesting):** Massive destructive power; rational thought increasingly suppressed.
3. **Version 2 / Near-full transformation:** Near-Bijū form; corrosive to surroundings; Jinchūriki loses autonomy.
4. **Full Bijū transformation:** The Bijū itself acts. Jinchūriki is suppressed. Kage-level threat minimum.

### Scale Overview [WL-POWER-SCALE]
| Rank | Description | Reference Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Civilian** | No chakra training. Vulnerable to any trained shinobi. | Teuchi (Ichiraku); most villagers |
| **Genin** | Basic Academy graduation. Knows fundamental three jutsu. Limited control, small chakra pool. | Fresh Naruto (pre-Part 1 development); most fodder shinobi |
| **Chūnin** | Demonstrated tactical judgment and field competence. Can lead small teams. | Iruka; Shikamaru (early); most named mid-tier characters |
| **Tokubetsu Jōnin** | Specialist rank — elite in one narrow domain (genjutsu, medical, analysis) but not general Jōnin breadth. | Anko Mitarashi; Genma Shiranui; Ibiki Morino |
| **Jōnin** | Full elite shinobi. Multi-domain competence. Village frontline. | Kakashi (standard); Asuma; Kurenai; Guy; most named adults in Part 1 |
| **Kage** | Village military head. Peak of demonstrated power. Capable of engaging any Jōnin simultaneously, battlefield-reshaping jutsu. | The Five Kage; Kakashi (Hokage era); Orochimaru (peak) |
| **Beyond Kage** | Transcends standard power axes. Combination of unique bloodline, mastered techniques, and often external power sources (bijū, Sage Mode, Six Paths). | Naruto (Sage/bijū mode); Sasuke (Mangekyō/Rinnegan); Hashirama (Wood Release); Madara; Kaguya |

### Combat Rule [WL-POWER-COMBAT-RULE]
A 2+ rank gap is effectively decisive in direct combat without significant environmental or circumstantial advantage. The exceptions that shift outcomes:
- **Terrain control** (a Chūnin who has trapped an area thoroughly)
- **Elemental hard-counter** (water vs. fire; wind feeds fire; earth blocks lightning)
- **Genjutsu initiation** (any rank can attempt genjutsu if they make eye contact first)
- **Preparation and intel** (knowing exactly how an opponent fights allows a one-rank-lower fighter to compete)
- **Numbers** (three Chūnin can present a serious threat to a Jōnin via coordination)
- **Sealing** (a prepared fūinjutsu trap bypasses combat rank entirely)

### Kage-Level Indicators [WL-POWER-KAGE-INDICATORS]
An NPC should be treated as Kage-level if they can do most of the following:
- Survive sustained combat from multiple Jōnin without retreat
- Deploy area-of-effect attacks large enough to destroy a district
- Maintain their own sustained assault while defending
- Have a jutsu or ability that forces opponents to solve a specific problem (not just "be strong")

### Beyond Kage Indicators [WL-POWER-BEYOND-KAGE]
Beyond Kage characters redefine the terms of engagement entirely. They do not just win fights — they change what fighting means. Naruto in Sage Mode+ can sense intent before movement. Sasuke with Rinnegan can switch places with any object in his visual field. Hashirama's Wood Release can subdue Bijū. These characters are narrative forces, not mission targets.

## JUTSU SYSTEMS [WL-JUTSU]

### Chakra Basics [WL-JUTSU-CHAKRA]
Chakra is the fundamental energy of the shinobi world — produced by combining physical energy (from the body's cells) and spiritual energy (from mind and experience). Chakra is stored in chakra coils (a network throughout the body) and released through hand seals, will, and technique.

**Chakra Natures (Five Basic):**
| Nature | Countered by | Primary Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Fire (Katon) | Water | Combustion, area, ranged |
| Water (Suiton) | Earth | Suppression, flexibility, drowning |
| Wind (Fūton) | Fire | Cutting, acceleration, projectile |
| Lightning (Raiton) | Earth | Speed, paralysis, precision |
| Earth (Doton) | Lightning | Defense, terrain, durability |

**Advanced Natures:** Kekkei Genkai (bloodline limit) combine two elements (Ice = Water+Wind; Wood = Water+Earth; Lava = Earth+Fire; etc.). Kekkei Tōta combine three (Dust = Wind+Earth+Fire). These cannot be learned — they are inherited.

### Jutsu Categories [WL-JUTSU-CATEGORIES]
- **Ninjutsu:** Chakra-based technique. The broadest category. Most offensive and utility jutsu.
- **Taijutsu:** Physical combat. Chakra enhances speed and strength but the technique itself is physical. Cannot be blocked by most jutsu countermeasures.
- **Genjutsu:** Illusion technique. Works by disrupting the target's chakra flow via the caster's chakra entering their system. Breaking genjutsu: self-disruption of own chakra flow; external disruption by ally; Kai (Release) hand sign + focus.
- **Fūinjutsu (Sealing Jutsu):** Inscribes chakra into surfaces or objects to create persistent effects — containment, storage, triggers. The most complex and longest-lasting category. Uzumaki clan are historically the premier specialists.
- **Senjutsu (Sage Arts):** Draws natural energy from the environment and mixes it with chakra. Extremely powerful but requires stillness to gather. Excess nature energy turns the user to stone. Requires training at a Sage source (Mount Myōboku for toads, Ryūchi Cave for snakes, Shikkotsu Forest for slugs).
- **Medical Ninjutsu:** Surgical application of chakra to heal, diagnose, or harm biological systems. The most demanding specialty — requires exceptional chakra control. A poor medical nin can make injuries worse.

### Summoning Contracts [WL-JUTSU-SUMMONING]
Shinobi can sign contracts with animal summoning clans (toads, snakes, slugs, dogs, hawks, weasels, etc.). The contract is written in blood and establishes a mutual agreement. A shinobi can then summon any animal on their contract by using their own blood as a seal activator. Larger/more powerful summons require more chakra.

Notable contracts: **Toads (Myōboku)** — Jiraiya, Minato, Naruto; **Snakes (Ryūchi Cave)** — Orochimaru, Sasuke (later severed); **Slugs (Shikkotsu Forest)** — Tsunade, Sakura; **Dogs** — Kakashi (Pakkun and pack); **Hawks** — Sasuke (post-Orochimaru period).

## SOCIAL & POLITICAL STRUCTURES [WL-SOCIAL]

### The Mission System [WL-SOCIAL-MISSIONS]
Villages assign missions through the Hokage (or equivalent Kage) office. Teams register, receive a briefing, take the contract, execute, and return for debrief and payment. The client pays the village; the village pays the team after a percentage cut. Mission ranks reflect expected danger. Misrepresenting mission difficulty by a client is common — a C-rank can escalate to A-rank conditions without warning; this is a classic campaign hook.

Missions are the economic backbone of the hidden village. Political tension between villages often appears as mission interference — deliberately escalating another village's C-rank, hiring rogue-nin to attack their teams, or competing for the same high-value client.

### The Chūnin Exams [WL-SOCIAL-CHUNIN-EXAMS]
A multi-village tournament and evaluation held periodically (roughly annually). Designed to assess Genin for promotion to Chūnin. Also functions as a massive geopolitical event — nations send teams partly as soft-power demonstration. Foreign dignitaries attend; the host village gains prestige and intelligence on other villages' upcoming talent.

Structure varies by era but typically involves a written test (information gathering under pressure), a survival/elimination phase, and individual/team combat exams. The host Kage evaluates not just win/loss but tactical judgment, leadership, and composure.

[ERA-SPECIFIC: The Konoha Chūnin Exams that Naruto participates in are invaded by Suna/Sound; this fundamentally changes the political context of subsequent exams.]

### Clan Politics [WL-SOCIAL-CLAN-POLITICS]
Within hidden villages, clans are power nodes. They control their own compounds, their own technique inheritance, and their own internal hierarchies. The village Kage governs the military apparatus but cannot unilaterally override clan traditions without political consequence. Clan heads have direct access to council processes and can block or delay policy through obstruction.

Inter-clan marriages are political instruments. Children of high-ranked shinobi are often enrolled in the Academy young and fast-tracked; this creates a de facto aristocracy of shinobi bloodlines. Commoner-born shinobi who rise to Jōnin rank or above are noticed and sometimes resented.

### The Shinobi Code [WL-SOCIAL-CODE]
The working code of conduct for shinobi in the field:
- **Complete the mission.** The mission objective is the primary directive; personal survival is secondary.
- **Do not abandon your comrades.** This is in philosophical tension with the above — Konoha especially treats this as a core cultural value; see Sakumo Hatake's tragedy.
- **Do not reveal your village's secrets.** Torture resistance training is part of standard ANBU conditioning; standard Jōnin training includes limited resistance.
- **A shinobi who abandons the mission is a disgrace; one who abandons their comrades is worse than trash.** (Kakashi's stated code, post-Sakumo.)

The code is enforced by social pressure, hunter-nin, and the threat of bingo book listing. It is routinely violated by everyone who is interesting.

## KEY LOCATIONS — NON-VILLAGE [WL-LOCATIONS]

### The Valley of the End [WL-LOC-VALLEY-OF-END]
A massive waterfall and river gorge on the border between the Land of Fire and the Land of Sound/Rice. Site of the legendary battle between Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha (statues of each flank the falls). Later the site of Naruto vs. Sasuke in the Part 1 climax. Deeply symbolic — every confrontation here is treated by the narrative as elemental, identity-defining.

### The Forest of Death (Training Area 44) [WL-LOC-FOREST-OF-DEATH]
Located in Konoha. Used as the second phase of the Chūnin Exams. Contains enormous predatory fauna (giant snakes, insects, etc.), thick canopy that blocks visibility, and a central tower. Also used for advanced training and dangerous mission prep. Entry without authorization is heavily restricted.

### The Land of Waves [WL-LOC-LAND-OF-WAVES]
A poor island-adjacent coastal region, accessible by bridge (the Great Naruto Bridge, formerly Tazuna's Bridge). Oppressed by the shipping magnate Gatō before Team 7 intervenes. Economically underdeveloped; emotionally significant as Team 7's first real high-stakes mission. A good analog for any underdog, economically strangled community.

### Tanzaku Town [WL-LOC-TANZAKU]
A mid-size civilian city in Fire Country known for its gambling halls, entertainment, and festivals. Tsunade's preferred hiding spot before she resumes being Hokage. A practical, seedy city with a functioning black market for medical goods and information.

### Ryūchi Cave [WL-LOC-RYUCHI-CAVE]
Home of the White Snake Sage and the snake summoning clan. Located in a remote mountain area. Access requires invitation or surviving the approach. Offers Snake Sage training — more aggressive nature energy, faster gathering, but the transformation into a snake on failure is faster and more extreme than toad Sage mode. Orochimaru's original Sage source; Sasuke signs the snake contract here.

### Mount Myōboku [WL-LOC-MYOBOKU]
Home of the Great Toad Sage (Elder Toad) and the toad summoning clan. Accessible by reverse summoning. Offers Toad Sage training — nature energy gathered standing still; slower and safer than snake mode; the transformation is a process (mossy, frog-like) rather than instant. Jiraiya's training ground; later Naruto's.

### Shikkotsu Forest [WL-LOC-SHIKKOTSU]
Home of the slug summoning clan. Tsunade and Sakura's summoning source. Less detailed in canon but the principle is the same: Sage training possible; slug summoning for medical shinobi especially useful given the healing properties of slug chakra.

### Amegakure (Village Hidden in the Rain) [WL-LOC-AMEGAKURE]
A mid-size hidden village in a perpetually rain-soaked nation that serves as a buffer zone between larger powers. Chronically caught in the crossfire of inter-village wars. Under Pain/Nagato and Konan's leadership in the Shippuden era — they rule it as a closed theocratic state, using Pain's Deva Path ability (Shinra Tensei) to keep it sealed from outsiders. The village is filled with war orphans and trauma. A place of immense suffering with genuine ideological complexity.

### Otogakure (Village Hidden in the Sound) [WL-LOC-OTOGAKURE]
Orochimaru's fake hidden village, established in the Land of Rice Fields. Not a true hidden village — it's a network of hidden bases and research facilities disguised as a nation. The "village" exists to provide Orochimaru cover for forbidden research, body experimentation, and loyalty-building via Curse Marks. After Orochimaru's decline it fragments into various remnant factions.

## SPIRITUAL & METAPHYSICAL LAYER [WL-METAPHYSICAL]

### The Sage of Six Paths [WL-META-SAGE]
Hagoromo Ōtsutsuki — the original user of chakra; the son of Kaguya; the creator of ninshū (the origin of ninjutsu). He defeated the Ten-Tails by sealing it within himself (becoming the first Jinchūriki), then split its chakra into the nine Bijū at his death. He established the moon to contain the Ten-Tails' body (the Gedo Mazō). His teachings form the philosophical foundation of shinobi culture. He appears in spirit form to both Naruto and Sasuke during the Fourth War.

### Kaguya Ōtsutsuki [WL-META-KAGUYA]
The original chakra user — not human; an alien entity who came to Earth and consumed the fruit of the God Tree. The Ten-Tails is the God Tree + Kaguya essentially reconstituted. She sought to reclaim all chakra. She is Beyond Kage by a categorical margin — her threat is existential, not tactical. The Team 7 + Kage confrontation with her is the climax of the canon.

### Ninshu vs. Ninjutsu [WL-META-NINSHU]
Hagoromo intended chakra as a communication tool — Ninshu, meaning "sharing one's spirit." His sons (Asura and Indra) diverged: Asura believed connection and cooperation were the path; Indra believed individual power and control were the path. The shinobi system as it exists is a corruption of Hagoromo's intent — techniques designed to share became techniques designed to dominate.

### Kekkei Genkai & Bloodline Limits [WL-META-KEKKEI]
Bloodline traits are not magic — they are specific chakra natures or physical mutations inherited genetically. They cannot be learned; they can be transplanted with varying success (Sharingan eyes can be transplanted; Wood Release via Hashirama cell implantation is possible but rare and dangerous). Some nations have historically abducted or killed people with Kekkei Genkai to absorb them — this is a source of profound inter-village resentment.

### Recurring Political Tension Points [WL-FACTIONS-TENSIONS]
1. **Jinchūriki weaponization:** Every village with a Bijū debates openly using them as military deterrents vs. keeping them covert. This is never settled.
2. **Missing-nin jurisdiction:** When a missing-nin crosses a border, which village has the right to pursue? This creates regular diplomatic incidents.
3. **Bloodline abduction history:** Kumo's history of targeting Hyūga and others poisons diplomatic relations with Konoha. Never formally addressed.
4. **Akatsuki neutrality:** Small nations and minor villages sometimes quietly pay Akatsuki for protection — acknowledging this openly would destroy alliances with the Five Great Villages.
5. **The Danzō problem:** Root's off-books operations have manipulated multiple nations' politics. After his death, the extent of the damage is slowly uncovered — a natural campaign source of "why is X happening" mysteries.
6. **Economic disparity:** Suna's daimyō-caused poverty vs. Konoha's relative stability creates resentment. Lesser nations resent the mission system's fee structures.

## CULTURAL DETAILS [WL-CULTURE]

### Food & Daily Life [WL-CULTURE-FOOD]
- **Ramen** is the working-class comfort food of the shinobi world. Ichiraku's is the gold standard in Konoha.
- **Dango** (skewered rice-flour sweets) — Rin's food; Anko's food; festival staple.
- **Soldier pills (Hyōrōgan):** Konoha-developed emergency rations that restore chakra and energy rapidly. Also cause extreme fatigue afterward. Akimichi clan pills are a more extreme version.
- **Sake** is the universal adult social lubricant. Tsunade's drinking habit is legendary.
- Market towns and post towns along civilian roads serve travelers. Food gets worse the further from a major village you go.

### Communication [WL-CULTURE-COMMS]
- **Hawk messenger** — primary long-distance communication. Classified messages are sealed with chakra.
- **In-village:** Runner-nin for inter-office comms; chakra signal pulses via seals for emergency alerts.
- **Clan-to-clan:** Direct personal meeting preferred; written messages risk interception.
- **With enemies:** Neutral ground meetings via third parties (couriers, Land of Iron envoys, etc.). Violating neutral ground parley is a significant diplomatic act.

### Holidays & Festivals [WL-CULTURE-FESTIVALS]
- Villages hold festivals tied to founding days, harvest cycles, and memorial events for war dead.
- Konoha's founding is annually commemorated; the Hokage Rock is ceremonially lit.
- The Night of the Nine-Tails (the attack anniversary) is observed differently depending on who you are — a memorial for some, a day of grief and fear for others, and for Naruto a complicated absence.
- The Land of Snow/Spring festival (canon film) is regionally specific but illustrative of how smaller nations build identity around celebration.

### Death & Burial [WL-CULTURE-DEATH]
Shinobi dying in the field is the norm, not the exception. Bodies are cremated where possible; ashes returned to family if possible. ANBU deaths are not officially recorded in public records. Missing-nin killed by hunter-nin are destroyed on-site — no ceremony, no marker.

Konoha maintains a memorial stone with the names of shinobi killed in duty. Kakashi visits it. It is the most emotionally significant landmark in the village for active shinobi.
