# Atrum — World Lore Compendium

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW

### OVERVIEW -- Atrum at a Glance
**Genre:** Medieval high fantasy with shonen/seinen energy. Castles, kingdoms, swords, magic, feudal politics, mythical creatures. No technology, no gunpowder, no sci-fi. Magic is universal and structural (circles and aura), not soft or mysterious.
**Tone:** GRAY MORALITY, SHONEN/SEINEN — serious stakes, loud personalities, comedic beats. People make terrible decisions for good reasons. Rivals become allies. The world is dark but the people in it burn bright.
**Year:** 1247 of the Third Age
**Population:** ~40 million across three continents
**Continents:** Aethmark (feudal kingdoms, universities, mercantile cities), Veranthos (wild primordial lands, druidic councils, tribal confederations), Solthea (holy theocratic states, Inquisition, faith-mandated magic)
**The Path:** Every person awakens Mage OR Knight at age 12. Permanent. No switching. Mages cast circles (geometric mana patterns). Knights project aura (will-made-force). Both are equally powerful, equally necessary. The rivalry is real and mostly friendly.
**Core Quality:** Awakening forms a raw mana core. The Stone of Eden at Triarch University solidifies it to S-rank over 4 ceremonies (4 cohorts). Without the Stone, cores solidify at C-rank at best (royal stones) down to F (village/no stone). S-core: recover in minutes, deep reserves, breakthroughs are survivable. C-core: recover in hours, thin reserves, every breakthrough is a gamble with death. No A or B rank. You're either Triarch or you're not. This gap defines the world.
**Triarch University:** Island neutral ground between three continents. 150 students per year (50/continent, 30 noble/20 civilian). 4 cohorts ages 15-25. Three houses: Gryphon (martial), Raven (knowledge), Stag (duty) — sorted by temperament, not path, not continent. Every protagonist walks these halls. Every faction sends their children here. Drop out = core reverts. The Stone is why Triarch exists. The Stone is why nobody attacks it. The Stone is why your character's story starts here or circles back to here.
**Magic:** No element lock. Any Mage can learn any of the 8 domains (Fire, Water, Wind, Thunder, Earth, Light, Shadow, Spirit). You learn what you train. The circle shapes the mana, not the Mage's birth.
**9 Tiers:** 1st (baseline) → 9th (myth). Breakthroughs are dramatic, dangerous events with rising mortality. S-core gives dramatically better odds. C-core breakthrough mortality is ~10-20% higher per tier. Higher Tiers are earned through risk — but core quality determines how many survive the climb.
**Forbidden Arts:** Necromancy (Shadow 6th+), Dominion (Spirit 5th+), Blood Exploitation (Water 4th+), Demonology (Shadow/Spirit 7th+). Universally condemned. Punished by execution, exile, or Core Severing.
**Core Conflicts:** Religion vs. secular magic (Solthea vs. Aethmark). Nature vs. civilization (Veranthos vs. colonizers). Quota wars (Triarch seats are worth more than armies). Faith vs. faith (Radiant Throne vs. Schismatics). Mage vs. Knight rivalry (cultural, not hostile). Continental tensions inside Triarch walls (Aethmark pragmatists, Veranthos druids, Solthean zealots sharing dorms).
**Coin:** 1 gold sovereign = 10 silver marks = 100 copper pence. A peasant family lives on 20-30 copper/week. A trained battle Mage or Knight earns 5-10 gold/month. An enchanted weapon: 50-500 gold. A siege-level combat scroll: 50 gold.
**World State:** The Age of the Fracture. Kingdoms wage war, faiths clash, the wilds push back, forbidden arts fester. People still find time to drink, argue, fall in love, and make terrible decisions for good reasons.

## 2. POWER SYSTEM

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Core Divide: Mage and Knight
**Type:** Universal dual-path system
**Core Principle:** Every person on Atrum is born with a mana core. At age 12, the core awakens. At the moment of awakening, the core crystallizes into one of two forms: Mage Core or Knight Core. This is not a choice — it's determined by factors no one fully understands. The crystallization is permanent. There is no known way to change your path. Mage and Knight are not social classes — they're biological realities, like being right-handed or left-handed. Both are respected. Both are powerful. They do different things.
**The Mage Path:** The core crystallizes outward, forming a casting matrix — a structure of nested rings around the core that shapes mana into patterns. Mages cast circles. They draw, trace, or visualize geometric patterns that channel mana into specific effects. A Mage can learn any magical domain. Fire, water, shadow, thunder — the element is determined by the circle, not the Mage. Most Mages specialize because mastery of one domain takes years, but a Mage with the time and will can cross domains freely. No element locking. No born affinity for one type. You learn what you train.
**The Knight Path:** The core crystallizes inward, condensing mana into the body itself. Knights don't cast circles. Their mana reinforces their physical form and projects outward as aura — raw, shaped willpower made visible. Aura can form shields, extend blade-range, create shockwaves, enhance speed and strength beyond human limits. A Knight's aura is their fighting spirit made manifest. The angrier, more determined, or more desperate a Knight becomes, the brighter and harder their aura burns. This is not metaphor — Knight aura literally intensifies with emotional conviction.
**The Mutual Respect (Mostly):** Mages and Knights need each other. A Mage can obliterate at range but is vulnerable up close. A Knight dominates close combat but has limited reach. Battles are won by combined formations. The best armies integrate both. That said, every Mage has been told "just stand behind me" by a cocky Knight, and every Knight has been told "catch up" by a smug Mage. The rivalry is real, usually friendly, occasionally bloody.
**Mage-Knight Duels:** A persistent cultural phenomenon. Mages and Knights test themselves against each other constantly — in arenas, in training, in bars after too many drinks. The rules are usually informal. The bragging rights are eternal. The results are unpredictable. A 3rd Tier Mage versus a 3rd Tier Knight is anyone's fight — it comes down to skill, timing, and who wants it more.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Awakening and Core Quality
**Type:** Foundation system — determines mana/aura efficiency for life
**Awakening:** At age 12, every person's mana core crystallizes. The path (Mage or Knight) is determined. The core is raw — unformed, unstable, leaking mana. It must be solidified through contact with an awakening stone, or it remains fragile permanently.
**The Stone of Eden:** An ancient artifact on Triarch Island, positioned between all three continents. The Stone is the only S-rank awakening catalyst on Atrum. It was discovered (or created — accounts differ) before recorded history. All three continental powers signed the Triarch Treaty expressly to guarantee shared access. The Stone is why Triarch exists. Everything else — the university, the houses, the prestige — is built on top of the Stone.
**Core Ranks:**
- **S-Rank (Stone of Eden only):** Deep reserves. Recovery measured in minutes. Breakthroughs are painful but survivable. S-core individuals can fight multiple engagements in a day, recover from mana/aura exhaustion rapidly, and attempt higher breakthroughs with meaningfully better odds. The best foundation on Atrum. Exclusive to Triarch graduates.
- **C-Rank (Royal/provincial stones):** Moderate reserves. Recovery measured in hours. Breakthroughs are dangerous — every one is a gamble with death. The best non-Triarch foundation. Royal families possess C-rank stones. Provincial capitals might have one. A king's heir gets C-rank. The king's second child gets C-rank or whatever's available.
- **D-Rank (Lesser stones, common in cities):** Thin reserves. Recovery requires a full night's rest. Breakthroughs carry high mortality. Most soldiers, city-trained Mages and Knights operate at D-rank. Functional, but the gap between D and C is significant.
- **F-Rank (No stone / village awakening):** Minimal reserves. Recovery takes days. Breakthroughs are near-suicidal. Village awakenings — cores that solidified without any stone — produce F-rank cores. The hardest path on Atrum.
**The Gap:** There is no A-rank or B-rank. The drop from S to C is catastrophic. S-core recovers in minutes. C-core recovers in hours. Over a battle, over a career, over a lifetime, that gap separates the powerful from everyone else. A C-core 3rd-tier who reached that rank through sheer will is a legend because they survived breakthroughs that should have killed them. An S-core student at Triarch hasn't earned anything yet — the Stone gave them the foundation. What they build on it is up to them.
**The Stone Ceremony (4 Uses, 4 Cohorts):** The Stone of Eden requires four contact ceremonies to permanently solidify an S-core. Each ceremony hardens the core further. Students enter Triarch at age 15 and undergo one ceremony per cohort:
- **1st Cohort (age 15-17):** First Stone ceremony. Core begins forming S-rank structure. Fragile — if the student drops out now, the core reverts completely to unranked (no rank at all). They must find a C or D stone to re-solidify. Many who drop out in 1st cohort never recover.
- **2nd Cohort (age 18-20):** Second Stone ceremony. Core is hardening. Recovery is noticeably better than C-rank. Getting dangerous. Dropping out now means reversion to C-rank at best — the partial solidification leaves some structure.
- **3rd Cohort (age 21-23):** Third Stone ceremony. Core is nearly solid. Outperforming almost anyone not from Triarch. The finish line is close. Drop out reverts to C-rank — enough structure remains to hold.
- **4th Cohort / Final (age 24-25):** Fourth and final Stone ceremony. S-core becomes permanent. Unbreakable. The student graduates Triarch with the best foundation on Atrum. They carry this advantage for life.
**Dropout Penalty:** Leaving Triarch before completing all four Stone ceremonies is catastrophic. The partially-formed S-core reverts. 1st cohort dropouts revert to unranked — they must seek out a C or D stone immediately or remain fundamentally flawed. 2nd and 3rd cohort dropouts retain C-rank at best. This means students don't leave Triarch — they endure. The pressure is immense. The dropout rate from the first-year survival exams is 40%. Those who leave lose everything they've built.
**Noble Advantage (Redefined):** Nobles no longer hoard breakthrough catalysts as their primary advantage. The new advantage is the Triarch quota — every noble family fights for their 30 seats per continent. Private tutors from age 12-15 give noble children a head start in basic mana control before they even reach Triarch. A noble arrives at 15 already trained, already disciplined, already familiar with mana flow. A civilian arrives raw. Both get the same Stone. The noble still has an advantage — just not in core quality.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Magic Domains (No Element Lock)
**Type:** Free-choice magic system
**Core Rule:** Any Mage can learn any domain. There are no born affinities. No locked elements. You pick what you study and you get good at what you practice. The circle determines the effect, not the Mage's birth.
**The Eight Domains:**
- **Fire:** Combat, forging, warmth, illumination. Cauterization healing. At 6th Tier+: thermokinesis, plasma, magma. The loudest domain. Fire Mages are dramatic and they know it.
- **Water:** Flesh-healing (the body is mostly water), naval control, irrigation, ice at sufficient control. Blood manipulation at 6th Tier+ (forbidden application). The most versatile domain — everyone needs water.
- **Wind:** Speed, flight (3rd Tier+), ranged attacks, enhanced senses, communication relays. Suffocation at 6th Tier+. Every army's scouts. Wind Mages are insufferably fast and they never let you forget it.
- **Thunder:** Lightning, paralysis, magnetic effects, ward-breaking. Poorly understood. Passes through specific bloodlines (~40% inheritance for Mage awakening as Thunder-capable, but Thunder circles are complex — anyone can learn them, some families find them easier). A Thunder Mage is feared even by allies.
- **Earth:** Fortification, construction, agriculture, terrain manipulation. Mud healing (mineral application). At 6th Tier+: earthquakes, petrification, metal extraction. Earth Mages built civilization. Literally.
- **Light:** Disease-healing, purification, truth detection, illumination. Memory manipulation at 6th Tier+ (sketchy territory). Sacred in Solthea — the Radiant Throne considers Light the most divine domain.
- **Shadow:** Concealment, fear induction, mana absorption, shadow manipulation. Necromancy at 6th Tier+ (forbidden application). Stigmatized everywhere — Shadow Mages hide their specialty in polite society. The domain isn't evil. The reputation kind of is.
- **Spirit:** Beast-bonding, empathy, ancestral communion, nature resonance. Dominion (mind control) at 5th Tier+ (forbidden application). Spirit Mages are watched carefully. The peaceful applications are beautiful. The potential for abuse is terrifying.
**Domain Specialization:** Most Mages pick one or two domains and master them. Learning a new domain from scratch takes years — the circle patterns are different, the mana flow feels different, the muscle memory doesn't transfer. A 4th Tier Fire Mage deciding to learn Water starts at 1st Tier effectiveness for Water circles until they train up. Cross-domain Mages exist but are rare because the time investment is enormous.
**Creative Applications:** No domain is locked to one role. Fire heals (cauterize, kill infection). Earth heals (mud poultices, mineral absorption). Water kills (drowning, blood-freezing). Light corrupts (erase memories). The circle pattern and intent determine the effect. The domain is the medium, not the message. The Mage is the one who decides what the message says.
**Domain Discovery:** A child's Mage or Knight path manifests at age 12 when the mana core awakens. Before awakening, a child is magically inert. A Mage child's first circle is usually simple — a spark of fire, a trickle of water, a gust of wind. They're not locked to that domain — it's just what felt natural first. What they choose to study after is up to them.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Forbidden Applications
**Type:** Prohibited magical practices
**Status:** Universally condemned. Not because the domains are bad — because the applications violate the fundamental rule: you don't use magic to destroy someone's soul or will.
**Necromancy:** Animating corpses with Shadow mana. At 6th Tier+, necromantic circles can bind wandering spirits into corpses — creating intelligent undead that remember fragments of their former lives. This is worse. The Pale Architect perfected this during the Necromancer Wars.
**Dominion:** Overriding another's will through Spirit mana. Mind control, forced servitude, personality erasure. The dominated person is aware but helpless — a prisoner inside their own body. Long-term dominion causes permanent psychological fragmentation. Even short-term use corrodes the caster's empathy over time.
**Soul Manipulation:** Shadow or Spirit mana used to tear at the soul. Permanent damage no healing circle can undo. Practitioners develop a mana taint that other Mages can sense — they smell wrong, their mana feels greasy.
**Blood Exploitation:** Water mana used to manipulate another's blood while they live. Distinct from healing — this is weaponized physiology. A Water-healer touching blood for healing is sacred. A Water-mage crushing blood vessels is execution-worthy. The line is intent, and intent is hard to prove.
**Punishment:** Execution, exile, or Core Severing — a ritual that permanently burns out the mana core, leaving the person alive but magically dead. Considered worse than death by most. The Burning Light favors burning for theatrical value. Aethmark prefers imprisonment. Veranthos buries the condemned alive.
**The Gray Area:** Not all Shadow Mages are necromancers. Not all Spirit Mages are dominators. Domain prejudice is real — Shadow Mages hide their specialty in polite society. Thunder Mages are feared even by allies. The domain isn't the crime. The application is. But the world rarely makes that distinction, and that's the tragedy of it.
**scan_depth:** 5

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Ideological Fault Lines
**Type:** Core conflicts
**Religion vs. Secular Magic:** Solthea teaches that certain domains are divine gifts (Light, Spirit) and others are temptations (Shadow, Thunder). Aethmark teaches that all domains are natural forces — no inherent morality. This disagreement has fueled centuries of crusades. Each side considers the other spiritually or intellectually bankrupt.
**Nature vs. Civilization:** Veranthos druids believe magic should work with the land. Aethmarkian colonial magic — earth for excavation, fire for forges, water for irrigation — is seen as rape of the living world. The colonial wars are ideological as much as territorial.
**Accepted vs. Forbidden:** Every culture agrees necromancy is evil. Other lines vary wildly. Is using Shadow mana for concealment inherently suspicious? Is Spirit empathy an invasion of privacy? The boundaries are cultural, shifting, and never stable.
**Mage vs. Knight:** Not a war — a rivalry. Both paths are necessary. But every culture has its debates. Should Mages be exempt from frontline combat? Should Knights be given command authority by default since they "understand the field"? Are Mages smarter? Are Knights braver? The arguments are endless, mostly good-natured, occasionally violent. The real tension comes from the fact that Mages shape the world and Knights protect it — and both sides think they're doing the harder job.
**Tier Aristocracy vs. Common Power:** Higher Tiers are earned through dangerous breakthroughs — but the rich have advantages. Noble houses possess breakthrough catalysts, ancient circle knowledge, and master trainers. A peasant's child must risk death for what a noble's heir receives on a silver platter. The gap fuels resentment.
**Faith vs. Faith:** Competing Solthean sects disagree on which domains are truly divine. The Radiant Throne blesses only Light and Spirit. The Schismatics argue all eight domains are equally sacred — including Shadow. This theological dispute has spilled more blood than any foreign war.
**scan_depth:** 5

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Tier Progression and Breakthroughs
**Type:** Tier advancement mechanics (applies to both Mages and Knights)
**Core Principle:** Everyone awakens at 1st Tier at age 12 when their mana core crystallizes. Paths are permanent — Mage or Knight for life. Core quality (S through F) determines mana/aura efficiency and recovery speed, not Tier ceiling. Progression requires breakthroughs — pushing the core past its current limit. A breakthrough is not training. It is a singular, dangerous event where the core forces itself to expand, forming additional structure (nested rings for Mages, condensed layers for Knights). The body must survive the higher mana throughput.
**Breakthrough Mechanics:**
- Requires the person to be at their current Tier's peak (maximum mastery of that Tier's capabilities)
- Mana must be compressed and forcibly expanded simultaneously — Mages describe it as trying to breathe fire while holding your heart still; Knights describe it as trying to crush a diamond with your bare fists while your bones scream
- Physical toll: internal burns, mana fever, core fractures, temporary or permanent scarring
- Mortality rate rises sharply with Tier: 1st→2nd (~2%), 2nd→3rd (~8%), 3rd→4th (~15%), 4th→5th (~25%), 5th→6th (~40%), 6th→7th (~55%), 7th→8th (~75%), 8th→9th (near-certain death, zero confirmed survivors in centuries)
- Catalysts (rare alchemical preparations, nexus sites, enchanted breakthrough circles/aura anchors) reduce failure rate but are expensive and hoarded by the powerful
- A failed breakthrough doesn't always kill — it can scar the core, reducing capacity permanently or, in worst cases, cause Core Severing (magical death while alive)
**Tier Capability Reference:**
- **1st Tier:** Core awakened. Mage: cantrip-level circles, light candles, warm water, heal small cuts. Knight: basic aura reinforcement, slightly faster, slightly stronger, can harden knuckles. Every human reaches this. Baseline.
- **2nd Tier:** Combat basics. Mage: fire bolt, ice shard, personal ward. Knight: aura shield, aura-enhanced strikes, short burst speed. Standard soldier threshold. Most people reach 2nd or 3rd and plateau.
- **3rd Tier:** Professional line. Mage: fire lance, barrier wall, flesh-healing, terrain shift. Knight: projected aura slashes, sustained aura armor, wall-running, aura burst propulsion. Backbone of armies. Career fighters live here.
- **4th Tier:** Command power. Mage: firestorm, fortress barrier, limb regen, weather magic. Knight: ranged aura attacks (10m+), aura flight (short bursts), structural aura reinforcement (punch through walls). University graduates, noble elites, veteran commanders.
- **5th Tier:** Strategic asset. Mage: wall-breaker, army-barrier, mass healing. Knight: sustained flight, aura shockwave (building-leveling), aura construct weapons. Fewer than 500 alive worldwide. Regional power.
- **6th Tier:** Sovereign-class. Mage: localized weather manipulation, fortress-level barriers, mass resurrection (within hours). Knight: aura domain (projected will over a city block — everything inside answers to them), sustained aura constructs, conceptual-grade defense. ~100 alive worldwide. The elite of the elite. Sovereigns and legends.
- **7th Tier:** Natural disaster. Mage: regional weather control, true resurrection, elemental manifestation as terrain. Knight: expanded aura domain (over kilometers), aura that rewrites local physics, near-invulnerability to conventional attack. ~20 alive worldwide. Their presence shifts wars.
- **8th Tier:** Mythic. Mage: portals, mana storms, elemental avatar state (become the element). Knight: aura domain over a battlefield that responds to thought alone, conceptual-level aura attacks that bypass physical defense, regeneration through aura. ~5 alive worldwide. Living strategic deterrents. Nations calculate around their existence.
- **9th Tier:** Theoretical apex. Creation-level. Time manipulation, life-from-nothing, reshaping reality within the domain. No confirmed living Mage or Knight. The Pale Architect may have touched 9th briefly. Attempting 8th→9th breakthrough is considered elaborate suicide. The last confirmed 9th Tier existed during the Necromancer Wars and their name is still spoken with fear and awe.
**Breakthrough Moments Are Dramatic:** This is a world where people matter. Breakthroughs don't happen quietly. When someone pushes past their limit, it's a scene. The air shifts. Mana becomes visible. People nearby can feel the pressure. A 3rd→4th breakthrough in a crowded room will stop conversation. A 5th→6th is an event that draws witnesses from miles away. A 7th→8th reshapes local weather and is felt across the continent. Failures are just as dramatic — and just as visible. Everyone knows when someone tried to break through. Everyone knows whether they succeeded.
**scan_depth:** 5

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Knight Aura
**Type:** Physical combat enhancement system
**Core Principle:** Knight cores crystallize inward. The mana doesn't form a casting matrix — it condenses into the body itself, reinforcing muscle, bone, and nerve beyond human limits. The excess projects outward as aura — visible, tangible willpower. Knight aura isn't subtle. When a Knight gets serious, you can see it. The air around them shimmers. Their eyes catch light that isn't there. And when they move, they move like something that shouldn't exist in a world that hasn't caught up.
**Aura Basics:**
- **Aura Reinforcement:** The foundation. Enhanced strength, speed, durability, senses. A Knight's body is tougher, faster, and more responsive than any unenhanced human. This is passive — Knights are always slightly reinforced once trained. A Knight who isn't trying can still punch through a wooden door.
- **Aura Projection:** The offense. Knights project aura outward as raw force — slashes, bursts, shockwaves. An aura slash can cut steel at 3rd Tier. An aura burst can shatter a charging horse at 4th Tier. The projection takes the Knight's will and turns it into a weapon.
- **Aura Shield:** The defense. Condensing aura into a barrier in front of the body or around allies. Not as versatile as a Mage's ward circles but faster to deploy and harder to break with raw force. A Knight's shield is their will made wall. The harder they believe, the harder the wall.
- **Aura Sense:** Advanced technique. Reading the aura of others — detecting presence, gauging strength, sensing hostility. Not as precise as Mage divination but instant and instinctive. Experienced Knights can tell a Mage's Tier by the pressure of their mana.
**Aura and Emotion:** Knight aura literally intensifies with emotional conviction. This isn't metaphor. A Knight who is angry, determined, desperate, or protective projects harder, faster, brighter. A Knight fighting for something they believe in is measurably stronger than the same Knight fighting for a paycheck. This makes Knight duels unpredictable — the one who wants it more has a real, physical advantage. It also makes Knights dangerous when they're emotional. Aura spikes under rage can cause collateral damage.
**Knight Duels and Rivalries:** Knight culture centers on direct conflict as a form of communication and respect. Two Knights who disagree settle it with fists and aura. The winner is right (at least in the immediate practical sense). Rivalries are intense, personal, and often last for years. A good rivalry is considered healthy — it pushes both Knights to improve. A bad rivalry ends with someone in the ground.
**Aura Training:** Like mana depth for Mages, aura strength is trainable. Daily exertion to exhaustion, combat practice, meditation, and conditioning. A Knight who trains for twenty years hits harder than a Knight who barely shows up, regardless of Tier. The difference between Tier (power ceiling) and Aura Strength (how hard you hit right now) is the difference between potential and practice.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Knight Aura Tier Progression
**Type:** Knight capability reference (parallel to Mage Domain Expression by Tier)
- **1st Tier:** Aura reinforcement (passive). Slightly faster, slightly stronger, harden knuckles on command. Punch through wood. Aura invisible to others, felt as slight pressure by nearby Mages.
- **2nd Tier:** Aura shield (personal, forward-facing). Enhanced strikes that crack stone. Short-burst speed enhancement (sprint distance). Aura flickers visible when projecting. Standard soldier.
- **3rd Tier:** Projected aura slashes (melee range). Sustained aura armor (full-body, drains over time). Wall-running. Aura burst propulsion (explosive dash). Aura sense begins — can feel hostility nearby. Career fighter.
- **4th Tier:** Ranged aura attacks (10m+). Aura flight (short bursts, draining). Structural aura reinforcement (punch through walls, reinforce weapons). Aura sense sharp enough to detect Tier and path. Veteran commander.
- **5th Tier:** Sustained flight. Aura shockwave (building-breaking). Aura construct weapons (swords, shields, projectile shapes formed from pure aura). Multi-target aura distribution (shield allies nearby). Regional power.
- **6th Tier:** Aura domain activation — a zone around the Knight where their will exerts influence. Inside their domain, movement is faster, allies are reinforced, enemies feel crushing pressure. Aura constructs persist without active focus. Sovereign-class.
- **7th Tier:** Expanded aura domain (city block to kilometer range). Aura rewrites local physics — the Knight's domain becomes their terrain. Gravity shifts for enemies, air thickens, ground softens. Conceptual-grade defense: shields that resist magic by pure conviction. Near-invulnerable to conventional attack.
- **8th Tier:** Battlefield-scale aura domain. Thought-speed response within domain — every hostile action is countered reflexively. Aura regeneration: the Knight heals by cycling aura through their own body. Aura attacks bypass physical defense entirely. Living strategic deterrent.
- **9th Tier:** Theoretical apex. Domain becomes reality. The Knight's will is law within their sphere — gravity, time perception, movement itself bends to conviction. No confirmed living example.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Mana Depth and Aura Strength
**Type:** Trainable endurance/force axes
**Core Principle — Mages (Mana Depth):** Unlike Tier, mana reserves grow through consistent use. The mana core is like a muscle — cast regularly, and your depth deepens. Neglect it, and you atrophy. A high-Tier Mage who never trains has pitiful endurance. A 2nd Tier soldier who has fought for twenty years can outlast nobles twice their Tier.
**Core Principle — Knights (Aura Strength):** Aura output grows through daily training, combat, and conditioning. A Knight's aura strength determines how hard they hit, how long they can sustain projection, and how much punishment their shields can take. Like physical conditioning — painful, repetitive, necessary. The Knight who collapses from aura exhaustion today will hit harder tomorrow.
**Depth/Strength Categories (both paths, parallel terms):**
- **Shallow / Untrained:** Exhausts after a handful of combat exchanges. Untrained civilians, fresh graduates.
- **Moderate / Drilled:** Can sustain combat for a full engagement. Standard military conditioning. Most active soldiers.
- **Deep / Hardened:** Fights through a siege. Sustains high-output for hours. Veterans, specialists, career professionals.
- **Abyssal / Relentless:** Functionally bottomless in most engagements. Can fight from dawn to dawn again. Generations of training. These are the people armies build doctrines around.
**Exhaustion Mechanics:**
- **Mage:** Draining mana to zero causes headache, tremor, nosebleed, tunnel vision. Continued casting past zero damages the core permanently. Every Mage knows the feeling of empty. The wise stop before they reach it.
- **Knight:** Draining aura to zero causes muscle failure, bone micro-fractures, exhaustion collapse. Continued fighting past zero causes the body to tear itself apart — aura that should reinforce the muscles instead pulls from the flesh itself. Knights who've gone past zero describe their own body turning against them.
**Regeneration:** Passive regeneration accelerates with rest, sleep, and food. Active meditation roughly doubles recovery speed. Alchemical restoratives accelerate further but with diminishing returns and eventual toxicity. There are no shortcuts.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Circle Mechanics (Mage Only)
**Type:** Mage casting framework
**Core Principle:** All Mage magic requires a circle. Without a defined geometric pattern, mana has no shape and produces no effect. The circle is the container. The mana is the substance. The domain is the color.
**Circle Components:**
- **The Ring:** The base circle. Required. One ring for simple spells, nested concentric rings for complex ones.
- **The Core Point:** Center where mana pools. The Mage channels mana here before it flows outward.
- **Conduits:** Lines connecting rings, directing mana flow through the pattern.
- **Glyphs (optional):** Symbols within or around the ring that refine effect. Not required for basic spells but essential for precision — healing circles need healing glyphs, siege circles need geometric amplification glyphs. Like punctuation — the spell works without it, but not as well.
**Circle Complexity by Tier:**
- 1st Tier: Single ring, zero or one glyph. Cantrip level.
- 2nd Tier: Single ring, up to three glyphs. Basic combat.
- 3rd Tier: Single ring with conduits, four to five glyphs. Professional combat.
- 4th Tier: Nested rings (two), six to eight glyphs, multiple conduits. High magic.
- 5th Tier: Triple-nested rings, nine to twelve glyphs, multi-conduit geometry. Siege magic.
- 6th Tier: Multi-layered nested with geometric amplification. Strategic-scale magic.
- 7th Tier: Self-sustaining circle patterns that draw ambient mana. City-scale effect radius.
- 8th Tier: Living circles — patterns that adapt in real-time to counter resistance. Reality-bending.
- 9th Tier: Theoretical — patterns that exist partially in higher dimensions. Creation-grade.
**Casting Speed by Method:**
- **Physical Drawing** (slowest, precise): 10 seconds to hours depending on complexity. Preserved and reusable.
- **Air Tracing** (medium, combat): 1-30 seconds. Disappears after casting.
- **Mental Visualization** (fastest, master): Instant. No visible circle. Requires thousands of repetitions to internalize.
**Circle Inscription:** Carved circles in objects remain permanently active with maintenance (enchantment). Anyone of equal or higher Tier can activate an inscribed circle.
**Circle Failure:** A mistraced circle channels mana improperly. Minor errors produce weak or warped effects. Major errors cause backfire — mana reverses through the body, causing burns, nerve damage, or death. Higher-tier circles are more dangerous to miscast. Training reduces risk but never eliminates it.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Domain Expression by Tier
**Type:** Mage capability reference (representative examples — any domain can be learned)
**Fire:**
- 1st: Spark, candle-light, minor warmth, cauterize small wounds

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Enchantment and Inscription
**Type:** Permanent magic application (Mage-only craft)
**Core Principle:** A physically inscribed circle, properly maintained, remains permanently active. This is enchantment. The Mage imprints their mana into the circle pattern — a Fire-specialist produces heat weapons, an Earth-specialist produces barrier shields.
**Weapon Enchantment:** Circles carved into blades, armor, and shields. A sword with a Fire circle ignites on command. A shield with an Earth circle absorbs impacts beyond what steel alone can hold. Quality of inscription determines quality of effect. Hale the Mute Smith produces generational enchantments. Street-corner inscribers produce work that lasts months.
**Structural Enchantment:** Circles carved into buildings, walls, infrastructure. City walls with Earth barriers and Light wards. Bridges with Earth support circles. Homes with Fire warming circles. Expensive but permanent with maintenance. Most major cities bear centuries-old enchantments still functioning.
**Ward Enchantment:** Defensive circles designed to detect, repel, or destroy specific threats. Ward circles on city walls detect hostile mana and trigger counter-circles automatically. Personal wards on armor deflect projectiles. Ward maintenance is a profession — ward-keepers walk city walls daily, checking inscriptions and refreshing faded patterns.
**Aura-Forged Weapons:** Knights can't enchant — that's a Mage craft. But Knights can channel aura through weapons forged to accept it. Aura-forged steel is a specialized alloy that conducts Knight aura without degrading. A Knight's aura extends through the weapon, adding impact force, reach, and sometimes elemental properties if the Knight has trained elemental aura infusion (an advanced technique). Not every Knight uses aura-forged weapons — some prefer raw fists — but the best usually do.
**Scroll Enchantment:** Circle patterns on parchment with mana-conductive ink. Single-use pre-drawn circle. The user channels mana through it and the spell fires without drawing anything. Expensive, consumable, invaluable in emergencies. Limited to the user's Tier — a 3rd Tier Mage cannot activate a 5th Tier scroll.
**Enchantment Decay:** All enchantments degrade over time. Stone: centuries. Metal: decades. Wood: years. Cloth, leather, parchment: months. Maintenance extends lifespan indefinitely. Neglected enchantments fail, sometimes catastrophically.
**scan_depth:** 5

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Combat Doctrine
**Type:** Military magic and aura integration
**The Changed Nature of War:** Magic and aura have fundamentally altered medieval warfare. Mage formations rain destruction from range. Knight formations charge through anything that isn't a prepared defensive line. The combination is devastating. An army with only Mages gets overrun at close range. An army with only Knights gets shredded at distance. Combined doctrine wins wars.
**Mage Formations (Chorus Casting):** Mages of the same domain casting in synchronized formation amplify output significantly. Twenty 2nd Tier Fire Mages casting together produce a combined firestorm equivalent to a single 4th Tier Mage — but it requires flawless coordination. Losing one Mage destabilizes the pattern. Chorus casting is the backbone of Mage army doctrine.
**Knight Formations (Aura Sync):** Knights in close formation can synchronize their aura — individual shields merge into a unified barrier, their combined charge hits like a battering ram. Ten 2nd Tier Knights in aura sync can breach a shield wall that would stop fifty unenhanced soldiers. The formation requires discipline and trust — one Knight breaking sync leaves a gap the enemy will exploit.
**Combined Doctrine:** The most effective military formations integrate both. Mage chorus casting provides ranged devastation and barriers. Knight aura formations provide mobile assault and close defense. Standard Aethmark battle formation: Earth Mage barriers as base, Fire Mage lines for assault, Water Mages for healing, Wind Mages for scouting, Knight companies for the charge and the hold.
**Siege Warfare:** Mage-intensive. Earth Mages crack foundations, Fire Mages burn defenses. Knight companies assault breaches — a Knight with aura reinforcement can survive falling masonry and fight through gaps that would kill unenhanced soldiers. Sieges without both are sieges that drag on or fail.
**Counter Tactics:**
- **Circlebreaking:** Disrupting Mage casting. Kill the caster mid-trace. Break drawn lines. A Knight charge that reaches a Mage formation is a circlebreaker by nature — the Mage can't trace while running for their life.
- **Aura Cracking:** Overwhelming a Knight's shield through sustained impact. Arrow volleys, Mage bombardment, or a stronger Knight's projected aura. A Knight's shield is only as strong as their will and their mana — both exhaust.
- **Thunder Disruption:** Thunder Mage mana destabilizes all active circles and scrambles aura patterns in proximity. A Thunder Mage near a Knight formation is like a jammer near a radio — everything gets static. Both sides hate fighting near Thunder Mages.
**Mana/Aura Management:** Armies manage resources like supply lines. A mana-depleted Mage formation is a dead formation. An aura-exhausted Knight company can't fight. Soldiers rotate between front line and rest positions during prolonged engagements. Alchemical restoratives distributed before and during battle. The side that exhausts first loses, regardless of Tier advantage.
**The Duel Culture:** Single combat between Mages, between Knights, or across paths is a persistent cultural phenomenon. Military officers settle disputes with duels. Mercenaries prove credentials with duels. University students duel for ranking. Tavern patrons duel because someone looked at someone wrong. Duels have informal rules. The winner gets bragging rights. The loser gets bruised pride (or worse). The audience gets a show.
**scan_depth:** 4

## 3. CONTINENT -- AETHMARK

### OVERVIEW -- Continent of Aethmark
**Type:** Continental region
**Geography:** Temperate to cold. Rolling plains, dense forests, mountain ranges, river valleys, rocky coastlines. Harsh winters in the north, mild in the south. Rich in iron, timber, and arable land.
**Culture Base:** European medieval — Germanic, Frankish, English influences. Stone castles, walled cities, feudal hierarchy, knightly orders. Mage universities and Knight training halls are institutionalized. Mage-Knight rivalry is a cultural pastime here — every tavern has an opinion.
**Major Powers:** Kingdom of Valdrennor, The Arcane Concord, The Iron Creed, The Free Cities of Keld, Triarch University (sovereign island nation)
**Population Estimate:** Approximately 18 million. Densest population on Atrum.
**Climate Threat:** Winter wars are the most brutal. Mana regeneration slows in extreme cold. Knight aura costs more to maintain in freezing conditions. Northern campaigns are avoided when possible.
**Unique Feature:** The Universities — Aethmark hosts the world's greatest concentration of formal training institutions. The Arcane Concord operates six universities for Mage advancement. The Iron Creed runs the premier Knight training hall. And Triarch University — on a neutral island between all three continents — trains both paths under one roof and controls the Stone of Eden, the only S-rank awakening artifact on Atrum. Every continental power sends students there. Not going is not an option.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Pragmatic and institutional. Magic is a tool, a resource, and a weapon. Aura is strength, discipline, and power. Both paths should be studied, refined, and deployed effectively. Sentimental or spiritual approaches are tolerated but considered backward.
**Current State:** Border wars between Valdrennor and neighboring kingdoms. Tension between crown authority and university autonomy. Peasant unrest over Tier-bloodline aristocracy. Mercenary companies growing in power. The most "stable" continent, which means it's merely unstable instead of apocalyptic.
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- Kingdom of Valdrennor
**Type:** Feudal monarchy
**Leader:** King Aldric Varen (current reigning monarch — dynamic, can change in play)
**Location:** Aethmark, central-northern territory
**Structure:** Autocratic monarchy with a Royal Council — seven advisors, a mix of Mages and Knights. The King holds absolute authority but rules through the Council's consensus when wisdom demands it. Noble houses hold regional governance. Each house maintains its own Mage-knights and enchanted armaments.
**Population:** Largest kingdom in Aethmark. Approximately 4 million within its borders across multiple provinces.
**Military:** The Valdrennor Combined Guard — 10,000 professional soldier-Mages and 5,000 Knight-errants. Mages organized into chorus-casting formations of 50 by domain. Knights organized into lances of 20 for charge and hold doctrine. Additional 3,000 mounted Knight-errants in heavy cavalry formations. The rivalry between Mage officers and Knight officers is legendary and occasionally physical.
**Domain Policy:** All universities within Valdrennor territory operate under royal charter. Graduates owe five years of military service. Noble houses must submit their spell libraries for royal review every decade. Forbidden application knowledge is confiscated and archived.
**Economy:** Iron mining, enchanted weapon forging, grain agriculture, elemental-inscribed pottery and tools. The Valdrennor arms industry is the continent's largest — their enchanted steel and aura-forged weapons command premium prices everywhere.
**Relations:** Cold war with the Free Cities over trade routes. Alliance-by-convenience with the Iron Creed against Veranthos druidic incursions. Tension with the Arcane Concord over academic independence.
**Internal Tension:** The King's health is failing. Three noble houses have credible claims to succession. The Royal Council is divided. Everyone is positioning. Nobody has moved yet. The Knight Commander and the Grand Mage are barely speaking after a "disagreement" that may or may not have involved a punch.
**Key Institutions:** The Royal Archives (largest spell library in Aethmark), the Forge Districts (enchanted weapon production), the Wall Guard (northern border defense against raids)
**Surprise Types:** SUCCESSION_CRISIS, NOBLE_HOUSE_BETRAYAL, FORBIDDEN_APPLICATION_DISCOVERY, MILITARY_DEFEAT, RIVALRY_ERUPTION
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- The Arcane Concord
**Type:** Mage university coalition
**Leader:** Grand Arcanist Serena Calloway (elected by the Council of Six)
**Location:** Headquartered in Northkeep, operates universities across Aethmark
**Structure:** A coalition of six major universities and dozens of smaller institutions, bound by the Concord Charter. The Council of Six — one archmage from each major university — governs collectively. The Grand Arcanist is first among equals. The Concord is not a nation but wields power like one.
**University System:** Each university specializes:
- Ironhold University (war casting and siege applications) — the most militant Concord school, graduates feed directly into military service across Aethmark
- Thorne University (healing applications and medical magic) — produces the continent's best battle-healers and medical researchers
- Valcircle University (enchantment and preservation) — the premier circle-inscription and enchantment school, their graduates are the most sought-after enchanters in the world
- Northkeep University (combat casting and defensive magic) — the Concord's headquarters university, emphasizes rapid circle tracing under pressure
- Millhaven University (agricultural and industrial applications) — produces the engineers, agriculturalists, and infrastructure Mages that keep kingdoms running
- Stillwater University (divination and information gathering) — the most secretive Concord school, their graduates end up in intelligence services, noble courts, and places nobody talks about
**Political Power:** The Concord trains most of Aethmark's Mages. Every kingdom's court has Concord-trained advisors. The Concord's libraries are the most extensive in the world. Knowledge is their currency, and every nation owes them debts.
**Independence:** The Concord maintains armed neutrality between kingdoms. Each university is sovereign territory — entering one with hostile intent is considered an act of war against all six universities simultaneously. This neutrality is respected because violating it means every Concord-trained Mage in every court turns against you.
**The Knight Question:** The Concord is Mage-only by charter. They don't train Knights. This is a point of friction — the Iron Creed argues that combat training without Knight integration produces Mages who can't fight alongside Knights. The Concord argues that Knights have their own institutions. Both sides have a point. Neither side admits it. Triarch University, which accepts both, just watches the argument like someone eating popcorn at a bar fight.
**Relationship with Valdrennor:** Complicated. The Concord operates within Valdrennor's borders but accepts students from all kingdoms. The King wants more control. The Concord wants more autonomy. The standoff is diplomatic and continuous.
**Rivalry:** The six universities compete relentlessly — ranking tournaments, research publications, breakthrough statistics, graduate placement rates. Ironhold and Northkeep have a bitter rivalry over combat methodology. Thorne and Stillwater compete for the brightest minds (Thorne offers to save lives; Stillwater offers to know things). The annual Concord Exhibition is a week of ranked duels, circle-casting competitions, and research presentations that decides prestige rankings for the coming year. Betting pools are enormous. Alumni take this more seriously than the students.
**Key Members:** Grand Arcanist Serena Calloway (Northkeep), Archmage Theo Brandt (Ironhold), Archmage Lyra Voss (Thorne)
**Surprise Types:** ACADEMIC_DISCOVERY, CIRCLE_BREAKTHROUGH, POLITICAL_EDICT, KNOWLEDGE_LEAK, MAGE_KNIGHT_RIVALRY
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- Triarch University
**Type:** Sovereign educational institution (Mage AND Knight) — neutral ground between three continents
**Leader:** The Triarchy — Archon-Mage Varen Thistle (academic head), Archon-Knight Brin Ashvard (combat head), Archon-Civilian Dessa Moal (administrative head)
**Location:** Triarch Island — a large island dead-center between Aethmark, Veranthos, and Solthea. Not owned by any continent. Accessible by sea from all three. Fortified, warded, and politically untouchable.
**The Stone of Eden:** The reason Triarch exists. An ancient artifact of unknown origin — discovered or created before recorded history — that produces S-rank core solidification upon contact. The only S-rank source on Atrum. Every other awakening stone on the continent produces C-rank at best. The Stone requires four contact ceremonies to permanently lock an S-core. The stone is why all three continental powers signed the Triarch Treaty. Without the Stone, your next generation's best warriors cap at C-rank. With it, they recover in minutes and fight all day. This is not ideological. It is existential.
**The Triarch Treaty:** Signed 800 years ago by all three continental powers. The agreement: Triarch Island is neutral. No nation claims it. No army lands on it. The Stone is shared. Each continent receives 50 seats per year (30 noble, 20 civilian/non-noble). Attempted corruption of the exam or quota system is considered an act of war by all three signatories. Violating Triarch neutrality means your enemies get S-core soldiers and you don't. This threat has prevented more wars than any diplomacy in Atrum's history.
**Admission (The Exam):** Annual examinations held simultaneously across all three continents. Several thousand applicants. 150 accepted (50 per continent: 30 noble, 20 civilian). The exam tests mana/aura control, combat aptitude, and psychological resilience. Proctored by the Triarchy. Guarded by neutral enforcement. Corruption has been attempted. Exposed attempts have started wars.
**The Four Cohorts:**
- **1st Cohort (age 15-17):** First Stone ceremony. Core begins forming S-rank structure. Fragile. Students are fresh, overwhelmed, and hazed relentlessly by upper cohorts. Dropout rate is highest here (~40%). Dropping out means core reverts to unranked — devastating. Many who drop out never recover.
- **2nd Cohort (age 18-20):** Second Stone ceremony. Core is hardening. Recovery noticeably better than C-rank. Students are becoming dangerous. House rivalries intensify. Inter-university competitions begin.
- **3rd Cohort (age 21-23):** Third Stone ceremony. Core nearly solid. Outperforming almost anyone not from Triarch. Senior students — leading exercises, mentoring 1st cohort, running house affairs. The finish line is close.
- **4th Cohort / Final (age 24-25):** Fourth and final Stone ceremony. S-core becomes permanent. Unbreakable. Students graduate with the best foundation on Atrum. They carry this advantage for life.
**Dropout Penalty:** Leaving Triarch before completing all four Stone ceremonies is catastrophic. 1st cohort dropouts lose everything — core reverts to unranked, they must find a C or D stone immediately. 2nd/3rd cohort dropouts retain C-rank at best. Students don't leave Triarch — they endure. The pressure is immense. The 40% first-year attrition rate is mostly survival exams and psychological breakdown.
**The Three Houses:** Students are sorted by temperament and aptitude — not path. Each house contains both Mages and Knights from all three continents. House assignments are for life. Alumni maintain fierce loyalty for decades.
- **House Gryphon** — Martial excellence, courage, strike first. The house of those who fight. Gryphon Knight and Gryphon Mage are both warriors — they just fight differently. Colors: iron gray and red. Best duelists, worst diplomats. Every continent's hardliners end up here.
- **House Raven** — Knowledge, theory, mastery. The house of those who understand before they act. Raven Mages push circle theory. Raven Knights study aura mechanics with academic intensity. Colors: midnight blue and silver. Brilliant theorists, chronic overthinkers. The Solthean scholars and Aethmark researchers compete for dominance here.
- **House Stag** — Duty, protection, service. The house of those who believe power exists to shield others. Stag graduates become commanders, protectors, and the most stubborn people alive. Colors: forest green and gold. Most reliable, most insufferable, most likely to die protecting someone they just met.
**Inter-House Rivalry:** Constant. Monthly rankings based on duel records, research output, and service evaluations. Gryphon dominates combat. Raven dominates research. Stag dominates field evaluations. The annual Triarch Tournament determines house ranking for the year — best dormitories, first course picks, and bragging rights that alumni reference in arguments for decades.
**Continental Tensions Within Walls:** The houses mix continents deliberately. A Solthean Inquisitor's daughter and a Veranthos druidic scout in the same dorm. An Aethmark noble's son and a colonial Marcher sharing a sparring pit. The designers intended this to build cross-continental understanding. In practice, it builds rivalry layered on rivalry — house rivalry, continental rivalry, path rivalry, cohort rivalry. Triarch students don't just compete. They compete on four axes simultaneously.
**Political Neutrality:** Triarch's neutrality is enforced by self-interest, not goodwill. Attacking Triarch means losing Stone access. Every king, every inquisitor, every warchief has family or alumni inside those walls. It is the only place on Atrum where a Solthean priest and a Veranthos druid eat at the same table — not because they like each other, but because neither can afford to leave.
**The Exhibition:** Triarch hosts the annual Atrum Grand Exhibition — a week of ranked duels, circle-casting competitions, aura trials, and team combat tournaments. Every major university and training institution sends competitors. Betting pools are continent-wide. The Exhibition has started three minor wars and prevented at least one.
**Surprise Types:** INTER_HOUSE_RIVALRY, EXAMINATION_CRISIS, BREAKTHROUGH_INCIDENT, FORBIDDEN_KNOWLEDGE_DISCOVERY, TOURNAMENT_CHALLENGE, DORMITORY_DRAMA, MAGE_KNIGHT_SPARRING_GONE_WRONG, STUDENT_POLITICAL_INTRIGUE, CONTINENTAL_TENSION_IN_DORMS, STONE_CEREMONY_GONE_WRONG, COHORT_HAZING, QUOTA_PROTEST
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- The Iron Creed
**Type:** Chivalric military order (Knight-dominant, Mage-integrated)
**Leader:** Commander Hael Strom (elected by the Creed's Circle of Knights)
**Location:** Fortress-monastery at Ironveil Keep, northern Aethmark
**Structure:** A knightly order bound by oath to defend Aethmark's northern borders and uphold the Creed — a code that dictates the honorable use of both aura and magic in warfare. The Creed is majority Knight (roughly 70/30) but maintains Mage specialists for siege, healing, and support. Recruitment is by trial. Survivors join. Many do not survive.
**Philosophy:** Strength is earned, not given. The Iron Creed believes that both paths — Mage and Knight — require discipline, sacrifice, and the willingness to stand between danger and the people you protect. Creed Knights are deadly with blade and aura alike. Creed Mages restrict themselves to simplified combat circles, forcing themselves to win through skill first and magic second. The Creed doesn't care which path you walk. It cares how hard you're willing to fight.
**Role:** The Creed holds the Northern March — the buffer zone between Aethmark's kingdoms and the wilder territories beyond. They fight raiders, beasts, and occasionally each other when disputes arise. They are the wall that keeps the north from spilling south.
**Internal Culture:** Brutal meritocracy. Rank is earned through combat and trial. No inheritance, no nobility within the Creed. A peasant-born Knight who passes the trials commands the same respect as a noble-born one. The same applies to Mages — a hedge-trained Mage who earns their place in the Creed is worth more than a university graduate who skated on family money. This makes them popular with commoners and unpopular with aristocrats.
**Key Members:** Commander Hael Strom (Knight, 6th Tier, aura like a battering ram), Knight-Captain Freya Dain (Knight, 5th Tier, fastest aura in the Creed), Knight-Commander Roth Valen (Mage, 5th Tier, siege specialist)
**Surprise Types:** CREED_TRIAL, BORDER_INCURSION, HONOR_DISPUTE, RECRUITMENT_OFFER, KNIGHT_MAGE_RIVALRY

### FACTION -- The Free Cities of Keld
**Type:** Mercantile confederation
**Leader:** The Coin Council (five elected Merchant-Princes)
**Location:** Coastal Aethmark, southeastern region
**Structure:** Five independent city-states bound by the Keldish Trade Compact. Each city is governed by a Merchant-Prince elected by the merchant guilds. The Coin Council coordinates trade policy, mutual defense, and dispute resolution. No central army — each city maintains its own guard and hires mercenary companies.
**Economy:** The wealthiest faction per capita in Aethmark. The Free Cities control the continent's most important trade routes — both sea and land. Their ports connect Aethmark to Veranthos and Solthea. Everything flows through Keldish harbors. They don't fight wars of conquest — they fund them.
**Mercenary Culture:** The Free Cities don't maintain large standing armies. Instead, they hire mercenary companies — professional soldiers, both Mage and Knight, who sell their services to the highest bidder. The largest companies are small armies unto themselves. The Iron Fang Company fields 2,000 fighters. Loyalty is contractual.
**Magical Industry:** The Keldish enchantment workshops produce the finest inscribed goods in Aethmark — weapons, armor, tools, jewelry, household items. If it can hold a circle, a Keldish artisan can inscribe it. The Keldish aura-forged smiths are equally renowned — their Knight weapons are sought after across all three continents. Their export economy runs on enchanted and aura-forged goods.
**Political Position:** Neutral in the wars between kingdoms. The Free Cities trade with everyone, fight for no one, and profit from all conflicts. This neutrality is maintained through careful bribery, strategic trade denial, and the implicit threat of hiring every mercenary company on the continent.
**Key Members:** Merchant-Prince Orin Savel (Keld Prime), Merchant-Prince Thessa Vane (Port Keld), Captain-General Marcus Dray (commander of the Iron Fang Company, Knight, 4th Tier)
**Surprise Types:** TRADE_EMBARGO, MERCENARY_CONTRACT, SMUGGLING_RING, PORT_BLOCKADE, AUCTION_FIGHT

### LOCATION -- Triarch Island
**Significance:** Home of Triarch University and the Stone of Eden — the most important piece of territory on Atrum. An island positioned dead-center between all three continents. Fortified, warded, politically untouchable. The university and surrounding settlement house ~150,000 people (swells during academic terms). Not owned by any continent.
**The Stone Chamber:** Deep beneath the university, a vault contains the Stone of Eden — an ancient artifact of unknown origin that produces S-rank core solidification. The only S-rank source on Atrum. Four times per year, students file into the chamber for their cohort ceremony. The Stone is guarded by neutral enforcement 24 hours a day. The chamber itself is a perfect circle cut into living rock, with mana circuits visible in the walls that predate any known civilization.
**The Grand Library:** The largest collection of spell patterns, domain catalogues, and magical theory in existence. Seven floors of preserved circles, scrolls, and engraved stone tablets dating back to the First Age. Scholars journey from across Atrum to study here. Students are supposed to be quiet. They are not.
**The Lecture Rings:** Open-air circular amphitheaters where master Mages demonstrate casting to hundreds of students simultaneously. The rings are permanently inscribed with masterwork circles that can be activated for demonstrations.
**The Sparring Pits:** Combat arenas where students practice — Mage vs. Mage, Knight vs. Knight, and Mage vs. Knight. The rivalry is fierce. The betting pool is larger. At least three legendary duels started in the Sparring Pits.
**The Artisan Quarter:** Enchantment shops, inscription services, mana potion brewers, and magical component dealers. Also aura-forged weapon shops — Keldish imports and local smiths. Students spend too much money here.
**The Harbor:** Three major docks — one facing each continent. Ships arrive daily from Aethmark, Veranthos, and Solthea. The harbor is the only place on the island where continental politics are visible. Aethmark traders, Solthean pilgrims, and Veranthos druids share the same dock space. Tensions are constant but contained.

### LOCATION -- Ironveil Keep
**Significance:** Fortress-monastery of the Iron Creed. Northernmost major fortification in Aethmark. Commands the Northern Pass — the only viable invasion route from the wilder territories. ~8,000 garrison, ~30,000 surrounding settlement population.
**The Wall of Circles:** The keep's outer wall is inscribed with hundreds of overlapping defensive circles — barriers, detection wards, alarm triggers, and counter-circle defenses. Maintained by a permanent garrison of circle-wardens. Knight and Mage wardens work in shifts. The wall has never been breached.

## 7. RULES

### RULES -- Core Magic and Aura Rules
**Type:** Immutable system rules for AI GM reference
**Rule 1 — Mage or Knight, Born Not Chosen:** Every person awakens as one or the other at age 12. This crystallization is permanent. No known method exists to change your path. Mage cores form outward casting matrices. Knight cores condense inward. Both are equally valid. Both are equally powerful. Neither is "better."
**Rule 2 — No Element Locking:** Any Mage can learn any domain. Fire, Water, Wind, Thunder, Earth, Light, Shadow, Spirit — all are available to any Mage. No born affinity. No locked elements. You learn what you train. Most Mages specialize because mastery takes time, but cross-domain study is possible for the dedicated.
**Rule 3 — Tier Is Climbed, Not Born:** Everyone starts at 1st Tier. Advancement requires breakthroughs — dangerous core-expansion events with rising mortality. Tiers are earned through risk, not inheritance.
**Rule 4 — Mana/Aura Is Trainable:** Mana depth (Mages) and aura strength (Knights) grow through consistent use. Train to exhaustion, rest, repeat. No shortcuts. No ceiling, but practical limits apply.
**Rule 4b — Core Quality Determines Efficiency, Not Ceiling:** The Stone of Eden produces S-rank cores (deep reserves, minutes recovery, best breakthrough odds). All other stones produce C-rank at best (moderate reserves, hours recovery) down to F-rank (thin reserves, days recovery, near-suicidal breakthroughs). S-core does not prevent reaching any Tier — it makes the climb survivable. A C-core can reach 9th Tier in theory. In practice, few survive the attempt past 5th.

**Type:** Base mortality rates (S-core). C-core and below add roughly +10-20% per rank gap.

Catalysts, enchanted breakthrough circles/aura anchors, nexus sites, and master supervision reduce each rate by roughly half — but these resources are expensive and hoarded by the powerful.
**Rule 5 — Circle Is Required (Mages):** All Mage magic requires a drawn, traced, or visualized circle. No circle = no spell. The circle pattern defines what the mana does. Complexity is limited by Tier. Speed is limited by method. Failure risks backfire.
**Rule 6 — Aura Is Will Made Force (Knights):** Knight aura is projected willpower. It reinforces the body, forms shields and weapons, and intensifies with emotional conviction. A Knight who believes in what they're fighting for is measurably stronger. Aura can't create complex effects — no circles, no spells — just raw, directed force.
**Rule 7 — Domain Is Neutral, Application Is Not:** No domain is inherently good or evil. Fire can warm or burn. Shadow can conceal or kill. Spirit can heal bonds or shatter minds. The domain is a tool. The Mage's intent and the circle's design determine the effect.
**Rule 8 — Forbidden Arts Have Minimum Tier:** Necromancy (Shadow, 6th+), Dominion (Spirit, 5th+), Blood Exploitation (Water, 4th+), Demonology (Shadow/Spirit, 7th+). Below these thresholds, attempting forbidden applications produces no effect or catastrophic backfire.
**Rule 9 — Magic Is Finite:** All magic costs mana. All aura costs aura. Exhausted Mages cannot cast. Exhausted Knights cannot project. Casting/fighting past zero causes permanent core/body damage. There is no unlimited power in Atrum.
**Rule 10 — Enchantments Require Maintenance:** Inscribed circles degrade over time. Stone: centuries. Metal: decades. Wood: years. Cloth and parchment: months. Neglected enchantments fail. Maintenance extends lifespan indefinitely.
**Rule 11 — Breakthroughs Are Dramatic:** When someone breaks through a Tier, it's a scene. The air shifts. Mana/aura becomes visible. People nearby can feel the pressure. This is not a quiet process. Failures are equally visible and equally devastating.

### RULES -- Noble Advantage (Core Quality Changed Everything)
**Type:** Socioeconomic mechanics
**The Stone Gap:** Before the Triarch Treaty, noble houses hoarded C-rank stones. A C-core heir versus a D-core peasant was a generational advantage. The Stone of Eden — S-rank, shared through quotas — changed everything. A peasant's child who passes the Triarch exam gets the same core quality as a king's heir. The new battlefield is admission. Quota seats are worth more than armies. Buying, threatening, or cheating your way into Triarch is the most valuable crime on Atrum.
**Pre-Triarch Tutoring:** Noble children receive private training from age 12-15 before entering Triarch. They arrive knowing basic mana control, circle tracing, or aura shaping. A civilian arrives knowing nothing. Both get the same Stone. The noble still has a head start — just not in core quality. Three years of private tutoring is a real advantage. It's just not insurmountable anymore.
**The Quota System:** Each continent receives 50 seats per year (30 noble, 20 civilian). Every noble house on Atrum fights for those 30 seats. Every civilian who passes the exam is stealing a seat from someone who thought they deserved it by birth. The exam is proctored by Triarch's Triarchy — one Mage, one Knight, one civilian — and guarded by neutral enforcement. Corruption has been attempted. Exposed attempts have started wars.
**Catalyst Economy:** Breakthrough catalysts are still among the most valuable commodities. A 5th→6th catalyst can cost more than a small castle. But core quality matters more than catalysts. An S-core Mage at 4th Tier with a catalyst has dramatically better odds than a C-core Mage at 4th Tier with the same catalyst. Catalysts help you survive. Core quality determines how much help you need.
**The Peasant's Path (Updated):** A C-core or lower Mage or Knight who reaches 5th+ Tier is legendary. They survived breakthroughs that would have killed most others. They earned every step with will, luck, and nothing else. These people have the strongest opinions about inherited privilege and the combat skills to express those opinions without fear. An S-core graduate who reaches 5th Tier is expected. A C-core who reaches 5th Tier is a story people tell.

## 8. ECONOMY

### ECONOMY -- The Magical Medieval Economy
**Type:** Economic system
**Currency:** Gold, silver, and copper coinage minted by kingdom authorities. The standard across Atrum:
- 1 gold sovereign = 10 silver marks = 100 copper pence
- A peasant family lives on 20-30 copper pence per week
- A trained battle Mage or Knight earns 5-10 gold sovereigns per month in military service
- An enchanted or aura-forged weapon from a master smith costs 50-500 gold sovereigns depending on the circles inscribed and aura-forging quality
**Enchanted and Aura-Forged Goods Trade:** The single largest luxury market. Mage-crafted enchanted weapons, armor, tools, and household items command prices orders of magnitude above non-enchanted equivalents. Knight-crafter aura-forged weapons are equally sought after. The Keldish workshops dominate both trades. Smaller operations exist in every city.
**Mana/Aura Potion Industry:** Alchemical preparations that accelerate mana regeneration (for Mages) and aura recovery (for Knights). Produced from magical herbs, mineral extracts, and prepared through specialized circle-activated brewing. Three tiers:
- **Common (2-5 copper):** Mild regeneration boost. Tastes terrible. Every soldier carries one.
- **Military (1-3 silver):** Significant regeneration boost. Standard issue for battle Mages and Knights. Mild toxicity with repeated use.
- **Premium (5-20 gold):** Powerful regeneration with minimal side effects. Produced by specialized alchemists. Armies stockpile these for sieges.
**Scroll Market:** Pre-drawn circle scrolls for emergency Mage casting. Prices range from 5 copper for a basic light scroll to 50 gold for a siege-level combat scroll. The market is growing as armies recognize the tactical value of instant casting without draw time. Knights can't use scrolls — no circle matrix to channel through.
**Mercenary Companies:** Professional military organizations that sell their services. The largest companies field 500-2,000 mixed fighters (both Mages and Knights). Contracts range from garrison duty (cheap) to siege assault (astronomical). The Free Cities are their primary employers but they serve any payer. Company reputation is everything — a company that breaks a contract is blacklisted.
**Resource Control:** Access to magical materials drives economic and military competition:
- **Ironwood (Veranthos):** Wood that naturally holds circle inscriptions. Essential for enchanted staffs and structural enchantment.
- **Sunstone (Solthea):** Solar mana-storing mineral. Powers Solthean defensive enchantments. Strictly controlled by the Radiant Throne.
- **Fire-crystals (Ashspine):** Stored fire-aspected mana. Siege applications. Trade controlled by the Ashwalkers.
- **Mage-silver (Aethmark):** Metal alloy that amplifies circle precision. Essential for high-quality enchantment.
- **Aura-forged Steel:** Specialized alloy that conducts Knight aura without degrading. Essential for Knight weapons. Produced primarily in Aethmark and Keld.
**scan_depth:** 4

### ECONOMY -- Trade Routes and Continental Commerce
**Type:** International trade network
**The Keldish Sea Lanes:** Maritime trade routes connecting Aethmark, Veranthos, and Solthea through Keldish-controlled ports. The Free Cities tax all goods passing through their harbors — the single largest source of their wealth. Disrupting these lanes is a casus belli for every trading nation.
**The Veranthos Land Routes:** Overland trade routes from colonial ports to interior resource extraction sites. Vulnerable to Primal Clan raids and beast attacks. Caravans employ guard-Mages and guard-Knights. Losses are factored into pricing — Veranthosian reagent costs include a "raider tax."
**The Solthean Pilgrim Paths:** Routes connecting holy sites across Solthea. Technically religious infrastructure, practically trade corridors. Pilgrims spend gold on lodging, food, and blessed items. The Radiant Throne maintains circle-warded rest stops along major routes. Merchant caravans use the same paths — the wards protect trade as effectively as pilgrims.
**Smuggling Routes:** Unauthorized trade in forbidden circle knowledge, banned reagents, and contraband enchanted goods. Routes change constantly to avoid the Burning Light, kingdom patrols, and rival smugglers. Vash the Whisper-Merchant knows them all. The profit margins are enormous. The death rate is worse.

## 9. EVENTS

### ARC SUMMARY -- The First Awakening
**Type:** Origin myth / historical event
**Date:** Beginning of the First Age (approximately 3,000 years ago)
**Summary:** The First Awakening is the origin story of magic and aura in Atrum. According to every tradition, humans did not always possess mana or the ability to cast circles or project aura. Something happened. What exactly depends on who you ask.
**The Arcane Version:** A natural phenomenon. Mana always existed in the environment. Humans evolved the ability to absorb and channel it. The first Mage drew a circle by accident and the mana flowed. The first Knight pushed their will outward and the aura answered. The Arcane Concord treats this as a scientific discovery, not a miracle.
**The Divine Version:** God breathed mana into humanity as a gift. The circle is the shape of the divine — perfect, endless, complete. The aura is God's gift of protection — will made manifest to defend the faithful. The First Circle was the first prayer, and God answered. The First Aura was the first act of courage, and God rewarded it. The Radiant Throne builds its entire authority on this narrative.
**The Druidic Version:** The land gave mana to humanity, not a god. The first circles were patterns copied from nature — tree rings, river eddies, the orbits of celestial bodies. The first aura was the first warrior's will meeting the land's response. Magic is not a gift from above. It is a borrowing from below. The land can take it back.
**What Is Known:** Circle magic and Knight aura appeared approximately 3,000 years ago. Before that, humans existed but had no magical capability. The transition was relatively rapid — within a few generations, all humans had mana and the crystallization into Mage or Knight paths began. No one knows why. The First Circle (Solharis) is real and genuinely ancient, but its origin is genuinely unknown. Every tradition claims it. None can prove it.
**scan_depth:** 5

### ARC SUMMARY -- The Necromancer Wars
**Type:** Historical catastrophe
**Date:** 847-863 of the Second Age (approximately 400 years ago)
**Summary:** The most devastating conflict in Atrum's history. A coalition of necromancers, blood mages, and demonologists — collectively called the Hollow Circle — attempted to establish a nation where forbidden magic was legal and practiced openly. They raised armies of undead, used forbidden casting to power war magic beyond normal limits, and opened demonology conduits that summoned entities into the battlefield. The resulting war consumed all three continents for sixteen years.
**The Hollow Circle:** Led by a figure known only as the Pale Architect, the Hollow Circle began as an academic movement arguing that forbidden arts should be studied rather than suppressed. Within a decade, it had become a military force. The Pale Architect developed circle patterns that allowed a single necromancer to control hundreds of undead simultaneously — an unprecedented feat that nobody has replicated since.
**The Alliance:** The war forced an unprecedented alliance between Aethmark kingdoms, Veranthos druidic circles, and Solthean theocracies. Enemies who had fought each other for centuries stood shoulder-to-shoulder against the Hollow Circle. Mage and Knight fought side-by-side in a way that redefined military doctrine. The alliance fractured immediately after victory, but the cooperation during the war established tactical frameworks still used today.
**The Casualties:** Estimated 2-3 million dead across all continents. Entire regions rendered uninhabitable by necromantic residue. Several "dead zones" still exist, avoided by all travelers.
**The Aftermath:** Universal prohibition of forbidden arts was codified across all continents. Enforcement mechanisms — the Inquisition in Solthea, the Concord investigators in Aethmark, the druidic wardens in Veranthos — were created or strengthened. The Pale Architect was never confirmed killed. The body was never found. This haunts every authority on Atrum.
**Legacy:** The Necromancer Wars are the reason forbidden magic is universally taboo. The argument that "it could be used responsibly" died in the ashes of the Hollow Circle's camps. The scars — physical, political, and psychological — are still fresh four centuries later. Every child learns about the Wars. Every Mage is warned. Every Knight is told what they might have to face.
**scan_depth:** 5

### ARC SUMMARY -- The Solthean Schism
**Type:** Religious division
**Date:** 1189-1204 of the Second Age (approximately 50 years ago)
**Summary:** The theological crisis that created the Schismatic movement. A Solthean theologian named Danya published a treatise arguing that all magic — including forbidden arts — originates from the same divine source, and that the Radiant Throne's distinction between acceptable and forbidden magic was a political construct designed to maintain theocratic power.
**The Treatise:** "On the Unity of Magic" circulated secretly for years before the Throne discovered it. The argument was elegant: if mana is God's breath in every person, and if circles channel mana and aura expresses will, then all magical expression is divine by definition. The circle doesn't change based on intent. The aura doesn't discriminate by domain. The sin is in the caster, not the magic.
**The Throne's Response:** Immediate condemnation. Danya was declared a heretic. The Burning Light was deployed to find and execute everyone who possessed the treatise. Danya vanished before capture and has evaded the Inquisition for fifty years — either through exceptional cunning or, as the Schismatics believe, divine protection.
**The Schismatic Movement:** Danya's followers didn't disappear — they went underground. The movement has grown steadily for five decades, particularly among the poor, the marginalized, and people who've witnessed the Inquisition's excesses. The Schismatics don't practice forbidden magic (publicly) — they argue for the right to study it. The Throne considers the distinction meaningless.
**Current State:** The Schism is the defining internal conflict of Solthean politics. The Burning Light hunts Schismatics relentlessly. Schismatic cells operate in every major Solthean city. The movement has spread to Aethmark and even Veranthos — the idea that all magic is one resonates across traditions. Prophet Danya (now called Danya the Wise) is still alive, still hidden, still writing.
**scan_depth:** 5

### ARC SUMMARY -- The Veranthos Colonial Wars
**Type:** Ongoing conflict
**Date:** Beginning approximately 100 years ago, ongoing
**Summary:** The slow, brutal expansion of Aethmarkian settlements into Veranthos. What began as trading posts and fishing villages has become a colonial project extracting magical resources from the wild continent. The Veranthos natives — druidic circles, primal clans, and the land itself — resist continuously.
**Phase One (Trading Posts):** The first century was relatively peaceful. Aethmarkian merchants established coastal trading posts, exchanging manufactured goods and circle knowledge for natural resources. Both sides profited. Cultural exchange was limited but respectful.
**Phase Two (Expansion):** As the value of Veranthosian reagents became clear, Aethmarkian merchant companies pushed deeper. Settlements expanded. Resources were extracted at increasing scale. The land was cleared for agriculture using Arcane circles — efficient but destructive. Druidic circles in cleared areas weakened or died. Knight logging operations pushed deeper than any Mage survey had authorized. The Greenhollow druids protested. They were ignored.
**Phase Three (Resistance):** The Primal Clans began raiding settlements forty years ago. The Greenhollow Circles stopped negotiating and started fighting. The colonial militias couldn't hold the expanded territory. Mercenary companies were hired. The conflict escalated. Beast attacks on settlements increased — the druids claim this is nature's response to desecration. The colonists claim it's coordinated aggression.
**Current State:** A grinding war of attrition. The colonies hold the coast and river routes. The interior belongs to the natives. Every push inland is met with fierce resistance. Every native raid is met with retaliatory strikes. The Ashwalkers remain neutral, trading with both sides. The conflict is slowly escalating. Neither side can win decisively. Neither side will stop.
**scan_depth:** 5

### ARC SUMMARY -- Current State of Atrum (1247 Third Age)
**Type:** Ongoing situation
**Aethmark:** The Valdrennor succession crisis simmers. The Arcane Concord pushes for autonomy while training the next generation. The Iron Creed holds the north against threats that are growing more frequent. The Free Cities profit from everyone's wars. Peasant unrest over Tier-bloodline aristocracy is reaching a tipping point. Something is going to break. Meanwhile, the Mage-Knight rivalry in every tavern has produced at least three bar fights this week alone, and the annual Triarch Grand Exhibition ended with House Valdren winning the combat bracket, House Calloway taking the research prize, and House Ironguard's Knight captain putting Ironhold's champion through a wall during an "exhibition match."
**Veranthos:** The colonial wars grind on. A new Primal Clan warchief — Brynn Fire-Mane — has united more clans than any leader in a generation. Her aura turns red when she's angry, which is every time she sees a colonial settlement. The Greenhollow Circles are debating whether to escalate from defense to offense. The colonial settlers are beginning to demand independence from Aethmark. Three factions, three wars, one continent.
**Solthea:** The Radiant Sovereign is dying. The succession will be contested between Cardinal Voss (who wants to expand the Inquisition's power) and the reformist faction (who want to restrain it). The Schismatic movement is growing bolder. Prophet Danya has released a new treatise. The Burning Light is stretched thin between hunting Schismatics, pursuing forbidden practitioners, and maintaining order during the succession crisis. Something is going to burn.
**The Unanswered Question:** Three thousand years after the First Awakening, no one knows where mana came from. No one knows why humans crystallize into Mage or Knight. No one knows if the division is natural or imposed. No one knows what the Dead Circles in the White Desert are. No one knows if the Pale Architect is still alive. No one knows what God is, or if God exists, or if mana is divine or natural. Every tradition claims to know. None can prove it. The mystery persists. And somewhere, a 10-year-old girl sees futures that shouldn't be possible, a cursed knight refuses to die, and an old man in a bar is about to discover that his latest spell theory actually works.
