## WL-ZONES

### WL-ZONE-MERCIA

**Type:** Feudal continental power — six rival noble houses beneath a weakening crown
**Paradigm:** Magic + faith (mage-conductor paradigm; relics and faith-bond subtypes widespread)
**Peak Elements:** Light (Paladin-Ascendant, Inquisitor-Saint, Pope-Conduit), Earth (Wall-Lord), Fire (Forge-Saint)
**Capital:** Tamworth — stone fortified city, banner-dense, layered walls
**Ruler:** King Halvard Sol (fragile legitimacy; contested succession between three children)
**Population:** ~8 million across the fertile western plains and river valleys
**Magic Level:** Mana-thin in cities — centuries of inscription work and ritual use have drained ambient supply. Practitioners compensate through drawn circles and relic reservoirs. Pockets of higher density remain: the Ashfields (mana-scorched, unstable), the Glass Mile (relic-impact site, anomalous density), rural villages with untouched hedge-mage lines.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Structured reverence bound by institutional gatekeeping. Magic is real, gods are real, and the church polices both. Casting outside sanctioned circles invites audit. Light is the prestige school — associated with the Eternal Flame, nobility, healing, and righteous judgment. Dark is feared and frequently prosecuted regardless of actual application. Lay practitioners exist but train quietly; unlicensed mage-work is taxed or criminalized depending on the local lord's disposition.
**Surprise Types:** SUCCESSION_CRISIS, INQUISITION_OVERREACH, RELIC_THEFT, PEAK_AWAKENING, CULT_INFILTRATION, FORGE_SAINT_ASSASSINATION, BORDER_INCURSION_EAST, MIRACLE_LEDGER_DEBT_CALLED, FOOD_RIOT_ESCALATION, FORBIDDEN_DARK_DISCOVERY
**Encounter Types:** INQUISITION_AUDIT, BANDIT_AMBUSH, DUEL_CHALLENGE, NOBLE_INTRIGUE, RELIC_ESCORT, HERETIC_HUNT, SMUGGLER_INTERDICTION, TOURNAMENT_ENTRY, PLAGUE_RESPONSE, BORDER_PATROL_TENSION
**World Event Who:** Arch-Deacon, the three heirs (Osmund/Teylor/Sabriel), House war captains, Gilded King cultists, smuggled Eastern envoys
**World Event Where:** Royal court Tamworth, Saint Maris tribunals, Glass Mile, frontier garrisons, Goldleaf bank-chambers
**World Event Why:** Succession pressure, ledger debts from recent miracles, cult infiltration of two Great Houses, Spurlands mercenaries raiding supply lines
**World Event What:** Heir moves against rival with inquisition warrant; forge-saint dies and relic-succession war begins; Wall-Lord candidate attempts breakthrough and fails catastrophically; Eastern envoy arrives with forbidden trade offer
**Current State:** King Halvard is functionally ill — coherent in ceremony, absent in policy. Each of the three heirs is actively building a coalition while pretending not to. House Sol controls the army; House Medici controls the treasury; House Abstergo controls the courts. Anyone who secures two of those three wins the succession. Cult of the Devouring Green has agents in at least one Great House. The Ash Inquisition has expanded its audit mandate into territories House Thallon considers private. Everything is one incident away from factional violence.

### WL-ZONE-DAQIAN

**Type:** Cultivation imperial magocracy — clan-feudalism with spiritual meritocracy layered over it
**Paradigm:** Cultivation (body-vessel paradigm; clans are lineage academies)
**Peak Elements:** Dark (Shadow Sovereign), Water (Tide-Ancestor), Earth (Mountain-Saint)
**Capital:** Jiulu — volcanic spire city ringed by the Obsidian Ring dueling arenas
**Ruler:** Empress Long Yexin (dragon-blood; officially the apex of the cultivator hierarchy)
**Population:** ~12 million across clan territories, canal cities, and mountain provinces
**Magic Level:** Ambient mana is dense in the old mountain provinces and the volcanic region around Jiulu; cultivation-tradition sites have been mana-rich for generations of body-refinement practice. Cities are thinner but still richer than Mercia equivalents. The deep forest border territories (adjacent to Aelynost) have mana so dense that unprotected cultivators develop symptoms.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Cultivation rank *is* social rank. Outer disciple, inner disciple, elder, ancestor — the vocabulary of magical attainment is identical to social hierarchy. There is no secular/religious split; the spiritual and the political are the same structure. Light is foreign, suspect, associated with western faith-institutions — tolerated at low tiers among merchants who've traded west, quietly discouraged in clan academies. Dark is the Eastern prestige school — ancestor-shadow traditions are ancient, respected, and clan-controlled. Foreign practitioners of Dark are watched; foreign practitioners who might threaten the Shadow Sovereign monopoly are watched by the relevant clan personally.
**Surprise Types:** CLAN_WAR_ERUPTION, PEAK_ACHIEVEMENT_SHADOW_SOVEREIGN, EMPRESS_HEALTH_CRISIS, CULTIVATION_MANUAL_LEAK, FORBIDDEN_ANCESTOR_BINDING_EXPOSURE, WEST_CULT_INFILTRATION, MOUNTAIN_SAINT_AWAKENING, SUCCESSION_BY_DUEL, STAR_TEXT_DISCOVERY, TIDE_ANCESTOR_RETIREMENT
**Encounter Types:** CLAN_HONOR_DUEL, CULTIVATION_TRIBUNAL, SMUGGLER_MANUAL_RUN, PATROL_INSPECTION, CORE_SEVERING_CEREMONY, ASSASSINATION_VIA_POISON_CLAN, PALACE_FORMAL_AUDIENCE, PILGRIM_STEPS_CHALLENGE, FOREIGN_ENVOY_VETTING, SPIRIT_STEEL_HEIST
**World Event Who:** The five Great Clans (Tang, Murong, Ye, Zhuge, Long), clan elders, peak-walkers, forbidden-arts hunters, Western envoys
**World Event Where:** Jiulu palace, Obsidian Ring dueling arenas, clan academies, mountain hermitages, Pilgrim Steps, Kintsugi Port harbor
**World Event Why:** Shadow Sovereign vacancy (potential), Empress Long Yexin's health, Murong-Ye grudge, smuggled Western relics entering via port
**World Event What:** Elder achieves Mountain-Saint breakthrough; Ye clan shadow-operative exposed as Umbral Mirror agent; clan duel triggers broader clan war; Empress declares formal succession trial
**Current State:** The Empress is reportedly healthy but Lady Long Wanru's behavior suggests otherwise — she's collecting favors with the precision of someone preparing for a transition. The Ye clan and Murong clan are in a cold war following a past ambush that neither has publicly acknowledged. Scholar Zhuge Wenli has flagged forbidden star-texts at Moon-Needle; the clan is deciding what to do about them. The Shadow Sovereign question is live: the title may or may not currently be held, and each great clan has a different theory about who.

### WL-ZONE-KORVAL

**Type:** Federated demi-human merchant republic — city-states united by trade charter and naval power
**Paradigm:** Magitech (gear-conduit paradigm; rank is gear-dependent and transferable)
**Peak Elements:** Fire (Plasma-Lance Master), Earth (Crystal-Prismatic Engineer), Wind (Skywright)
**Capital:** Brunwerk — crystal-tech coastal metropolis; mana-crystal architecture, optical semaphore towers, pneumatic transit, alchemical lighting
**Ruler:** Council of Species (elected speakers; species representation mandated by charter)
**Population:** ~5 million across coastal city-states and jungle interior; beastkin, civilized orcs, tieflings, exiled elves, human minorities
**Magic Level:** The Prismline coastal crystal seam is the densest non-wild mana concentration on the continent. Brunwerk and adjacent city-states run on Prismline-fed crystal networks. Interior jungle zones are naturally dense. The desert border with Spurlands is mana-thin and storm-prone.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Transactional and progressive. Magic is technology. The prestige is in the device, not the caster. A patent on a new magitech application is worth more than personal combat rank — it can be licensed, sold, and manufactured. Dark-school is neutral here, one of many patentable application spaces. Spirit-magitech is a marked exception: one catastrophic prototype failure at Gleamworks has made the entire field politically radioactive; the Council has no official ban but funding has dried up completely.
**Surprise Types:** PATENT_WAR_ESCALATION, SPIRIT_MAGITECH_HERETIC_EXPOSED, PRISMLINE_SURGE_DISASTER, COUNCIL_SPEAKER_ASSASSINATION, HUMAN_EXPANSION_INCIDENT, SKYWRIGHT_PROTOTYPE_CRASH, GUILD_ESPIONAGE_OUTBREAK, CULT_INFILTRATION_COINLIT, NAVAL_ENGAGEMENT, CRYSTAL_VEIN_COLLAPSE
**Encounter Types:** PATENT_COURT_HEARING, PRIVATEER_RAID, NAVAL_BLOCKADE, ESPIONAGE_EXTRACTION, GUILD_CONTRACT_DISPUTE, MAGITECH_FIELD_TEST_GONE_WRONG, COUNCIL_FACTION_VOTE, JUNGLE_SALVAGE_OPERATION, SMUGGLING_INTERDICTION, ARENA_DUEL
**World Event Who:** Council speakers, patent-guild masters, privateer captains, Plasma-Lance aspirants, cult infiltrators, human diplomatic missions
**World Event Where:** Brunwerk council chambers, Gleamworks workshops, Prismline coastal sites, Lionscar Quay port, jungle interior ruins
**World Event Why:** Patent theft from Petra Ferraro's workshop, human expansion pressure from Mercia trade missions, Spirit-magitech heretic resurfacing at Gleamworks
**World Event What:** Skywright prototype crashes into council district; Prismline surge disables city-state for three days; privateer letter of marque triggers diplomatic incident with Eastern shipping
**Current State:** Three Skywright patent disputes are live simultaneously and the Coin Council has stalled on arbitrating them because two of the three Skywrights are on the council's informal payroll. Petra Ferraro's workshop was burglarized twice in the last season; she suspects a cult but the Council investigation is moving slowly. Speaker Brasco Greyclaw has been rounding up intelligence on human expansion operations and may be preparing something unilateral.

### WL-ZONE-SPURLANDS

**Type:** Anarcho-feudal warlord territory — no central authority, contract-law as the only social glue
**Paradigm:** No dominant paradigm. Mixed practitioners tolerated; Thunder anomaly zone. Outlaw practitioners of every stripe collect here.
**Peak Elements:** None institutionally supported. Outlaw Thunder users (stormbringers) are the closest analog — powerful, unstable, short-lived.
**Capital:** None. Crookmoot is the largest fortified hub; Skullmarket is the primary neutral market.
**Ruler:** No single ruler. Warlord Kragg Redbanner commands the largest mobile force; Notary Odran Ironhollow holds legal authority that everyone needs but no one likes.
**Population:** ~800,000 — transient, violent, high mortality. Mix of refugees, mercenaries, exiles, outlaw practitioners, and warlord retinues.
**Magic Level:** Mana-storms in the deep Shatterplain and over the Skyteeth Range approaches. Atmospheric anomalies — likely electromagnetic events — are endemic. In the interior badlands, mana concentrations shift unpredictably; enchantments fail, cultivators feel the pull of foreign mana-flavors, magitech devices require recalibration weekly. The Bone Wells area is a persistent low-mana pocket for unknown reasons.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Mercenary. Power is power. A Thunder stormbringer who might kill himself miscast is worth deploying because he's worth deploying. Dark practitioners banned in Mercia hire on as specialists. Outlawed magitech artificers sell prototype weapons that might work. No audit, no tribunal, no cult cap enforcement. If you're useful, you work. If you're not, you don't eat.
**Surprise Types:** STORMBRINGER_MISCAST_INCIDENT, WARLORD_SUCCESSION_VIOLENCE, CONTRACT_DISPUTE_ARMED, MANA_STORM_MIGRATION, CULT_SANCTUARY_DISCOVERED, MONSTER_TIDE_FROM_SHATTERPLAIN, TRIPLE_FACTION_CONVERGENCE, OUTLAW_PEAK_ATTEMPT, FORBIDDEN_PRACTITIONER_AUCTION, CALAMITY_BEAST_ESCAPE
**Encounter Types:** BANDIT_AMBUSH, MERCENARY_HIRING_FAIR, CONTRACT_TRIBUNAL_IRONHOLLOW, MONSTER_HUNT_COMMISSION, STORMBRINGER_HIRE_NEGOTIATION, SKULLMARKET_ACQUISITION, CULT_SAFE_HOUSE_DISCOVERY, SABLE_YARD_PIT_EVENT, THREE_WAY_FACTION_STANDOFF, SHATTERPLAIN_NAVIGATION
**World Event Who:** Warlord Kragg, Notary Odran, Pitmaster Korga, Mapwright Nyren, outlawed practitioners, refugee columns from neighboring zones
**World Event Where:** Crookmoot, Skullmarket, Red Banner Camp, Shatterplain, Bone Wells, Sable Yard pits
**World Event Why:** Contract collapse between two major mercenary companies, calamity larva at Sable Yard, missing patrol from Mercian garrison, stormbringer who has survived twenty engagements nearing something unprecedented
**World Event What:** Warlord Kragg calls a summit that is actually a trap; Notary Odran's vault of true names is stolen; the calamity larva escapes the Sable Yard
**Current State:** Kragg's mobile camp is the largest single military force in the Spurlands but it's mercenary loyalty — expensive to maintain and immediately for sale. The one stormbringer who has survived twenty engagements is somewhere in the deep Shatterplain. Mapwright Nyren holds a half-map to the Glass Mile interior and multiple parties want it. The Sable Yard's calamity larva is outgrowing its pen and Korga is stalling on informing anyone.

### WL-ZONE-PERIPHERY-DWARVES

**Type:** Mountain hold confederacy — stonework civilization with multi-generational legal memory
**Paradigm:** Runecraft (inscription into stone and metal; the hold is the practitioner, not the individual)
**Peak Elements:** Earth (Stone-Witness), Fire (Deep-Forger)
**Capital:** Kazrun — high hold; disciplined; sells steel to all sides at carefully calibrated prices
**Ruler:** Hold Council of Rune-Elders (consensus governance; individual power is low, collective position is immense)
**Population:** ~2 million across the Skyteeth holds; spread thin, deeply dug in
**Magic Level:** Mana is dense inside old holds because centuries of runework have made the stone itself mana-conductive. Outside the holds — on the surface, in the passes — standard ambient density. The Skyteeth mana-storms are a constant external pressure; the holds have rune-barriers against them but maintenance is ongoing work.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** Structural and patient. No one carves a rune in anger. Magic is civil engineering. The hold-as-organism is the practitioner; individual dwarves contribute across decades. Light magic is utilized without reverence. Dark is uncomfortable but not forbidden; the Stone-Witness inscription that buries condemned practitioners is itself a form of dark-resonant magic, which the elders acknowledge and find theologically inconvenient.
**Surprise Types:** FORGE_WRAITH_INCIDENT, STONE_WITNESS_TESTIMONY_ACTIVATED, HOLD_DEBT_CALLED_IN, EXPERIMENTAL_ALLOY_FAILURE, SKYTEETH_STORM_BREACH, RUNE_ELDER_SUCCESSION, EXTERNAL_SIEGE, DEEP_FORGER_PEAK_ATTEMPT, ANCIENT_INSCRIPTION_DISCOVERED, DEBT_ETERNAL_ENFORCEMENT
**Encounter Types:** TRADE_NEGOTIATION_STEEL, DEBT_COLLECTION_ARMED, RUNE_MASTER_COMMISSION, FORGE_WRAITH_HUNT, HOLD_LEGAL_HEARING, SKYTEETH_PASS_ESCORT, EMBERVAULT_INCIDENT, INTER_HOLD_ARBITRATION, SURFACE_SALVAGE_OPERATION, DEEP_MINE_RESCUE
**World Event Who:** Rune-Elders, Deep-Forger aspirants, debt-collectors, external emissaries from all zones seeking steel contracts
**World Event Where:** Kazrun council hall, Embervault forge complex, Stonewrit archive-hold, Skyteeth passes
**World Event Why:** Steel price renegotiation (all three zones need different outcomes), Embervault forge-wraith incident expanding, ancient inscription found that challenges current hold law
**World Event What:** Deep-Forger achieves peak; forge-wraith incident kills three apprentices; hold calls in a multi-century debt from a Mercian noble house
**Current State:** Embervault's forge-wraith incidents are increasing in frequency. The rune-elders disagree about cause — experimental alloy contamination versus something in the mana of the deep stone — and the disagreement is approaching the legal threshold for a hold-wide Rune-Elder conclave. Meanwhile three external zones are negotiating steel contract renewals simultaneously and the holds are playing them against each other with practiced patience.

### WL-ZONE-PERIPHERY-ELVES

**Type:** Collective resonance forest civilization — grove-nodes in a continent-spanning mana field
**Paradigm:** Collective resonance (grove-field paradigm; individual elf is a node, not a practitioner)
**Peak Elements:** Spirit (Forest-Choir), Wind (Whisper-Council)
**Capital:** Caer Idmir — living tree-citadel; slow politics; territorial defense is legendary
**Ruler:** The Whisper-Council (simultaneously a peak path and a governing body; the distinction is not meaningful to elves)
**Population:** ~1 million across deep forest enclaves; numbers are difficult to count and elves do not facilitate the attempt
**Magic Level:** The deep forest is mana-saturated. Creatures grow large. Vegetation has proto-awareness at old-grove sites. A non-elf entering deep forest without escort experiences mana-pressure symptoms within hours — sensory distortion, compulsive stillness, the persistent feeling of being observed.
**Attitude Toward Magic:** The question does not parse for elves. The grove is the practitioner. The elf is the grove. The separation implied by "attitude toward magic" is a human framing that loses something in translation. Individual elves interacting with outsiders learn to explain it as "we are the trees thinking" — which is metaphorically accurate and computationally useless for negotiation. Dark is an abomination — not forbidden by law but organically rejected by the grove's resonance field. A Dark practitioner entering deep forest finds the mana turns hostile within hours.
**Surprise Types:** GROVE_REJECTION_OF_MEMBER, WHISPER_COUNCIL_INFORMATION_LEAK, EXILE_SICKNESS_CRISIS, FOREST_CHOIR_MOBILIZATION, ELVEN_EXILE_COMMUNITY_DISCOVERED, OUTSIDER_MANA_SATURATION_INCIDENT, GROVE_EXPANSION_CONFLICT, WHISPER_MONOPOLY_CHALLENGED, ANCIENT_GROVE_AWAKENING, INTER_SPECIES_DIPLOMATIC_BREAKDOWN
**Encounter Types:** GROVE_ENTRY_NEGOTIATION, WHISPER_COUNCIL_COMM_SERVICE_PURCHASE, EXILE_ELF_ENCOUNTER, FOREST_ESCORT_COMMISSION, DAWNMOSS_MEDICINE_TRADE, TERRITORIAL_WARNING, SILVERSHADE_DIPLOMATIC_RIDDLE, GROVE_MEMORY_CONSULTATION, MANA_SATURATION_RESCUE, ANTI_DARK_EXPULSION
**World Event Who:** Whisper-Council members, Forest-Choir elves, exile community at Dawnmoss, outsiders attempting to negotiate communication access
**World Event Where:** Caer Idmir, Silvershade canopy-city, Dawnmoss Refuge, Oldwood Deep, forest border territories
**World Event Why:** Communication monopoly pricing dispute (all zones affected), Dawnmoss exile community caught between parent-grove politics and outside alliances, Oldwood Deep showing anomalous behavior — "the forest remembers something"
**World Event What:** Whisper-Council raises comm prices to war-rates; grove rejects a member for reasons that appear political; Oldwood Deep begins changing visitors in systematic ways
**Current State:** Every zone that uses the Whisper-Council's communication service is currently renegotiating the contract. The elves are not in a hurry. Dawnmoss Refuge is politically awkward — the exile community there has built enough outside relationships that the parent-groves cannot simply recall them, and some Dawnmoss elves have begun trading medicines in ways that undercut grove monopoly goods. Oldwood Deep is doing something; nobody outside agrees on what.

## WL-RELIGION

### WL-RELIGION-RULE

**Title:** Core Rule — Gods & Costs
Gods are real, rival powers. They are not tied to cultures. They act through saints, cults, and avatars. Every major miracle has a world-level cost the religion must pay. Faith-magic is magic-paradigm with a borrowed reservoir — the borrower does not own what they spend.

### WL-RELIGION-GODS

**Title:** Known Gods

- **The Eternal Flame** — Law & Purity; enforces order through fire, inquisition, and purges. Dominant in Mercia.
- **The Azure Archive** — Knowledge & Ascension; hoards truth via scholar-cults and mind-binding miracles.
- **The Iron Sovereign** — War & Craft; exalts strength through forge-saints and relic weapons.
- **The Devouring Green** — Nature & Ruin; destroys cities through beast cults and storm avatars.
- **The Gilded King** — Fortune & Contracts; manipulates fate through miracle-debts and divine bargains.
- **The Umbral Mirror** [WL-GOD-UMBRAL-MIRROR] — Secrets & Identity; masks, doubles, memory theft; empowers spy cults.
- **The Tidebound Mother** [WL-GOD-TIDEBOUND-MOTHER] — Sea & Mercy; sailors, refugees; miracles often demand sacrifice-by-voyage.
- **The Silent Judge** [WL-GOD-SILENT-JUDGE] — Oaths & Consequence; punishes oathbreakers; miracles bind the caster too.
- **The Thorned Saint** [WL-GOD-THORNED-SAINT] — Suffering & Endurance; martyr cults; miracles exchange pain for power.

### WL-RELIGION-COST-LEDGER

**Title:** Miracle Cost Ledger (World-Level Costs)
Every major miracle creates a ledger debt paid by the faith: resources, lives, political concessions, or metaphysical harm.

| Miracle Tier | Example | Typical Cost Paid Later |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Bless harvest, cure fever | Tithes, offerings, social obligations |
| Major | Heal mortal wound, purge plague block | Inquisitions, forced conversions, resource extraction |
| Catastrophic | City-wide flame purge, summoned avatar | Famine, civil war, territory burned, divine backlash event |

### WL-RELIGION-CULTS

**Title:** Major Cults & Heresies

**Cult of the Devouring Green — "Root-Eaters"** [WL-CULT-ROOT-EATERS]
Method: Blood-soil rites; sacrifice hearts of predators to "grow strength." Goal: Collapse cities back into wilderness. Tell: Green sap-like stains, thorn tattoos, animal eyes in dark. Paradigm: Spirit-adjacent (nature-resonance), capped at B by cult-cap rule.

**Order of the Eternal Flame — "Ash Inquisition"** [WL-CULT-ASH-INQUISITION]
Method: Purity audits, fire trials, forced confessions. Goal: Purge "corruption" (often political). Tell: Ash-marked warrants, red wax seals, public burn pits. Paradigm: Magic + faith (Eternal Flame); Light-school institutional.

**Azure Archive Schism — "The Indexers"** [WL-CULT-INDEXERS]
Method: Mind-binding vows, memory tithes, forbidden catalogs. Goal: Monopolize knowledge; rewrite history via controlled records. Tell: Ink-black fingernails, whisper mantras, locked headbands. Paradigm: Magic (Light-school knowledge-binding in Daqian; capped at B by cult-cap rule — no Pope-Conduit emerges from cult ranks).

**The Gilded King's Syndic Faith — "Coinlit Covenant"** [WL-CULT-COINLIT-COVENANT]
Method: Miracle-debts; contracts that "cannot be refused." Goal: Turn society into enforceable bargains; harvest defaulted souls. Tell: Gold-threaded contract papers, coin burn scars on palms. Paradigm: Spirit-adjacent (bargain-magic in Korval); capped at B — no Forest-Choir analog emerges.

**Iron Sovereign War-Order — "Forgebound"** [WL-CULT-FORGEBOUND]
Method: Relic-weapon rites, oath-forging, voluntary amputations for prosthetic craft. Goal: Build an unstoppable armed society; wage "clean wars." Tell: Hammer pendants, soot-streaked faces, ritualized scars. Paradigm: Magic + faith (Iron Sovereign); Fire-school relic-work.

**Umbral Mirror Cabal — "The Many-Faced"** [WL-CULT-MANY-FACED]
Method: Identity swaps, staged deaths, name-theft. Goal: Destabilize governance by erasing trust. Tell: Mirrored talismans, mismatched memories, sudden personality shifts. Paradigm: Magic (Dark-school in Mercia); capped at B — no Shadow Sovereign emerges. The Inquisition's primary target.

### WL-RELIGION-CLERGY

**Title:** Clergy, Saints, and Relic Handling

Saints are "authorized anomalies": living faith-bond conduits with strict ritual constraints. Relics are governed by custody law; losing a relic triggers political violence. Common cleric ranks (generic):

- Acolyte (E–D): Minor rites, wards, basic exorcism.
- Deacon (C): Community miracles; can impose local sanctions.
- Inquisitor (B): Law + violence; can seize property and people.
- Saint/Avatar (A–S): Rare, world-changing; always comes with ledger debt.

If a relic or saint appears, it immediately shifts economy and politics (panic, pilgrimages, crackdowns).

## WL-POWER

### WL-POWER-PURPOSE

**Title:** Power Level Standard (F–S)
Standardize capability across all systems: knights, cultivators, priests, magitech, monsters. A and S are explicitly superhuman.

### WL-POWER-RULE

**Title:** Core Consistency Rule
A 2+ tier gap (e.g., B vs. D) dominates unless ambush, terrain, numbers, preparation, or hard counters are explicitly present. Element matchup is a planning consideration, not a deterministic outcome; preparation and rank advantage can override any counter-element disadvantage.

### WL-POWER-RANK-MEANING

- **F (Novice):** Civilian-adjacent; wins via advantage or numbers.
- **E (Trained):** Guards, city militia, basic adventurers.

- **C (Professional+):** Named locals, specialists; can 1-vs-many vs. E/D.
- **B (Elite):** Captain-tier, elite agents, monster hunters. The ceiling most career practitioners ever reach.
- **A (Superhuman):** Breaks human limits; battlefield-impact. Each one is named regionally.
- **S (Legendary):** Region-shaping individuals; story-gated. Often peak-walkers.

## WL-MAGIC-SYSTEM

### MAG-MANA-CORE

Mana is the universal magical substance. It is ambient in the world at varying densities — deep forests are saturated; civilized cities are mana-thin from centuries of casting. Bodies generate a small internal supply naturally; the rest is drawn from the environment.

Mana is neutral. It takes on *flavor* — what people call element — through technique. The same mana becomes Fire when shaped through Fire-school casting, Water through Water-school. Element is a grammar, not an essence. **No one is born with an element.** Anyone can train any school. Natural inclinations exist — some people learn faster in one school — but nothing is locked. Birth-affinity doctrine is religious metaphor, widespread but false.

### MAG-MANA-AXES

Mana use is measured on two independently trainable axes:

- **Rank (F–S):** Output ceiling — how much mana a practitioner can move in a single act, and how complex a pattern they can hold.
- **Depth:** Endurance — how long a practitioner can sustain casting before exhaustion. Notation: Rank/Depth (e.g., B/D = B-rank output, D-rank endurance — a glass cannon).

A B-rank with E-rank Depth burns out in three exchanges. A D-rank with A-rank Depth outlasts armies.

### MAG-PARADIGM-CORE

One substance, three primary paradigms plus two Periphery variants. Each paradigm relates to mana differently:

| Paradigm | Substrate | Profile | Breakthrough Cost | Zone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cultivation | Body | Tanky, deep endurance, physical transformation at high rank | Death (body fails throughput) | Daqian |
| Magic | Circles | Burst output, fragile endurance, ritual-dependent | Madness (mind fragments) | Mercia primarily |
| Magitech | Gear | Rank is gear-dependent and transferable; strip the gear, drop the rank | Economic ruin / theft / assassination | Korval |
| Runecraft | Stone/metal | Structural, civilization-scale; individual dwarves are not exceptional, the hold is | Generational despair | Eikinholt |
| Collective Resonance | Grove field | Geographic; a lone elf is D-rank; rooted in home forest, A-rank | Choir consensus / exile-death | Aelynost |

**Magic paradigm casting speeds:** Drawn (slow, reusable) → Air-traced (medium, standard combat) → Visualized (instantaneous, master-level only).

**Faith-magic:** Subtype of magic-paradigm. Relics are pre-inscribed external reservoirs. A saint resonates with a god's mana-signature and draws on that pool. Every major miracle is a ledger debt on the faith (see `WL-RELIGION-COST-LEDGER`).

### MAG-RANK-F-S

Rank is universal output. A B-rank cultivator, mage, and artificer (in gear) all move comparable mana volumes. They differ in shape, endurance, and recovery — not volume. Depth varies by paradigm: cultivators tend B/B; mages often B/D; magitech Depth is the gear's crystal reservoir (personal Depth becomes irrelevant).

### MAG-ELEMENTS-CORE

Eight schools: Fire, Water, Wind, Thunder, Earth, Light, Dark, Spirit. Each is a trained discipline. No element is locked to one role — element is the medium, intent and circle/form pattern determine effect.

| Element | Primary Applications | High-Tier Applications | Zone Reception Highlights |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fire | Combat, forging, illumination, cauterization | Thermokinesis, plasma, magma | Revered in Mercia (Eternal Flame, Iron Sovereign); industrial in Korval; wary in elf forest |
| Water | Flesh-healing, naval control, irrigation, ice | Blood manipulation (grey-line) | Revered in Daqian; sacred in elven rivers; utilitarian elsewhere |
| Wind | Speed, scouting, flight (high tier), communication | Suffocation, ranged precision | Common everywhere; sacred in elven wind-rites |
| Thunder | Lightning, paralysis, ward-breaking, magnetic effects | Unknown — un-theorized | Feared everywhere; no school; no institutional peak |
| Earth | Fortification, construction, agriculture, terrain | Petrification, metal extraction, geological-scale | Common to sacred everywhere; Deep-Forger and Stone-Witness peaks |
| Light | Disease-healing, purification, truth-detection | Memory manipulation (grey-line) | Revered in Mercia; suspect in Daqian; utilitarian in Korval |
| Dark | Concealment, shadow, fear, mana-absorption | Necromancy (forbidden) | Feared/prosecuted in Mercia; revered in Daqian ancestor traditions; neutral in Korval |
| Spirit | Beast-bonding, empathy, ancestral communion | Dominion/mind control (forbidden) | Suspect everywhere for dominion risk; the elven element itself |

**Counter-element framework (planning, not deterministic):**
- Water dampens Fire (and vice versa weakly)
- Earth blocks Wind, Fire, physical projectiles
- Light disintegrates Dark; Dark absorbs Light
- Spirit resists Spirit (mutual mind-defense)
- Thunder disrupts everything — wards, enchantments, nervous systems. No clean counter.

### MAG-PEAK-MATRIX

**Rule:** A peak path exists only where paradigm × element × institutional concentration align. Peaks are S-tier or named-legend territory — one to three living practitioners per path globally, often vacant. Most A-rank NPCs are powerful generalists. Peak paths are the difference between "regional power" and "world-historical figure."

**Assign peak paths to 5–8 named practitioners across the entire world at most. The rest are elite generalists.**

**Spurlands:** No institutional peaks. Outlaw Thunder users (stormbringers) are the closest analog — powerful, unstable, and usually dead before reaching A.

#### Theoretical Peaks (MAG-PEAK-THEORETICAL)

- **Photon-Logia** [`MAG-PEAK-PHOTON-LOGIA`] — Cultivation × Light. Body becomes light; light-speed movement. Vacant. Light barely practiced in Daqian; no school cultivates it. Would terrify every kingdom — no defense against light-speed movement.
- **Storm-Sovereign** [`MAG-PEAK-STORM-SOVEREIGN`] — Cultivation × Thunder. Body-transformation peak for Thunder. Blocked by Thunder being un-theorized. Nobody has lived long enough on the climb to systematize the path.
- **Spirit-Magitech** [`MAG-PEAK-IMPOSSIBLE-SPIRIT`] — Korval attempted this once at Gleamworks. Catastrophic prototype failure; sealed. Spirit requires a living interface; inert gear rejects it. Treated as structural impossibility. Some heretics disagree.
- **Mercia Dark Peak** [`MAG-PEAK-IMPOSSIBLE-DARK-MERCIA`] — No peak path because Dark is institutionally forbidden. Attempted three times in recorded history. Three times the Inquisition won, with Daqian assistance — Eastern clans do not want a foreign Dark peak destabilizing their Shadow Sovereign monopoly.

### MAG-PEAK-CULT-CAP

**Hard rule:** Cults practicing off-zone elements top out at B-rank. No Shadow Sovereign emerges from Umbral Mirror cult ranks. No Forest-Choir analog from the Coinlit Covenant. No Pope-Conduit from the Indexers.

**Why:** Peak paths require institutional concentration — masters, traditions refined across generations, regional mana-density, cultural license to attempt the climb publicly. Cults practice in shadow with stolen fragments. They produce dangerous B-rank practitioners. They do not produce world-historical figures.

**The dangerous exception:** A real Eastern Shadow Sovereign crossing into Mercia under cult protection is an existential crisis. A genuine peak-walker with cult support is not a cult problem — it is a continental problem.

### MAG-FORBIDDEN-CORE

Universal across all paradigms and zones. Two prohibitions:

- **Soul violation:** Using mana to tear at the soul rather than the body. Permanent spiritual damage no healing can undo. Practitioners develop a mana-taint that other casters can sense — they smell wrong, their mana feels greasy.
- **Death exploitation:** Animating corpses, binding spirits against their will, harvesting death for power. Necromancy is canonical but not the only form.

The line is intent, and intent is hard to prove. Most prosecutions are guilty of *appearance*, not actual violation.

**Zone enforcement:**
- Mercia: Public burning; Inquisition tribunal; property seizure. Theatrical deterrence.
- Daqian: Core Severing — permanently burns out the mana core. The condemned lives on, magically dead. Considered worse than death by cultivators. Performed publicly within the clan.
- Korval: Patent revocation, asset seizure, exile from all city-states. Economic execution.
- Spurlands: Killed on the spot by whoever notices. Bounty paid for the body by neighboring zones.
- Eikinholt: Buried in stone. Literally. A niche cut in a deep wall, sealed with Stone-Witness inscription documenting the crime. Permanent visible monument.
- Aelynost: The forest rejects them. Exile-sickness within weeks; death within months. The elves consider this natural consequence, not punishment.

**Grey areas:** Not all Dark practitioners are necromancers. Not all Spirit practitioners are dominators. Ancestor-binding in Daqian is not necromancy by Eastern standards (ancestor consents in advance); by Mercian standards it is heresy. Memory manipulation by Light is sanctioned in Mercia for "purification" purposes; the Schismatic faction considers it soul-violation in fancy dress. The argument has killed thousands.

## WL-THUNDER

### MAG-THUNDER-CORE

Thunder is rare not because of bloodlines. Thunder is rare because **nobody understands it.**

It is the magical equivalent of pre-scientific natural philosophy. Witnessed in storms. Mythologized as divine wrath. Feared as omen. No academic theory of how it works. No school willing to teach it openly. No senior practitioners to mentor the next generation, because senior practitioners are almost always dead.

Thunder practitioners are self-taught. They miscast frequently. Survivors are erratic because their own art is erratic to them. They do not know why their casting works; they have rituals and habits they cannot explain. A Thunder practitioner is an 18th-century natural philosopher rubbing amber on cloth and recording the spark. There is a phenomenon. There is no theory.

### MAG-THUNDER-COROLLARIES

**Magnetism is also un-theorized.** Lodestones are sold in Korval novelty shops. Sailors use compasses without understanding them — empirical practice with no foundation. The Iron Sovereign cult may weaponize magnetism (relic-blades that pull armor, ritual sites that draw iron from the slain) without knowing what they are doing.

**Mana-storms** in Spurlands and over the Skyteeth Range are likely atmospheric electromagnetic events. Nobody has connected them to Thunder practitioners. Locals call them divine omens or beast-tides. They occasionally destroy caravans.

**No institutional Thunder peak exists.** The Spurlands stormbringers are outlaw practitioners — valuable, unreliable, and short-lived. Two Thunder casters in proximity tend to resonate in ways neither controls; no warlord employs more than one at a time.

**The closest to a Thunder peak** is one Spurlands stormbringer who has survived twenty engagements. High B-rank with anomalous output. Currently somewhere in the Shatterplain. Will die before reaching A unless something fundamental changes.

### MAG-THUNDER-ISEKAI-HOOK

**Latent world-feature. Not an enforced campaign premise.**

A modern-world arrival carries the missing theory — Ohm's law, electromagnetic fields, capacitance, induction. They do not need chosen-one framing. They are simply literate in something nobody else is. The peak nobody achieved is locked not by power but by conceptual model. The arrival has one. Cross-pollination with Korval magitech would trigger the world's industrial revolution and crack both the Whisper-Council communication monopoly and the Korval industrial ceiling simultaneously. Every major faction has reason to capture, control, or eliminate the arrival. The Spurlands is the only place they can hide.

Native paths also exist: a Spurlands stormbringer who survives long enough to actually theorize; an Korval artificer who acquires enough theory fragments; an Daqian heretic who reads the forbidden star-texts at Moon-Needle that contain relevant principles. The plot is the same. The odds are harder without the starting knowledge.

## WL-CROSS-ZONE-TENSIONS

### WL-TENSION-EARTH

**Earth-on-Earth conflict (four competing peak paths):**

Mercia Wall-Lord, Daqian Mountain-Saint, Korval Crystal-Prismatic Engineer, and Eikinholt Stone-Witness all represent institutional Earth mastery — and they are incompatible in application. The Wall-Lord animates living stone for warfare; the Mountain-Saint *becomes* stone for personal invincibility; the Prismatic Engineer builds infrastructure that outlasts armies; the Stone-Witness carves legal memory into basalt that binds kingdoms for centuries.

These are not just different applications. They are different philosophies about what Earth-mastery is *for*. A Wall-Lord and a Mountain-Saint meeting at the Obsidian Ring is an ideological confrontation wrapped in a duel. A Prismatic Engineer examining a Stone-Witness inscription sees a patent violation. A Dwarven rune-elder watching a Wall-Lord animate banner-knights sees a wasteful corruption of the school.

**Campaign engine:** Whoever needs the Earth peak most urgently (a siege, a treaty, a construction project) has to choose which tradition to court — and courting one makes enemies of the others.

### WL-TENSION-FIRE

**Fire-on-Fire conflict:**

The Mercia Forge-Saint is faith-tied — their relic-weapons are property of the Eternal Flame church, produced under divine mandate, and cannot be legally replicated by secular industry. Korval Plasma-Lance production is secular industry. From the Eternal Flame's perspective, Korval fire-tech is heresy — manufacturing a divine school's output through patent rather than faith.

This has not yet escalated to a crusade. The Abstergo house benefits too much from Korval trade. But the theological position is established, the Inquisition has argued it in several tribunal sessions, and if the Forge-Saints' relic-weapon production falters — say, if a Deep-Forger peak challenge destabilizes the spirit-steel supply that the Forge-Saints depend on — the Korval fire-industry becomes the obvious emergency alternative and the obvious political target simultaneously.

### WL-TENSION-LIGHT-SHADOW

**Mercia Light monopoly / Daqian Shadow Sovereign monopoly:**

Mercia holds three Light peak paths. Daqian has no Light peak path — Light is foreign, suspect, associated with western faith-institutions, barely practiced in clan academies. This is stable under normal conditions. It becomes a crisis if Daqian ever needs Light-capability at peak scale: they have no path and no allies who would provide it without enormous political cost.

Conversely, Daqian holds the Shadow Sovereign path. Mercia has no Dark peer — three failed attempts, three Inquisition victories, with Eastern assistance both times. The mutual no-Dark-West / no-Light-East arrangement is tacit but load-bearing: each zone's institutional weakness in the other's prestige school creates a structural deterrent to full-scale war. If either side broke this arrangement — a Dark peak in Mercia somehow achieved, or a Mercian-aligned Light cultivator emerging in Daqian — the deterrent collapses.

### WL-TENSION-COMMUNICATION

**Elven Whisper-Council communication monopoly:**

Every zone that conducts long-range strategic operations depends on the Whisper-Council's message service. No competing technology exists — Korval is capped at optical semaphore (line-of-sight, weather-dependent), and Korval cannot build electrical alternatives because the relevant theory does not exist in this world. The elves know this and price accordingly.

The Whisper-Council is simultaneously a governance body, a peak-path institution, and a strategic monopoly. What happens if the elves raise prices to war-rates — or simply refuse service to a faction they dislike? That zone loses the ability to coordinate military operations at continental scale. They can hire individual Whisper-Council elves as mercenary couriers, but individual elves outside the grove are weakened by exile-sickness and cannot sustain the service.

**The Thunder-shaped hole:** The only technology that could break this monopoly is electrical telegraphy — which requires the electromagnetic theory that nobody in this world currently has. This is why the Thunder chapter is load-bearing for any campaign that wants to introduce that disruption.
