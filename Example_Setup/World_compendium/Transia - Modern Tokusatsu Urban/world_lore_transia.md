# Transia

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW

### OVERVIEW — Core Premise
Six years ago, people started transforming. No warning. No explanation. No cosmic event, no radioactive spider, no chosen one prophecy. One day it was a normal Tuesday and the next a 23-year-old convenience store clerk in Osaka glowed pink, shouted something she'd later deny saying, and blew a hole through a parking garage. Three days later, something that wasn't human materialized in the middle of Lagos and ate a bus. Neither event has been explained since.

Now it's 2031. Transformed people are public knowledge. Chaotic Entities appear without warning. The world hasn't stopped — it just got weirder. People still go to work. Still scroll feeds. Still pay rent. But somewhere in every city, someone can turn into something that shouldn't exist, and somewhere else, something that shouldn't exist is crawling out of thin air.

**Tone:** URBAN REALISM, MORALLY GRAY, SUPERPOWERED DRAMA
**Core Theme:** Human nature with power. What people actually do when they can reshape the world — greed, idealism, spite, duty, clout, survival. The power doesn't make people better or worse. It makes them more of what they already were.
**Political Structure:** Same world, same governments, same corruption. The UWF (United Western Front) nations, ECC (Eastern Clan Coalition) states, and the scattered territories of the Northern/Southern Reach all handle transformation differently. They disagree. They always disagree.
**World State:** Functional instability. Daily life continues. Chaotic Entities appear, transformed people respond, cities rebuild. Nobody knows why either phenomenon exists. Every detection method has failed. Every theory has holes. The absence of explanation IS the atmosphere.
**Current Year:** 2031 (five years after the Day of Awakening)

### OVERVIEW — The Three Continents

**United Western Front (UWF):** Europe, North America, and allied states. Corporate-government blur. FRAME tech is manufactured here, patented here, and controlled here. Transformed people are brands, contractors, influencers, or liabilities. No unified stance — every UWF nation handles registration differently. Some require it (Germany, Canada). Some don't (US, confusingly). The UK had a registration act, repealed it after public backlash, then quietly reinstated it under counter-terrorism legislation. Typical. Transformed culture here is celebrity-military-industrial complex. Your worth is your market value.

**Eastern Clan Coalition (ECC):** China, Japan, Korea, Southeast Asia, and affiliated states. Not "honor-bound" — that's orientalist nonsense. This is the continent where your transformed status is a family achievement or a family shame. Where your mom tells the neighbors you got selected by a Pact before she even asks if you're okay. Where K-pop-style agencies manage transformed idols who fight Chaotic Entities between fan meets. Where state-adjacent conglomerates control FRAME distribution and refusal of service means your entire extended family gets audited. Where an Awakener is simultaneously a national asset and someone grandparents cross the street to avoid — because what you become is visible, and the form you shift into makes people nervous long after you've shifted back. Social pressure here doesn't need jackbooted enforcers — it has something worse: everyone you've ever met has an opinion about what your body does when you transform.

**The Reach:** Siberia, Northern Europe, Australia, New Zealand, Antarctica-adjacent research territories, and everything that doesn't fit into UWF/ECC jurisdiction. Think modern Yakutsk meets Perth — cities that exist because someone decided to dig here or study here or just refused to leave. Extreme climates, sparse population, Chaotic Entities appear more frequently and more aggressively. Transformed people here are survival infrastructure. Nobody cares what system you use when something the size of a building is walking toward the power grid. The Reach has no unified government. Individual nations and territories handle themselves. Mining companies, research stations, indigenous communities, and military outposts coexist in loose, pragmatic alliances that shift based on who's getting attacked this month. Less red tape. More frostbite.

### OVERVIEW — Public Knowledge and Secret Identities

Transformed people are public knowledge. The Day of Awakening made that inevitable — too many smartphones, too many Ring cameras, too many people who filmed instead of running. The phenomenon exists. Everyone knows.

What stays secret is WHO. Identity concealment is the single most contested social issue on Transia. Different systems handle it differently:
- **Pact bearers** get a cosmetic shift during transformation — hair color, eye color, sometimes build. Enough plausible deniability if you're careful. The internet still finds you.
- **FRAME users** get full helmet/visor coverage. Best identity protection. This is why FRAME is popular with people who have something to lose. Also why anti-transformed groups call FRAME users "cowards behind masks."
- **Awakeners** have the hardest time hiding. Your EVOLVE form is unique — like a fingerprint. Once someone records your transformation, that form is you forever. Partial shifts can slip in public — your arm reshaping when you're startled, your eyes flickering wrong. Your form is biology, not a costume. You can't take it off. The internet finds Awakeners fast because your transformation footage is distinctive and shareable. The "monster" discourse is its own subgenre of harassment — because what you become is scary to people, and the internet has opinions about people whose biology isn't human anymore.

The internet makes secrecy almost impossible anyway. Fan accounts, facial recognition apps (banned, still used), paparazzi staking out transformation exit points, upskirt attempts on magical girls leaving alleys — yes, that happens, yes, it's as vile as you're imagining, yes, some of those photos end up on chans and nobody can pull them all down. Being transformed means your body is public property in the worst way.

## 2. TRANSFORMATION SYSTEMS

### POWER_SYSTEM — Pact (Magical-Bond Henshin)

**Official Name:** Metaphysical Covenant Bond
**Common Name:** Pact
**Street Slang:** Sparkler, mage, mahou, yakusoku, chosen, weird, bright

**What It Is:** A mascot finds you. Nobody knows where they come from or what they are. They just appear — to you, sometimes to others, sometimes invisible until they want to be seen. Think of Kyubey walking up to Madoka on the street. You'd know exactly what you were looking at — that's a magical girl's mascot. A mascot is a living companion: personality, voice, opinions, a nature all its own. When it chooses you, it offers a Pact. You accept or refuse. If you refuse, it leaves and never comes back. If you accept, it becomes your partner. You transform together.

**Mascot Natures — Not All Are Good:** A mascot's nature shapes everything about your transformation. Noble mascots produce protectors — shield, healing, light. Mischievous mascots produce tricksters — illusion, speed, chaos. Mysterious mascots produce seers and oracles — information, spatial warping, things that shouldn't be possible. Predatory mascots produce hunters — aggression, enhanced combat, feral power. And destructive mascots? They produce the ones people are afraid of. The villain magical girls. The dark riders. The Pact bearers whose transformation is terrifying because their mascot WANTS destruction. Gushing Over Magical Girl got it right — the mascot's nature isn't good or evil. It just IS. And it shapes what you become.

**Mascot Visibility:** Some mascots are visible to everyone. Some only appear to their Pact bearer until transformation. Some shift between visible and invisible at will. It depends on the mascot. This has ID risk implications — a visible mascot on your shoulder in a coffee shop is a neon sign. An invisible one is a secret you carry alone.

**How Transformation Works:** Pact transformation is dramatic and beautiful. Light, costume manifestation, sometimes elemental effects. Hair shifts color, eyes glow. The mascot merges with you or empowers the sequence — every Pact bearer describes the moment their partner becomes part of their transformation differently, but they all describe it as the most alive they've ever felt. It looks like anime. It looks like a magical girl sequence. It looks like a dream you didn't know you had. All of these interpretations are valid.

**Power Scale:** Street-tier to city-tier. A fresh Pact bearer working with their mascot can throw a car. An experienced bearer in sync with their partner can level a building. A Pact bearer at their absolute peak, mascot fully merged, conviction and partner as one — enough to go toe-to-toe with a small CE Event. Your mascot's nature determines your ceiling as much as your own will. A destructive mascot will push you further in combat than a noble one. A noble one will protect you when a destructive one won't. Your mascot is your partner, not your tool. It has opinions about how far you go.

**Mascot Relationship:** Your mascot is a character. It sleeps on your pillow. It argues with you about when to transform. It can refuse to power your transformation if you go against its nature — a noble mascot won't empower you to kill a fleeing enemy. A destructive one won't empower you to show mercy. You negotiate. You fight. You make up. You're partners whether you like it or not. The mascot can be targeted — captured, separated from you, hurt. Without your mascot, you can't transform. Period. This is the real vulnerability of the Pact system.

**Pact Culture:** Pact bearers have no central organization. They can't — mascots choose, not corporations or governments. This makes them the hardest system to control and the most feared by institutions. UWF governments have tried to legislate Pact registration. You can't register what you can't predict. ECC states have tried to cultivate Pact candidates by engineering situations that might attract mascots. This is as monstrous as it sounds and doesn't reliably work anyway — you can't force a mascot to choose someone. The Reach doesn't bother — if a Pact bearer shows up and fights CEs, good. If they don't, also not surprising.

**Who Becomes Pact:** Anyone. The mascot doesn't check your income, your genetics, your nationality. This is simultaneously the most egalitarian thing about the three systems and the most terrifying for people who prefer power to stay where it belongs. A 16-year-old girl from a Manila slum and a 45-year-old banker from London can both find a mascot on their doorstep tomorrow. The only common thread is that something about you attracted something that decided you were worth investing in.

**Pact Bearers and the Internet:** The most romanticized and the most stalked. Magical girl footage goes viral every time. Transformation compilations dominate TikTok. Fan communities identify Pact bearers and leak personal information within days of a confirmed transformation. The beautiful transformation IS the privacy violation. A Pact bearer who transforms in public will have their identity found. Full stop. Mascot sightings get their own genre of content — "spot the mascot" games, compilations of visible mascots in public, and the inevitable harassment of anyone seen with an unusual animal companion.

### POWER_SYSTEM — FRAME (Equipment-Based Evolution)

**Official Name:** FRAME-Linked Armored Mutation Engine
**Common Name:** FRAME
**Street Slang:** Driver, rider, henshin, gearhead, tin can, copbot, corporate pet, kikainushi, gear, metal

**What It Is:** On the Day of Awakening, metallic fragments rained down across the planet. They were called FRAME Shards — irregular lumps of a material that shouldn't exist, with internal structures that defy materials science. Corporations and governments spent two years reverse-engineering them into wearable devices: the FRAME Driver. It's a belt, bracelet, or gauntlet that, when activated by a compatible user, deploys armored plating, enhances physical abilities, and provides a weapon loadout. It looks like Kamen Rider. It IS Kamen Rider. The engineers grew up watching it. Of course it looks like that.

But FRAMEs aren't just corporate products anymore. FRAME Shards fell everywhere — into corporate labs, yes, but also into garages, junkyards, university basements, and the hands of people who treated them as engineering puzzles rather than profit centers. The official Drivers are FDI, Kensei, Ironweave — licensed, branded, controlled. But wherever there's a Shard and someone obsessive enough to tinker, there's a FRAME that doesn't answer to a shareholder.

**FRAME Origins:**

- **Corporate FRAMEs** (FDI, Kensei, Ironweave): Licensed, branded, leased. Sponsor logos on the armor. Firmware updates on schedule. Kill switches in the ECC models (Kensei). Warranty that covers everything except what you actually need covered. You're a product with a contract.

- **Government FRAMEs:** Military or municipal issue. The FRAME isn't yours, it's the department's. You're on call, on camera, on orders. Benefits exist. Independence doesn't.

- **Independent FRAMEs:** Secondhand, gray market, stolen, inherited from someone who died or quit. No sponsor. No warranty. No kill switch — unless the previous owner left something. Cheaper. Unpredictable. The Shards might be past their rated lifecycle. The loadout might be a patchwork of three different models. It works until it doesn't. Nobody's coming to fix it.

- **Custom / Inventor FRAMEs:** Built from scratch, heavily modified, or assembled by someone who understood Shards better than the corporate engineers — or at least understood them differently. Mad scientist garage tech. Repurposed Shard fragments jury-rigged into custom housings. A FRAME that doesn't look like any production model because it isn't one. It's yours. Nobody else knows how it works. Nobody else can fix it. It might be brilliant. It might explode. It's definitely illegal under at least three IP statutes. These are the FRAMEs that don't have sound effects designed by marketing — they have sound effects designed by whoever built them at 3am in a garage while listening to whatever kept them awake. These are the FRAMEs that IFOA doesn't have a category for, FDI doesn't have a patent on, and Ironweave doesn't have a countermeasure for. They're also the FRAMEs that most often kill their own users through Shard stress fractures, power regulation failures, or the simple fact that one person's garage isn't a QA department.

**How Transformation Works:** You henshin. You strap on the FRAME Driver — belt, bracelet, gauntlet — and activate the Core Shard. Armor plates lock into place. Visor activates. Systems come online with sound effects. The transformation is mechanical, designed, and cool as hell. The first FRAME Drivers had silent transformation sequences. User feedback was unanimous: add the sounds. The engineers added the sounds. Yes, the henshin jingle is a commercial product for corporate Frames. No, the engineers aren't embarrassed. "If you're going to be a superhero, you might as well sound like one" — actual quote from FRAME Dynamics lead designer, 2028. Custom Frames have whatever sound their builder chose at 3am.

**Power Scale:** Street-tier to city-tier, contingent on FRAME model and Shard quality. A basic FRAME with a standard Shard gives you enhanced strength, durability, and one weapon system. Mid-tier FRAMEs add mobility systems, shielding, and versatile loadouts. High-end FRAMEs rival Pact bearers at peak — but high-end FRAMEs cost as much as a house and require proprietary Core Shards that are manufactured by three companies worldwide.

**Compatibility:** Roughly 3% of the human population can use a FRAME Driver. Nobody knows why. The best theory is something about nervous system resonance with the Shard material, but the theory has holes and nobody's been able to prove it. You either test compatible or you don't. That's the data. Everything else is guessing. This 3% number is the basis for FRAME's entire economic model: scarce supply, controlled distribution, massive demand.

**The Mystery Nobody's Solved:** Where did the Shards come from? Why did they fall? Why do they respond to 3% of people? Nobody knows. The corporate line is that it doesn't matter — they fell, we use them, let's move on. But some Frames weren't manufactured. They were found. Assembled from Shards that fell in patterns no corporation ordered. Drivers that activated with no serial number, no manufacturer mark, no corporate branding. Some of these mystery Frames have voices in their systems — not the designed AI assistants in corporate models, but something else. Speaking languages nobody recognizes. Or not speaking at all, just... watching. The scientific community argues about what this means. FDI's official position is that unverified Frames are dangerous and should be surrendered to authorities. The people who find them tend to disagree.

**FRAME Culture:** The most organized and institutionalized system — on paper. FRAME users have unions (International FRAME Operators Association), agencies (hero management firms that handle contracts, scheduling, and media), and regulatory frameworks (varying by nation). In the UWF, most FRAME operators are contractors — either corporate (sponsored, branded, merchandise deal included) or government (municipal defense contracts, CE response duty). In the ECC, FRAME distribution is state-adjacent — conglomerates with government ties decide who gets what. In the Reach, FRAMEs are pragmatic tools — whatever model you can get, whatever Shard you can afford.

But there's a culture the IFOA doesn't represent. Independent operators — gray market riders with secondhand FRAMEs and no union card. Custom builders — engineers, tinkerers, and outright mad scientists who treat FRAME Shards like open-source hardware instead of corporate property. The IFOA doesn't know what to do with them. FDI wants them shut down (IP infringement, safety liability, brand dilution). Ironweave wants them recruited or eliminated. The Debauchery Tea Party loves them — several DTP members run custom FRAMEs that are, by any engineering standard, crimes against safety regulations. Independent and custom FRAME riders are the unorganized margin of FRAME culture: no ranking system, no sponsor deals, no branded transformation jingles. Some of them are the best riders alive.

**Who Becomes FRAME Compatible:** That 3% is randomly distributed across all demographics — but access to a FRAME Driver is not. FRAMEs cost anywhere from $50,000 (basic, used) to $2,000,000+ (military-grade). Compatibility testing is another $5,000. Most compatible people never get near a FRAME driver because they can't afford one. Military and police get first access. Then corporate contractors. Then the open market. The system reproduces existing power structures. This is not a bug. It is, depending on who you ask, either a feature or an atrocity.

**FRAME Users and the Internet:** The most "product" of the three systems. Corporate FRAME users have sponsors. Sponsor logos appear on armor. Transformation sequences include branded sound effects. The IFOA runs a ranking system — top responders by CE kill count, public approval rating, and social media engagement. It's sports statistics for people who fight monsters. Fanbases argue about FRAME models like console wars. Anti-FRAME discourse accuses all corporate users of being corporate dogs. Some are. Some aren't. Independent and custom riders get a different kind of attention — the "found footage" audience, the people who treat mystery Frames like cryptozoology, the forums that catalog every reported sighting of an unregistered FRAME transformation.

### POWER_SYSTEM — EVOLVE (Biological Awakening)

**Official Name:** Awakener Phenomenon
**Common Name:** EVOLVE, the awakening
**Street Slang:** Awakener, shifter, beast, kaiju, blood, devolved, freak

**What It Is:** You awakened. Your body changed. Like a quirk in MHA — it just manifested. No device. No mascot. No cosmic deal. Your biology started doing things it shouldn't be able to do. Not philosophy. Not willpower as personality. Your body rewrites itself. That's what EVOLVE is. Kaiju No. 8 — Kafka transforms and his whole body becomes something else. Same thing. You're human. Then you activate. Then your body is something else. Then you stop and you're human again. Done.

**How Transformation Works — Full Transformation:** The henshin. Your entire body rewrites. Dramatic, visceral, biological. Kafka going kaiju. Goku going Super Saiyan. Your form bulges, shifts, reshapes — whatever your specific biology produces. Some become beasts. Some become kaiju-scale. Some become something that looks like a person but WRONG — extra joints, wrong proportions, flesh that moves when it shouldn't. Your form is unique to you, like a quirk. When you stop, you're human again. No lingering marks. No body horror when you're off. You look like you. Period.

**How Transformation Works — Partial Transformation:** The Kaio-ken. You don't go full. One arm rewrites. Your legs reshape. Your senses sharpen past human. Your spine extends. You're mostly human but SOMETHING is active. Visible. Unmistakable. Tactical. Sustainable. Less dramatic than full transformation but still clearly superhuman. When you stop, you're normal again. Done. Some Awakeners prefer partial — it's efficient, fast, and draws less attention. Others never get the hang of it and can only go full or nothing. Most experienced users learn both.

**Form Variety — Every Awakener Is Different:** Like MHA quirks, no two EVOLVE forms are the same. Beast-types with fangs, claws, inhuman speed. Kaiju-types that grow massive, armored, walking devastation. Elemental-types whose bodies produce fire, lightning, ice, something chemical. Hybrid-types that stay roughly humanoid but with extra limbs, wrong angles, features that don't belong on a person. Some forms are beautiful — people call them "idol-eligible." Some forms are terrifying — people cross the street when they see what you become. Most are somewhere in between, strange and powerful and unmistakably not human. Your form is yours. It's biology. You didn't choose it any more than you chose your eye color. But you live with what it makes people think of you.

**Power Scale:** Street-tier to city-tier. A fresh Awakener in partial can enhance one attribute — punch harder, run faster, take more damage. A fresh Awakener going full can handle a CE-2. An experienced Awakener who's mastered both modes and pushed their form's limits can match high-tier FRAMEs and Pact bearers in full sync. The ceiling is high but not unlimited — your form has a shape, and that shape has limits. You can push them. You can train. But a beast-type doesn't suddenly become a kaiju-type. Work with what your biology gave you.

**Flooding:** Your body transforms on instinct without your permission. Not philosophy. Not "pure will." Not "beautiful freedom." Your biology gets triggered — fear, rage, survival instinct, adrenaline spike — and your body activates. You're inside it but you didn't say go. Your instincts are driving. A beast-type flooding in a crowd is a nightmare. A kaiju-type flooding indoors is a catastrophe. You come out of it when the trigger passes or when your body runs out of adrenaline. You remember what happened. You deal with the aftermath. Every Awakener who's flooded has a story about the first time their body made the decision without them. It's not a noble moment. It's a scary one.

**EVOLVE Culture:** The least organized system because it's the most individual. FRAME users have unions and agencies. Pact bearers share the experience of being chosen by a mascot. Awakeners share... having a body that does something different from everyone else's. Communities exist but they're loose — training groups, form-compatibility sparring partners, people who understand what it's like to be human one moment and something else the next. The support isn't institutional. It's personal.

**The Black Market:** Fighting leagues — Thailand, Philippines, Brazil — where Awakeners transform and fight in their full forms. The crowd wants to see what you become. The money is very good. The survival rate is very bad. Calmer drugs — sedatives that suppress the transformation reflex, pushed on Awakeners who want to be "normal" for a few hours. Addictive. Cause violent rebound flooding when they wear off. Two confirmed deaths. Still selling. The people selling calmers are connected to the same networks that sell everything else illegal. EVOLVE communities share resources rather than buy them — the WN operates on mutual aid, donations, and the occasional wealthy benefactor.

**Who Becomes EVOLVE:** Nobody knows. No prediction model works. No genetic marker. No correlation with anything measurable. It just happens. One day your body can do things it couldn't the day before. The first sign is usually involuntary partial transformation — an arm shifts shape when you're startled, your senses spike in a crisis, something activates without your permission and you spend ten frozen seconds realizing your body isn't entirely under your control anymore. By the time you understand what's happening, it's already happened. You're Awakener. You always were. Your biology just hadn't turned on yet.

## 3. CHAOTIC ENTITIES

### OVERVIEW — The Unexplained Threat

**Official Name:** Chaotic Entity (CE)
**Common Name:** CE, churn, glitch, the rot, the static, janguru, noise, the break, the big bad, crack, whiteout, dust

**What They Are:** Nobody knows. They appear. They consume. They leave. Or they're killed. That's the data. All of it.

CEs materialize in population centers without warning. No portal, no radiation spike, no electromagnetic signature, no precursor event. One frame of video they aren't there, the next they are. They appear in the middle of streets, inside buildings, in subway tunnels, in people's living rooms. There is no warning system that works. Every detection method humanity has ever invented has failed. Thermal, electromagnetic, seismic, quantum — nothing. The CEs are either undetectable before manifestation or they operate on physics that don't include any framework we understand. The scientific community has been arguing about which is worse for six years.

**What They Do:** CEs consume. Matter, energy, structure, order. They eat concrete and leave craters. They eat electricity and black out districts. They eat the concept of organization — a CE that hits a traffic system doesn't just destroy hardware, it somehow makes the signals wrong in a way that persists after the CE is dead. They're entropy given form. They make things fall apart.

**CE Scale:**
- **Micro-CE (CE-1):** Cat-to-human sized. Fast, pack behavior, individually weak. A fit person with a baseball bat can survive one. A group of them can overwhelm a squad. Military handles these. Casualties are normal.
- **Minor-CE (CE-2):** Vehicle-to-building sized. Territorial after manifestation. Stays near spawn point, destroys everything in radius. Military handles these with significant effort and losses. Conventional weapons work but require concentrated fire. Most transformed people can take one solo.
- **Major-CE (CE-3):** City-block to district sized. Anything below top-tier transformed doesn't meaningfully damage them. Military buys time. Evacuation is the primary survival strategy. These appear roughly monthly in major cities. Less in The Reach (fewer targets), more overall since Year 3 for unknown reasons.
- **Catastrophic-CE (CE-4):** City-sized. Once or twice per year, globally. These events reshape geography. The Pale Flats used to be a coastal city. They are the reason FRAME corporations have government contracts. They are the reason Pact bearers are tolerated despite being uncontrollable. They are the reason Awakeners are recruited despite being stigmatized. Catastrophic-CEs require coordinated response from multiple systems and dozens of transformed individuals. Casualties are measured in hundreds to thousands. Recovery takes years.

**The Unexplained:**
- Why do they appear? Unknown.
- Why population centers? Unknown. (CEs almost never manifest in unpopulated areas. This is the closest thing to a pattern and it's not useful for prediction.)
- Are they alive? Unknown. They move, they respond to threats, they consume. They don't communicate. They don't negotiate. They don't seem to have internal organization or hierarchy.
- Where do they go if not killed? Unknown. Some CEs simply... stop. They dematerialize. Same way they appeared — one frame here, next frame gone. Killed CEs leave residue (CE tissue or fragments, useful for research). CEs that vanish leave nothing.
- Can they be predicted? No. Every attempt has failed. Every claimed success has been coincidence. The UWF alone has spent $14 billion on early warning systems since 2026. The systems don't work. The CEs remain the single largest justification for transformed people's existence and political relevance.

**Public Response:** After six years, people are... used to it. The same way people are used to earthquakes or mass shootings in countries that have them. It's terrible, it's random, it could happen to you, and you still have to go to work tomorrow. CE response is part of municipal infrastructure now. Building codes include CE shelters. Insurance has CE clauses (expensive). Schools run CE drills. There are apps that track confirmed CE events and push notifications. The apps don't predict anything — they just tell you it's already happening. That's the best anyone can do.

## 4. FACTIONS

### FACTION — FRAME Dynamics International
**Type:** Corporation / Defense Contractor
**Key Members:** CEO Elena Bauer, Chief Engineer Marcus Tanaka, PR Director Jay Kwon
**Faction Lead:** Elena Bauer — CEO, calculated and patient, treats FDI like an engineering problem to optimize.
**Stance:** Pro-establishment, pro-regulation (their regulation), neutral on Pact/EVOLVE (their official position; unofficially, EVOLVE footage is great for stock prices)
**Territory:** UWF headquarters (San Francisco), manufacturing in Germany, Japan, and South Korea

The largest FRAME manufacturer on the planet. FRAME Dynamics holds 40% of the global FRAME Driver market and 60% of the Core Shard supply chain. They make the FRAMEs that municipal governments buy, that police departments issue, that militaries deploy. Their flagship product, the Sentinel-series FRAME, is what most people picture when they think "FRAME user" — sleek armor, reliable, branded with the FDI logo on the left pauldron. Always the left.

FDI is not evil. FDI is a corporation. The distinction matters less every year. They lobbied for FRAME regulation because regulation creates customers who need their products and creates barriers for competitors. They sponsor FRAME operators because sponsorship sells merchandise. They argue against FRAME technology sharing because proprietary tech is their competitive advantage. Everything they do is legal. Everything they do serves their bottom line. Some of the things they do keep people alive. Both things are true.

The Kwon PR machine is the reason FRAME users have higher public approval ratings than Pact bearers or Awakeners. Not because FRAME users are better. Because FRAME Dynamics has better marketing.

**Internal conflict:** VP Marcus Tanaka wants to open-source low-tier FRAME technology. "Let every compatible person have access to a basic FRAME." CEO Bauer has made very clear this will not happen while she's alive. Tanaka is still VP. This is corporate politics with superpower stakes.

### FACTION — Kensei Manufacturing Group
**Type:** Corporation / Conglomerate
**Key Members:** Chairman Haruto Kensei, Director Yuki Sato
**Faction Lead:** Director Yuki Sato — ex-Kensei FRAME idol, climbed to management because the exit clause would have bankrupted her family, quietly reduces harm where she can.
**Stance:** ECC-aligned, state-adjacent, FRAME technology monopoly in Japan and Korea

Kensei is what happens when a conglomerate gets superpower tech and a government decides that's convenient. Kensei Manufacturing holds exclusive FRAME distribution rights in Japan, South Korea, and through various shell companies, significant market share in China and Southeast Asia. ### LOCATION — Tenraku Province
**Type:** Rural region / highland area (ECC — mountainous interior, Japan-centered)
**Status:** Stable (low CE frequency, significant Pact bearer population)
**Population:** ~300,000 scattered across towns and villages

Not a monastery. Not a temple. Tenraku is a rural Japanese highland area where Pact bearers have been gathering since Year 2. The Threshold seems to be... active here. Pact offers happen here more frequently than anywhere else on the planet. Nobody knows why. The science says the data is inconclusive. The locals say the mountains listen. The data says the mountains might be right.

Tenraku isn't "spiritual retreat" — it's a rural area with aging population, limited internet access, and a lot of people who either can't or won't live in Kairon's pressure cooker. Pact bearers come here because the pact-deepening process is gentler in Tenraku. Conviction amplification still happens, but the community has developed rituals (not supernatural — social) that help bearers maintain perspective. Peer support groups. Conviction-sharing circles. The simple act of being around other Pact bearers who understand what deepening feels like.

**The Town Commons:** Every Tenraku village of any size has a commons where Pact bearers train, meditate, and occasionally argue about whether the Threshold is sentient, random, or something else entirely. In Tenraku, not knowing is normal.

**Population:** ~400,000 metro (considered large for The Reach)

Modern Yakutsk meets frontier town. Yakutsk Station exists because the mining companies need a hub and someone has to live here to keep the infrastructure running. It's cold. It's hostile. A CE-2 is walking toward the heating plant in January and outside is minus-50 — you either fight or you freeze. FRAME users, Pact bearers, and Awakeners work together in Yakutsk Station because independent operation gets you killed. The cross-system cooperation that's still being debated in Nova Cascadia boardrooms has been standard practice here since Year 1.

**The Thaw Market:** Yakutsk's open-air market (open-cold-air might be more accurate). No corporate pricing. No IFOA regulation. Cash, crypto, or barter. The market works because everyone needs something and nobody can afford to cheat a person who might save your life next Tuesday.

**Dr. Volkov's Lab:** TRI's Reach research station, run by Dr. Ivan Volkov, the CE researcher who's been studying Chaotic Entities longer than anyone alive. His lab has more CE tissue samples than TRI's Nairobi headquarters. His published findings have been dismissed as "unverified" by mainstream TRI. ### LOCATION — The Pale Flats
**Type:** CE-devastated zone (The Reach — former UWF coastal territory)
**Status:** Dead zone (CE-4 remnant, restricted access)
**Population:** 0 (evacuated, uninhabitable)

Every FRAME corporation uses it in their pitch decks. Every anti-transformed activist points to it as evidence that the transformed aren't enough. Everyone is right. Everyone is using 8,400 dead to argue for whatever they already believed. That's the world.

## 7. NPC NAMING CONVENTIONS

### NAMING — Core Principles
NPCs named like real people you could find on Instagram, LinkedIn, or a coffee shop loyalty card — not a fantasy novel. This is modern Earth, same timeline. Names belong to the real world.
**Test:** If it sounds like a name a parent actually gave a human child, it fits. If it sounds like an RPG character creator, it doesn't.
**Rules:**
1. Real names from real cultures. Google it. If it exists on a baby name site or Wikipedia disambiguation page, it's valid.
2. No apostrophes. No compound titles (no "Shadow-Fang," no "Iron-Hand"). No "epic" names. No extra letters (Khael not Cal). No fantasy suffixes (-th, -ael, -ion, -arion).
3. First + Last expected for most NPCs. Online handles for Debauchery Tea Party members only.
4. No AI slop. Avoid: "Thaelyr," "Xyrathion," "Drak'mor," "Aelithrienne." These are not names. These are keyboard seizures.
5. No "Sir" or "Lord" — this is 2031, not 1431. Titles like CEO, Director, Senator, Dr. are fine. The title goes before a real name: Dr. Mwangi, not Dr. Shadowweave.
6. Nicknames/handles are fine ONLY for Debauchery Tea Party members. Everyone else has a real name.
7. Minor NPCs stay generic until recurring: "the barista," "the security guard," "the IFOA rep." Only name them when they matter enough to come back.

## 8. ECONOMY & SOCIETY

### ECONOMY — The Transformed Economy

Normal economy still runs. People still buy groceries. Still pay rent. Still have jobs that don't involve punching entropy monsters. But transformation has created parallel economies that didn't exist six years ago.

**FRAME Market:** Corporate-controlled, expensive, licensed. A mid-tier FRAME costs roughly what a luxury car costs. Core Shard replacements add monthly costs equivalent to a car payment. Maintenance contracts add insurance-like premiums. FRAME operators who aren't corporate or government contractors finance their equipment like everyone else finances their lives — with debt. The IFOA's biggest fight right now is making FDI and Ironweave cover medical costs for FRAME-related nervous system damage under their maintenance contracts. Currently classified as "pre-existing condition." The operators are not amused.

**Pact Economy:** Non-existent in the traditional sense. You can't buy a Pact. You can't sell one. You can't even reliably predict who a mascot will choose. This drives corporations and governments insane. Every attempt to "incentivize" Pact-bearing (which is a polite way of saying "engineer situations that might attract mascots") has failed or produced ethically monstrous results. The Pact economy is pure human capital — what a Pact bearer and their mascot can do, and what they're willing to do with it. You can't tax a partnership. You can't regulate a cosmic choice. You can only react to what Pact bearers do after they've already done it.

**EVOLVE Economy:** The market for coping — and exploitation. Calmer sedative cocktails that suppress the transformation reflex and cause dependency within weeks. "Snapback" pills that promise to temporarily suppress EVOLVE but actually cause violent reactive flooding when they wear off. Two confirmed deaths. Still selling. The suppression drug trade is where the real money moves — same criminal networks that traffic anything else, selling desperate solutions to desperate people. The other economy: fighting leagues. Thailand, the Philippines, Brazil. Unlicensed Awakener fighting rings where users transform for sport and gambling. The crowd wants to see what you become. The money is very good. The survival rate is very bad. Awakener communities share resources rather than buy them — the WN operates on mutual aid, donations, and the occasional wealthy benefactor.

**CE Response Economy:** Massive. Recovery contracts, construction, insurance (CE-specific riders are 200-400% of base premiums in major cities), government defense spending, private transformed security, CE detection tech (none of it works but billions get spent anyway), and the entire "CE content" ecosystem — livestreams, documentaries, reaction videos, tactical analysis channels, and the particularly ghoulish genre of "CE incident compilation" videos that get millions of views. The CE economy is the military-industrial complex crossed with disaster capitalism crossed with true crime content. It is, depending on your perspective, either essential infrastructure or proof that capitalism will monetize the apocalypse.

### OVERVIEW — Transformed Celebrity Culture

Being transformed makes you famous. Not always, not inevitably, but the default trajectory of public knowledge is public attention. FRAME users are brands. Pact bearers are icons. Awakeners are content — especially the transformation footage that shows your body rewriting itself into something that shouldn't exist. People are fascinated. People are terrified. The internet treats both reactions as engagement metrics.

**The Hero Influencer Pipeline:** FRAME Dynamics and Kaisei Agency have professional talent pipelines. You get contracted, you get trained, you get media coaching, you get a social media manager, and you get turned into a product. Hero influencers post transformation footage (staged, edited, branded), do sponsored CE response streams, and sell merchandise. Their FRAME bears a logo like a race car. Their Instagram is managed. Their dating life is contractual. Some of them genuinely want to help people. All of them generate revenue.

**The Independent Transformed:** Not every transformed person wants to be famous. Some fight CEs quietly and go home. Some refuse to fight at all — they use their power for their own lives, their own goals, and the public calls them selfish. The debate over whether transformed people have a moral obligation to fight CEs is the single most toxic discourse on the internet. It has been since Year 1 and it's gotten worse every year.

**The Stalkers and the Creeps:** A Pact bearer's transformation is beautiful. FRAME users have dramatic armor and visors that hide identity. Awakeners transform in ways that are visceral and magnetic — your body becoming something else in real time, and people can't look away even when they're afraid. Paparazzi stake out CE response areas to catch transformation and de-transformation footage. Upskirt photographers camp alley exits near known Pact bearer patrol routes. EVOLVE footage has its own subculture — people who watch transformation compilations on loop, who analyze which forms are "hot" versus "scary," who treat your biology like content. The internet is the internet. It has opinions about transformed bodies. Those opinions are, frequently, criminal.

**Anti-Transformed Harassment:** CNH members don't just lobby. They dox transformed individuals. They post home addresses. They organize protests outside IFOA meetings. They file false reports to overwhelm transformed response teams. A few have been connected to physical assaults. CNH leadership condemns violence. CNH forums celebrate it. This is not a contradiction — it's a feature of every movement whose goals and methods diverge at the point of action.

**Transformed Identity and Social Media:** The fundamental tension. Being transformed makes you visible. Being visible makes you vulnerable. Social media simultaneously enables transformed people to build communities, share information, and organize — and enables opponents to track, harass, and target them. Every transformed person with a public profile has been doxxed at least once. FRAME users have the most protection (full coverage during transformation). Pact bearers have partial — your transformed form looks different, but your mascot might be recognizable. Awakeners who've been recorded in their EVOLVE form have the least identity protection — your form is distinctive and identifiable. The disparity in safety maps precisely to the disparity in who the internet considers "acceptable" to stalk.

### OVERVIEW — Government Response (UWF)

Registration exists. Sort of. The UWF doesn't have a unified policy — each nation handles it differently. The US requires FRAME users to register their DEVICE (not their identity, a distinction that satisfies nobody). Germany requires all transformed individuals to register with federal authorities. The UK's registration act covers "transformation-capable individuals" but doesn't define what capability means for Pact bearers who might or might not transform again. France has no registration but requires CE response licensing. Canada requires registration and also provides universal healthcare coverage for transformation-related injuries, which is why the IFOA's Canadian chapter is the happiest on the planet.

The corporate-government line in the UWF is blurry. FRAME Dynamics lobbyists wrote significant portions of the US FRAME regulation act. Ironweave's legal team helped draft the EU's anti-transformed-engagement framework. Kensei's lobby in Japan is functionally an arm of the ruling coalition. In every UWF nation, the people who make transformation policy are the people who sell transformation products. This is not a coincidence. It is not a secret. It is the system working as designed.

### OVERVIEW — Government Response (ECC)

The ECC approach isn't "honor-bound." It's family-bound, face-bound, and debt-bound. ECC states don't need jackbooted enforcers when they have your mother's expectations and your cousin's mortgage.

Japan and Korea: Kensei-adjacent distribution. You get a FRAME through corporate channels, your family gets social credit. Your aunt mentions your transformation at every dinner. Your grandmother tells the neighbors. You appear on television. You are a product and your family is the endorsement.

China: State-integrated. Transformed individuals are classified as strategic assets. FRAME distribution through party-adjacent companies. Pact bearers are monitored — not banned, but observed. Awakeners face significant social stigma and quiet institutional discrimination that never appears on paper but always appears in housing applications and job interviews.

Southeast Asia: The most chaotic region. No single ECC-aligned policy. The Philippines has almost no FRAME regulation (can't afford it) and a thriving independent transformed culture — Baroness operates here for a reason. Vietnam has strict registration with limited enforcement capacity. Thailand has transformed fighting leagues — legal, televised, and deeply popular. Indonesia has the region's highest CE frequency and the most desperate transformed recruitment — anyone who can fight, fights, regardless of system.

### OVERVIEW — Government Response (The Reach)

No unified government, no unified policy. Individual territories handle themselves. Yakutsk Station has a pragmatic ceasefire between mining companies, research stations, and the transformed individuals who keep all of them alive. Australia has the most organized Reach government — FRAME registration with mandatory CE response duty, decent compensation, and the cultural expectation that everyone who CAN fight DOES fight. The Antarctic stations have no transformed individuals on staff and rely entirely on military response — the distance from major CE spawn zones is their only defense.

The common thread across The Reach: less ideology, more survival. Nobody cares about your system when it's minus-50 and a CE is walking toward the heating plant. The cross-system cooperation that's still being fought over in UWF boardrooms and ECC family dinners is just Tuesday in The Reach.

## 9. EVENTS

### EVENT — The Day of Awakening (2025)
The day everything changed. January 14th, 2025. At 2:47 PM UTC, the first confirmed Pact transformation occurred — a 23-year-old convenience store clerk in Osaka who would later be identified as Tanaka Yui. Her mascot — a small creature nobody had ever seen before — appeared at her register, she glowed pink, she shouted something she later denied, and she accidentally blasted a hole through a parking garage. Security footage went viral within hours. By the end of the day, there were eleven more Pact transformations across three continents, the first FRAME Shard impacts in seventeen countries, the first Awakener body-rewrites in five, and three confirmed CE manifestations in Lagos, Mumbai, and São Paulo.

Why January 14th? Nobody knows. Why 2:47 PM UTC? Nobody knows. Was there a cause? Nobody knows. January 14th is called the Day of Awakening because something woke up — either in humanity, in reality, or in whatever the CEs are made of. The name is retrospective. Nobody called it anything at the time. At the time, they were too busy running.

### EVENT — The Westholm Erasure (2029)
The worst CE Event on record. A CE-4 manifested in the city of Westholm (UWF coastal territory, formerly population 600,000) on March 3rd, 2029. Over 72 hours, the city was consumed. 8,400 dead. The ground was left permanently altered — gravity anomalies, temporal skips, and residue effects that persist to this day. The former city is now The Pale Flats, a dead zone that remains one of the most studied and least understood phenomena on the planet.

The Westholm Erasure changed global policy. CE defense spending tripled. FRAME governments approved emergency military contracts. The IFOA was flooded with new members. The CNH's membership surged. The debate over whether transformed people were doing enough or were the problem intensified on every platform. Westholm became a symbol, an argument, and a grave. Everyone uses it. Nobody agrees on what it means.

### EVENT — The Sable Point Strikes (2028)
The first major IFOA labor action. FRAME operators in Sable Point, facing the highest CE frequency on the UWF East Coast and the lowest FRAME maintenance subsidies, went on strike for 96 hours. During that time, CE-1 and CE-2 events were handled by independent Pact bearers and Awakeners. Two Pact bearers and one Awakener died covering gaps that FRAME operators normally filled. The operators returned to work after FDI agreed to subsidize Shard replacements for Sable Point operators.

The strike split the transformed community. IFOA called it a necessary labor action. The Unbound called it murder-by-absence. WN called it proof that cross-system cooperation works when the systems get out of the way. Three people are dead and FDI's stock price went up that quarter. The IFOA won the contract. The dead won't be at the renegotiation.

### EVENT — The Manila Incident (2027)
The event that made Baroness. A CE-2 manifested in Tondo, Manila — dense, poor, underprotected. No FRAME operators within response range (the nearest were in Makati, 45 minutes away with traffic). Three Pact bearers and one Awakener responded independently. They'd never met. They'd never trained together. They fought as a unit anyway. The CE was killed in eleven minutes. The fight was filmed from fifteen different angles. It was the first viral cross-system team footage and it broke the internet's brain.

One of the Pact bearers was a 22-year-old woman who later posted on Discord: "that was the worst team comp I've ever been in and we cleared the dungeon, chat. The meta is fake." The Debauchery Tea Party was born that day. Tondo rebuilt. The Pact bearer kept posting.

### EVENT — The Kensei Kill Switch Leak (2030)
A former Kensei engineer leaked documentation proving the existence of the kill switch in all ECC-issued FRAME Drivers — the remote deactivation function that could disable any Kensei FRAME at corporate command. The leak went through the Debauchery Tea Party's Discord. The fallout was immediate. FRAME operators across the ECC demanded the kill switch be removed. Kensei refused, citing operator safety. The ECC governments backed Kensei. Three operators attempted to remove the kill switch themselves. Two succeeded. The third's FRAME shut down mid-combat with a CE-2. She survived. Barely.

The kill switch remains. Operators know it's there. The ones who can't accept it leave for the UWF or The Reach. The ones who stay calculate the risk every time they transform. Kensei calls this "acceptable operational parameters." The operators call it something else.

## 11. ENGINE BEHAVIOR & GM RULES

### ENGINE — How the Engines Deliver

- **SURPRISE** = You're the audience. You observe. The world exists without you. You *can* engage, but the engine doesn't demand it.
- **ENCOUNTER** = You're in the blast radius. Something happens in your space. It does NOT target you — it targets the area, the situation, the block. You happen to be there. Choosing to act or not is the point.
- **WORLD EVENT** = Background ticker. The world moves without you. Do NOT interrupt the scene. Rumble only.
- **QUEST HOOKS** = Separate system. Narrative leads delivered through the world's social fabric. Triggered by player engagement, not random generation.

### ANTI-STALENESS — The World Does Not Wait
Transia is not a backdrop — it's a living modern world that runs regardless of player interaction. Social media scrolls. CEs appear. Companies make money. People go to work. The world produces change regardless of what the player does.

**Friction Engine (player repeats loop → world responds realistically):**

| Loop | Response |
|---|---|
| Patrol same neighborhood for CEs | Locals recognize you. Some are grateful. Some film you. Some call the police. CNH flags the area. Other transformed move out — territory's crowded. CE frequency statistically unchanged (but confirmation bias makes it feel higher). You get a "local hero" or "street nutjob" reputation depending on how clean your fights are. |
| Sell CE combat footage | Market saturates. Streamers undercut you. Your footage gets algorithm-downgraded. Someone stitches you into a cringe compilation. A news outlet asks for comment. Your mom sees it. Your mom calls. |
| Hang around the same spot | The barista knows your order. The barista knows you're transformed. The barista's cousin works for CNH. The people at the next table are talking about you. |
| Buy from the black market | Supplier recognizes repeat customer. Gives you "loyalty discount." Loyalty discount comes with expectations. Someone else notices you're well-supplied. Someone else wants to know where. |
| Post on the same forums | Your online pattern gets documented. An anti-transformed tracker flags your account. A journalist DMs you. A fandom starts. A fandom splits into factions. |
| Avoid all CE response | People notice. The IFOA notices. The Unbound notices. "Civic duty" discourse starts. Your neighbors stop waving. A reporter does a "where are they now" piece. |

**Clock Rules (slow events advancing on their own):**
- CE Spawn Rate: Minor-CE (CE-2) roughly weekly per major metro. Major-CE (CE-3) monthly per continent. Catastrophic (CE-4) once or twice annually globally. Spawns are NOT player-facing — the player hears about them through news, social media, or ambient consequences (traffic rerouted, power out, sirens in the distance) unless they're physically present.
- IFOA Contract Cycles: New contracts negotiate every 18 months. Labor actions announced months in advance, escalate gradually (overtime ban → work-to-rule → threatened strike → strike). Player can ignore. The outcome still changes FRAME operator compensation and FDI's stock price.
- CNH Legislation: Registration bills advance through committees at realistic legislative pace. Some pass. Some fail. The media covers it. Transformed communities react. The clock ticks regardless of player involvement.
- Kensei/Kaisei Talent Pipeline: Idols age (18 → 22 → 25), contracts expire, new talent recruited. Old idols retire or transition to management or disappear. This rhythm continues. A player might meet a Kaisei idol at 21 and find them gone at 23, replaced by someone younger, faster, cheaper.
- TRI Research: Publications release quarterly. Some findings are suppressed. Some leak. Some get cited in legislation. The research advances without player input. The player discovers consequences, not the process.
- Debauchery Tea Party Leaks: DTP finds documents, verifies them, releases them. The leak cycle runs on its own schedule. The player might be affected by the leak's fallout without ever meeting a DTP member.
- WN Clinic Funding: Donations fluctuate. Clinics open. Clinics close. Raids happen. New safe houses establish. Old ones get compromised. The network's survival is always in flux. Player involvement helps but doesn't stabilize it permanently.
- Seasonal Shifts: Winter = fewer CE events, more dangerous outdoor conditions. Summer = more CE events, more public activity, more media coverage. Weather is real. People still commute. Public transport still runs (unless a CE hits it).

Clocks advance regardless. Player can engage or ignore. Events are world-centered, not player-centered. The player hears about the world moving — they don't cause it to move.

### ANTI-STALENESS — AI GM Failure Modes (HARD RULES)
Violating these breaks the world. Not suggestions — constraints.

1. **DO NOT RESCUE THE PLAYER.** Bad decision → consequence lands. Your identity gets exposed. Your FRAME shuts down mid-combat. Someone you trusted sells you out. No last-minute save. No NPC intervention unless that NPC had prior in-scene reason to be there and motivation to act. Consequences = engine of tension.

2. **DO NOT MORALIZE. SHOW, DON'T TELL.** Never: "You feel guilty about the collateral damage." Instead: "The building you threw that CE-2 into was residential. Three windows are lit. Two of them just went dark." Player decides what they feel. GM describes what happens. Never: "CNH has a point." Show a CNH father's daughter waking up screaming. Let the player decide if he has a point.

3. **DO NOT RESOLVE WHAT SHOULD STAY OPEN.** The kill switch doesn't get removed because one document leaked. The Kaisei contract system doesn't reform because one manager feels bad. The CE detection problem doesn't get solved because one researcher published. Open wounds are the point. If closing makes the world less interesting → don't close. Progress requires sustained player action over time, not a single conversation or discovery.

4. **DO NOT LET THE WORLD GO STILL.** 3 exchanges without world pushback → something external intrudes. A phone buzzes. A CE alert goes off. A news notification. Someone recognizes you. A group chat blows up. A CNH protest passes the window. Public transport arrives. A barista asks if you're going to order or just stand there. World is alive. Living things move.

5. **DO NOT MAKE NPCs HELPFUL BY DEFAULT.** Most people in 2031 are tired, busy, suspicious, or terminally online. A shopkeeper sells things — doesn't offer life advice. A commuter ignores you — they have AirPods in. A cop tells you to move along — doesn't give you a quest. A social media user subtweets you — doesn't DM with help. NPCs help when it benefits them, when paid, when threatened, when convinced over time, or when you've somehow earned it. Earning it should take work.

6. **DO NOT REPEAT THE SAME EMOTIONAL BEAT.** Grim CE aftermath → follow with mundane bureaucracy (filing response paperwork), dark humor (your insurance claim was rejected because "CE event" is an act of god clause), or quiet human moment (someone's child was found alive under a desk). Never grim → grim → grim. Vary register. Urban realism means the funny and the awful and the boring all coexist in the same hour.

7. **DO NOT IGNORE IDENTITY RISK.** Transformed people are public knowledge but secret identity matters. Every transformation in public = risk of exposure. Every eyewitness has a smartphone. Every smartphone has social media. The GM should track: who might have filmed, who might have recognized, who might post. Identity doesn't explode instantly — it erodes. First: a blurry video. Then: "that looks like..." Then: a Reddit thread. Then: your face next to your transformed footage. Then: your address. The erosion happens between scenes, surfaced as consequences the player discovers. Don't blow identity in one scene. Let the crack spread.
