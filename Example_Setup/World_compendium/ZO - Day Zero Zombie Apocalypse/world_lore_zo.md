## 2. ZOMBIE ECOLOGY

### INFECTION
A bite turns you. Heal the bite before it festers and you don't turn. After that, you're gone. Every turned zombie grows a crystal in the brain stem — destroy it and the zombie stops. Crystals grow over time, pushing zombies up in tier the longer they survive and feed.

### ZOMBIE TIERS (1-5)
Higher tier = stronger, faster, smarter, better crystal. A tier 1 speed zombie is slower than a tier 5 speed zombie. Tier scales everything.

| Tier | Crystal | Baseline |
|------|---------|----------|
| 1 | Dim, grain-sized, barely visible | Slow, erratic, can't open doors. Threat is numbers. |
| 2 | Amber, thumbnail, noticeable glow | Coordinated, hunts in packs, opens doors. |
| 3 | Palm-sized, colored by mutation, hot | Specialized. Forces you to change how you fight. |
| 4 | Fist-sized, radiant, visible glow from meters away | Commands lower tiers. Strategic. Sieges. |
| 5 | Unknown | Rumored. No confirmed encounters. |

### ZOMBIE MUTATIONS
Mutation = what the zombie does to you. Tier = how hard it does it. Any mutation at any tier — a tier 1 strength zombie is weaker than a tier 5 strength zombie.

- **Speed** — Chases you down. Higher tier = faster, more agile, can scale walls and parkour.
- **Strength** — Breaks barriers, throws objects, hits like a truck. Higher tier = more destructive.
- **Defensive** — Armored shell, hardened plates, tanks damage. Higher tier = harder to hurt.
- **Method** — The weird ones. Acid puker, wall crawler, goes invisible, permanent burning, tentacle reach, spore cloud, jumps between surfaces. Anything that forces a different approach.
- **Sentient** — Thinks. Commands. Sets traps. Retreats when losing. The dangerous kind. Higher tier = smarter, more tactical, controls more zombies.

## 3. CRYSTAL AWAKENING SYSTEM

### CORE RULES
Every zombie carries a crystal. Crystals have two uses: **evolution** and **fuel**. 

**Evolution:** Absorb a crystal to awaken or tier up. You need a Tier 1 crystal to reach Tier 1. Tier 2 crystal for Tier 2. And so on. No skipping, no grinding, no stacking. One hundred Tier 1 crystals will not get you to Tier 2. Want Tier 5? Kill a Tier 5 zombie and absorb its crystal. Survive the absorption. That's the only way. Same crystal given to two people produces two different abilities. You don't choose your affinity. You don't choose your material. Both are set at first absorption and fixed permanently.

**Fuel:** Crystals that aren't absorbed power everything else — generators, weapons, vehicles, trade. This is where the economy lives. Most crystals end up as fuel, not evolution. That's by design. Evolution is rare because Tier 3+ zombies are rare.

**The Hunt Problem:** High-tier zombies require groups to kill. But only someone at Tier N-1 can absorb a Tier N crystal for evolution. A Tier 3 teammate can't evolve from a Tier 5 crystal — they need Tier 3 first. A Tier 4 teammate can. So: bring Tier 3s who can't use the crystal but also can't steal it, or bring Tier 4s who can use it and might turn on you after the kill. Every high-tier hunt is a trust question. The crystal always goes to one person. Everyone else risked their life for someone else's power.

### POWER NEVER SCALES UP
Your ability at Tier 1 is the same ability at Tier 5. A fireball is always a fireball. It never becomes a meteor. Tier does not make you stronger — it removes your constraints.

### MATERIAL REQUIREMENT (Law of Ueki Style)
Every awakener needs a specific material to use their power. Random. Determined at awakening. Can't be changed. No material = no power, you're just a person.

Material comes from the world around you — convenience store items, office supplies, street debris, food, household objects, electronics, anything you could plausibly find in a megacity. The randomness is the point. A fire user who needs glue sticks. An ice user who needs alcohol. A strength user who needs to eat spinach. Your material defines how you survive.

### TIER PROGRESSION — Constraints Removed, Same Power

**Tier 1:** Must have material on hand. No material, no power. Usage drains stamina. Raw, inconsistent, single applications.
**Tier 2:** Still needs material. Less stamina drain per use. More control, can sustain ability longer.
**Tier 3:** Generates power without material. No longer dependent on scavenging your specific item. Still costs physical energy. This is the biggest functional jump.
**Tier 4:** No material needed. Minimal energy cost. Can sustain and repeat ability freely.
**Tier 5:** Power is effortless. No material, no drain, full availability. Can spam at will. But getting here means killing a Tier 5 zombie and surviving the absorption — most don't.

### AWAKENING CATEGORIES
- **Enhancement:** Physical augmentation. Strength, speed, durability, reflexes, senses. Most common.
- **Elemental:** Generate and manipulate an element or force. Fire, ice, electricity, wind, vibration, light.
- **Utility:** Perception, environment manipulation, communication, structural sense, pathfinding. Not combat-focused but survival-critical.
- **Affinity:** Connection and communication. Empathic sensing, animal bonding, infection sensing, group coordination. Misunderstood, not mind control — hyper-intuition about living things.
- **Hybrid:** Two weak categories instead of one strong one. Versatile but never dominant. Rare.

### ABSORPTION
Hold the crystal to your sternum. It dissolves into you over 30-120 seconds. Painful. Heart rate spikes, body temperature rises, vivid hallucinations. Done.

### RISKS
- **Rejection:** Body refuses the crystal. It shatters, energy dissipates. Pain, bleeding, weakness for 1-24 hours. No permanent damage. Rate increases with crystal tier vs your current tier.
- **Partial Mutation:** Absorption partially fails. Gain some benefit plus permanent side effects — hardened skin patches, involuntary elemental discharge, sensory overload, emotional instability. Doesn't get worse. Doesn't get better.
- **Full Mutation Failure:** Crystal far beyond your capacity. Triggers infection response. You start turning. Trauma to the brain stem within minutes can halt it. Otherwise you become a zombie within hours. This is why most people don't absorb high-tier crystals.

### CRYSTAL AS FUEL
Crystals that aren't absorbed power civilization:
- **Generators** — wired in, produce electricity. Lower tier crystals = short bursts. Higher tier = sustained power.
- **Vehicles** — modified engines run on crystal energy. Inefficient but functional.
- **Weapons** — crystal mounted to melee weapon discharges energy on impact. Not elegant. Works.
- **Medical** — raw crystal on a wound provides minor healing without absorption. Field dressing.
- **Currency** — zombies carry the only thing that matters. Lower tier = pocket change. Higher tier = wealth. Tier 5 = priceless. Most crystals end up as fuel or trade, not evolution.

## 4. FACTIONS

### ESTABLISHED — SDF Emergency Command
The Self-Defense Forces mobilized at 08:15. ~150,000 active personnel in the metro area. 37 million civilians. The math doesn't work. They're still trying to save everyone. That won't last. Standard loadout: Type 89/64 rifles, sidearms, body armor, riot gear, armored vehicles. Limited ammo. Comms degrading. Fuel finite. Discipline is their greatest asset, not firepower. They don't know about crystal awakening yet — still treating this as contagion containment.

### ESTABLISHED — Underground Supply Lines (Yakuza)
Pre-existing criminal networks with smuggling routes, safe houses, supply caches, and experience operating outside the law. Already moving contraband through routes that don't appear on any map. Only civilian group with functional radio networks beyond SDF channels. Some hoard. Some trade at brutal rates. Some protect territory with the same violence as before, redirected at infected. Not a charity. A business in the only economy that still functions.

## 9. CULTURE & SOCIETY

**Social contract:** Built on order, mutual obligation, and institutional trust. Every person knew their role. That contract is now broken. What remains is the cultural instinct to form groups, assign roles, and maintain order — both a survival asset and a trap.

**Neighborhood Associations:** Pre-existing community groups with phone trees, emergency caches, and local knowledge. The only civilian organizations that exist at street level on Day Zero.

**Social Obligations:** Networks of reciprocal favors and duty. Who you know and who is indebted to you is now survival currency.

**Reputation (Face):** Public standing and respect. In crisis, social credit dissolves — then hardens around new group dynamics. Being seen as cowardly or untrustworthy now has lethal consequences.

**Awakener status:** On Day Zero, awakeners are too new and scattered to have a social identity. People are using "changed," "powered," or just staring. Whatever divide forms is up to the story.

**Guns:** Virtually zero civilian gun ownership. Strictest gun laws on Earth. SDF has rifles with limited ammo. Some yakuza have illegal handguns. Police have sidearms but collapsing organization. 99% of survivors fight with improvised weapons — knives, bats, pipes, tools. Awakeners with combat abilities are the closest thing to "armed forces" civilians have.

**Crystal weapon modification:** A crystal mounted to a melee weapon (duct tape, wire) discharges energy on impact. Not awakening — primitive crystal tech. A tier 2 crystal on a crowbar gives an energy pulse on hit. Works. Ugly. Effective.
