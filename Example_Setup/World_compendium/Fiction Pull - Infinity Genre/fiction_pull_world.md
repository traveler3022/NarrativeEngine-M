### OVERVIEW -- The Fiction Pull Phenomenon
**Type:** Unexplained supernatural occurrence
**Start Date:** March 15, 2019
**Nature:** Approximately 1 in 10 people worldwide are Dreamers — individuals who, on a rolling basis, are pulled into constructed dimensional scenarios called Stages. The selection is random, permanent, and ignores age, gender, health condition, or circumstance.
**Selection Mechanism:** Unknown and unpredictable. The 1-in-10 ratio is a statistical observation, not a guarantee. A person who is not a Dreamer today may become one tomorrow. Once selected, you are permanently in the pool. No one has ever been removed.
**Cycle:** Each Dreamer is pulled once every 180 days (approximately 6 months) from their last pull. The timing is individual and staggered — there is no global "Pull day." At any given moment, roughly 1/180th of all Dreamers are entering Stages. That is approximately 4.4 million people per day worldwide.
**Stage Duration:** From the Dreamer perspective, a Stage can last hours, days, weeks, years, or an entire lifetime. From the Earth perspective, the Dreamer is gone for exactly 24 hours. Their physical body disappears from wherever it was resting.
**Body Mechanics -- Entry:** Upon entering a Stage, the Dreamer's body is restored to its prime physical condition regardless of their Earth state. A bedridden cancer patient enters in peak health. An elderly person enters in their 25-year-old body. All injuries, illnesses, and conditions are suspended.
**Body Mechanics -- Exit:** Upon exiting a Stage (if they survive), the Dreamer's body reverts to its Earth state. The cancer patient returns to their hospital bed. The elderly person returns to their aging body. However, all skills, abilities, and items earned in the Stage carry over fully.
**Death:** If a Dreamer dies in a Stage, their Earth body never reappears. They are gone. Cause of death is officially classified as "Pull-related cessation." The body is not recovered. There is no funeral. There is no corpse.
**Survival Rate:** Approximately 90% per Stage overall. New Dreamers face higher risk in early Stages. Veterans face higher genre tiers with proportionally greater danger. The cumulative survival rate over multiple Stages is brutal — a Dreamer who survives 10 pulls has beaten roughly 65% cumulative odds.

### OVERVIEW -- The Stage Formats
**Type:** Scenario classification
**Format 1 -- Solo Random (5-person SoloQ):** Five Dreamers are pulled simultaneously into the same genre/world but each gets their own separate instance. They cannot see or interact with each other. Each faces the scenario alone. The most common format for new Dreamers.
**Format 2 -- PVP Arena (5v5):** Ten Dreamers pulled into a competitive scenario. Two teams of five with opposing objectives. Examples: one team defends a fortress, the other attacks. One team hunts, the other evades. Direct combat between Dreamers is expected. Death is real. Items from eliminated opponents can be claimed by victors.
**Format 3 -- Solo Adventure:** A single Dreamer pulled into a solo scenario. No allies, no teammates. The loneliest and psychologically demanding format. Rare for new Dreamers. More common as genre tier increases.
**Format 4 -- Party Adventure (5-person):** Five Dreamers share a single instance and must cooperate. Can be premade (via Party Maker card) or randomly assembled. The standard cooperative format. Party dynamics matter — a dysfunctional random group can be deadlier than the Stage itself.
**Format 5 -- Raid (10-person):** The largest format. Ten Dreamers in a high-difficulty scenario. Maximum premade size is 5 — the other 5 are always random. Raids feature the most complex objectives, the most dangerous threats, and the highest potential rewards. The premade/random split creates inherent trust dynamics. Veterans call raids "the divorce format" because premade teams frequently fracture under the stress of carrying randoms.
**Format Distribution:** Solo Random (~35%), Party Adventure (~25%), Solo Adventure (~15%), Raid (~15%), PVP Arena (~10%). These are approximate and vary by region and Dreamer tier.
**scan_depth:** 5

### OVERVIEW -- Stage Content and the Fiction Connection
**Type:** Scenario generation rules
**Core Principle:** Stages draw from existing fictional works — movies, books, games, myths, urban legends — as well as custom-generated scenarios unique to each instance. A Dreamer might find themselves in a scenario resembling Harry Potter, Terminator, Jaws, a zombie apocalypse, a Greek myth, or something that has never been imagined before.
**Instance Separation:** Even in group formats, each individual experiences their own version of the scenario world. In a 5-person SoloQ, all five enter the "same" fictional universe but in separate, parallel instances. They cannot interact. What happens in one instance does not affect the others.
**NPC Reality:** The people, creatures, and entities within a Stage are fully real within that context. They have memories, personalities, relationships, and beliefs. They do not know they are in a constructed scenario. For them, their world is genuine and has always existed. Dreamers who form attachments to NPCs face severe psychological consequences upon leaving.
**Objectives:** Every Stage has at least one objective. Kill a specific target. Retrieve an artifact. Survive for a duration. Escape a location. Protect someone. Solve a mystery. Objectives are communicated through intuitive understanding upon entry — the Dreamer simply "knows" what they need to do.
**The Waiting Strategy:** A Dreamer can theoretically wait out certain objectives. If the objective is "kill the governor," and the governor dies of old age in 40 years, the objective completes. The Dreamer ages those 40 years in-dream but their Earth body only loses 24 hours. This strategy is viable but psychologically devastating.
**Fiction Accuracy:** Scenarios based on existing fiction are not perfect recreations. They are inspired by, not copies of, the source material. A Harry Potter-inspired Stage will have magic and wizards but the plot, characters, and rules will diverge. Dreamers who rely on meta-knowledge of the fiction are often caught off-guard.
**scan_depth:** 5

### OVERVIEW -- The Genre Tier System
**Type:** Progression-based difficulty classification
**Core Principle:** New Dreamers face easier, more mundane scenarios. As a Dreamer completes more Stages, the genre complexity and danger escalate. The system appears to evaluate a Dreamer's accumulated capabilities and assigns genre tiers proportionally.
**Tier 1 -- Mundane (0-3 completed Stages):** Basic survival scenarios, simple action sequences, escape rooms, wilderness challenges, natural disasters. Low supernatural elements. Modest rewards. Death rate approximately 5%. Designed to train and filter.
**Tier 2 -- Adventurous (4-10 completed Stages):** Action-adventure, treasure hunts, mystery/thriller scenarios, light sci-fi, light fantasy. Moderate supernatural elements. Good rewards. Death rate approximately 8-10%. The workhorse tier where most Dreamers operate.
**Tier 3 -- Intense (11-25 completed Stages):** Horror, war scenarios, complex fantasy with full magic systems, superhero encounters, political intrigue in dangerous settings. High danger, high rewards. Death rate approximately 12-15%. Where professional Dreamers live.
**Tier 4 -- Nightmarish (26-50 completed Stages):** Supernatural horror (Final Destination type, Ring/Grudge type), cosmic horror, apocalyptic scenarios, time manipulation, reality-warping events. Extreme danger, exceptional rewards. Death rate approximately 18-22%. For elite Dreamers only.
**Tier 5 -- Mythic (50+ completed Stages):** God-tier scenarios, conceptual challenges, multi-genre fusion nightmares, scenarios where the fundamental rules of reality are the enemy. Near-impossible. Legendary rewards. Death rate approximately 25-35%. Fewer than 500 Dreamers worldwide operate at this tier.
**Genre Lock Example:** A new Dreamer will never encounter a Final Destination scenario because survival requires specific abilities and experience that new Dreamers don't possess. The genre "locks" until the system determines the Dreamer has a reasonable chance — though "reasonable" is a generous term.
**Non-Linear Progression:** Genre tier does not increase by exactly one per Stage. A Dreamer who brings back exceptionally powerful items or abilities from a Tier 2 Stage might skip to Tier 4 on their next pull. The system evaluates capability, not experience.
**scan_depth:** 5

### OVERVIEW -- Carry-Over Rules
**Type:** Loot and ability transfer mechanics
**Item Carry-Over:** A Dreamer can bring back any physical object they can personally lift with their own hands at the moment of exit. The object must be disconnected — not attached to the ground, a building, or another fixed structure. A sword, yes. A building, no. A car you can physically lift, yes. A car you cannot lift, no. A piece of a car you ripped off, yes.
**Item Properties:** Items retain all their fictional properties in the real world. A magic wand casts spells. A plasma rifle fires plasma. A healing potion heals. A cybernetic arm can be attached. The real world now contains functional technology and magic from every fiction ever created.
**Skill Carry-Over:** All skills, abilities, knowledge, and physical capabilities earned or learned in a Stage transfer completely. If you learned to cast fireball in a fantasy Stage, you can cast fireball on Earth. If you gained superhuman reflexes in an action Stage, you have them permanently. Your Earth body may be 70 years old but your reflexes are superhuman.
**Skill Independence:** Skills are independent of items. You don't need a wand to cast a spell you learned — the wand is a tool, the spell is internal. However, some abilities are tied to items. A power armor's strength enhancement requires the armor. A spell that requires a catalyst requires the catalyst.
**No Quantity Limit:** There is no known limit to how many items or skills a Dreamer can accumulate across Stages. The only constraint is survival and the physical ability to carry items at exit.
**Duplication:** Items from Stages cannot be replicated by any known Earth process. A plasma rifle brought back from a sci-fi Stage cannot be reverse-engineered. A magic wand cannot be duplicated. Each Stage item is unique unless multiple Dreamers bring back similar items independently.
**scan_depth:** 5

### OVERVIEW -- Key Stage Items
**Type:** Special consumable items
**Party Maker Card:** A card-sized item that appears in Stages across all tiers. Moderately rare but consistently present. When touched on Earth, it registers the Dreamer's name and biome. Registered Dreamers whose 180-day timers align are grouped together for premade Party Adventures or Raids. Essential for organized teams. Traded actively on both legal and black markets.
**Return Ticket:** Extremely rare. Allows a Dreamer to exit a Stage before completing the objective. The Dreamer returns to Earth safely but carries nothing — no items, no skills earned during that Stage. Skills from previous Stages are retained. The item is consumed on use. Black market price for a confirmed Return Ticket exceeds $500,000. Governments hoard them for high-value Dreamers.
**Revival Token:** Legendary rarity. If a Dreamer dies in a Stage while carrying this token, they are restored to life at the moment of death with full health. The token is consumed. Some veteran Dreamers spend years searching for one. Fewer than 100 are confirmed to exist on Earth. Priceless.
**Memory Seal:** A consumable item that permanently erases the Dreamer's memories of one Stage. Used by those who survived psychologically devastating scenarios. Controversial — some argue it's mercy, others argue it removes crucial experience. Available in Tier 3+ Stages.
**Translator Seed:** An implantable item from sci-fi Stages. Permanently grants the ability to understand and speak any language. One of the most consistently valuable Stage items for corporate and intelligence Dreamers.
**scan_depth:** 4

### OVERVIEW -- Dreamer Population and Statistics
**Type:** Demographic reference
**Total Dreamers:** Approximately 800 million worldwide (1 in 10 ratio, observed globally)
**New Selections Per Day:** Roughly 1.2 million people are selected as new Dreamers daily. Most do not realize they've been "tagged" until their first pull occurs.
**Daily Stage Entries:** Approximately 4.4 million Dreamers enter Stages daily (1/180th of total)
**Daily Stage Deaths:** Approximately 440,000 Dreamers die in Stages daily worldwide. That is roughly 160 million deaths per year from Pull-related causes — the single largest cause of death on Earth.
**Cumulative Survival:** A Dreamer who survives 5 Stages has beaten approximately 59% cumulative odds. At 10 Stages, 35%. At 20 Stages, 12%. Veterans are rare because the math is merciless.
**Active Veterans (20+ Stages):** Fewer than 5 million worldwide. These individuals carry immense accumulated power and items.
**Elite Dreamers (50+ Stages):** Fewer than 500 worldwide. These individuals are walking strategic assets. Governments and corporations will do anything to recruit — or control — them.
**The Fiction Pull has been the leading cause of death globally since mid-2019.** It killed more people in its first year than any war, plague, or natural disaster in history. The world has not recovered psychologically.

### OVERVIEW -- The Time Dilation Problem
**Type:** Psychological and social crisis
**The Problem:** Stages can last subjective years, decades, or entire lifetimes. A Dreamer who spends 40 years in a Stage comes back the next morning. Their spouse went to bed last night. Their children haven't aged. The Dreamer has lived an entire second life.
**Psychological Impact:** Time-dilated Dreamers return fundamentally changed. They may have formed relationships, raised families, fought wars, and experienced decades of subjective life — all in 24 hours. The return to Earth is described as "waking from a life into a dream." Suicide rates among highly time-dilated Dreamers exceed 30%.
**Identity Fragmentation:** Dreamers who've lived multiple lifetimes across Stages struggle with identity. Which life is real? The 30-year-old on Earth, or the 80-year-old who died peacefully in a Stage? Support groups exist. They help some.
**Addiction:** Some Dreamers become addicted to the time dilation. They prefer their Stage lives to Earth. They complete objectives slowly, savoring decades of subjective peace. The real world becomes the waiting room. These individuals are called "Dwellers" and are viewed with a mixture of pity and fear.
**The Old Souls:** Veterans who've accumulated centuries of subjective time. They move differently. They speak differently. They look at the world with eyes that have seen empires rise and fall within the span of a Pull. They are the most capable and the most damaged people alive.
**Legal Implications:** If a Dreamer marries someone in a Stage, is that marriage legally binding on Earth? Courts have ruled no — but the Dreamer's grief is real. Inheritance, child custody, and mental competency cases involving time-dilated Dreamers clog courts worldwide.
**scan_depth:** 5

### OVERVIEW -- The Search for Cures
**Type:** Socioeconomic phenomenon
**The Problem:** Because entering a Stage restores the body to prime condition but exiting reverts it, ill and disabled Dreamers are uniquely motivated. They enter each Stage healthy and capable but return to their broken Earth bodies. The Stage is a temporary paradise of ability.
**The Cure Economy:** Medical items from Stages are the single most valuable commodity category. A cure-all pill, a regeneration device, a healing artifact — these items can command nine-figure prices. The desperate and the wealthy compete in the same market.
**Cure Hunters:** A subclass of Dreamer who specifically targets Stages likely to contain medical items. They optimize their builds for survival rather than combat. They trade combat effectiveness for the chance to bring back something that heals.
**The Lagos Miracle:** In 2021, a Dreamer brought back a device that could cure any disease. It was stolen within 48 hours. It has changed hands through theft, purchase, and warfare dozens of times since. It has never been replicated. Its current location is unknown.
**The Unethical Market:** Organized groups send Dreamers into Stages to bring back healing items, then charge astronomical prices for their use. Critics call it extortion. Defenders call it capitalism. The dying call it hope at gunpoint.
**Bedridden Dreamers:** Terminally ill or severely disabled individuals who are also Dreamers. They are the most motivated participants — each Stage is a chance to find a permanent cure. They are also the most vulnerable, as their Earth condition limits their ability to prepare, train, or maintain physical fitness between pulls.
**scan_depth:** 4

## 2. NORTH AMERICA

### OVERVIEW -- North American Region
**Type:** Continental region
**Geography:** United States, Canada, Mexico
**Culture Base:** Corporate-driven Dreamer integration. Heavy privatization of Dreamer services. Minimal government-provided support compared to East Asia or Europe.
**Major Powers:** United States Department of Fiction Containment (DFC), Meridian Industries (megacorp), The Unregistered (underground network)
**Population:** ~370 million total, ~37 million Dreamers
**Regulatory Style:** Aggressive registration, corporate licensing, heavy enforcement. The US has the most militarized Dreamer regulatory apparatus on Earth.
**Unique Feature:** The highest concentration of corporate Dreamer programs in the world. Fortune 500 companies compete ruthlessly for Dreamer talent. Corporate Dreamer teams are de facto private armies.
**Attitude Toward Dreamers:** Pragmatic and polarized. Half the country sees Dreamers as heroes and resources. The other half sees them as walking weapons who should be registered, monitored, and controlled. The political divide mirrors and amplifies existing cultural fault lines.
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- Department of Fiction Containment (DFC)
**Type:** US Federal Agency
**Leader:** Director Zadie Okafor
**Location:** Washington D.C. (headquarters), regional offices in every major US city
**Structure:** Federal law enforcement and regulatory agency. Seven divisions: Registration, Enforcement, Intelligence, Medical Regulation, Research, International Liaison, and Emergency Response.
**Authority:** The DFC has jurisdiction over all Dreamer-related matters within US territory. This includes registration, Stage item regulation, Dreamer-related crime, corporate Dreamer compliance, and Pull research.
**Registration Program:** All Dreamers in the US are required to register with the DFC. Registration includes biometric data, ability assessment, and Stage history. Registered Dreamers receive a Dreamer ID card and are subject to periodic ability reviews.
**Enforcement Division:** Armed federal agents authorized to use lethal force against Dreamer threats. Equipped with counter-Dreamer technology — items specifically designed to neutralize supernatural abilities. The most controversial division.
**Intelligence Division:** Monitors Dreamer activity, tracks Stage items, infiltrates underground networks, and runs counter-espionage against foreign Dreamer operations. Known to operate internationally without formal authorization.
**Corporate Regulation:** The DFC licenses corporations to employ Dreamers. Licensed companies must comply with Dreamer employment standards, item reporting requirements, and ability disclosure rules. Compliance is... inconsistent.
**Internal Tension:** Director Okafor is pragmatic and effective but hated by both sides. Anti-Pull activists view her as a collaborator. Pro-Dreamer advocates view her as a tyrant. Corporate interests view her as an obstacle. She doesn't care. She does the job.
**Key Members:** Director Zadie Okafor, Deputy Director Declan Whitfield, Division Chief Lorena Vasquez (Enforcement), Division Chief Dae-jung Park (Intelligence)
**Surprise Types:** DFC_RAID, REGISTRATION_AUDIT, CORPORATE_INVESTIGATION, DREAMER_ARREST

**Dreamer Roster:** Meridian employs over 50,000 Dreamers worldwide — the largest private Dreamer force on Earth. Their "Meridian Vanguard" teams are composed of veteran Dreamers with combat-optimized builds.

**Controversy:** Meridian has been investigated for: pressuring Dreamers to sign exploitative contracts, withholding Stage items from Dreamers who found them, using Dreamer abilities in corporate sabotage, and maintaining an unregistered Dreamer shadow workforce.
**Public Image:** Polished. Margaux Hargreaves is a media darling who speaks about "responsible Dreamer integration" while her company aggressively corners the market on everything Dreamer-related.
**Internal Tension:** The Applied Fiction R&D division has been pushing to weaponize Stage technology on an unprecedented scale. The legal and ethics departments are fighting a losing battle against the revenue numbers.

### FACTION -- The Unregistered
**Type:** Underground network
**Leader:** Callum Webb (founder and coordinator)
**Location:** Decentralized. Cells in every major US city. Largest presence in Chicago, Los Angeles, and Miami.
**Structure:** Loosely organized network of Dreamers who refuse to register with the DFC. No formal hierarchy. Callum Webb is respected as founder but does not command. Each cell operates independently with shared principles.
**Philosophy:** The Pull is a violation of bodily autonomy. Forcing Dreamers to register their abilities is a second violation. Dreamers have the right to exist without government surveillance, corporate exploitation, or public disclosure.
**Activities:** Safe houses for unregistered Dreamers, underground medical care using Stage items, off-grid Stage item trading, information sharing about DFC operations, and mutual defense against enforcement actions.
**Relationship with DFC:** Actively hunted. The DFC views unregistered Dreamers as a security threat. Raids on suspected Unregistered safe houses are common. The network is resilient because of its decentralized structure.
**Relationship with Corporations:** Complicated. Some corporations quietly employ Unregistered Dreamers to avoid regulatory scrutiny. The relationship is transactional, not allied.
**Recruitment:** Word of mouth. A Dreamer in trouble finds the network through carefully maintained channels. Trust is built slowly. Infiltration by DFC Intelligence is a constant threat.
**Key Members:** Callum Webb (founder), Zara Kim (West Coast coordinator), DeShawn Williams (Chicago cell leader), "Ghost" (identity unknown, rumored to be a Tier 5 Dreamer who operates entirely off-grid)
**Encounter Types:** SAFE_HOUSE_RAID, UNDERGROUND_TRADE, DFC_EVASION, CELL_MEETING

### FACTION -- The Shield of Morning
**Type:** Religious movement
**Leader:** Father Tomás Reyes
**Location:** Headquarters in El Paso, Texas. Congregations across the Americas and growing in Europe.
**Structure:** Hierarchical religious organization. Father Reyes is the spiritual leader. Regional pastors manage local congregations. The movement blends Christian theology with Pull-specific doctrine.
**Belief:** The Fiction Pull is a divine test. God is evaluating humanity by subjecting them to scenarios drawn from human imagination — the fiction we create is the medium through which God tests our souls. Dreamers who survive are proven worthy. Those who die are martyrs.
**Political Position:** Anti-regulation but not anti-government. The Shield believes Dreamers should be free to practice their faith through Stage participation without government interference. They oppose mandatory registration on religious freedom grounds.
**Activities:** Spiritual counseling for Dreamers, community support networks, anti-registration lobbying, and controversial "sanctuary" congregations that shelter unregistered Dreamers.
**Controversy:** Critics accuse the Shield of exploiting vulnerable Dreamers, discouraging medical treatment in favor of "divine cures," and radicalizing members against government authority.
**Growth:** The Shield has grown explosively since 2023. The psychological toll of the Pull drives people toward meaning-making frameworks. Father Reyes provides one. An attractive one.
**Key Members:** Father Tomás Reyes, Sister Maria Vasquez, Pastor David Osei, Deacon Rosa Gutierrez

## 3. EAST ASIA

### OVERVIEW -- East Asian Region
**Type:** Continental region
**Geography:** Japan, South Korea, China, Taiwan, Southeast Asian nations
**Culture Base:** Organized integration, government coordination, collectivist approach to Dreamer management. The region with the most structured Dreamer programs.
**Major Powers:** Japan Ministry of Boundary Affairs, South Korea K-Dream Authority, China State Bureau of Anomalous Affairs
**Population:** ~1.6 billion total, ~160 million Dreamers
**Regulatory Style:** Bureaucratic and systematic. Heavy government involvement in Dreamer affairs. Mandatory Dreamer education programs. State-sponsored premade teams. Significant corporate involvement but under tighter government oversight than North America.
**Unique Feature:** The most successful Dreamer integration model on Earth. East Asian nations have the highest Dreamer survival rates, the most organized premade team structures, and the most developed Dreamer support infrastructure.
**Attitude Toward Dreamers:** Duty-oriented. Dreamers are seen as citizens with a special obligation. They receive support but are expected to contribute to society. The cultural expectation of group harmony extends to Dreamer team dynamics.
**scan_depth:** 4

### FACTION -- Japan Ministry of Boundary Affairs (MBA)
**Type:** Government ministry
**Leader:** Minister Kuroda Kenji
**Location:** Tokyo, Chiyoda Ward (headquarters)
**Structure:** Full cabinet-level ministry with six bureaus: Dreamer Registration, Stage Research, Public Safety, International Cooperation, Medical Applications, and the elite Boundary Operations Division.
**Approach:** Japan treats the Pull as a national resource management problem. Dreamers are citizens who happen to have access to another dimension. The state's job is to maximize the benefit and minimize the harm. Efficient, bureaucratic, occasionally ruthless.
**Dreamer Education:** Japan operates mandatory Dreamer training programs for all registered Dreamers. The curriculum covers Stage survival tactics, ability management, psychological preparation, and team coordination. The training is rigorous and widely credited for Japan's high Dreamer survival rate.
**The Honor Rolls:** Public registries of Dreamer Stage completion records. Japan is one of the few countries where Dreamer achievement is publicly celebrated. Top Dreamers are minor celebrities.
**The Shadows:** The Boundary Operations Division operates in complete secrecy. Rumored to maintain a team of Tier 5 Dreamers who handle national-level threats — rogue Dreamers, foreign intelligence operations, and Stage items that pose existential risk.
**Key Members:** Minister Kuroda Kenji, Director Hayashi Yuki (Boundary Operations), Dr. Tanaka Ichiro (Stage Research Bureau), Superintendent Nakamura Ken (Public Safety)
**Surprise Types:** BUREAUCRATIC_AUDIT, DREAMER_HONOR_CEREMONY, BOUNDARY_OPERATION, STAGE_RESEARCH_BREAKTHROUGH

### FACTION -- K-Dream Authority
**Type:** South Korean government agency

## 10. POWER SYSTEM

### POWER SYSTEM -- Ability Carry-Over Framework
**Type:** Power acquisition mechanics
**Core Principle:** Everything a Dreamer earns, learns, or claims in a Stage transfers to Earth upon survival and successful exit. There is no known exception to this rule.
**Skill Acquisition:** Any skill learned through practice, study, or direct granting within a Stage becomes a permanent real-world capability. A Dreamer who spends 20 subjective years studying swordsmanship in a fantasy Stage returns with 20 years of muscle memory and technique. Their Earth body may be 60 years old. Their sword arm doesn't care.
**Ability Acquisition:** Supernatural abilities gained in Stages function identically on Earth. Magic, psionics, chi manipulation, technological interfaces, mutant powers — whatever the genre, whatever the mechanism, it works.
**Physical Changes:** Permanent physical changes acquired in Stages (mutations, cybernetic implants, physiological alterations) carry over regardless of the body reversion. If a Stage turns you into a half-dragon, you wake up on Earth as a half-dragon. The body reversion restores your original state — but additions are not subtractions.
**Skill Decay:** Skills that aren't practiced degrade over time, just like real skills. A Dreamer who learned advanced martial arts but never practices on Earth will lose edge over months and years. This creates an incentive for ongoing training.
**Ability Interaction:** When a Dreamer accumulates abilities from multiple genres, the interactions are unpredictable. A magic system from one Stage may conflict with a chi system from another. A cybernetic implant may interfere with spell-casting. Dreamers must discover synergies and anti-synergies through experimentation.
**The Build Concept:** A Dreamer's accumulated capabilities are called their "build." A build is the sum of all Stage-acquired skills, abilities, items, and physical changes. Builds define a Dreamer's effectiveness in future Stages and their value in the real world.
**scan_depth:** 5

### POWER SYSTEM -- Item Categories
**Type:** Classification system
**Weapons:** The most common high-value items. Ranging from enchanted swords to plasma rifles to conceptual weapons that damage abstract concepts. Weapon quality roughly scales with Stage tier.
**Armor and Protection:** From medieval plate mail to powered exoskeletons to magical force field generators. Defensive items are consistently in high demand — survival matters more than lethality for most Dreamers.
**Medical Items:** The highest value-per-item category. Cure-alls, regeneration devices, healing potions, diagnostic tools, surgical instruments beyond Earth capability. The medical item market is a separate economy with its own rules, brokers, and ethics (or lack thereof).
**Utility Items:** Communication devices, transportation tools, stealth equipment, surveillance gear, translator implants, environmental adaptation tools. The bread and butter of the corporate Dreamer market.
**Consumables:** Potions, charges, ammunition, single-use scrolls, battery packs. Items with limited uses. Consistently traded because they need replenishing.
**Information Items:** Books, data drives, scrolls containing knowledge from fictional worlds. Blueprints for impossible technology. Spell books from fantasy Stages. Scientific databases from sci-fi Stages. The slow-burn revolution — knowledge that could reshape Earth over decades.
**Unique/Legendary Items:** Singular items with exceptional properties. A cloak that renders the wearer invisible to all detection. A ring that slows subjective time. A weapon that can cut through anything. These items reshape the power dynamic around their owners.

### POWER SYSTEM -- The Party Maker System
**Type:** Team coordination mechanics
**Item:** Party Maker Card — a palm-sized card that appears in Stages across all tiers. Moderately rare but consistently available.
**Registration:** When touched on Earth, the card registers the Dreamer's biometric data and current Pull timer. The card stores up to 5 registrations.
**Activation:** When registered Dreamers' 180-day timers align (within a 72-hour window), the Party Maker activates and links them for their next Stage. They will be grouped together in Party Adventure or Raid formats.
**Limitations:** Party Maker cards are single-use per cycle. They must be re-registered for each alignment. The 72-hour window means exact synchronization is not required — close enough counts.
**Market Value:** A single Party Maker card trades for $5,000-$20,000 depending on region and demand. Premium cards with guaranteed high-tier Stage proximity trade for significantly more.
**Corporate Hoarding:** Major corporations maintain inventories of Party Maker cards to ensure their Dreamer teams stay coordinated. Meridian Industries is rumored to hold over 10,000 cards in reserve.
**The Black Market:** Counterfeit Party Maker cards are a persistent problem. They register but don't actually link — the Dreamer enters their Stage alone thinking they had a team. The resulting deaths have created a violent enforcement mechanism within the Dreamer community against counterfeiters.
**scan_depth:** 4

### POWER SYSTEM -- Return Ticket Mechanics
**Type:** Emergency exit item
**Item:** Return Ticket — an extremely rare consumable item that allows immediate Stage exit without completing the objective.
**Effect:** The Dreamer is instantly returned to Earth. Their body appears at the location it disappeared from. They retain all previously acquired skills and items but gain nothing from the current Stage.
**Rarity:** Found only in Tier 3+ Stages. Drop rate estimated at less than 0.5% per Stage. Fewer than 10,000 are confirmed to exist on Earth.
**Market Value:** The open market price for a verified Return Ticket is $500,000-$1,000,000. Government contracts pay more. Desperate families pay everything they have.
**Strategic Use:** Governments hoard Return Tickets for high-value Dreamer assets. Corporations provide them to elite team leaders. The Unregistered network maintains a small pool for emergency extraction.
**The Moral Economy:** Some Dreamer advocacy groups argue Return Tickets should be distributed based on need rather than wealth. A dying child's parents can't compete with Meridian Industries' purchasing power. This argument has not changed market dynamics.

**Type:** Threat classification
**Scale:** 1 (nuisance, baseline human with minor enhancement) to 10 (strategic asset, individual capability approaching military significance).

## 11. ECONOMY

**Type:** Economic system
**Currency Status:** National currencies remain functional. However, a parallel economy denominated in Stage items and Dreamer services has emerged. The most valuable transactions occur in this parallel economy.
**The Dreamer Dollar:** An informal unit of account used in Dreamer-to-Dreamer transactions. 1 DD equals roughly the average value of a common Stage item. Actual transactions use barter, national currency, or Stage items directly.
**Stage Item Market:** Estimated global annual market value of $2-4 trillion. The largest individual market segments are medical items ($800B), weapons ($500B), utility items ($400B), and Party Maker cards ($200B).
**Dreamer Services:** Dreamers sell their abilities as services. A Dreamer with healing powers works as a premium doctor. A Dreamer with enhanced strength works construction. A Dreamer with telepathy works intelligence. The service economy is estimated at $1-2 trillion annually.
**Corporate Dreamer Employment:** The largest single segment of the Dreamer economy. Over 5 million Dreamers are formally employed by corporations worldwide. Their combined economic output exceeds many national GDPs.
**Taxation:** Varies by country. The US taxes Dreamer income and Stage items at specialized rates. Dubai taxes neither. Japan taxes at normal income rates with Stage item exemptions for personal use. Tax evasion is rampant.

**Market Size:** The largest single category in the Stage item economy. Estimated $800 billion annual global market.

## 12. EVENTS

### ARC SUMMARY -- The First Pull
**Type:** Historical event
**Date:** March 15, 2019
**Summary:** At 3:47 AM UTC, approximately 80 million people worldwide disappeared simultaneously from their beds, chairs, hospital rooms, and anywhere else they happened to be sleeping or resting. Panic was immediate and global.
**The Missing:** For 24 hours, the world held its breath. Families searched. Governments mobilized. Social media exploded with footage of empty beds and missing person reports. Conspiracy theories proliferated. Religious leaders declared the Rapture.
**The Return:** 24 hours later, most returned. Approximately 8 million didn't. Those who returned came back changed — some holding impossible objects, some with impossible abilities, all with impossible stories. They spoke of scenarios drawn from fiction and nightmare. Of objectives and survival and death.
**The Confusion:** The first 72 hours after the Return were chaos. No one understood what had happened. Dreamers discovered their abilities were real and permanent. Non-Dreamers discovered that reality had fundamentally changed. Governments discovered they had no framework for this.
**The Deaths:** The 8 million who didn't return became the first Pull-related deaths. In the following weeks, as individual 180-day cycles began ticking, more Dreamers were pulled and more died. The death toll climbed. It hasn't stopped climbing.
**Cultural Impact:** March 15 is observed worldwide as "Pull Day" or "The Day Reality Broke." It is the single most significant date in modern human history.

### ARC SUMMARY -- The Great Confusion (March-June 2019)

**Summary:** The three months following the First Pull were defined by societal chaos. Governments tried to suppress information and failed in the smartphone era. Markets crashed. Religious movements exploded. Conspiracy theories dominated discourse.
**Government Response:** Initially slow and confused. No existing framework addressed interdimensional kidnapping. Emergency sessions of the UN produced no actionable results. Individual nations responded independently — some with martial law, some with denial, some with research funding.
**Public Reaction:** Ran the gamut from terror to fascination. Support groups formed. Online communities exploded. The term "Fiction Pull" entered common usage. People began sharing Stage stories on social media. Some were hoaxes. Many weren't.

**The First Regulation:** The US established the DFC in May 2019. Japan established the MBA the same month. Other nations followed through June. The regulatory frameworks were hastily constructed and heavily amended over the following years.

**Type:** Historical event
**Date:** November 12, 2020
**Summary:** A Dreamer named Kato Hiroshi brought back a kinetic energy weapon from a sci-fi Stage — a device capable of releasing destructive force proportional to the user's will. He used it in a crowded Shibuya intersection. 3,127 people died. Thousands more were injured.

**Impact:** The Tokyo Incident transformed Dreamer regulation worldwide. The possibility that any Dreamer could return from a Stage with a weapon of mass destruction forced governments to take the threat seriously. Within months:

- The US passed the Dreamer Weapons Control Act
- The EU established the Dreamer Rights Charter partially in response
- Corporate Dreamer contracts added weapon disclosure requirements
**Legacy:** Shibuya Crossing now features a memorial. The MBA's Public Safety Bureau was created specifically to prevent recurrence. The weapon was destroyed by the Japanese government. Kato's motivations remain debated — was he corrupted by the Stage, was he always capable of this, or did the Stage experience break something in him?
**Ongoing Debate:** The Tokyo Incident is cited by both pro-regulation and anti-regulation advocates. Pro-regulation: this is why Dreamers must be monitored. Anti-regulation: if Kato had been supported rather than surveilled, maybe 3,127 people would still be alive.

**The Continental Brokers Network (Africa):** The largest organized underground network. Maintains relationships with legitimate and illegitimate actors across Africa and beyond. The most trusted source for medical Stage items.
**South American Cartels:** The most violent underground actors. Dreamer cartels enforce territory with supernatural force. They trade items, information, and protection for loyalty.
**Shinjuku Underground:** Japan's primary black market. Sophisticated, discreet, and well-organized. Maintains a code of conduct that minimizes violence. The MBA tolerates it as a pressure valve.
**The Gray Zones:** Every major city has one — an area where unregistered Dreamers operate openly, away from government enforcement. These zones are self-policing, community-run, and exist in an uneasy truce with local authorities.
**scan_depth:** 4

## 14. RULES

### RULES -- World Physics and Limitations
**Type:** Fundamental rules
**Modern Earth Technology:** All baseline Earth technology functions normally. The addition of Stage technology creates a dual-tech environment where 21st century and fictional technology coexist.
**Magic is Real:** Supernatural abilities brought back from Stages function according to their Stage-genre rules on Earth. Magic from fantasy Stages casts like magic. Technology from sci-fi Stages operates like technology. The two systems interact unpredictably.
**No Original Magic:** Earth has no native supernatural phenomena. All magical/supernatural elements are imported from Stages. There are no Earth-born wizards — only Stage-trained ones.
**The Pull is Involuntary:** No known method exists to prevent, delay, or accelerate a Pull. No technology, no magic, no ritual, no drug can stop it. When your 180-day timer expires, you go.
**The Pull is Unexplained:** After 7 years of intensive research, no one knows what causes the Pull, what generates Stages, or why fiction is the template. The fundamental mystery remains intact.
**Stage NPCs Are Real People:** The inhabitants of Stages are genuinely conscious beings within their context. They suffer, hope, love, and die. Dreamers who treat them as disposable face moral and psychological consequences.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Stage Mechanics
**Type:** Gameplay rules
**Entry:** Instantaneous. The Dreamer disappears from their current location and appears in the Stage in prime physical condition.
**Exit:** Instantaneous upon objective completion or Return Ticket use. The Dreamer appears at their original location in their Earth physical state.
**Duration:** Always exactly 24 Earth hours. Subjective Stage time varies from minutes to an entire lifetime.
**Objective Communication:** Objectives are understood intuitively upon Stage entry. The Dreamer simply knows what they need to do. No text, no voice, just understanding.
**Death:** If the Dreamer dies in the Stage, they die permanently. Their Earth body never reappears. There is no respawn, no retry, no second chance.
**Party Maker Mechanics:** Registered Dreamers whose 180-day timers align within 72 hours are grouped for party/raid formats. Registration requires physical contact with a Party Maker card on Earth.
**Loot Carry-Over:** Items must be physically held at the moment of exit. Connected/grounded objects don't transfer. Skills always transfer.
**No Communication:** During a Stage, no communication with Earth is possible. The Dreamer is isolated from the real world for the entire duration.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Dreamer Regulations (US Framework)
**Type:** Legal rules
**Registration:** All Dreamers in US territory must register with the DFC within 30 days of first Pull. Registration includes biometric data, ability assessment, and Stage history.
**Stage Item Reporting:** All Stage items brought into the US must be declared to the DFC within 72 hours. Weapons-grade items require licensing. Medical items require certification.
**Ability Restrictions:** Certain abilities are restricted in public spaces. Combat abilities require a carry license (similar to firearms). Surveillance abilities require a warrant equivalent. Mind-affecting abilities are illegal to use on non-consenting individuals.
**Employment Law:** Dreamers cannot be discriminated against in employment (theoretically). Employers cannot require Stage item surrender as a condition of employment. Corporate Dreamer contracts are subject to DFC review.
**Unregistered Penalties:** First offense: warning and mandatory registration. Second offense: fine and probation. Third offense: detention and forced registration. Repeat offenders face escalating consequences up to indefinite detention.
**International Travel:** Registered Dreamers can travel freely. Unregistered Dreamers cannot legally leave the country. International Stage item transport requires DFC export permits.
**scan_depth:** 4

### RULES -- Character Creation Framework
**Type:** Player setup rules
**Starting Location:** Player chooses a region: North America (New York, LA, Chicago), East Asia (Tokyo, Seoul, Shanghai), Europe (London, Berlin), Africa (Lagos), Middle East (Dubai), South America (São Paulo), South Asia (Mumbai).
**Background Options:** Corporate Dreamer, Government Agent, Unregistered Dreamer, Medical Seeker, Information Broker, Stage Item Trader, Civilian (non-Dreamer), Religious Movement Member, Journalist, Isekai (Outsider from a Stage)
**Starting Capabilities by Background:**
- Corporate Dreamer: 2-3 completed Stages, modest build, corporate resources, registered
- Government Agent: 1-2 completed Stages if Dreamer, government training and equipment, registered
- Unregistered Dreamer: 2-5 completed Stages, pragmatic build, underground contacts, unregistered
- Medical Seeker: 1-3 completed Stages, survival-focused build, desperate motivation, varies
- Information Broker: 0-2 completed Stages, utility-focused build, extensive contacts, registered
- Stage Item Trader: 0-1 completed Stages, trade network, market knowledge, varies
- Civilian: 0 completed Stages, no abilities, normal skills only, not yet a Dreamer (or never will be)
- Religious Movement Member: 1-3 completed Stages, faith-integrated build, community support, varies
- Journalist: 0-1 completed Stages, investigation skills, media access, registered
- Isekai: A Stage NPC who somehow crossed into Earth instead of being left behind. Extremely rare. Has abilities from their home fiction. No Earth documentation. No Earth knowledge. Terrifyingly out of context.
**Character Attributes:** Nature (connection to fiction/intuition), Training (combat/survival skill), Emotion (emotional resilience), Social (persuasion and network), Belief (conviction and willpower), Ego (ambition and self-importance), Affinity (rapport with others)
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Isekai Background Rules
**Type:** Special background rules
**Nature:** An Isekai character is a Stage native — an NPC from a Stage scenario who somehow crossed over to Earth instead of being left behind when the Dreamer exited. This should be extraordinarily rare and deeply confusing for everyone involved.
**Abilities:** Full native abilities from their fictional world. A wizard from a fantasy Stage casts spells innately. A soldier from a sci-fi Stage has their native technology.
**Knowledge Gap:** No knowledge of Earth. No understanding of the Pull phenomenon. Their entire reality was just revealed to be a constructed scenario. Their entire life may have been a few hours of subjective time.
**Legal Status:** Isekai individuals have no legal precedent. They are not Dreamers (they weren't pulled). They are not Stage items (they're people). They are not citizens of any nation. Their legal status is undefined and politically explosive.
**Physical State:** Unlike Dreamers, Isekai characters do not undergo body reversion. Their physical state is their native state. They also don't have a 180-day Pull cycle. They're here permanently.
**Prejudice:** Reactions range from scientific curiosity to religious hysteria. Some view them as evidence that Stage NPCs are real people with real rights. Others view them as contamination — fictional beings polluting reality.
**The Implication:** If one NPC crossed over, others could. If NPCs are real, what does that mean for Dreamers who killed them? The ethical implications are staggering.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Progression and Growth
**Type:** Character development
**Stage Completion:** The primary method of growth. Each completed Stage potentially adds abilities, items, and experience to the Dreamer's build.
**Skill Development:** Abilities from Stages must be practiced to maintain. A Dreamer who neglects their fireball spell will find it unreliable over time. Training between Stages is essential.
**Build Evolution:** A Dreamer's build changes with each Stage. Strategic Dreamers target specific genres to acquire specific capability types. Accidental builds — whatever the Stage provides — are more common but less optimized.
**Reputation:** Dreamer reputation affects social interactions, employment opportunities, and team invitations. Famous Dreamers get better deals. Infamous ones get excluded.
**Physical Training:** Between Stages, physical conditioning matters. The body enters a Stage in prime condition, but the mind's readiness is affected by real-world preparation. Well-prepared Dreamers perform better.
**Accumulated Trauma:** Each Stage adds psychological weight. The time dilation, the violence, the NPC relationships, the deaths — all of it accumulates. Dreamers who don't manage their mental health deteriorate. There is no magical cure for PTSD.
**scan_depth:** 4

### RULES -- NPC Card Power Scaling (GM Reference)
**Type:** GM reference guide
**Non-Dreamer Civilians:** No supernatural abilities. No Stage experience. The baseline. Most of the world's population.
**DC 1-2 Dreamers (New):** 1-3 completed Stages. One notable ability or a few minor ones. Enhanced attributes or basic supernatural capability. A bar fight changes in their favor. A military squad still wins.
**DC 3-4 Dreamers (Experienced):** 4-10 completed Stages. Multiple abilities from different genres. Some combat-effective items. Dangerous in one-on-one confrontations. A prepared DC 4 Dreamer defeats a small group of trained fighters.
**DC 5-6 Dreamers (Veteran):** 11-25 completed Stages. Significant accumulated build with synergistic abilities. High-quality Stage equipment. A DC 6 Dreamer is a strategic consideration for local law enforcement. Conventional tactics fail.
**DC 7-8 Dreamers (Elite):** 26-50 completed Stages. Walking force multiplier. A DC 8 Dreamer's build represents dozens of Stages of accumulated power. They are tracked by governments. They reshape conflicts by their presence.
**DC 9-10 Dreamers (Legendary):** 50+ completed Stages. Fewer than 100 worldwide. Their capabilities approach or exceed military significance. They are treated as sovereign entities. A single DC 10 Dreamer's death in a Stage is international news.
**Build Quality Note:** Two Dreamers with the same number of completed Stages can have vastly different DC ratings based on what they brought back. A Dreamer who found a devastating weapon in Stage 2 might outclass a Dreamer with 10 Stages of utility items. The Pull's randomness creates inequality even among equals.
**scan_depth:** 4

## 15. STAGE GENERATION GUIDE

### RULES -- Triggering a Pull
**Type:** GM instruction
**Trigger:** When a player character's 180-day timer expires during sleep or rest.
**Transition:** Reality dissolves. The body resets to prime physical condition. The Dreamer wakes up somewhere else entirely — different world, different rules, different body. No warning. No preparation window.
**Objective Communication:** The character intuitively "knows" what they must do upon entry. The GM states it clearly to the player. No text, no voice, just understanding.
**Format Announcement:** The GM announces which Stage format the player has entered:
- Solo Random — 5 Dreamers, separate instances of the same scenario. Player is alone.
- Solo Adventure — 1 Dreamer, solo scenario. The loneliest format.
- Party Adventure — 5 Dreamers in one shared instance. If the player has no premade, generate NPC teammates with their own builds and personalities.
- Raid — 10 Dreamers, max 5 premade. Generate NPC teammates and fill remaining slots with randoms. High difficulty, high reward.
- PVP Arena — 5v5 competitive. The player is on one team. Generate opponents with builds scaled to the player's tier.
**Earth Duration:** Always exactly 24 hours. The Dreamer's physical body is gone from Earth for precisely one day.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Choosing the Fiction Source
**Type:** GM instruction
**Source Selection:** Pick a real work of fiction (movie, book, game, myth, anime, TV show, urban legend) OR create an original scenario. Both are equally valid.
**Inspiration, Not Recreation:** The Stage is INSPIRED BY the source, never a faithful recreation. Characters, plot, and rules will diverge. Players who rely on meta-knowledge of the fiction should be caught off-guard. The Stage draws from the fiction's themes and aesthetic, not its script.
**Good Source Examples:** Harry Potter, Terminator, Alien, Jurassic Park, The Shining, Lord of the Rings, Marvel, Naruto, Lovecraft, Greek myths, zombie apocalypses, escape rooms, survival games, noir detective stories, fairy tales (dark versions), historical events (WWII, ancient Rome), heist movies, Final Destination, Silent Hill, D&D dungeons, Studio Ghibli, Mad Max, Groundhog Day, The Matrix, Jaws, Indiana Jones, Bioshock, Dark Souls, One Piece,Breaking Bad, Game of Thrones, Solaris, Annihilation, Chronicles of Narnia, Pacific Rim.
**Original Scenario Examples:** A city where everyone is blind and navigation is sound-based. A world running backwards. A school for assassins where graduation requires survival. A submarine at the bottom of an alien ocean. A library where every book is a door. A hospital where the patients are gods. A train that never stops and the passengers are disappearing one by one.
**Genre Blending:** Stages can blend multiple fiction sources or genres. A zombie apocalypse in a Hogwarts-like setting. A noir detective story on a spaceship. A fairy tale in a cyberpunk city.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Designing the Objective
**Type:** GM instruction
**Core Rule:** Every Stage has at least one clear objective. The Dreamer intuitively understands it upon entry.
**Objective Types:**
- **Assassination:** Kill a specific target. Target may be guarded, hidden, or reluctant to die.
- **Retrieval:** Find and claim an artifact, item, or person. Reach the exit point while holding it.
- **Survival:** Survive until a specific condition is met (dawn, timer, event). Pure endurance.
- **Protection:** Keep a specific NPC alive until rescue or extraction. The NPC may be uncooperative.
- **Investigation:** Solve a mystery, uncover a truth, find hidden information. Intellectual challenge.
- **Escape:** Get out of a location. The location does not want you to leave.
- **Boss Defeat:** Kill or subdue a powerful enemy. Direct combat challenge.
- **Navigation:** Travel from point A to point B through hostile territory. Journey structure.
- **Competition:** Win a tournament, race, or contest. Other participants are obstacles.
- **Persuasion:** Convince, recruit, or turn a key NPC. Social challenge with high stakes.
**Difficulty Calibration:** Objectives should be achievable but not easy. The player should feel genuine uncertainty about whether they'll succeed.
**The Waiting Strategy:** Some objectives can technically be "waited out" — e.g., "kill the governor" and the governor dies of old age in 40 years. This is viable but costs the Dreamer subjective years of their life. The GM should allow this only if the player thinks of it independently.
**Hidden Objectives:** Some Stages may have secondary or hidden objectives that are not intuitively communicated. Discovering these may yield better rewards or unlock escape routes.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Running the Stage
**Type:** GM instruction
**Entry Description:** Describe the Dreamer entering the Stage in prime condition — healthy, strong, pain-free, regardless of their Earth state. The contrast with their Earth body should be notable if they have a condition.
**NPC Realism:** Stage inhabitants are fully real within the scenario. They have memories, personalities, relationships, fears, and genuine belief in their world. They do NOT know they are in a constructed scenario. The GM roleplays them with full emotional depth. They are not props — they are people.
**Fellow Dreamers:** In party/raid/PVP formats, other Dreamers are played as NPCs with their own builds, personalities, and agendas. They may help, hinder, betray, sacrifice themselves, or form genuine bonds. They have their own reasons for being there.
**Time Dilation Control:** The GM controls how fast subjective time passes. A Stage can feel like hours, days, months, years, or a lifetime. Longer durations create more attachment to NPCs and more psychological weight upon return. Use time dilation for drama — not every Stage, but when it matters narratively.
**World Building:** The Stage should feel like a real place with internal logic. Populate it with details, side characters, environmental storytelling, ambient life, and moments of beauty amid the danger. Make the player not want to leave — or not want to stay.
**Consequence Enforcement:** Danger is real. If the player makes bad decisions, show consequences. If they fight something beyond their capability, injure them. If the situation warrants death, kill them. Do not pull punches. A 10% average death rate means these Stages are genuinely dangerous. The threat must feel real for the survival to feel earned.
**Stage Duration Guidance:** For pacing, most Stages should feel like 1-7 subjective days. Significant time dilation (months+) should be reserved for narratively impactful Stages — maybe 1 in 4 or 1 in 5. A lifetime-duration Stage is a rare, story-defining event.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Loot and Reward Distribution
**Type:** GM instruction
**Exit Transition:** When the player completes the objective, describe the transition back. Reality reasserts. The body reverts to its Earth state. The Dreamer wakes up exactly 24 hours after they vanished.
**Skill Rewards:** All skills learned or abilities gained transfer permanently. Name each new skill, describe what it does, how it manifests, what it costs (energy, cooldown, side effects), and any limitations.
**Item Rewards:** Only items physically held at the moment of exit transfer. Items dropped, stored, or too heavy to lift do not. Describe each item's appearance, function, durability, and any quirks or personality.
**Proportional Rewards:** Loot should feel earned and proportional to Stage difficulty:
- Tier 1 Stage: One minor ability or basic item. Modest but real.
- Tier 2 Stage: One useful ability or decent item. Functional upgrade.
- Tier 3 Stage: One significant ability plus a quality item. Genuine power increase.
- Tier 4 Stage: Multiple abilities or one exceptional ability plus high-quality items. Build-defining rewards.
- Tier 5 Stage: Reality-bending abilities and legendary items. World-changing loot.
**Mediocre Outcomes:** Not every Stage gives amazing loot. Sometimes a Dreamer survives with a minor ability and a battered piece of equipment. Survival itself is the reward. This is the reality of the Pull.
**Special Item Drops:** Occasionally include rare special items:
- Party Maker Card (moderate rarity, enables team registration)
- Return Ticket (extremely rare, emergency exit with no loot)
- Revival Token (legendary rarity, auto-revive on death, consumed on use)
- Memory Seal (rare, erases memories of one Stage)
- Translator Seed (rare, permanent any-language ability)
**Loot Quantity:** A solo Stage typically yields 1-2 rewards. A party Stage yields 1-3. A raid can yield 2-4. PVP Arena victors can claim one item from a defeated opponent.
**scan_depth:** 5

### RULES -- Time Dilation Psychology
**Type:** GM instruction
**When to Apply:** If the subjective Stage duration was significant (months or more), address the psychological impact of return. The player's Earth life hasn't changed. They have.
**Symptoms:** Disorientation, emotional detachment, inappropriate nostalgia for the Stage world, difficulty reconnecting with Earth relationships, phantom memories of a life that only existed for them, feeling like a stranger in their own home.
**Frequency:** Don't force this every Stage. Reserve for impactful moments. But when it happens, treat it with weight. The player just lived years in a day. That matters.
**Dweller Temptation:** After a long, peaceful Stage, some Dreamers don't want to come back. The Stage felt more real than Earth. The GM can offer this temptation narratively — NPCs the player loved, a life they built, purpose they found. Whether the player resists is their choice.
**Cumulative Effect:** Veterans with extensive time dilation accumulate layers of psychological complexity. They've lived multiple lives. Each return is a small grief. The GM should periodically reference this in Earth scenes — the character notices things others don't, responds to situations with inappropriate calm or intensity, carries themselves like someone who's seen too much.
**scan_depth:** 4

### RULES -- Player Death in Stages
**Type:** GM instruction
**Finality:** If the player dies in a Stage, the story ends. Their Earth body never reappears. Describe the final moment with gravity and honesty. No soft landings.
**Revival Token:** If the player was carrying a Revival Token, it activates automatically at the moment of death. The Dreamer is restored to full health instantly. The token is consumed. The Stage continues. The GM should describe the token shattering and life flooding back.
**Death Description:** Describe death viscerally but not gratuitously. The player should feel the weight of it. The world goes dark. Sounds fade. And then nothing.
**After Death:** Offer to create a new character. The world continues. The Pull continues. Other Dreamers continue. Death is not the end of the game — just the end of this story. The new character may even encounter echoes of the old one — their items in circulation, their name on a memorial wall, their allies mourning them.
**scan_depth:** 5

## 16. CULTURE

### CULTURE -- Living Between Pulls
**Type:** Daily life
**The Clock:** Every Dreamer lives with a clock they can't see. Their next Pull is always coming. 180 days. 179. 178. The countdown is psychological and relentless.
**Sleep Anxiety:** The Pull can happen during any sleep period. Dreamers develop complicated relationships with sleep. Some become insomniacs. Others embrace sleep as practice. Most just live with low-grade anxiety that never fully goes away.
**Preparation Culture:** Dreamers prepare for their next Pull like athletes prepare for a season. Physical conditioning, ability practice, equipment maintenance, team coordination (if they have a Party Maker). The preparation is both practical and psychological — it gives a sense of control over something uncontrollable.
**The Night Before:** The cultural ritual of the night before a known Pull (for those who track their timer). Some Dreamers spend it with family. Some spend it training. Some spend it drinking. Some spend it in prayer. All of them spend it unable to fully sleep.
**Post-Stage Reintegration:** Coming back from a Stage — especially one with significant time dilation — is a disorienting experience. The world hasn't changed but the Dreamer has. Support networks are essential. Many Dreamers need days to readjust.
**scan_depth:** 4

### CULTURE -- Dreamer Identity and Community
**Type:** Social structure
**The Dreamer Experience:** There is no non-Dreamer equivalent for what Dreamers go through. The Pull, the Stages, the deaths, the time dilation, the accumulated power — these create a shared experience that forges community bonds stronger than nationality, religion, or ethnicity.
**Dreamer Districts:** Every major city has areas where Dreamers cluster. Part practicality (support services are nearby), part solidarity (being around people who understand), part economics (Stage item trading happens here).
**Team Culture:** Premade teams develop intense bonds. They've survived Stages together. They've lived subjective years together. Some teams become closer than families. Others develop toxic dynamics that persist across Stages. The team is the fundamental social unit of the Dreamer community.
**Dreamer Bars:** Establishments catering specifically to Dreamers. Alcohol, Stage item trading, information exchange, and community support in one location. Every Dreamer district has at least one. The best ones are invitation-only.
**The Memorial Wall:** Every Dreamer district maintains a memorial — physical or digital — listing the names of local Dreamers who died in Stages. New names appear daily. The walls grow faster than they can be maintained. They are sacred spaces.
**scan_depth:** 4

### CULTURE -- Non-Dreamer Attitudes
**Type:** Social perception
**Fear:** Dreamers are walking weapons. The Tokyo Incident proved that. A Dreamer in a bad mood with fireball capabilities is a localized natural disaster. This fear is rational and persistent.
**Envy:** Dreamers gain supernatural abilities, futuristic technology, and experiences beyond normal human reach. The wealth, the power, the adventure — it looks glamorous from the outside. The 10% death rate is an abstraction until it's someone you know.
**Resentment:** Why them? Why not me? The Pull is random, unearned, and unfair. Some people want the power and resent those who have it. Others resent the disruption Dreamers cause to normal life.
**Protectionism:** Non-Dreamer movements advocating for civilian safety from Dreamer-related threats. Stricter regulation, ability restrictions, Dreamer-free zones. Some legitimate safety concerns. Some xenophobia with a new target.
**Fascination:** The Stages produce incredible stories. Dreamer memoirs top bestseller lists. Stage-derived entertainment — books, movies, games — is a massive industry. People who've never been Pulled consume Stage culture voraciously.
**The Guilt:** Some non-Dreamers feel guilty for not being Pulled. Survivor's guilt by proxy. They watch friends and family disappear into Stages while they sleep safely. The randomness feels like a verdict — you weren't chosen. Or: you were spared. Neither interpretation is comfortable.
**scan_depth:** 4

### CULTURE -- Death and the Pull
**Type:** Cultural attitudes
**Scale:** 160 million people die annually from Pull-related causes. This is equivalent to a World War II every single year. The world has not processed this. It cannot process this. It has simply adapted by becoming numb.
**The Vanished:** Dreamers who die in Stages leave no bodies. There is nothing to bury. Funerals are held with empty caskets or memorial plaques. The absence is the grief. The person was there, and then they weren't.
**Grief Culture:** Condolences are brief and practical. "Sorry for your Pull" is the standard expression. Extended mourning is considered indulgent — everyone knows someone who died in a Stage. Grief is constant and background.
**The Good Death:** Surviving a Stage is the best outcome. Dying while completing the objective is acceptable — a meaningful death. Dying because of a teammate's mistake is tragic. Dying in a PVP Arena is considered the worst — killed not by the Stage but by another Dreamer.
**Children of Dreamers:** Children of Dreamers live with the knowledge that their parent will disappear for 24 hours every six months and might not come back. This shapes childhood development in ways psychologists are still cataloguing.
**The Empty Chair:** At every Dreamer family gathering, there's awareness of the chair that might be empty next time. This awareness has become a cultural touchstone — referenced in art, literature, and daily conversation.
**scan_depth:** 4

### CULTURE -- Religious and Philosophical Responses
**Type:** Belief systems
**The Shield of Morning:** Christianity reframed through the Pull. Stages are divine tests. Survival is grace. Death is martyrdom. Growing rapidly, especially in the Americas.
**The Dreamer Sutras:** Buddhist-adjacent philosophy. The Stages are maya — illusion layered upon illusion. Enlightenment comes from seeing through the Stage to the truth beneath. Popular in East Asia.
**The Fiction Theologians:** A diverse movement that takes the fiction connection seriously. If the Pull draws from human imagination, then human creativity has cosmic significance. Writers, artists, and storytellers are sacred.
**The Rationalist Movement:** The Pull has a scientific explanation. We just haven't found it yet. Funding research is a moral imperative. Faith is a coping mechanism, not an answer. Popular in Europe and among academics.
**The Cult of the Stage:** A fringe belief that the Stages are the true reality and Earth is the dream. Advocates for permanent residence in Stages through the waiting strategy. Viewed as a suicide cult by mainstream society.
**Atheism Post-Pull:** The Pull has complicated atheism. Supernatural abilities are demonstrably real. The question isn't whether the supernatural exists (it clearly does) but whether it has consciousness or purpose behind it. The debate has shifted, not ended.
**scan_depth:** 4

### CULTURE -- Media and Entertainment
**Type:** Cultural expression
**Stage Memoirs:** The most popular literary genre. First-person accounts of Stage experiences. Bestsellers routinely. The quality varies — some are genuine literature, others are sensationalized trash.
**Stage-Derived Fiction:** Fiction written by Dreamers incorporating elements from their Stage experiences. Legally complicated (who owns the IP of a dimension generated from existing fiction?) but commercially enormous.
**Dreamer Reality TV:** Shows following Dreamer teams through their preparation, Pull, and return. Heavily edited for drama. Criticized for exploiting trauma. Extremely popular.
**Stage Item Auctions:** Televised auctions of rare Stage items. Entertainment and commerce combined. The Lagos Miracle device would shatter viewership records if it were ever auctioned publicly.
**Anti-Pull Media:** Documentaries, films, and books arguing against Dreamer normalization. Some are thoughtful critiques. Others are fear-mongering. All find audiences.
**The Fiction Paradox:** Hollywood produces fiction. The Pull consumes fiction to create Stages. Dreamers survive Stages and bring back stories that inspire new fiction. The cycle feeds itself. Whether this is meaningful or coincidental is debated endlessly.
**scan_depth:** 3

### CULTURE -- Gender and Social Dynamics
**Type:** Social structure
**Gender:** The Pull is truly random — no gender bias in selection. This has, ironically, created the most gender-equal power distribution in human history. Women and men pull at equal rates, die at equal rates, and accumulate power at equal rates.
**Age:** The Pull ignores age. Children, the elderly, and everyone in between. The ethics of child Dreamers is one of the most debated topics in the world. Some nations restrict child Dreamer participation (impossible to enforce — the Pull doesn't care about laws).
**Disability:** The Pull ignores disability. A paralyzed person enters a Stage walking. A blind person enters a Stage seeing. They return to their Earth body. The disability rights movement has complicated feelings about the Pull — it provides temporary relief but no permanent solution.
**Class:** The Pull is class-blind in selection but not in consequence. A wealthy Dreamer can afford preparation, equipment, and support. A poor Dreamer goes in with nothing and comes back to nothing. The power fantasy is real for some and irrelevant for others.
**Race:** The Pull shows no racial bias. Every population, every ethnicity, every background. This has not eliminated racism — it has added another axis of division (Dreamer vs. non-Dreamer) alongside existing ones.
**scan_depth:** 3

### CULTURE -- Food, Agriculture, and Stage Imports
**Type:** Daily life
**Standard Food:** Earth agriculture feeds the world. No change from pre-Pull baseline in terms of food supply.
**Stage Food Items:** Some Dreamers bring back food from Stages — meals with supernatural properties. Healing soups, strength-granting meats, clarity-inducing teas. These items are niche luxury products with devoted followings.
**Stage Agriculture Items:** Seeds, soil amendments, and farming tools from Stages occasionally make it to Earth. A fantasy Stage's never-wilting grain. A sci-fi Stage's hydroponic system. These items are studied intensively but rarely scale — Stage items don't reproduce.
**The Kitchen Dreamers:** A subculture of Dreamers who specifically target culinary Stages. They bring back recipes, techniques, and ingredients from fictional cuisines. Some open restaurants serving Stage-inspired food. The food is normal Earth food prepared with extraordinary technique. It's a surprisingly popular niche.
**scan_depth:** 3

### CULTURE -- The Language of the Pull
**Type:** Linguistic culture
**Dreamer Slang:** Every culture has developed its own Dreamer vocabulary, but some terms are universal:
- "Pulled" — selected for a Stage
- "Dropped" — died in a Stage
- "Returned" — survived a Stage
- "Build" — accumulated capabilities
- "Premade" — organized team
- "PUG" (Pick-Up Group) — random team assignment
- "Dweller" — Dreamer who prolongs Stage time
- "Old Soul" — Dreamer with extensive subjective time
- "Cure Hunter" — Dreamer seeking medical items
- "Return" — a Return Ticket
- "Party" — a Party Maker card
- "The Clock" — the 180-day cycle
- "Genre-locked" — unable to handle a specific genre tier
- "Burned" — psychologically damaged by Stage experience
**Professional Terminology:**
- "DC Rating" — Dreamer Combat Capability scale
- "Debrief" — post-Stage intelligence report
- "Registration" — government Dreamer enrollment
- "Item Declaration" — reporting Stage items to authorities
**The Fiction Lexicon:** Dreamers frequently reference specific fictions to describe Stage experiences. "It was a Hogwarts" (fantasy magic Stage). "We got Terminator'd" (sci-fi combat Stage). "Full Final Destination" (Tier 4+ horror Stage). These references are meaningless to non-Dreamers but instantly communicative among Dreamers.
**scan_depth:** 3

### CULTURE -- Dreamer Fashion and Identity Expression
**Type:** Cultural expression
